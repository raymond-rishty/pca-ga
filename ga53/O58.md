# O58 — Amend BCO 10-4 to Specify that Clerks Must Be Elders
**Targets:** BCO 10-4 · **Source:** Calvary · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-58_Calvary_10-4.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-58_Calvary_10-4.pdf)
**What it does:** Adds a sentence to BCO 10-4 — "The clerk shall be a Ruling or Teaching Elder within the Presbyterian Church in America" — so that all court clerks (Session, Presbytery, GA) must be ordained elders.
**Cites in its own grounds:** None. The grounds cite only BCO provisions (12-1, 13-1, 35-8, 8-9, 7-2), RAO 3-5.a (GA Stated Clerk must be a TE or RE), and the PCA *Handbook for Clerks of Session* — no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
None found. (See Note — all seven spine cases cite 10-4 only for the records / extracts-as-evidence function, not the elder-qualification of clerks; pruned as incidental.)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
None found. (See Note — both spine inquiries construe the stated clerk's records/dissemination *duties*, not who may serve as clerk; pruned as incidental.)

### Prior overtures
None found. (See Note — the one spine match, GA50 O20 on electronic records, does not bear on clerk eligibility.)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 10-4** — 2 citations, both records/election-keeping rather than eligibility: Heritage (28th, 2000) re a complaint referenced in minutes but missing from the appendix; Southern New England (33rd, 2005) re "no record of election of stated clerk" (self-rectified). Bears only weakly, in that it confirms presbyteries formally elect a stated clerk under 10-4; neither touches the elder-qualification question. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)

### Note
BCO 10-4 packs two distinct subjects into one section: (a) the election and qualification of clerks — what O58 changes — and (b) the clerk's duty to preserve records and grant extracts that serve as evidence. The provision-exact join is high-recall but every action it returns construes subject (b), not (a). Pruned judicial cases: 2001-6 and 2002-1 (RE Kim v. Korean Eastern, [minutes](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p96) / [minutes](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p114)); 2010-18 and 2010-20 ([Review of Presbytery Records citations](../cases/ga39_2011__2010-18_2010-19_2010-20_2010-21_2010-22_2010-23.md), [minutes](../markdown/ga39_2011.md#ga39-p603)); 2011-7 (PCA v. Warrior, [minutes](../markdown/ga40_2012.md#ga40-p563)); 2012-10 ([PCA v. Korean Capital](../cases/ga41_2013__2012-10.md), [minutes](../markdown/ga41_2013.md#ga41-p618)); 2012-5 (Hedman v. Pacific Northwest, [minutes](../markdown/ga41_2013.md#ga41-p585)) — all about records, citations, or extract evidence. Pruned inquiries: 17-82 III ([certificate of dismissal must bear the stated clerk's signature](../inquiries/ga17_1989__ci01.md)) and App. O IV.B ([scope of the stated clerk's duty to disseminate information](../inquiries/ga44_2016__ci05.md)) — both on the clerk's duties, not eligibility. Pruned overture: GA50 O20 ([electronic records research](../markdown/ga50_2023.md#ga50-p1015)), unrelated. No bearing actions survive: the corpus contains no case, inquiry, or prior overture construing whether a court clerk must be an ordained elder. The overture rests on RAO 3-5.a (already requiring the GA Stated Clerk to be a TE/RE), which O58 would extend down to all courts. No renumbering affects BCO chapter 10 (10-4 has been stable). Topical search of clerk-related overtures surfaced only RAO / Stated-Clerk-of-GA term and commendation overtures, none bearing on the elder-eligibility of court clerks.
