# O80 — Erect Ad Interim Committee on Critical Theory and Critical Race Theory
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the negative *(vote 105-25-2)*
**Targets:** (committee — no BCO provision; erect AIC under RAO 9-1) · **Source:** Calvary · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-80_Calvary_CRT.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-80_Calvary_CRT.pdf)

**What it does:** Asks GA53 to erect and convene a new Ad Interim Study Committee (RAO 9-1) to study Critical Race Theory from a Reformed exegetical, confessional, and theological perspective — assessing whether CRT conforms to Scripture and the Westminster Standards — and to give the Assembly pastoral guidance on addressing CRT; moderator-appointed, budget $10,000/year from designated AC gifts.

**Cites in its own grounds:** GA52 (2025) Christian Nationalism study committee (cited as the right-wing counterpart this CRT study would "compare and contrast"); GA52 (2025) overture directing the Stated Clerk not to collect ethnicity statistics — noted as answered in the affirmative (Overtures Cmte 76-28-3; floor 1184-361). No prior judicial cases or constitutional inquiries cited.

### Note
O80 is a committee-erection overture with no BCO target, so the deterministic provision-join returned no spine hits; all bearing actions were found by concept search in /workspace/index (OVERTURES.md). It is a near-verbatim re-run of **[GA48 O47](../overtures/ga48_2021__o47.md)** (Form Study Committee to Examine CRT, declined 2021) and shares ground with GA48 O36 (White Supremacy), GA49 O41 (declare CRT inconsistent), and GA50 O18 (reject critical-theory ideology) — every prior CRT/critical-theory request has been declined or "answered" without a committee, even as parallel ideology committees succeeded (GA44 O45 racial reconciliation; GA52 O47 Christian Nationalism, which O80 cites as its counterweight). Cross-link the GA53 Christian Nationalism companion overture (**O90**, append Reformed Christian Politics to the Christian Nationalism AIC report) noted in the first pass. **Pruned as incidental:** SJC cases 2008-18 (Hutchinson & Bulkeley v. Western Carolina) and 2009-02 (Morton Smith v. Western Carolina) — both concern an RE's published views on "race and IQ" and turn purely on discipline/due-process (BCO 38-1, 24-6.4), not on whether GA should study CRT; they do not bear on the committee question. No constitutional inquiries or CCB advice address CRT or study-committee erection. No renumbering caveat applies (no provision targeted).
