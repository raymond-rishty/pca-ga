# O83 — Require Courses on Scripture and Doctrine at Covenant College and Covenant Seminary Be Taught by Ordained Elders
**Targets:** (institutions — no BCO amendment; grounds invoke BCO 7-2, 8-1, 8-5, 21-4, 12-5, 21-5, 24-1) · **Source:** Northwest Georgia Presbytery (to CC, CTS) · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-83_NWGA_BibleInstructors.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-83_NWGA_BibleInstructors.pdf)

> *Referred by the Stated Clerk to CC and CTS; not considered by the Overtures Committee.*

**What it does:** Asks the Assembly to affirm that courses systematically teaching the Bible or Christian doctrine at Covenant College or Covenant Seminary should be taught only by men examined and ordained as teaching or ruling elders in NAPARC denominations, and to direct both Agencies to amend their bylaws to enforce this. It rests on the prohibition of women teaching or exercising authority over men (1 Tim. 2:12–13) read as a binding creation ordinance reaching beyond gathered worship.

**Cites in its own grounds:** No prior GA cases, overtures, or inquiries cited by number — only Scripture and BCO provisions (7-2; 8-1; 8-5; 21-4; 12-5; 21-5; 24-1).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[Case 99-1 — The John Wood Matter (Investigative Report)](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p70)** (GA29/2001, other — no strong presumption of guilt; process declined) — The single judicial action squarely on the question O83 presses: whether a woman teaching/speaking authoritatively (Dr. Linda Eure at Cedar Springs' evening worship) and a TE's views favoring women preaching fall outside the Standards. The SJC declined process, finding "no clear evidence that TE Wood's views are outside the bounds of our Standards" — i.e., the Assembly's courts have not treated the 1 Tim. 2:12 boundary as settled in the maximal direction O83 assumes. [minutes](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p70)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **GA47 CCB II.B** (GA47/2019) — On Northwest Georgia Presbytery's own Overture 2 to release CTS from Assembly oversight, the CCB found the overture "creates a conflict with the RAO and Corporate By-Laws." Directly bearing: O83 (same presbytery, same institutions) likewise directs the Agencies to amend their bylaws, and the same RAO/Bylaws-governance objection is likely to recur. [page](../inquiries/ga47_2019__ci02.md)
- **GA50 CCB II.K** (GA50/2023) — On Bryce Avenue's Overture 15, the CCB found that amending BCO 53 to disallow women from exhorting/preaching/teaching in corporate worship is "not in conflict with the BCO." Bears as the closest recent CCB reading of the women-teaching boundary; note it concerns *worship* (BCO 53), whereas O83 reaches into *academic* instruction outside worship. [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci11.md)
