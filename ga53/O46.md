# O46 — Add BCO 29-5 on Public Allegations without Prior Reporting to the Court of Jurisdiction as Probable Violations of the Ninth Commandment
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the negative *(vote 107-25-1)*
**Targets:** BCO 29-5 (new) · **Source:** New York State · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-46_NewYorkState_29-5.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-46_NewYorkState_29-5.pdf)

> **Grounds:** The Ninth Commandment and WLC 144–145 (cf. BCO 29-1) already make such behaviors offenses and should be used when necessary. Further, this language may have unintended consequences of silencing those who are rightly speaking out against sin.

**What it does:** Adds a new BCO 29-5 declaring that any public statement made outside a church court alleging an offense or unconstitutional action of a church, Presbytery, agency, committee, or member — that was not first reported to the court of original jurisdiction (ordinarily after a reasonable effort to contact the alleged errant party) — "shall be considered a probable violation of the ninth commandment and an offense under this chapter to be addressed by normal judicial process (BCO 31, etc.)."

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the rationale quotes only Westminster Larger Catechism Q/A 144 and 145; it cites no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[1993-11 — McDade v. Susquehanna Valley Presbytery](../cases/ga23_1995__1993-11.md)** (GA23/1995, denied) — Directly on the overture's premise: a slander / ninth-commandment complaint where the SJC upheld the Presbytery's choice to *investigate before charging* and found proper procedure followed. Construes how 9th-commandment allegations move through judicial process. [minutes](../markdown/ga23_1995.md#ga23-p110)
- **[2002-14 — Lachman v. Philadelphia Presbytery](../cases/ga32_2004__2002-14.md)** (GA32/2004, denied) — Ninth-commandment offense charged through the Matthew 18 process before the session as court of original jurisdiction — the "proper channels first" model O46 would codify. [minutes](../markdown/ga32_2004.md#ga32-p73)
- **[2021-6 — Herron et al. v. Central Indiana Presbytery](../cases/ga49_2022__2021-06.md)** (GA49/2022, not sustained) — Squarely tests whether a *public statement* by a court (a press release about an investigation) was proper. Most on-point on the line O46 draws between public airing and reporting to the court. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p785)
- **[2021-13 — Dudt v. Northwest Georgia Presbytery](../cases/ga49_2022__2021-13.md)** (GA49/2022, sustained) — RE convicted by his Session partly for a 9th-commandment violation via an email to the congregation; SJC reversed. Cuts *against* the overture's premise — shows the risk in treating a member's out-of-court public communication as an offense. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p909)
- **[2022-7 — Harrell et al. v. Covenant Presbytery](../cases/ga50_2023__2022-07.md)** (GA50/2023, sustained) — Indictment-specificity case (BCO 29/32) in which slander / ninth-commandment counts were charged; bears on how a "probable 9th-commandment violation" must be pleaded as an offense. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga50_2023.md#ga50-p860)
- **[2022-10 — Presbyterian Church in America v. TE Daniel Herron](../cases/ga50_2023__2022-10.md)** (GA50/2023, other) — Trial touching a defamation lawsuit and the public dimension of allegations within BCO 29/31 process; bears on policing public allegations as offenses. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga50_2023.md#ga50-p892)
- **[2024-2 — Gleason v. Houston Metro Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-02.md)** (GA52/2025, dismissed) — Holds that charging a *court* (session/committee) with a ninth-commandment violation is a category error. Directly cautions against O46's reach to public allegations against "a church, Presbytery, agency, committee." ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p971)
