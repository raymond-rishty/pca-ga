# O13 — Amend BCO 15-2 Regarding Session Commissions
**Targets:** BCO 15-2 · **Source:** Great Lakes · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-13_GreatLakes_15.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-13_GreatLakes_15.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds a third paragraph to BCO 15-2 governing commissions appointed by a **Session** (distinct from Presbytery commissions): each shall consist of at least two elders "with any combination of teaching or ruling elders," and the quorum shall be the greater of two elders or one-half of the membership. Resolves a disputed practice — some argue Session commissions should mirror the Presbytery rule (2 TE + 2 RE), which would leave many Sessions (e.g., all-RE Sessions) unable to form one.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the grounds cite only BCO 15-1; no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2021-3 — DeJong v. Session of Village Seven Presbyterian Church](../cases/ga49_2022__2021-03.md)** (GA49/2022, sustained) — directly construes BCO 15-2 as applied to a **Session** governance commission: holds the list of matters commissions may execute under 15-2 is not exhaustive, but a commission's scope must be specifically defined by the referring court (BCO 15-1); two provisions of the Village Seven governance commission were held to extend unconstitutionally beyond those limits. This is the only spine case construing BCO 15-2 on a Session commission's constitutional scope — precisely O13's target. [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p881)
- **[2009-10 — Woodward v. Western Carolina Presbytery](../cases/ga38_2010__2009-05_2009-08_2009-09_2009-10.md)** (GA38/2010, sustained in part) — Presbytery appointed a commission under BCO 15-1 & 2; the Woodward sub-complaint (2009-10) was dismissed on standing (BCO 43-1/11-4) rather than on commission-composition grounds, but the case documents how BCO 15-2 commissions are constituted and deployed in practice. [minutes](../markdown/ga38_2010.md#ga38-p197)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CI #15** (GA13/1985, advice given) — Scope of presbytery commissions under BCO 15-2: the list of assignable matters is typical but not exhaustive. Construes the breadth of a 15-2 commission, the provision O13 amends. [page](../inquiries/ga13_1985__ci11.md)
- **14-44** (GA14/1986, advice given) — A judicial commission requires appointment under BCO 15-2 (plus BCO 32-3 approval of charges). Construes how a 15-2 commission is constituted. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci02.md)
- **CI #14** (GA14/1986, advice given) — A presbytery visitation commission may not be granted judicial power absent charges/a pending case (BCO 15-2, 31-2). Construes the limits of 15-2 commission authority. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci17.md)
- **II.M** (GA49/2022, not in conflict; Adopted by the CCB) — A proposed amendment to **BCO 15-2** clarifying presbytery commission **membership and quorum** (Overture 19, Northwest Georgia) was found not in conflict with the BCO. Most directly bearing: the prior, recent, CCB-cleared attempt to amend the very text and subject (commission membership/quorum) O13 now extends to Session commissions. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci13.md)

### Prior overtures
- **[GA6 O19](../overtures/ga06_1978__o19.md)** (1978, Answered in the negative) — Covenant Presbytery, "Amend BCO 15-2 to Remove Cap on Commissioners from a Single Congregation": an early attempt to amend 15-2's commission-composition rule. [page](../markdown/ga06_1978.md#ga06-p38)
- **[GA18 O11](../overtures/ga18_1990__o11.md)** (1990) — Eastern Canada, "Amend BCO 15-2 Regarding Quorum of Commission as Session": directly on point — a prior attempt to set the **quorum** for a commission acting as a Session, the same subject O13 addresses. [page](../markdown/ga18_1990.md#ga18-p71)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 15-2** — 105 citations across GA18 (1990) through GA52 (2025); overwhelmingly presbyteries forming commissions without the required 2 TE / 2 RE or without recording a quorum (e.g., [Pacific](../rpr/pacific.md) 33rd/2005 outstanding; [Korean Eastern](../rpr/korean-eastern.md) 34th/2006; [Palmetto](../rpr/palmetto.md) 49th/2022; [Rocky Mountain](../rpr/rocky-mountain.md) & [Platte Valley](../rpr/platte-valley.md) 52nd/2025 "commission of one person"). Heavy, persistent real-world friction over commission composition and quorum — exactly the gap O13 fixes for Session commissions. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)

### Note
O13 concerns **Session** commissions; almost the entire exact-provision spine for BCO 15-2 concerns **Presbytery** commissions (the 2-TE/2-RE quorum rule O13 explicitly declines to impose on Sessions). The spine hits therefore bear as context — establishing the existing presbytery rule and its enforcement record against which O13 carves out a distinct, lighter Session-commission rule — not as prior litigation of the precise question. **Pruned as incidental** (cite 15-2 only in passing on unrelated judicial-procedure grounds): Mays v. New River (GA8), Marshall v. Eastern Carolina (GA19), both Fitzsimmons v. Evangel entries (GA26), Kim v. Korean Eastern (GA34), the GA36 p.101 SJC decision, the Lee v. Korean Eastern cluster (2007-09/2007-10/2009-16, GA37), Dunn v. Phila Metro West (GA41/GA42), and Speck v. Missouri (GA49) — Speck does not cite 15-2 in its case text at all. **Restored: DeJong v. Village Seven (2021-3)** — prior note incorrectly characterized this as incidental; DeJong directly construes BCO 15-2/15-1 on the constitutional scope of a Session governance commission, the only spine case on point for a Session (not Presbytery) commission. **Woodward (2009-10)** is retained as background context only: its 2009-10 sub-complaint was dismissed on standing (BCO 43-1/11-4), not commission-composition grounds. Also pruned: GA22 O6 (Amend BCO 13 re dissolving churches — only an incidental 15-2 cross-reference) and the lone RAO 15-2 RPR row (Overtures Committee tenure — unrelated provision). **Renumbering caveat:** the 1989 (17th GA) amendment inserted new 15-1..15-5; "BCO 15-2" has denoted the commissions section since then, so the GA13/GA14 inquiries and later actions citing "15-2" are on-point. No companion GA53 overture targets Session-commission composition.
Pruned as incidental: 2016-14 Wills v. Metro Atlanta Presbytery — BCO 15-2 appears only once as a parenthetical citation ("BCO 15-2") identifying the authority for appointing a commission; the case is about church dissolution under BCO 13-9(f) and bears no analysis of commission composition or quorum.
