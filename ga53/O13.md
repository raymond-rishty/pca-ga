# O13 — Amend BCO 15-2 Regarding Session Commissions
**Targets:** BCO 15-2 · **Source:** Great Lakes · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-13_GreatLakes_15.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-13_GreatLakes_15.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds a third paragraph to BCO 15-2 governing commissions appointed by a **Session** (distinct from Presbytery commissions): each shall consist of at least two elders "with any combination of teaching or ruling elders," and the quorum shall be the greater of two elders or one-half of the membership. Resolves a disputed practice — some argue Session commissions should mirror the Presbytery rule (2 TE + 2 RE), which would leave many Sessions (e.g., all-RE Sessions) unable to form one.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the grounds cite only BCO 15-1; no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2009-10 — Woodward v. Western Carolina Presbytery](../cases/ga38_2010__2009-05_2009-08_2009-09_2009-10.md)** (GA38/2010, sustained in part) — construes the validity of a commission acting under BCO 15-2 (composition/authority of a commission); a worked example of how 15-2 commission-constitution rules are litigated. [minutes](../markdown/ga38_2010.md#ga38-p197)
- **[2016-14 — Wills v. Metro Atlanta Presbytery](../cases/ga45_2017__2016-14.md)** (GA45/2017, denied) — turns on a commission's constitution/powers under BCO 15-2; illustrates the composition-and-quorum questions O13 seeks to settle for Session commissions. [minutes](../markdown/ga45_2017.md#ga45-p545)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CI #15** (GA13/1985, advice given) — Scope of presbytery commissions under BCO 15-2: the list of assignable matters is typical but not exhaustive. Construes the breadth of a 15-2 commission, the provision O13 amends. [page](../inquiries/ga13_1985__ci11.md)
- **14-44** (GA14/1986, advice given) — A judicial commission requires appointment under BCO 15-2 (plus BCO 32-3 approval of charges). Construes how a 15-2 commission is constituted. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci02.md)
- **CI #14** (GA14/1986, advice given) — A presbytery visitation commission may not be granted judicial power absent charges/a pending case (BCO 15-2, 31-2). Construes the limits of 15-2 commission authority. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci17.md)
- **II.M** (GA49/2022, not in conflict; Adopted by the CCB) — A proposed amendment to **BCO 15-2** clarifying presbytery commission **membership and quorum** (Overture 19, Northwest Georgia) was found not in conflict with the BCO. Most directly bearing: the prior, recent, CCB-cleared attempt to amend the very text and subject (commission membership/quorum) O13 now extends to Session commissions. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci13.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA6 O19** (1978, Answered in the negative) — Covenant Presbytery, "Amend BCO 15-2 to Remove Cap on Commissioners from a Single Congregation": an early attempt to amend 15-2's commission-composition rule. [page](../markdown/ga06_1978.md#ga06-p38)
- **GA18 O11** (1990) — Eastern Canada, "Amend BCO 15-2 Regarding Quorum of Commission as Session": directly on point — a prior attempt to set the **quorum** for a commission acting as a Session, the same subject O13 addresses. [page](../markdown/ga18_1990.md#ga18-p71)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 15-2** — 105 citations across GA18 (1990) through GA52 (2025); overwhelmingly presbyteries forming commissions without the required 2 TE / 2 RE or without recording a quorum (e.g., Pacific 33rd/2005 outstanding; Korean Eastern 34th/2006; Palmetto 49th/2022; Rocky Mountain & Platte Valley 52nd/2025 "commission of one person"). Heavy, persistent real-world friction over commission composition and quorum — exactly the gap O13 fixes for Session commissions. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)

### Note
O13 concerns **Session** commissions; almost the entire exact-provision spine for BCO 15-2 concerns **Presbytery** commissions (the 2-TE/2-RE quorum rule O13 explicitly declines to impose on Sessions). The spine hits therefore bear as context — establishing the existing presbytery rule and its enforcement record against which O13 carves out a distinct, lighter Session-commission rule — not as prior litigation of the precise question. **Pruned as incidental** (cite 15-2 only in passing on unrelated judicial-procedure grounds): Mays v. New River (GA8), Marshall v. Eastern Carolina (GA19), both Fitzsimmons v. Evangel entries (GA26), Kim v. Korean Eastern (GA34), the GA36 p.101 SJC decision, the Lee v. Korean Eastern cluster (2007-09/2007-10/2009-16, GA37), Dunn v. Phila Metro West (GA41/GA42), Speck v. Missouri (GA49), and DeJong v. Village Seven (GA49) — kept only Woodward and Wills as composition/constitution examples. Also pruned: GA22 O6 (Amend BCO 13 re dissolving churches — only an incidental 15-2 cross-reference) and the lone RAO 15-2 RPR row (Overtures Committee tenure — unrelated provision). **Renumbering caveat:** the 1989 (17th GA) amendment inserted new 15-1..15-5; "BCO 15-2" has denoted the commissions section since then, so the GA13/GA14 inquiries and later actions citing "15-2" are on-point. No companion GA53 overture targets Session-commission composition.
