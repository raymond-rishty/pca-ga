# O24 — Amend BCO 15-2 to Clarify that Presbytery Itself Must Conduct Ordination Exams
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 102-28-0)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Refer back to the court of origin
**Targets:** BCO 15-2 · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-24_Westminster_15-2.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-24_Westminster_15-2.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds language to BCO 15-2 — "Presbytery shall always conduct the final trials of examination for ordination. A committee of Presbytery may conduct preliminary examinations, but the examination required for ordination must be sustained before the Presbytery itself." It closes the gap that the current text (which only requires Presbytery to conduct the "previous examination" when ordination is committed to a commission) does not explicitly forbid a commission from conducting the entire ordination examination.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None — the grounds reason only from the existing text of BCO 15-2; no cases, overtures, or inquiries are named.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
None found. (All 15 spine cases cite BCO 15-2 only for commission quorum, judicial-commission constitution, or judicial process — none construes the ordination-examination clause this overture clarifies. See Note.)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CI #15** (GA13/1985) — CCB advised that BCO 15-2's list of matters assignable to commissions is "typical but not exhaustive," other matters assignable subject to review and control; bears on the outer limit of what Presbytery may delegate to a commission, which is the precise concern O24 sharpens. [page](../inquiries/ga13_1985__ci11.md)
- **II.M** (GA49/2022) — CCB found a proposed BCO 15-2 amendment (Overture 19 from Northwest Georgia) clarifying commission membership and quorum "not in conflict" with the BCO; bears as the most recent CCB engagement amending the same section and as a review template for a 15-2 amendment. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci13.md)

### Prior overtures
- **[GA6 O19](../overtures/ga06_1978__o19.md)** (1978, Answered in the negative) — Covenant, "Amend BCO 15-2 to Remove Cap on Commissioners from a Single Congregation"; earliest 15-2 commission overture, same provision but on composition, not exams. [p.38](../markdown/ga06_1978.md#ga06-p38)
- **[GA14 O2](../overtures/ga14_1986__o2.md)** (1986, Answered by reference) — Western Carolina, "Amend BCO to Require Final Ordination Exams on the Full Presbytery Floor"; a prior attempt at exactly O24's concern — that the whole presbytery, not a commission, must conduct ordination exams. [p.50](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p50)
- **[GA18 O11](../overtures/ga18_1990__o11.md)** (1990) — Eastern Canada, "Amend BCO 15-2 Regarding Quorum of Commission as Session"; same provision, commission-quorum question. [p.73](../markdown/ga18_1990.md#ga18-p71)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 15-2** — 105 RPR rows cite this section, but only a few construe the ordination-examination clause O24 targets (the rest concern commission *quorum/composition*, incidental here). Squarely on point: **[Mid-America](../rpr/exc/mid-america__003.md) 21st (1993)** — "Presbytery cannot entrust an ordination examination to a commission (BCO 15-2, last sentence)"; **[Southern New England](../rpr/exc/southern-new-england__015.md) 35th (2007)** — "Only Presbytery as a whole can conduct examination of minister from another denomination. BCO 15-2"; and **[Korean Southeastern](../rpr/korean-southeastern.md) 52nd (2025)** — incomplete record of ordination exam, citing "When the ordination of a minister is committed to a commission, the Presbytery…". These show recurring real-world friction over exactly the clause being clarified. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)

### Note

This is the direct precursor — same change, same rationale — and is recorded at [ga14_1986 p.50](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p50).
