# O15 — Amend BCO 42-4 and 43-3 to Clarify the Deadline for Filings
**Targets:** BCO 42-4, 43-3 · **Source:** SWFL · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-15_SWFL_FilingDeadlines.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-15_SWFL_FilingDeadlines.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 42-4 (appeals) and 43-3 (complaints) so that the 30-day filing clock runs from *written* notification of the lower court's decision, delivered by a prescribed mode (certified/registered/express mail, hand delivery, or confirmed e-mail/fax), and deems compliance fulfilled if a party cannot be located after diligent inquiry or refuses delivery. It seeks to resolve ambiguity over whether an in-person oral pronouncement of censure, versus a written decision, starts the appeal period.

**Cites in its own grounds:** SJC 2024-10 *Gozé v. Hills and Plains Presbytery* (52nd GA, 2025) as a recent example; and the 2013 GA amendment (broadly following Pacific Northwest Presbytery's overture) that first set the clock at "notification of the last court's decision."

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2024-10 — Gozé v. Hills and Plains Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-10.md)** (52/2025, dismissed) — Self-cited by the overture; appeal dismissed as untimely under BCO 42-4 because filed more than 30 days after "notification of the last court's decision." This is the exact friction the overture targets. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1013)
- **[2021-13 — Dudt v. Northwest Georgia Presbytery](../cases/ga49_2022__2021-13.md)** (49/2022, sustained) — Construes BCO 42-4 on appeal filing; SJC sustained that Presbytery erred in treating the November filing (without supporting reasons) as untimely: 42-4 distinguishes oral notice before adjournment from the 30-day written notice with supporting reasons. Bears on the deadline mechanics O15 addresses. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p909)
- **[1999-2 — Tan v. South Texas Presbytery](../cases/ga28_2000__1999-02.md)** (28/2000, dismissed) — Early case construing BCO 43-3 access-to-higher-court timing; complaint declared administratively out of order as not timely filed (dated and faxed on August 28 but not mailed until September 4, exceeding the 30-day period). [minutes](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p243)

### Note
The provision-exact spine returned 39 cases on BCO 42-4/43-3; these provisions govern timeliness for nearly every appeal/complaint, so most hits are incidental (the provision is cited only as the procedural basis for jurisdiction, not for the notification-timing question). I pruned ~29 incidental hits — e.g., 1981-2, 1983-15, 13-61, 2000-10, 2004-7, 2004-9, 2010-18, 2010-24, 2011-8, 2013-7, 2015-2, 2015-10, 2016-2, 2016-5, 2016-14, 2016-17, 2017-1, 2018-5, 2019-13, 2020-3, 2020-10, 2021-6, 2022-1, 2023-4, 2023-06/08, 2023-20, 2024-5, 2024-16, plus the pre-SJC GA9/GA12/GA40 case fragments — keeping only cases that turn on the filing deadline or notification trigger. The keystone bearing action is the self-cited **Gozé (2024-10)**. The other half of O15's grounds — the **2013 amendment** — the corpus locates precisely as **GA40 O13 (43-3) and O14 (42-4)** from Pacific Northwest Presbytery. No chapter renumbering applies — BCO 42 and 43 numbering is stable. CI 12-53 (matched on 42-4) was pruned: it concerns debate prohibitions during a complaint, not filing deadlines. GA14 O37 (end-of-life ethics) was a false body+title match and is dropped.
Pruned as incidental: **2019-3 Crouse v. Northwest Georgia Presbytery** — case is entirely about BCO 24-1 officer nomination process (a deacon's nomination for ruling elder was screened out by the Session before examination); 42-4 does not appear in the case text and the spine attribution appears to come from a procedural minutes reference; the description "Turns on BCO 42-4 appeal timeliness/notification" was incorrect. **2016-1 Aven v. Ohio Valley Presbytery** — case is about paedocommunion and compliance with an SJC remand on LC 177; 42-4 and 43-3 appear in the index's bco_cited_current but not in the case text and play no role in the holding; described as "one of the few cases citing both 42-4 and 43-3 on filing/timeliness" but the case has nothing to do with timeliness. **2018-4 Mapes v. Metropolitan New York Presbytery** — complaint was dismissed as moot (the congregational vote the complainant sought was held when Presbytery dismissed the congregation), not on timeliness grounds; the description "Complaint dismissed on BCO 43-3 timeliness grounds" was incorrect. **2018-6 Edwards v. North Texas Presbytery** — case is about failing to file the complaint with Presbytery before bringing it to the SJC (BCO 42-4 & 43-2 require adjudication below first), not about the written-notification trigger O15 amends; incidental. **2015-11 Thompson v. South Florida Presbytery** — BCO 43-3 is the procedural vehicle by which Thompson brought the complaint to Presbytery, but the case is about contumacy and improper pre-trial excommunication under BCO 32-6; 43-3 is not construed on its own terms in the holding.
