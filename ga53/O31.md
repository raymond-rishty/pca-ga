# O31 — Amend BCO 58-5 to Specify that Ruling Elders Shall Distribute the Elements
**Targets:** BCO 58-5 · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-31_Westminster_58-5.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-31_Westminster_58-5.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 58-5 (and adds an explanatory footnote) so that, apart from extraordinary circumstances, ruling elders shall assist the minister in distributing the elements — "distributing" defined as the act of handing elements to communicants, not the passive passing of trays down pews — thereby barring non-ordained members from the active serving role.

**Cites in its own grounds:** No PCA cases, overtures, or inquiries cited by number. Grounds rest on WCF 29.3; WLC 169, 173, 176; WCF 30.1–4; BCO 30-3; BCO 12-5.a; BCO 57; Matthew 16:19; 1 Cor 11:27–32; the Scots Confession of 1560 ch. 22; and a Charles Hodge quotation.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **II.H** (GA52/2025) — CCB found the GA52 amendment to BCO 58-5 limiting Lord's Supper distribution to officers "not in conflict" with the Constitution, adopted 8-0-0. Directly on point: the constitutional vetting of the immediately preceding attempt to restrict who distributes the elements under this exact clause; O31 is the narrower successor (ruling elders assist, vs. officers-only). ### Note
Companion to **GA53 O60** (Calvary, also BCO 58-5, "Only Men Lawfully Ordained Shall Distribute the Elements") — both amend the same distribution clause; O60 is the stricter ordained-men-only version, O31 the ruling-elders-assist version, and both descend from GA52 O12 (passed but not ratified). CCB has already cleared the officer-distribution concept as constitutionally sound (II.H, GA52), so the live question is policy, not constitutionality. Pruned as incidental from the spine: inquiry **6-111 III** (1978) and prior overture **[GA7 O22](../overtures/ga07_1979__o22.md)** (1979), which cite 58-5 only for the *membership reaffirmation-of-faith vows* — an unrelated portion of the chapter, not the Lord's Supper. The wine / leavened-bread / "broken" overtures are kept as same-provision amendment history (they bear on the Assembly's willingness to revise 58-5) but address different questions than *who distributes*. No BCO ch. 58 renumbering and no RPR exceptions for 58-5.
