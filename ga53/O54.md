# O54 — Amend BCO 42-2 to Clarify that a Person Judged Contumacious has the Right of Complaint Not Appeal
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the negative
**Targets:** BCO 42-2 · **Source:** Hills and Plains Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-54_HP_42-2.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-54_HP_42-2.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds a sentence to BCO 42-2 providing that "If the court has dealt with the party as contumacious, the party may not appeal but may complain against the court," so an appellate court cannot rehear an entire case where the trial court never reached judgment (BCO 32-15) because it found the accused contumacious.

**Cites in its own grounds:** No case numbers or overture numbers are named, but the rationale describes "a recent case" in which an SJC panel treated a BCO 43 complaint from a Session-found-contumacious accused as a BCO 42 appeal and reheard the case — this is **Gozé v. Hills and Plains Presbytery (2024-10)**, the overturing presbytery's own case. It also relies on BCO 42-1, 32-15, 32-6, and 34-3 for the definition of appeal/judgment/contumacy.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2024-10 — Gozé v. Hills and Plains Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-10.md)** (52nd/2025, dismissed) — The "recent case" the overture's rationale describes: the trial court found the appellant contumacious and suspended him from the sacraments; his later appeal was dismissed as untimely (BCO 42-4). The overture seeks to foreclose treating such a contumacy disposition as an appealable "judgment." ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1013)
- **[2019-10/2019-12 — Evans and Pitts et al. v. Arizona Presbytery](../cases/ga48_2021__2019-10_2019-12.md)** (48th/2021, sustained) — The SJC itself observed that "to avoid confusion in the future, perhaps BCO 38-1 and 42-2 could be amended," directly seeding the GA48 amendment lineage that this overture continues. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga48_2021.md#ga48-p788)
- **[2002-17 — Appeal of TE Sung K. Kim vs. Korean Capital Presbytery](../cases/ga32_2004__2002-17.md)** (32nd/2004, dismissed) — Held that Kim, found guilty of contumacy without submitting to a regular trial, was not "entitled to an Appeal" under BCO 42-2 — the very rule the overture codifies. [minutes](../markdown/ga32_2004.md#ga32-p105)
- **[1997-11 — Raymond Larsen vs. Pacific Presbytery (Appeal)](../cases/ga26_1998__1997-11.md)** (26th/1998, administratively out of order) — Held the appeal out of order because Larsen was deposed for contumacy (failure to appear for trial); cites BCO 42-2 that only those who submitted to a regular trial are entitled to appeal. Squarely on the appeal-entitlement question. [minutes](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p146)
- **[1993-10 — Clark v. Southwest Presbytery](../cases/ga23_1995__1993-10.md)** (23rd/1995, not sustained) — Construes contumacy where there was no trial on the original indictment (the court conceded "there was no trial on the original indictment") — the exact procedural posture the overture targets. [minutes](../markdown/ga23_1995.md#ga23-p102)
- **[2015-11 — Thompson v. South Florida Presbytery](../cases/ga44_2016__2015-11.md)** (44th/2016, sustained) — Heavy treatment of contumacy and the complaint mechanism in a case without process (excommunication imposed, complaints routed through Session and Presbytery), bearing on the appeal-vs-complaint distinction. [minutes](../markdown/ga44_2016.md#ga44-p541)

### Note
The exact-provision spine returned 18 case hits on BCO 42-2; I pruned 12 as incidental — cases that cite 42-2 in passing or for a different clause. Notably **2011-13 (Spann)** quotes 42-2 for the appeal-routing rule ("An appeal cannot be made to any court other than the next higher"), unrelated to contumacy; and **1997-13, 2002-5, 2003-3, 2004-4, 2004-6, 2009-28 (Ruff), 2010-3, 2015-10**, the **GA35 p.90** stub, and the **1993-5** administrative clarification stub surfaced no contumacy/appeal-entitlement holding (several have a null case_page, minutes-only). The six retained cases all construe the contumacy → appeal-entitlement question or the appeal-vs-complaint distinction. No renumbering affects chapter 42 (checked bco_renumberings.jsonl). Companion lineage: GA48 O19 (and CCB II.L/II.X, held over per CI Supplemental II.A) last revised this sentence; O54 is the next iteration, prompted by the overturing presbytery's own SJC loss in Gozé (2024-10).
