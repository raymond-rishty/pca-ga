# O59 — Amend BCO 38-3.a to Recognize the Non-Punitive Removal of Individuals from Membership Roll
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 121-12-0)*
**Targets:** BCO 38-3.a · **Source:** Calvary Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-59_Calvary_38-3.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-59_Calvary_38-3.pdf)

> **Grounds:** The overture as written appears to lower the view of church membership and church discipline, particularly as it leaves open the possibility that if the member did not follow through on their announced intention, they would be without the benefits of church membership.

**What it does:** Amends BCO 38-3.a so a member or officer in good standing may withdraw non-punitively either by affiliating with another branch of the visible Church (BCO 2-2) **or by communicating his intent** to do so — recording the irregularity, acknowledging "his new membership or intentions," and removing his name from the roll. This closes the current gap whereby, unless the person names an actual new membership, the only available tools are the punitive/disciplinary routes of 38-3.b and 38-4. It retains the original-jurisdiction court's option to keep the name on the roll and conduct any pending investigation (BCO 31-2) or charges (BCO 32-3).

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the grounds cite only BCO provisions — 2-2, 31-2, 32-3, 38-3, 38-4 — no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2019-6 — Presbyterian Church in America v. Presbytery of the Mississippi Valley](../cases/ga48_2021__2019-06.md)** (GA48/2021, sustained) — ⭐ Construes a presbytery's BCO 38-3 duties when a minister withdraws to another body; the General Assembly itself prosecuted the presbytery's handling of the withdrawal, directly framing the question O59 reaches. [minutes](../markdown/ga48_2021.md#ga48-p718)
- **[2020-12 — Speck v. Missouri Presbytery](../cases/ga49_2022__2020-12.md)** (GA49/2022, sustained) — ⭐ Turns on whether/how a court must record and dispose of a member's status under BCO 38-3 when withdrawal coincides with pending matters — the very "investigation in process / charges" branch O59 rewrites. [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p747)
- **[2023-7 — Evans v. Arizona Presbytery](../cases/ga51_2024__2023-07.md)** (GA51/2024, granted) — ⭐ Construes BCO 38-3 procedure for removal from the roll; bears on the mechanics O59 amends. [minutes](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p815)
- **[2024-1 — Moehn v. Westminster Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-01.md)** (GA52/2025, sustained) — ⭐ Recent SJC construction of BCO 38-3 in a withdrawal/erasure context. [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p951)
- **[Morse v. Covenant Presbytery (1992-3)](../markdown/ga20_1992.md#ga20-p156)** (GA20/1992, denied) — Early construction of BCO 38-3 as applied to a minister's withdrawal/erasure; shows the long-standing question of non-punitive removal the overture targets. [minutes](../markdown/ga20_1992.md#ga20-p156)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CI #3** (GA14/1986) — Declining case-specific questions, the CCB laid out the three BCO routes for removing members from the rolls (transfer, discipline, relocation) under BCO 38-3 et al.; O59 argues a fourth, non-punitive, intent-based route is missing. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci08.md)
- **15-83 III, 8** (GA15/1987) — Advised that releasing a minister to an independent church is handled by **erasure under BCO 38-3**, the precise non-punitive-vs-disciplinary tension O59 addresses. [page](../inquiries/ga15_1987__ci02.md)
- **25-22 IV, 1** (GA25/1997) — CCB drafted amended BCO 38-3 language for roll removal when a member joins another church, with options for retaining/removing names when charges are pending — a direct prior drafting effort on the same text and the same "charges pending" branch O59 rewrites. [page](../inquiries/ga25_1997__ci03.md)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 38-3** — 22 RPR citations, heavily clustered at recent assemblies (GA48–52, 2021–2025), almost all of the form "no record that presbytery made the determinations required by BCO 38-3" when a minister withdrew to another denomination — e.g. [Covenant](../rpr/covenant.md) (51st/2024, unsatisfactory), [Korean Southwest Orange County](../rpr/korean-southwest-orange-county.md) (51st/2024, outstanding), [Northern California](../rpr/northern-california.md) (51st/2024, outstanding), [Wisconsin](../rpr/wisconsin.md) (50th–52nd), [Pee Dee](../rpr/pee-dee.md) and [South Florida](../rpr/south-florida.md) (52nd/2025). This recurring presbytery confusion over how to remove a withdrawing minister non-punitively is the strongest real-world evidence of the friction O59 seeks to cure. ### Note
The spine is provision-exact on BCO 38-3 (no renumbering applies to 38-3; no companion GA53 overture targets this subsection). I pruned several incidental spine cases that cite BCO 38-3 only in passing or in unrelated disciplinary/appellate postures: the 1987 North Texas/North Dallas administrative report, JC 31-3 (out of order), the GA35 (2007) out-of-order decision, JC 2005-2 and 2007-8 (both dismissed), Doty v. Nashville (2016-8, dismissed), Gendy v. Central Florida (2019-13), Eagle (2020-10) and Eudaly/Light (2021-5, both administrative), and the BCO 34-1/Wilson petitions (2022-12, 2022-20, dismissed) — none construe the non-punitive-withdrawal mechanism O59 amends. I also pruned inquiries 27-43 III, 36-30 II.C, 37-29 IV.3, and App. O II.T, which touch BCO 38-3 only tangentially (appeal-vs-complaint wording, profession-of-faith subscription, BCO 2-2 investigative duty) rather than the roll-removal-on-withdrawal question. Note the repeated, defeated Potomac/Northeast/Southeast Alabama attempts (GA21–GA40) to amend exactly this text: O59 enters a long and previously unsuccessful line of overtures on non-punitive withdrawal, which the committee will likely weigh.
