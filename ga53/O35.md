# O35 — Amend BCO 31-2 to Establish a Deadline for the Issuing of an Indictment
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the negative *(vote 85-40-5)*
**Assembly action (2026):** Answer in the negative
**Targets:** BCO 31-2 · **Source:** Illiana · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Overture-35_Illiana_31-2.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Overture-35_Illiana_31-2.pdf)

> **Grounds:** The great diversity in complexity of cases makes problematic the setting of a time limit on only one part of the judicial process.

**What it does:** Adds a hard deadline to BCO 31-2: once an investigation raises a strong presumption of guilt and the court appoints a prosecutor, the indictment "shall be filed with the clerk of the court within sixty (60) days following the meeting of the court," and a prosecutor who neglects to file is rebuked, dismissed, and replaced. The drafters note the enforcement language is borrowed from BCO 42-7 (clerk's failure to transmit the Record of the Case).

**Cites in its own grounds:** None — the grounds rest only on Scripture (Eccl. 8:11; Deut. 16:19–20) and a textual cross-reference to BCO 42-7; no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries are cited.

### RPR exceptions

### Note
The spine is high-recall for BCO 31-2 (132 case hits) because 31-2 governs the investigation that precedes *every* disciplinary case, so it is cited in passing throughout the SJC corpus. The overwhelming majority of those hits construe the substantive "strong presumption of guilt" standard, not the *timing* of indictment — and no prior case construes a filing deadline because none exists. I therefore pruned ~130 incidental case hits and kept only two that reflect genuine real-world friction at the investigation→indictment stage (including Koerkenmeier, from Illiana itself).

Pruned as incidental: 2007-12 Grady v. Southwest Florida Presbytery — BCO 31-2 appears only parenthetically as "see for example BCO 31-2"; the case holds that a presbytery imposed admonition without due process (BCO 30-2) by reading a Shepherding Committee report into its minutes, not about investigation timing or indictment. Pruned as incidental: 2019-10/2019-12 Evans & Pitts et al. v. Arizona Presbytery — case holds that AZP failed BCO 38-1 confession requirements in deposing TE Evans; BCO 31-2 is cited because an Investigative Committee was formed, but the holding turns on BCO 38-1 (approval of facts in confession), not on investigation delay or indictment deadline. The bearing weight of O35 sits in the **CCB/inquiry line** and the **string of recent 31-2 procedural-amendment overtures** (GA36, GA48 ×3, GA51, GA52): GA has repeatedly attempted to amend 31-2's procedure but never its indictment timing — and notably the recent Phila. Metro West cluster (GA48 O12/O28/O29) was each referred back to presbytery without prejudice rather than adopted, even where the CCB found the proposal not in conflict. The **RPR exceptions** are the documented friction the deadline answers. Among prior overtures I pruned body/title-only matches that do not bear on a 31-2 indictment deadline (BCO 34-1 neighboring-presbytery GA19 O12 / GA20 O32; BCO 38-3 renunciation GA24 O26 / GA25 O6; BCO 33-1 GA29 O9; the Greg Johnson original-jurisdiction overtures GA48 O4/O25, GA49 O36/O37; BCO 57-5 GA36 O4; BCO 15-3 GA36 O7; BCO 32-2 GA40 O16; BCO 43-1 GA45 O15; BCO 31-10 GA50 O8; BCO 36 GA52 O32; the SJC-direction GA29 O19 / GA41 O23; and the AC-funding GA40 O15). No renumbering caveat: BCO 31-2 has carried this number throughout the corpus. O35 is referred to CCB and OC.
