# O44 — Divide Covenant Presbytery into Two Presbyteries
**MNA recommends:** Answer in the affirmative
**Assembly voted:** Answer in the affirmative
**Targets:** (boundaries) — BCO 14-6.e; BCO Chapter 13 · **Source:** Covenant · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-44_Covenant_DividingPresbytery.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-44_Covenant_DividingPresbytery.pdf)

> *Referred by the Stated Clerk to MNA; not acted on by the Overtures Committee.*

**What it does:** Asks the GA, pursuant to BCO 14-6.e, to remove the Arkansas counties from Covenant Presbytery (now 46 churches, ~7,000 members across W. Tennessee, N. Mississippi, and Arkansas) and erect a new "Arkansas Presbytery" comprising all Arkansas counties except Benton, Carroll, Washington, Madison, and Boone (~9 churches + 1 mission work, ~1,200 members), authorizing it to conduct business effective July 1, 2026 consistent with BCO Chapter 13.

**Cites in its own grounds:** No prior case, overture, or inquiry by number. Cites the 16th GA (1988) "guidelines for the multiplication of existing Presbyteries" and the 41st GA (2013) "Guidelines for Forming New Presbyteries through the Division or Multiplication of Existing Presbyteries"; grounds its prayer in BCO 14-6.e and BCO Chapter 13.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
None found. (The exact-provision spine returns no provisions; cases citing BCO 14-6 invoke the chapter's general enumeration of GA powers in complaints against presbyteries, not the GA's power to divide/erect a presbytery — incidental. See Note.)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **GA50 IV. Non-Judicial Reference 1** (2023) — "Whether one presbytery may involuntarily dissociate another presbytery from the PCA." Construes BCO 14-6.e — the exact provision O44 invokes; CCB held no involuntary-dissociation procedure exists, confirming that restructuring presbyteries runs through the GA-action route under 14-6.e that O44 follows. [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci21.md)
- **GA47 III.C** (2019) — "Effect of presbytery division on advice and consent voting." Directly addresses the mechanics of dividing a presbytery: a newly erected presbytery may give advice and consent on amendments already voted by its predecessor, but transferred presbyters may not vote twice — the housekeeping question the new "Arkansas Presbytery" will raise. [page](../inquiries/ga47_2019__ci24.md)
