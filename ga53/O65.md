# O65 — Amend BCO 38-1 re Confessions and Cases without Process
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the negative *(vote 128-1-1)*
**Targets:** BCO 38-1 · **Source:** Canada West · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Overture-65_CanadaWest_38-1.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Overture-65_CanadaWest_38-1.pdf)

> **Grounds:** See the rationale from CCB at p. 479, recommendation BG. Further, attempts to amend the proposal to cure the defects identified by CCB would have forced courts to proceed to a full trial in any situation other than a case without process, thus eliminating the right of a defendant to confess during the trial process and then allow the court, if it so desires, to sentence the defendant on the basis of that confession.

**What it does:** Rewrites BCO 38-1 to require, in a case without process, an approved, co-signed written "Confession" (sufficient summary of facts, specific confession, and evidence of repentance) appended to the minutes; bars any other information without written consent of accused and court (incl. prosecutors, committees, commissions); guarantees the right to appeal (BCO 42) and to counsel (BCO 32-19); codifies a procedure for informing and inviting comment from offended parties (BCO 29-3); and adds a track for when someone other than the offender reveals the offense (court investigates and may draw up a confession document the accused may sign, else proceed with process).

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the rationale cites no specific cases, overtures, or inquiries — only cross-references BCO 42, 32-19, and 29-3).

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 38-1** — 21 RPR citations, recurring across presbyteries and years for failing to record the case-without-process steps — e.g. [Calvary](../rpr/exc/calvary__092.md) 42nd/2014 (TE indefinitely suspended with no record of process), [Arizona](../rpr/exc/arizona__025.md) 49th/2022 (full 38-1 statement not recorded in minutes), [Northern Illinois](../rpr/exc/northern-illinois__060.md) 51st/2024 (record of case without process not in minutes), and [Fellowship](../rpr/exc/fellowship__030.md) 52nd/2025 (court failed to invite an offended party to comment on the Confession). This is the live real-world friction O65 directly addresses: missing written confessions and missing offended-party process. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)

### Note
O65 is essentially a consolidated re-proposal of the Pacific Northwest 38-1 reform series (GA48 O19/O33/O34/O35, GA50 O27) plus the offended-party mechanic that Great Lakes O14 failed to carry at GA52 — all of which the CCB repeatedly found "not in conflict," yet which were not fully ratified into the text. The 21 RPR exceptions and the steady stream of sustained SJC complaints (2011-17, 2011-18, 2016-15, 2019-10/12, 2021-11, 2023-12) evidence genuine, continuing friction over inadequate no-process records. PRUNING: the high-recall spine returned ~43 cases citing 38-1; I dropped roughly two dozen that merely cite 38-1 in passing or as part of a larger censure dispute without construing the confession / no-process / offended-party questions O65 changes — including the bare GA15 administrative reports (p340, p342), the unnumbered GA26/GA36 SJC entries, Chastain/Heritage 2006-7 (out of order on jurisdiction), Morton Smith 2009-2, Sherfey 2011-8, Sawyers 2011-6, Thompson 2015-10, Fordice 2016-9, Malone 2007-2 (cited only in a GA48 minute), Williams 2019-4, PCA v. MS Valley 2019-6, McWilliams 2020-3, Marusich 2020-4, the GA49 Pacific Presbytery cluster (2020-7/8/9 and Ozbalt & Barr), and the GA51 BCO 40-5 Metro NY matter. No constitutional inquiries returned (CCB advice only). No renumbering caveat: BCO 38-1 has retained its number throughout PCA history. The unrelated GA49 O26 (Statement on Political Violence) is a false body+title match and was excluded.
