# O16 — Amend BCO 43 to Clarify Circularizing the Court
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the affirmative *(vote 121-4-2)*
**Assembly action (2026):** Answer in the affirmative
**Targets:** BCO 43 (43-2, 43-3) · **Source:** Southwest Florida Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-16_SWFL_Circularizing.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-16_SWFL_Circularizing.pdf)

**What it does:** Moves the circularization prohibition out of BCO 43-2 (the original-court stage) and into 43-3, recasting it as a ban on circularizing only the *higher/appellate* court reviewing a complaint ("No attempt should be made by any party to circularize a higher court reviewing a complaint"). The stated aim is to free courts of original jurisdiction to deliberate openly on complaints (no defined "parties" exist before an adverse decision) while preserving the circularization ban for appellate review.

**Cites in its own grounds:** The 1984 amendment that first required complaints to be filed with the court of original jurisdiction and introduced the circularization ban; and the 1999 Pittsburgh Presbytery proposal — adopted by the GA in 2000 — clarifying that a respondent need only be appointed when a complaint ascends to a higher court (= GA27 O9). No SJC case cited by number.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[1990-09 — Hathaway v. Grace Presbytery](../cases/ga19_1991__1990-09.md)** (19th GA/1991, denied) — Cites BCO 43-2 and is topically indexed to "circularization of court" and "circularization of commission"; an early SJC treatment of the circularization rule the overture seeks to relocate. [minutes](../markdown/ga19_1991.md#ga19-p567)
- **[2013-01 — Dunn and Pesnell v. Philadelphia Metro West Presbytery](../cases/ga42_2014__2013-01.md)** (42nd GA/2014, not sustained) — Cites BCO 43-2 with the topic "circularizing a court"; bears on how the circularization prohibition operates at the complaint stage the overture targets. [minutes](../markdown/ga42_2014.md#ga42-p554)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **12-53 II, 56** (12th GA/1984) — Scope and duration of the BCO debate/discussion prohibitions during a complaint: they apply only to the particular matter, only until final adjudication, in the presence of the higher court, and to all members of the lower court. Directly construes the original-court deliberation constraints the overture says "chill helpful dialogue." [page](../inquiries/ga12_1984__ci05.md)
- **29-28 III.6** (29th GA/2001) — A complainant may not go to a higher court until the lower court has acted under BCO 43-2 and 43-3; bears on the 43-2/43-3 lower-vs-higher-court division the overture reorganizes. [page](../inquiries/ga29_2001__ci06.md)
- **CI #13** (13th GA/1985) — Notice-of-complaint sufficiency and timing of a suspension motion under BCO 43-4 and 43-2; touches the original-court complaint mechanics the amendment loosens. [page](../inquiries/ga13_1985__ci10.md)

### Prior overtures
- **[GA27 O9](../overtures/ga27_1999__o9.md)** (1999, Approved & ratified 2000) — Pittsburgh Presbytery, "Clarify BCO 43-5"; clarified that a respondent need only be appointed when a complaint reaches a higher court — the exact precedent O16's rationale cites for distinguishing original- from higher-court formalities. [page](../markdown/ga27_1999.md#ga27-p49)
- **[GA31 O10](../overtures/ga31_2003__o10.md)** (2003, Answered in the negative) — Central Carolina, "Amend BCO 43-2 Regarding Complaints Against Undebatable Judicial Commission Reports"; proposed adding text to 43-2 immediately before the circularization sentence — a prior attempt to edit the same clause. [page](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p201)
- **[GA40 O14](../overtures/ga40_2012__o14.md)** (2012, Approved & ratified 2013) — Pacific Northwest, parallel circularization clause for *appeals* in BCO 42-4 ("...before the case is heard"); the appellate analogue the overture says it leaves intact, and the model for the "higher court reviewing" language. [page](../markdown/ga40_2012.md#ga40-p697)
- **[GA49 O21](../overtures/ga49_2022__o21.md)** (2022, Answered in the affirmative as amended) — Northwest Georgia, "Amend BCO 43-2 and 43-3 Regarding Timing for Considering a Complaint"; recent attempt to edit the same two sections. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p1326)
- **[GA51 O21](../overtures/ga51_2024__o21.md)** (2024, Approved & ratified 2025) — Central Indiana, "Change the Prohibition Against 'Interlocutory Appeal' by Complaint in BCO 43-1"; recent successful amendment in the same complaint chapter, showing live legislative activity on BCO 43. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p1165)
