# O68 — Amend BCO 14-1 to Make AC Members Appointed by Committees and Agencies Non-voting
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 102-28-0)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Refer back to the court of origin
**Targets:** BCO 14-1 (specifically 14-1.12) · **Source:** Catawba Valley · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-68_CatawbaVly_14-1-1.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-68_CatawbaVly_14-1-1.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 14-1.12 (and conforming RAO 5-1) so the Administrative Committee consists of eleven voting members elected by the GA plus one *non-voting* representative from each of the nine program committees/agencies (CDM, CC, CTS, MNA, MTW, PCAF, Geneva, RUF, RH), who keep the privilege of the floor but lose the vote. Goal: confine AC voting authority to members directly elected by the Assembly.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None. The Whereas clauses argue solely from accountability/parity principles and cite no prior case, overture, or inquiry.

### Note
Direct predecessor: GA52 O9 (James River, 2025) proposed the identical change and was answered in the negative even though the CCB found it constitutional (GA52 II.F) — so the friction is policy, not constitutionality. The CCB has twice (GA51 II.Z, GA52 II.F) blessed BCO 14-1 committee-structure amendments as not-in-conflict, so a constitutionality objection is unlikely; the live question is whether the Assembly wants to strip the vote from program-committee/agency representatives. The spine was high-recall because BCO 14-1 is a large chapter (committees, SJC, nominations, terms, BCO-amendment routing); I kept only actions construing AC/committee-member composition, membership eligibility, or the .12 subsection, and pruned the rest. No renumbering applies — BCO 14-1 has retained its number; older actions cite the parenthetical form "14-1(12)" rather than "14-1.12" for the same subsection.
