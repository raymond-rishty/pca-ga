# O55 — Amend BCO 22-3 and 23-1 Concerning the Tenure of Assistant Pastors
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 116-13-0)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Refer back to the court of origin
**Targets:** BCO 22-3, 23-1 · **Source:** SW Florida · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-55_SWFlorida_22-1.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-55_SWFlorida_22-1.pdf)

**What it does:** Caps an assistant pastor's initial call at five years, after which the Session must put him forward to the congregation as an associate pastor or he is automatically released; extraordinary circumstances permit three-year renewals by 2/3 Presbytery vote. It amends BCO 23-1 to add a corresponding dissolution trigger requiring Presbytery to dissolve the call if an assistant is neither advanced nor granted an extension.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (relies on Preliminary Principle 6 and cross-references to BCO 12-4, 13-2, 20-1, 22-4; no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries cited).

### Note
Companion GA53 caveat: this overture pairs a substantive 22-3 tenure cap with a conforming 23-1 dissolution trigger; cross-check other GA53 overtures touching BCO 22 or 23. No chapter renumbering applies (BCO 22 and 23 retain their historic numbers). Pruning: the BCO 23-1 spine is extremely high-recall — 11 of 17 cases and 3 of 5 inquiries cite 23-1 only for generic pastoral-relation dissolution or congregational-vote mechanics, not the assistant-pastor tenure question this overture creates, so they are pruned above; kept actions construe assistant-pastor status (22-3 cluster) or the 23-1 dissolution/succession machinery the overture rewrites. Judgment call: 33-29 II.C and 49 II.A are the most pointed warnings — CCB has twice resisted GA-level micromanagement of the assistant-pastor/session relationship, precisely what an automatic five-year advance-or-release rule does.
