# O14 — Amend BCO 7-3, 7-4, 9-2, and 9-7 Regarding Titles and Offices for Unordained People
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer by reference to [O37](O37.md)
**Targets:** BCO 7-3, 7-4, 9-2, 9-7 · **Source:** SWFL · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-14_SWFL_Titles-1.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Overture-14_SWFL_Titles-1.pdf)

**What it does:** Expands BCO 7-3 to forbid any synonymous title for unordained persons (e.g. shepherdess, eldress, pastoress, minister, deaconess) and adds new BCO 7-4 barring congregations from inventing offices or conferring authority titles besides pastor/elder and deacon; amends 9-2 and 9-7 so that where deacons cannot be ordained the elders must directly execute diaconal duties (not delegate them to unordained staff), assistants to deacons may not be called "deaconess" or "members of the diaconate," and assistants may not be appointed where no ordained deacons serve.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the rationale cites only savethepca.com research documenting ~5–10% of churches using such titles, and the PCAAC BCO text — no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries by name).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2007-13 — Kniseley v. Rocky Mountain](../cases/ga37_2009__2007-13.md)** (GA39/2011, denied) — construes whether the title "minister" may be used for unordained church staff; the SJC denied the complaint holding the BCO does not proscribe such generic use, reasoning from BCO 7-2 and BCO 40-2(3); bears directly on O14's 7-3 expansion forbidding synonymous titles for unordained persons. [minutes](../markdown/ga39_2011.md#ga39-p557)
- **[2009-25 — Brown v. Northern California Presbytery](../cases/ga39_2011__2009-25_2009-26.md)** (GA39/2011, sustained) — applies BCO 9-2/9-7 to a diaconate/property dispute; same provisions O14 amends. [minutes](../markdown/ga39_2011.md#ga39-p550)
- **[2009-26 — Session of Grace PC v. Northern California Presbytery](../cases/ga39_2011__2009-25_2009-26.md)** (GA39/2011, sustained) — companion to 2009-25 on BCO 9-2/9-7. [minutes](../markdown/ga39_2011.md#ga39-p550)
- **[2011-14 — Reese and Bech v. Philadelphia Presbytery](../cases/ga42_2014__2011-14.md)** (GA42/2014, sustained) — construes BCO 9-2's "impossible for any reason to secure deacons" clause: City Church Philadelphia invoked it to justify not forming a diaconate on grounds of conscience regarding women deacons; the SJC sustained the complaint that Presbytery erred in not acting on TE Hsu's violation of his ordination commitments; bears on O14's 9-2 duty-execution and diaconate-formation provisions. [minutes](../markdown/ga42_2014.md#ga42-p530)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **II.S** (GA50/2023) — CCB found NO constitutional conflict in amending BCO 7-3 to regulate titling of unordained individuals; the direct CCB ruling on the immediate predecessor (O26) of O14's 7-3 expansion. [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci19.md)
- **38-34 II.A** (GA38/2010) — CCB found an overture *prohibiting* deaconesses in conflict, reasoning "deaconess" need not denote an ordained office; directly contrary to O14's premise that "deaconess" is barred. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci02.md)
- **38-34 II.D** (GA38/2010) — CCB found an overture prohibiting commissioned/installed assistants to deacons in conflict on the same grounds; bears on O14's restriction of 9-7 assistants. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci03.md)
- **38-34 II.E** (GA38/2010) — CCB found an overture appointing unordained persons to diaconal ministry in conflict (implied unordained office contra BCO 17-1); construes BCO 9-2/9-7/7-2 together as O14 does. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci04.md)
- **App. O II.H** (GA46/2018) — CCB found Grace Presbytery's deaconess/women-officer overture in conflict, noting the session (not congregation) appoints and vows are required; bears on O14's 7-4/9-7 appointment limits. [page](../inquiries/ga46_2018__ci04.md)

### Note
This is the deterministic provision-exact spine (BCO 7-3/7-4/9-2/9-7) plus a concept supplement. **Pruned as incidental:** the GA12 pre-SJC procedural "case" (num=null, out_of_order — accusations v. presbytery, no construction of 7-3); inquiry **27-43 II.1** (deacon authority to *sell donated property* under 9-2 — property mechanics, not titles/offices) and CCB **II.W** (GA52, RAO 16 statistical reporting — only incidentally cites 9-2); prior overtures **[GA10 O17](../overtures/ga10_1982__o17.md)** (trustees assuming deacon admin duties) and **[GA22 O36](../overtures/ga22_1994__o36.md)** (IAR legal opinions/interim board) which matched on 9-2 property language but are unrelated to the female-officer/titles question; and the **BCO 7-3 Korean Southwest RPR** (52nd/2025 — an unaddressed delinquency report, no relation to titling). No renumbering affects chapters 7 or 9 (bco_renumberings.jsonl: the only ch.8/9 event is the 1980 merge of old ch.9 into ch.8, predating the modern 9-2/9-7 text). **Companion context:** O14 is the most aggressive of the recurring deaconess/female-officer line — directly extending the ratified **[GA50 O26](../overtures/ga50_2023__o26.md)** (7-3) and reviving the failed **GA38 O2/O9** (9-7) approach; related GA38 study-committee fights (O7 reconsider women deacons, O16 affirm deaconesses) sit in the same controversy. Fixed descriptions: **Kniseley 2007-13** was described as "construes BCO 9-2/9-7 where an unordained M.Div. holder was involved" — corrected; the case is about the title "minister" for unordained staff (BCO 7-2 / 40-2(3)), which bears on O14's 7-3 target. **Reese and Bech 2011-14** was described as "construes BCO 9-2 (deacon care of congregational property)" — corrected; the case construes BCO 9-2's "impossible for any reason to secure deacons" exemption clause in the context of City Church's refusal to form a diaconate due to views on women deacons, not property. Both cases are genuinely bearing on O14's provisions but their prior descriptions misstated what each held.
