# O84 — Amend BCO 12 and 13 to Require Annual Attestation of Officer's Views
**Targets:** BCO 12, 13 · **Source:** NWGA (Northwest Georgia Presbytery) · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-84_NWGA_1213.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-84_NWGA_1213.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds BCO 12-5.g / 12-6 and 13-9.h / 13-10 (renumbering the following paragraphs) to make it a power of the Session and Presbytery "to maintain sound doctrine," and requires each court to *annually record an attestation* from every officer (RE/deacon; TE/licentiate) stating whether his views remain unchanged or, if changed, in what regard he is now dubious or in disagreement with the Confession or Catechisms — recorded in his own words with the court's judgment, per the RAO 16-3.e.7 categories.

**Cites in its own grounds:** No prior cases, overtures, or inquiries. Cites RAO 16-3 (Good Faith Subscription), BCO 24-6 (minister's ethical burden to disclose a change of views), and Scripture (Jude 3; 1 Tim. 4:15-16).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2020-05 — Speck v. Missouri Presbytery](../cases/ga49_2022__2020-05.md)** (GA49/2022, sustained) — Sets the standard a court must apply when examining whether a TE's views accord with the Standards (BCO 11-3, 11-4, 13-9(f), not the 34-5 "vitals" test); directly bears on what a court's "judgment" on an officer's attested views would mean. [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md)
- **[2008-14 — White v. Siouxlands Presbytery](../cases/ga38_2010__2008-14.md)** (GA38/2010, sustained) — Court erred in refusing a BCO 31-2 investigation into a TE suspected of teaching contrary to the Standards; bears on the existing review-and-control machinery O84 says is inadequate (the "no mechanism to initiate recertification" Whereas). [minutes](../markdown/ga38_2010.md)
- **[2011-06 — Sawyers et al. v. Missouri Presbytery](../cases/ga41_2013__2011-06.md)** (GA41/2013, other) — Court found to have erred in not finding a presumption that a TE held views contrary to the Westminster Standards; illustrates the friction O84 targets, where doctrinal drift surfaces only through contested judicial process rather than routine attestation. [minutes](../markdown/ga41_2013.md)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **31-56 IV** (GA31/2003) — CCB held that neither the Constitution nor the RAO *requires* a presbytery to record a minister's exceptions in its minutes; O84 would impose exactly such an annual recording requirement. [page](../inquiries/ga31_2003__ci02.md)
- **30-29 III (Westminster overture)** (GA30/2002) — An overture mandating that presbyteries record and report all exceptions was found *in conflict* with the Constitution because it would bind a man's conscience beyond what the BCO permits — the core objection any mandatory-attestation scheme must answer. [page](../inquiries/ga30_2002__ci04.md)
- **32-40 II.G** (GA32/2004) — Overture amending the exception-stating requirements (BCO Preface II, ch. 16, 21-4, 24-6.2) found in conflict with the second ordination vow. [page](../inquiries/ga32_2004__ci05.md)
- **33-29 II.D** (GA33/2005) — Mandate requiring officers to explain/account for their doctrinal exceptions found in conflict with BCO Preliminary Principle 1 and WCF 20 (liberty of conscience); bears on compelling officers to articulate disagreement. [page](../inquiries/ga33_2005__ci03.md)
- **38-18 II.1** (GA38/2010) — CCB advised that the BCO provides *no mechanism* for formally recognizing/recording an officer's differences; O84 would create one. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci01.md)
- **App. O IV.B (42-...)** (GA42/2014) — CCB advised that no constitutional procedure exists to record a candidate's views, and a candidate is not required to list his differences. [page](../inquiries/ga42_2014__ci04.md)
- **51-... II.G** (GA51/2024) — CCB found the overture *adding* to BCO 12-5 (Great Commission requirement) not in conflict, adopted 8-0-0; bears directly because O84 likewise adds a new 12-5 subparagraph. [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci07.md)
- **51-... II.H** (GA51/2024) — CCB found the overture *adding* presbytery-care duties to BCO 13-9 not in conflict, adopted 8-0-0; the structural twin of O84's 13-9.h addition. [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci08.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA51 O9** (2024, Answered in the negative) — Add Great Commission requirement to BCO 12-5; the most recent attempt to add a new power-subparagraph to the very provision (12-5) O84 amends — and it failed on the floor. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p1270)
- **GA51 O10** (2024, Answered in the negative) — Add Presbytery TE care to BCO 13-9; the companion failed attempt to add to BCO 13-9, the exact provision O84 expands. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p1270)
- **GA30 O10** (2002, Adopted/final) — "Specify Good Faith Subscription (Version 2) in BCO"; established the GFS framework and the RAO 16-3 categories O84 expressly builds its attestation on. [page](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p75)
- **GA31 O5** (2003, Adopted/final) — Amend RAO 14-3/14-8 (now RAO 16) regarding confessional subscription; part of the same GFS recording regime O84 invokes via RAO 16-3.e.7. [page](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p63)
- **GA34 O17** (2006, Answered in the negative) — Amend BCO 21-4 ¶7 regarding teaching approved exceptions; a prior attempt to legislate ongoing handling of an officer's stated differences. [page](../markdown/ga34_2006.md#ga34-p207)
- **GA35 O15** (2007, Answered in the negative) — Amend BCO 21-4 ¶7 regarding teaching of approved exceptions; same theme, again rejected. [page](../markdown/ga35_2007.md#ga35-p166)

### RPR exceptions
None found. The BCO 12 / 13 RPR threads concern review-of-records mechanics (failure to review session minutes, BCO 12-7 / 13-9b; examination of REs, 12-5.b) — not the recording of officers' confessional views. There is no RPR thread on annual attestation of views because no such requirement currently exists, which is the very gap O84 seeks to fill. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)
