# O81 — Amend RAO 1-1 to Require Serving Wine and Grape Juice for the Lord's Supper at GA
**Targets:** RAO 1-1 · **Source:** Potomac · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-81_Potomac_RAO1-1.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-81_Potomac_RAO1-1.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends RAO 1-1 (the order for the GA opening worship service) by adding a sentence requiring that, in the administration of the Lord's Supper at General Assembly, both wine and grape juice "shall be provided and clearly identified, to respect the consciences of all commissioners," and directs the Stated Clerk to implement it beginning with the next GA.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None — it cites only Scripture (Matt. 26:29, Ps. 104:15, Isa. 25:6), WCF 29.3, and WLC Q.168–169; no prior PCA cases, overtures, or inquiries by number.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **29-28 III.5** (GA29/2001) — CCB advised Great Lakes Presbytery that BCO 58-4 "allows no exception of practice in administering the Lord's Supper." Bears on how far a court may regulate the manner/elements of the Supper, which is the question O81 raises by mandating provision of both elements at GA. [page](../inquiries/ga29_2001__ci05.md)
- **21-56 III** (GA21/1993) — GA construed the permissible scope of communion practice under BCO 58-4 (denied closed communion; affirmed open/close communion). Background on the Assembly's prior treatment of regulating practice at the Table. [page](../inquiries/ga21_1993__ci01.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA18 O14** (1990, Answered in the negative) — Southwest's overture to amend BCO 58-5 by replacing "Wine" with "Fruit of the Vine" — the most directly on-point prior attempt to legislate the communion element. GA declined to alter the element language, leaving wine/grape-juice practice unregulated; O81 instead mandates providing both at GA. [page](../markdown/ga18_1990.md#ga18-p72)
- **GA52 O12** (2025, Approved but not ratified) — Providence's overture to amend BCO 58-5 on distribution of the Lord's Supper; the most recent live amendment activity around how the Supper is administered. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1189)
- **GA21 O25** (1993, Answered in the negative) — Third RPC's overture to change the invitation to the Sacrament of the Lord's Supper; another attempt to regulate Supper practice that GA declined. [page](../markdown/ga21_1993.md#ga21-p55)
