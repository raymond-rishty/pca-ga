# O64 — Amend BCO 23-2 to Establish Associate Membership for Honorably Retired TEs
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back
**Targets:** BCO 23-2 · **Source:** Canada West · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Overture-64_CanadaWest_23-2.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Overture-64_CanadaWest_23-2.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 23-2 so that a medically-disabled or honorably-retired TE retaining membership in his Presbytery must also maintain an active associate membership (or equivalent) in a faithful local church, subject to the same limitations as BCO 46-4 — securing local pastoral care, oversight, and accountability for retired ministers.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (cites only BCO 23-2 itself and BCO 46-4 as the "paradigm and precedent for associate membership"; no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries are named).

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **17-82 III,19** (GA17/1989, not in conflict) — Illiana Presbytery's standing rule relieving retired ministers from mandatory reporting was found not in conflict with BCO 23-2, with the presbytery advised to use its own wisdom under BCO 41-5. Bears directly: speaks to the very gap O64 targets — that retired TEs receive less active oversight/accountability under 23-2. [page](../inquiries/ga17_1989__ci04.md)
- **31-56 VI** (GA31/2003, in conflict) — Overture restricting presbytery/GA voting to pastors only held in conflict with BCO 3-1 and 14-2; 23-2 cited as part of the set defining TE membership rights. Bears on the "rights and privileges" of retired-TE presbytery membership that O64 leaves intact. [page](../inquiries/ga31_2003__ci09.md)
- **32-40 II.D** (GA32/2004, in conflict) — CCB reaffirmed (re-raising the same proposal) that restricting voting to pastors disenfranchises non-pastor TEs; same 23-2 membership-rights cluster. [page](../inquiries/ga32_2004__ci04.md)

### RPR exceptions

### Note
**Companion cross-link:** O64 is the 23-2 counterpart to **[GA52 O25](../overtures/ga52_2025__o25.md)** (which sought the same associate-membership-for-retired-TEs result via 46-4); read together. **Pruning:** the provision-exact spine's lone judicial hit (Bigelow 2024-5) cites 23-2 only in passing within a large discipline/jurisdiction appeal and does not bear — pruned from the cases section. The voting-restriction inquiries (31-56, 32-40) cite 23-2 within the TE membership-rights cluster rather than the retirement/oversight question, so they bear only obliquely; 17-82 (retired-minister oversight) is the on-point inquiry. **No renumbering** affects BCO 23-2 (no entry in bco_renumberings.jsonl for chapter 23). The RPR row from Missouri (retired TE released to a session's care) is the strongest evidence of live presbytery practice anticipating O64's local-church-membership requirement.
