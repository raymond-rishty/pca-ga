# O42 — Restore the Reference to the Westminster Standards at the end of BCO 29-1
**Targets:** BCO 29-1 · **Source:** NorCal · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-42_NorCal_29-1.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-42_NorCal_29-1.pdf)

**What it does:** Directs the Stated Clerk to restore the phrase "as interpreted in these Standards" to the end of BCO 29-1's final sentence, so an offense must be provable "from Scripture as interpreted in these Standards" (the Westminster Standards) — language the overture says was in the original 1973 BCO but disappeared from modern editions with no located GA action removing it. It treats this as a clerical restoration (no ratification needed), while welcoming an amendment process if CCB deems ratification required.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (cites only commentaries — Almquist's *Our Bond of Union* (2025) p.247, Ramsay's *Exposition* (1898), Morton Smith's *Commentary* (2007) — the original 1973 BCO, WLC 136, and the OPC Book of Discipline; no GA cases, overtures, or inquiries).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **2007-8 — Jones v. Louisiana Presbytery (Wilkins)** (GA36/2008, sustained) — Turns on the "proper Constitutional standard" for judging whether a TE's doctrine is out of accord with "the standards of our Constitution" / the Westminster Standards — exactly the offense-judging function the lost phrase performs. [minutes](../markdown/ga36_2008.md#ga36-p117)
- **2006-2 — Central Carolina memorial re: Louisiana & TE Wilkins** (GA36/2008, other) — Frames the charge as teaching doctrine "in flagrant contradiction to our Standards," i.e. whether a doctrinal offense is measured against the Westminster Standards. [minutes](../markdown/ga36_2008.md#ga36-p75)
- **[2011-11 — Hahn v. Philadelphia Metro West Presbytery](../cases/ga42_2014__2011-11_2011-12_2011-15_2011-16.md)** (GA42/2014, denied) — Quotes the final sentence of BCO 29-1 *already missing* "as interpreted in these Standards" — direct evidence of the omission O42 seeks to cure and of the SJC operating on the truncated text. [minutes](../markdown/ga42_2014.md#ga42-p502)
- **[2024-18 — Harris v. Wisconsin Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-18.md)** (GA52/2025, denied) — Construes BCO 29-1's definition of an offense as "the proper object of judicial process," cautioning "Sometimes, we take BCO 29-1 too far" — bears on the scope-limiting purpose of the restored phrase. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1037)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **App. O II.E** (GA46/2018, in conflict) — CCB found a proposed BCO 59 marriage-consent revision in conflict, reasoning in part from BCO 29-1; bears insofar as it uses 29-1's offense definition to test whether a contested practice may be treated as an offense. ### Note
O42 is a corrective/restoration overture, so the highest-value evidence is not adverse precedent but the corpus showing the *current* (truncated) text in operation — see Hahn (2011-11), which quotes 29-1's final sentence without the phrase. The provision-exact spine returned 32 cases citing BCO 29-1; the great majority cite it only for the boilerplate "anything... contrary to the Word of God" definition of an offense in passing (1990-8, 1993-10, 1993-12, the John Wood Matter, 2002-1, 2005-1, the bare GA36 p.85 entry, 2009-28, the 2009-16 and 2009-03 matters, 2010-26, 2011-6, 2012-4, 2012-5, 2013-1, 2015-10, 2015-11, 2016-8, 2016-9, 2016-11, 2019-1, 2019-3, 2020-4, 2020-6, 2021-13, 2022-7, 2024-2). These 27 are pruned as incidental. The four retained cases each engage the *standard by which a (especially doctrinal) offense is judged against the Westminster Standards* — the exact function of the restored phrase. The 2025 CI on BCO 32-2 (II.A) cites 29-1 only as part of the discipline framework and is pruned; the 2018 marriage advice (App. O II.E) is retained as borderline-bearing. GA40 O26 (in thesi statements on evolution/Adam) is pruned as too tangential. No renumbering applies — chapter 29 is not in bco_renumberings.jsonl. (This replaces a first pass whose CI ga27/1999 ci03 citation did not appear in the deterministic spine and could not be verified as a corpus deep-link; it has been dropped.)
Pruned as incidental: 2019-11 Crouse v. Northwest Georgia Presbytery — BCO 29-1 appears once in the majority opinion (alongside 29-2 and 32-18) for the narrow proposition that a Session need not bring charges before exercising election discretion; the case construes BCO 24-1/39-3 re-election procedure, not 29-1's Westminster Standards interpretive role. The Westminster Standards references in the case concern the candidate's examination questions, unconnected to 29-1.
