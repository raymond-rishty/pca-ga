# O39 — Amend BCO 22 to Affirm the Consent of the Governed for Assistant Pastors
**Targets:** BCO 22 (specifically 22-3 and 22-4) · **Source:** Central Florida Presbytery (CFL) · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-39_CFL_22.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-39_CFL_22.pdf)

**What it does:** Caps an assistant pastor's call at a definite period (ordinarily ≤5 years); to retain him beyond that, the Session must recommend him to the **congregation** for election as associate pastor (majority vote), or else move to dissolve the call under BCO 23 — giving the congregation a periodic consent vote over a continuing teaching elder. Adds an editorial comment grandfathering calls established before June 22, 2027.

**Cites in its own grounds:** **Overture 10, 52nd GA (2025)** — O39 expressly states it "improves Overture 10 from the 52nd General Assembly, which was referred back to the Central Florida Presbytery for refinement and potential resubmission" (O39 is that resubmission). It also invokes the editorial-comment precedent following BCO 24-10 to justify its effective-date carve-out (BCO 24-10 is a constitutional text, not a past judicial/legislative action, so it is not listed below). Scriptural/constitutional grounds cited: Preliminary Principle 6, BCO 3-1, 8-9, 16-2; Acts 6:3, 14:23; and the 1945 PCUS BCO (ch. xxiv ¶126).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2024-05 — Bigelow v. North Florida Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-05.md)** (GA52/2025, granted in part) — Directly construes BCO 22-2, 22-3, and 22-4 in dissolving an assistant pastor's call; the SJC held that no congregational vote was constitutionally required to dissolve an assistant pastor's call — the precise lay-consent gap O39 seeks to close. ⭐ [full text](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-05.md)
- **[2007-09/2007-10 — Complaints of TE Eliot Lee vs. Korean Eastern Presbytery](../cases/ga37_2009__2007-09_2007-10.md)** (GA37/2009, sustained) — Annulled higher-court action over a local pastoral relation taken "without congregational consent," articulating the non-hierarchical, consent-of-the-governed principle O39 builds its grounds upon. [full text](../cases/ga37_2009__2007-09_2007-10.md)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CCB II.G — advice on Overture 10** (GA52/2025) — The CCB's advice on O39's own predecessor: it found the consent-for-assistant-pastors amendment "ambiguous… [because] it did not address how current assistant pastors would be treated upon adoption." O39's new effective-date editorial comment is the direct fix for this objection — the single highest-value bearing action. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga52_2025__ci07.md)
- **CCB 33-29 II.C** (GA33/2005) — CCB found a prior overture detailing how assistant pastors are called to be in conflict with BCO 3-2 and 12-2 through 12-4, on the view that the assistant pastor's relationship to a congregation "is best left to the session" — the polity counter-argument O39 must overcome. [page](../inquiries/ga33_2005__ci02.md)
- **CI 17-82 III,17** (GA17/1989) — Tennessee Valley Presbytery asked whether the 1988 BCO 22-3/22-4 assistant pastor amendments applied retroactively; bears directly on O39's effective-date / non-retroactivity editorial comment. [page](../inquiries/ga17_1989__ci03.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA52 O10** (2025, Referred) — "Amend BCO 22 to Affirm the 'Consent of the Governed' Regarding Assistant Pastors" (Central Florida). The direct predecessor; referred back to CFL for refinement and resubmitted as O39. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1185)
- **GA49 O2** (2022) — "Amend BCO 22-3 to Allow Assistant Pastors to File Complaints Against Sessions" (Tennessee Valley); another attempt to recalibrate the assistant pastor / Session relationship under 22-3. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p1286)
- **GA48 O31** (2021, Approved & ratified 2022) — "Amend BCO 22 Process for Assistant to Associate Pastor" (Northwest Georgia); the most recent successful BCO 22 assistant-pastor amendment, establishing the assistant→associate conversion process O39 modifies. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga48_2021.md#ga48-p1131)
- **GA33 O7** (2005, Answered in the negative) — "Amend BCO 21-5, 6, 7, 8, 10 and 22-4 Regarding Assistant Pastors" (Chesapeake); a failed prior attempt to reshape assistant pastor calls (the overture the CCB 33-29 advice above addressed). [page](../markdown/ga33_2005.md#ga33-p195)
- **GA16 O3** (1988, Answered in the negative) — "Amend BCO 22-2 to Require Congregational Election of Assistant Pastors" (Northeast); the earliest and most on-point prior attempt at the very change O39 seeks — congregational consent/election for assistant pastors — and it failed. [page](../markdown/ga16_1988.md#ga16-p46)

- **BCO 22-3** — 6 citations faulting assistant pastor calls (e.g., Georgia Foothills 52nd/2025: assistant pastor appointed "without record of receiving a call"; Korean Capital 52nd/2025: assistant-to-associate change without proper election); recurring friction at the call/consent boundary O39 targets. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)
- **BCO 22-4** — 10 citations repeatedly faulting the lack of congregational/session action in changing or dissolving assistant pastor calls (e.g., Gulfstream 50th/2023 & 51st/2024: "No record of request from congregation to a change of call from assistant pastor to associate pastor"); concrete evidence that the consent gap O39 addresses generates records exceptions.
