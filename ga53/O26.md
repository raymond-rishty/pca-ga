# O26 — Amend BCO 26-2 to Only Count Presbyteries Who Submit Votes
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 102-28-0)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Refer back to the court of origin
**Targets:** BCO 26-2 · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-26_Westminster_26-2.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-26_Westminster_26-2.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 26-2.2 so the two-thirds (2/3) advice-and-consent threshold for ratifying constitutional amendments is computed only against presbyteries that actually submit a vote ("two-thirds (2/3) of the *voting* Presbyteries"), rather than counting a non-submitting presbytery's silence as a "no." It treats a non-reporting presbytery as absent (not abstaining) and points to BCO 26-6 deferral as the remedy when a presbytery later wishes to be heard.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (no prior PCA cases, overtures, or inquiries cited; the grounds rest on RONR § 44:3 and BCO 26-6).

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **III.C** (GA47/2019, advice given; Adopted by the CCB) — "Questions on the Effect of Presbytery Division upon Advice and Consent": the CCB advised that a newly erected presbytery may give advice and consent on amendments already voted by its predecessor, but individual presbyters may not vote twice after transfer (provisions: BCO 8-2.a.1, **BCO 26**). Directly construes how the advice-and-consent count under BCO 26 is composed — the same question O26 raises about which presbyteries count toward the 2/3. [page](../inquiries/ga47_2019__ci24.md)

### Note
Spine was provision-exact on "BCO 26-2" and high-recall: every one of its judicial-case and CCB hits proved incidental (26-2 appearing in a long citation list, or as boilerplate that an amendment must follow the 26-2 process), so they were pruned — only GA38 O5 (prior 26-2 amendment) and the adjacent GA30 O27 / CCB 30-29 III survive from the spine. The genuinely on-point action, GA47 CI III.C, was MISSED by the exact-provision spine because it is tagged "BCO 26" (not "BCO 26-2"); it was recovered by topical search and is the closest construction of the advice-and-consent count. Real-world friction confirming the need O26 addresses appears at [GA27 p.59](../markdown/ga27_1999.md#ga27-p59) (1999): an amendment to BCO 24-1 fell short of the required two-thirds (the floor vote was FOR 33, AGAINST 13) and so was "Deferred to the 28th General Assembly [BCO 26-2; 26-6]," with non-reporting presbyteries "urged to report their judgment to the next General Assembly" — exactly the scenario where treating silence as "no" can defeat ratification, which O26 seeks to correct. No renumbering of BCO 26-2 is recorded (bco_renumberings.jsonl has no Chapter 26 entry). No GA48–52 overture revisited 26-2, so no recent (⭐) overture applies.
