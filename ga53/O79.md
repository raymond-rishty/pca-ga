# O79 — Amend RAO 4-21 to Authorize Electronic Meetings for GA Committees and Commissions
**Targets:** RAO 4-21 (new; existing 4-21 renumbered → 4-22; conform cross-ref RAO 14-11.a) · **Source:** Calvary Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-79_Calvary_RAO4-21.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-79_Calvary_RAO4-21.pdf)

**What it does:** Inserts a new RAO 4-21 expressly authorizing General Assembly committees and commissions (except the Overtures Committee, Nominating Committee, and Committees of Commissioners) to meet electronically for called meetings under their own rules, and directly authorizing ad hoc/ad interim committees to meet electronically at the call of the Chairman or Convener; the existing 4-21 (minutes guidelines) is renumbered 4-22, with a conforming cross-reference fix in RAO 14-11.a.

**Cites in its own grounds:** RONR (12th ed.) 9:30–36, 9:35, 2:8, 2:25; BCO 12-6 (electronic Session meetings); BCO 13-4 (electronic Presbytery meetings); OMSJC 4.1 (SJC telephone conference) — cited as constitutional/parliamentary precedent, not as prior overtures/cases/inquiries by number.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **17-82 III, 8** (GA17/1989, advice given) — CCB construed BCO 13-4 (the Presbytery analogue O79 invokes) and ruled **telephone conference meetings are impermissible** absent express authorization; the historical baseline the recent BCO 12-6/13-4 amendments reversed and that O79 now extends to GA committees — directly bears on the requirement that electronic meetings be expressly authorized rather than left to custom. [page](../inquiries/ga17_1989__ci02.md)
- **27-43 II.2** (GA27/1999, advice given) — CCB advised a presbytery may receive a candidate via "interactive electronic means" to satisfy BCO 18-3's appearance requirement; an early recognition of electronic participation's legitimacy in church courts, supporting the overture's claim that the Church's framework already accepts electronic meetings. [page](../inquiries/ga27_1999__ci02.md)
- **GA50 CCB II.D** (GA50/2023, not in conflict; adopted 8-0-0) — found Southern New England's overture expanding RAO 4-21.d (Committees of Commissioners reviewing compliance policies) not in conflict; construes the same paragraph this overture renumbers. [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci04.md)
- **GA51 CCB II.I** (GA51/2024, not in conflict; adopted 8-0-0) — found Northwest Georgia's overture amending RAO 4-21.d (enrollment-data reporting) not in conflict; the most recent CCB construction of RAO 4-21, showing the section is live and routinely amendable. [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci09.md)

### Note
Companion/duplicate: **O78** (Southeast Alabama / SEAL Presbytery) has the identical title and the same RAO 4-21 target — O78 and O79 should be considered together by the Overtures Committee. This overture's question (electronic meetings) is novel for RAO 4: the exact-provision spine returns no judicial cases and no RPR. The two highest-value bearing actions (GA17 17-82 III,8 and GA27 27-43 II.2) are topical supplements the provision-exact spine missed, found by concept search on electronic/telephone meetings; they are included above. The BCO 12-6 / 13-4 electronic-meeting amendments the overture cites in its rationale do not appear as cleanly-titled rows in the OVERTURES index, so no deep-link is asserted for them. PRUNED as incidental: GA47 CCB inquiry III.A (SJC/agency-board eligibility — cites RAO 4-21 only inside a long RAO 4 list) and the GA47 CCB cluster II.G / II.J / II.O / II.P / II.R (non-ordained persons on GA committees, all citing RAO 4-21.c.6 on committee composition) — these construe membership/composition, not electronic meetings, and do not bear on this change. The kept inquiries and prior overtures all concern RAO 4-21.d; they bear because (a) they confirm RAO 4-21 is a live, frequently-amended rule, and (b) this overture renumbers the existing 4-21 to 4-22, so the renumbering mechanics must not break the 4-21.d cross-references those recent amendments created.
