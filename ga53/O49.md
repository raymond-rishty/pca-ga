# O49 — Amend BCO 13-1 and 14-2 to Increase Ruling Elder Representation at Presbytery and GA
**Targets:** BCO 13-1, 14-2 · **Source:** Northwest Georgia Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-49_NWGA_RE-Representation.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-49_NWGA_RE-Representation.pdf)

**What it does:** Raises each congregation's ruling-elder allotment from two to three RE representatives for the first 350 communing members in both presbytery (BCO 13-1) and General Assembly (BCO 14-2), keeping the existing "one additional RE per 500 members" tier. Aim: reverse the decline in RE participation (cited at ~20-30% of GA vs. 54% in 1973) without new mandated expense.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (cites BCO 7-1 on two-office parity and a 2018 byFaith article on RE participation; no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries by number).

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
None found. *(The provision spine returned 16 cases citing BCO 13-1/14-2, but all are incidental — quorum, geographic bounds, membership, and disciplinary disputes that cite these chapters in passing. None construes the RE-representation allotment this overture changes. Pruned; see Note.)*

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **47-II.A** (GA47/2019) — CCB found a prior Northwest Georgia Presbytery overture revising BCO 14-2 to permit additional RE representation at GA was **not in conflict** and adopted it; the single most on-point precedent — same presbytery, same goal, same provision. [page](../inquiries/ga47_2019__ci01.md)
- **31-56 VI** (GA31/2003) — CCB found an overture restricting presbytery/GA voting to pastors **in conflict** with BCO 3-1 and 14-2 (all TEs entitled to representation); construes the representation entitlement structure of 14-2. [page](../inquiries/ga31_2003__ci09.md)
- **32-40 II.D** (GA32/2004) — CCB reaffirmed the prior year's holding that limiting TE voting to pastors conflicts with BCO 3-1/14-2; bears on the parity/representation logic underlying this overture. [page](../inquiries/ga32_2004__ci04.md)
- **35-30 II.H** (GA35/2007) — CCB found an overture conditioning presbytery voting on church financial contributions **not in conflict** with BCO 13-1/14-2 (minority dissent); directly construes who is entitled to representation in the higher courts. [page](../inquiries/ga35_2007__ci03.md)

### Note
**Companion/lineage:** O49 is the latest in a long line of RE-representation overtures and the direct successor to Northwest Georgia's own GA47 O1 (2019). The current "two (2)" figure in BCO 13-1 was itself set by GA29 O21 (ratified 2002) — O49 simply raises that number to three and conforms 14-2 to match. **CCB precedent is favorable on constitutionality:** 47-II.A and 35-30 II.H both found representation-expansion/eligibility overtures not in conflict, while 31-56/32-40 only struck attempts to *restrict* (not expand) elder representation — so no constitutional bar to O49 appears in the record. **Pruning:** all 16 spine cases were dropped as incidental — the provision pair (13-1/14-2) is cited across quorum, geographic-bounds, membership, and discipline disputes, but none adjudicates the RE allotment count. Numerous additional 14-2 parity/delegated-assembly overtures from GA12–GA16 (e.g. GA13 O20, GA15 O20, GA16 O7) were pruned as restructuring efforts rather than simple allotment increases. **RPR caveat:** the join's "RAO 14-2" rows (8) concern commissioner *election* procedure under the RAO, not BCO 14-2, and were excluded. **Renumbering:** BCO 13 and 14 have not been renumbered; the old-number search was not needed.
