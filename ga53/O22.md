# O22 — Amend BCO 13-8 to Apply Only to Churches from Outside the PCA
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 102-28-0)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Refer back to the court of origin
**Targets:** BCO 13-8 · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-22_Westminster_13-8.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-22_Westminster_13-8.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds "from outside of the Presbyterian Church in America" to BCO 13-8 so the commission-to-examine and re-affirmation-of-ordination-vows requirement applies only to churches joining from outside the PCA — not to a PCA congregation already in the denomination that a General Assembly boundary change moves from one presbytery to another (such transfers to be regarded as immediate).

**Cites in its own grounds:** None — the whereas clauses reason from the text of 13-8 and the boundary-change scenario; no prior case, overture, or inquiry is named.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2020-06 — Gordon v. Southern New England Presbytery](../cases/ga49_2022__2020-06.md)** (GA49/2022, denied) — directly applies BCO 13-8: First Presbyterian Church North Shore left the OPC and joined the PCA, and the SNEP commission interviewed its ruling elders "in accordance with BCO 13-8." The appeal (and a dissent) turned on jurisdiction over a member when a church is received into the PCA. This is the exact "church from outside the PCA" reception that O22 preserves, and shows 13-8 functioning as intended on external reception. [minutes](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p719)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
None found. (The spine surfaced inquiry **10-23** (GA10/1982), but it lists BCO 13-8 only in passing among provisions on Committee-of-Commissioners authority over MtW cooperative agreements — wholly unrelated to receiving churches; pruned.)

### Prior overtures
- **[GA6 O33](../overtures/ga06_1978__o33.md)** (1978) — "Amend BCO by Adding BCO 14-7 Requiring Elder Examination Before Receiving Churches"; the historical origin of the elder-examination-on-reception duty now housed in BCO 13-8. Bears as the enacting rationale O22 narrows. [page](../markdown/ga06_1978.md#ga06-p38)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 13-8** — 29 citations of presbytery minutes lacking the required commission/elder-examination record on receiving a church; many concern churches *transferring into or petitioning to join the PCA* (e.g., [Korean Eastern](../rpr/korean-eastern.md) 49th/2022 & 51st/2024: "no record of examination of elders for a church that has petitioned to join the PCA"; [Korean Southern](../rpr/korean-southern.md) 49th/2022: "received a new church into its membership without examining its ruling elders"; [Korean Northwest](../rpr/korean-northwest.md) 48th/2021 & 51st/2024: "no record of commission of Presbytery meeting with church ruling elders before receiving the church"). This recurring friction is the live evidence behind O22's premise that 13-8's exam duty is meaningful on external reception. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)
