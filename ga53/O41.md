# O41 — Amend RAO 4-9 to Require 2/3 Majority to Elect Stated Clerk and Coordinators
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer by reference to [O43](O43.md) *(vote 105-20-1)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Answer with reference to the action on [O43](O43.md)
**Targets:** RAO 4-9 · **Source:** Savannah River · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-41_SavannahRiver_RAO4-9.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/Overture-41_SavannahRiver_RAO4-9.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends RAO 4-9 to add that a permanent-Committee coordinator and the Stated Clerk "shall be elected or re-elected only by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the commissioners present and voting," replacing the current simple-majority threshold; effective immediately upon adoption for all future elections and re-elections.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None by case/overture/inquiry. The grounds cite only provisions by analogy — supermajority precedents in BCO 26-2, 3 (amendments); BCO 31-10; 34-8, 10 (discipline); BCO 42-6 (judicial appeals); and RAO 4-11 (budget modifications).

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **Supplemental II.2** (GA52/2025) — Floor nominations for Stated Clerk when the AC brings no nominee; CCB ruled (8-0-0) that floor nominations are permitted under RAO 3-4 even if the AC brings no nominee. Construes the RAO 4-9 Stated-Clerk election mechanism a 2/3 threshold would re-condition. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga52_2025__ci35.md)
- **Supplemental III.2** (GA49/2022) — Floor nomination procedure for the office of Stated Clerk; CCB advised floor nominations follow the same procedure as other contested offices, with TEC examination required. Directly construes the RAO 4-9 Stated-Clerk election the overture would re-threshold. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci30.md)
- **Supplemental III.1** (GA49/2022) — Whether Committees of Commissioners may nominate substitute program coordinators; CCB ruled a CoC may only approve or disapprove a coordinator nominee, not nominate a substitute. Construes the RAO 4-9 coordinator-election framework the overture amends. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci29.md)

### Prior overtures
- **[GA39 O2](../overtures/ga39_2011__o2.md)** (2011, Answered in the negative) — Amend RAO 4-9 to set term limits for program-committee coordinators and the Stated Clerk. A prior, provision-exact attempt to tighten this same officer-election rule (term limits, not vote threshold); declined. [page](../markdown/ga39_2011.md#ga39-p77)
- **[GA19 O43](../overtures/ga19_1991__o43.md)** (1991, Answered in the negative) — Amend RAO 4-9 to limit coordinators and the Stated Clerk to two four-year terms. The earliest attempt to amend RAO 4-9's officer rules; also declined, showing the Assembly's long-standing reluctance to tighten this provision. [page](../markdown/ga19_1991.md#ga19-p60)

### Note
RAO 4-9 is a Rules-of-Assembly-Operations provision, not BCO, so there are no SJC/CJB cases or RPR exceptions on it (the spine returned both empty) — expected and correct. The two prior overtures are provision-exact RAO 4-9 amendments but targeted **term limits**, not a **vote threshold**; they bear as prior attempts to amend the same officer-election provision and as evidence the Assembly has twice declined to tighten it, though neither tested a supermajority specifically. All three inquiries are genuine RAO 4-9 construction (Stated Clerk / coordinator election procedure) and are retained; none pruned as incidental. The prior first-pass draft cited a GA29/2001 CCB inquiry, GA40 O9, and GA15 O37, plus an O40/O43/O73/O77 GA53 cluster — none of these appear in the deterministic RAO 4-9 spine and could not be verified as real corpus deep-links, so they have been dropped to keep every citation provision-exact and link-backed.
