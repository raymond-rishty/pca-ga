# O74 — Add a New Chapter BCO 17A 'Of Commissioning for Non-Ordained Ministry'
**Targets:** BCO 17A (new chapter) · **Source:** E Carolina · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-74_ECar_17A.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-74_ECar_17A.pdf)
**What it does:** Adds a new chapter (17A) creating a constitutional category of "commissioning," by which a Session or Presbytery may publicly set apart a qualified member — explicitly "men or women" — for a non-ordained ministry role (teaching, discipleship, mercy, leadership, administration, counseling) without conferring ordained office. It builds in safeguards that commissioning may not create new offices, confer ruling/teaching authority, or blur BCO 7 and 8.
**Cites in its own grounds:** No prior cases, overtures, or inquiries. The grounds cite only Scripture (Acts 13:1–3; 14:23; 15:22, 27, 32; etc.) and constitutional text BCO 7-2, 17-1, and BCO 7 and 8 generally.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **38-34 II.E** (GA38/2010) — CCB found Northern California's overture appointing unordained men and women to diaconal ministry roles in conflict, partly because it implied an unordained office contrary to BCO 17-1 — the most on-point precedent against a constitutional "non-ordained ministry" category. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci04.md)
- **38-34 II.A** (GA38/2010) — CCB held "deaconess" need not denote an ordained office equivalent to deacon (BCO 9-7); bears on whether commissioning women is constitutionally permissible. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci02.md)
- **38-34 II.D** (GA38/2010) — CCB found an overture prohibiting commissioned/installed assistants to deacons in conflict, on the same BCO 9-7 grounds; bears directly on commissioning non-ordained assistants. [page](../inquiries/ga38_2010__ci03.md)
- **46 II.H** (GA46/2018) — CCB found Grace Presbytery's deaconess/women-officer overture in conflict, noting the Session (not congregation) is the appointing body and vows are required (BCO 9-7, 11-2, 24-11); bears on O74's Session/Presbytery authorization mechanism. [page](../inquiries/ga46_2018__ci04.md)
- **47 II.Q** (GA47/2019) — CCB found Metro New York's overture letting local sessions decide whether women may serve as deacons NOT in conflict (BCO 7-2, 9-3, 17-3); a key data point that some women-in-service schemes pass CCB review. [page](../inquiries/ga47_2019__ci17.md)
- **50 II.S** (GA50/2023) — CCB found amending BCO 7-3 to regulate titling of unordained persons not in conflict; bears on the "what may an unordained commissioned person be called" boundary O74 raises. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci19.md)
- **51 II.J** (GA51/2024) — CCB found amending BCO 7-2 to limit ordination to biological males not in conflict; frames the ordination/non-ordination line O74 invokes. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci10.md)
- **12-53 III,69** (GA12/1984) — CCB advised that believing Scripture permits women deacons (while abiding by BCO in practice) is not alone grounds to deny ordination (BCO 7-2, 9-3, 21-5); early construal of the same office/non-office tension. [page](../inquiries/ga12_1984__ci09.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA6 O5** (1978) — "Add BCO Language Authorizing Sessions to Commission Non-Ordained Church Workers" (Western Carolinas) — the nearest prior attempt at exactly O74's reform; outcome not recorded in the index. [page](../markdown/ga06_1978.md#ga06-p35)

[page](../markdown/ga38_2010.md#ga38-p392)
- **GA38 O16** (2010) — "Affirm the Permissibility of Unordained Deaconesses in the PCA" (Tennessee Valley) — affirmative companion to the 2010 cluster. [page](../markdown/ga38_2010.md#ga38-p390)
- **GA38 O2** (2010) — "Amend BCO 9-7 to Prohibit Deaconesses" (Central Carolina) — the contrary side of the 2010 debate; basis of CCB 38-34 II.A. [page](../markdown/ga38_2010.md#ga38-p386)
- **GA46 O25** (2018, Answered in the negative) — "Address the Unordained Diaconate" (Savannah River) — directly about formalizing unordained service; rejected. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga46_2018.md#ga46-p774)
- **GA46 O9** (2018, Approved → presbyteries; ratification not located) — Amend BCO 9-7 and add 24-11 re Women Serving as Deaconesses (Grace) — passed the floor but did not ratify; basis of CCB 46 II.H. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga46_2018.md#ga46-p684)
- **GA50 O26** (2023, Approved & ratified 2024) — Amend BCO 7-3 to Prohibit Ecclesiastical Titles for Unordained People (Northwest Georgia) — the only one of this family that became constitutional text; constrains what a commissioned non-ordained person may be titled, a live tension for O74. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga50_2023.md#ga50-p1028)
- **GA51 O29** (2024, Answered by reference) — Amend BCO 53 to Prohibit Women from Preaching in Chapel and Campus Ministry Services (Bryce Avenue) — bounds the teaching/preaching roles O74 would open to commissioned women. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p1182)
- **GA12 O28** (1984, Answered in the negative) — "Study the Question of Women Serving in the Diaconate" (Delmarva) — earliest GA-level engagement with women in formal service. [page](../markdown/ga12_1984.md#ga12-p44)

### Note
Companion GA53 overtures on the same axis: **O14** (SWFL) and **O48** (NWGA), both amending BCO 7-2/7-3/9-7 on titles/commissioning of unordained persons, and **O37** (Pacific) to allow women to serve as ordained deacons — O74 should be read alongside these (it takes the "new category" route rather than amending existing office chapters). Renumbering: BCO chapter 17 has NOT been renumbered, so no old-number search was needed. Pruning: the high-recall topical sweep surfaced many incidental hits I dropped — GA "commissioner" allotment/parity overtures (BCO 14-2) and judicial-"commission" CCB inquiries (BCO 15) use the word "commission" in unrelated senses; women-in-combat resolutions, women on GA agency committees/boards (BCO 14-1), and the women-preaching-in-worship line (BCO 12-5/53) touch adjacent questions but do not construe the ordination/commissioning distinction O74 creates, so they were excluded except where (GA51 O29) they directly bound the roles O74 enables.
