# O7 — Amend RAO 16 Regarding RPR's Reporting on Presbytery Records
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the negative *(vote 75-54-1)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Answer in the negative
**Targets:** RAO 16-7 (with conforming changes to 16-3.e.(8/9) and 16-4.c.(3)) · **Source:** Pacific Northwest (to CCB, OC) · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-7_PNW_RPR_rev.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-7_PNW_RPR_rev.pdf)

> **Grounds:** The current rules with respect to RPR allow the Assembly to act on exceptions of substance which RPR has recommended with respect to a Presbytery's minutes. This overture would grant RPR the power to find such exceptions, submit them to Presbyteries, and require a response without action of the General Assembly. The committee believes it best not to grant RPR such powers.

**What it does:** Restructures the RPR Committee's reporting so that exceptions of substance and most recommendations are approved by the *Committee* itself (and merely recorded in the report) rather than coming to the GA floor as omnibus recommendations; only a recommendation that a presbytery's *second* response be judged Unsatisfactory (and OMSJC-15 / BCO 40-5 matters) would still come to the Assembly. Purpose: a 2,000-commissioner Assembly cannot meaningfully act on ~500 recommendations in a 215-page report posted 12 days before GA.

**Cites in its own grounds:** RAO 16-8 ("Neither the report of the committee nor the General Assembly's approval or disapproval of this report establishes doctrinal precedent"); RAO 16-10.c; OMSJC 15; BCO 40-5; BCO 13-11, 14-6.c, 40-1 (in the 16-3.e provision). No prior overtures, cases, or CCB rulings are cited by number.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **29-28 III.7 — New River Presbytery** (GA29/2001, advice given) — CCB lacks authority to give unsolicited advice to other committees, but the CRPR "may make any recommendation within its RAO 16 purview to the GA." Construes the scope of what RPR may bring/decide — the very allocation O7 shifts toward committee finality. [page](../inquiries/ga29_2001__ci07.md)
- **32-36 II.2** (GA32/2004, advice given) — A presbytery's BCO 21-4 exception-granting is reviewable by RPR, which "must report apparent violations." Bears on what RPR is obligated to surface vs. dispose of internally. [page](../inquiries/ga32_2004__ci02.md)
- **49 Supplemental II.B** (GA49/2022, not in conflict; adopted by CCB) — CCB found the RPR Committee's own proposed change to RAO 16-4c not in conflict with the Constitution. ⭐ Directly bears: O7 also amends 16-4.c.(3). [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci28.md)
- **51 II.W / O28** (GA51/2024, not in conflict) — CCB found the amendment to RAO 16-6.c.1 eliminating conflict with BCO 40-5 not itself in conflict; touches the same 40-5/RPR escalation interface O7 leaves intact. ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci23.md)
- **52 II.W / O34** (GA52/2025, in conflict) — CCB found an overture amending RAO 16 (session/diaconate statistical reporting) in conflict with the BCO; nearest recent precedent for CCB review of an RAO 16 reporting-scope amendment (O7 is referred to CCB). ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga52_2025__ci23.md)
