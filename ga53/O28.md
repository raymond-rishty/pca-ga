# O28 — Add BCO 41-7 to Provide a Right to Complain when a Presbytery Acts on a Reference
**Targets:** BCO 41-7 (new) · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-28_Westminster_41-7.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-28_Westminster_41-7.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds a new BCO 41-7 providing that when a Session makes a reference *with final disposition* to Presbytery and the Presbytery acts on it, the members of the congregation retain the right to complain against any action of the Presbytery related to the reference, as if the Session itself had taken the action. The grounds argue that referring (rather than deciding) a matter currently strips members of the BCO 43 right to complain against the lower court's action.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None (the grounds argue from general principles of BCO 43 complaint rights and due process; no specific prior case, overture, or inquiry is cited).

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CI #10, GA14/1986** (advice given) — Held that the Assembly's refusal to accept a judicial *reference* applies to the entire judicial process in PCA courts, ensuring no party is left without recourse. Directly bears: O28 likewise targets the recourse available to members when a reference (rather than a direct decision) governs a matter. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci13.md)
- **CI 2 (12-53 III), GA12/1984** (advice given) — When a judicial case is *transferred/referred*, the receiving court becomes the court of original jurisdiction and exercises the lower court's duties. Bears: O28's mechanism treats the Presbytery's action on a reference "as if the Session itself had taken the action," the same court-of-original-jurisdiction logic. [page](../inquiries/ga12_1984__ci03.md)
- **CI 20-75, GA20/1992** (advice given) — A presbyter may complain against a court's decision because that reception/decision is itself a court action under BCO 43-1. Bears: confirms the BCO 43-1 principle O28 seeks to preserve — that members may complain against any *action* of a court. [page](../inquiries/ga20_1992__ci04.md)
- **CI 29-28 III.6 (Reference 6), GA29/2001** (advice given) — A complainant may not go to a higher court until the lower court has acted on the complaint (BCO 43-2, 43-3). Bears: clarifies the complaint pathway O28 would preserve where a reference otherwise removes a layer of lower-court action. [page](../inquiries/ga29_2001__ci06.md)
- **CI Non-Judicial Reference 1, GA48/2021** (answered/adopted) — CCB opinions on procedure, anonymity, and standing for BCO 40-5 reports of sessional delinquency, addressing standing to complain (BCO 43-1) about Session-level acts. Bears on the standing-to-complain dimension of O28. [page](../inquiries/ga48_2021__ci29.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA29 O16** (2002, Approved & ratified) — Amend BCO 43-1 to Allow Complaint After Appeal Dismissed or Abandoned. Closest prior analogue: like O28, it closed a *gap* in the right to complain created by a particular procedural posture. [page](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p208)
- **GA41 O15** (2014, Approved & ratified) — Amend BCO 43-10 to Require Higher Court to Accept Reference in Doctrinal Cases. Bears: directly addresses how a higher court handles a *reference*, the same procedural mechanism O28 concerns. [page](../markdown/ga41_2013.md#ga41-p847)
- **GA44 O40** (2016, Answered in the negative) — Amend BCO 43-1 to Clarify Timing of Higher Court Complaint Review. Bears: a prior attempt to refine the right-to-complain framework O28 extends. [page](../markdown/ga44_2016.md#ga44-p641)
- **GA45 O15** (2017, Referred) — Amend BCO 43-1 Restricting Complaints During Judicial Process. Bears: companion debate over the scope of the BCO 43-1 right to complain against court actions. [page](../markdown/ga45_2017.md#ga45-p667)
- **GA49 O2** (2022) — Amend BCO 22-3 to Allow Assistant Pastors to File Complaints Against Sessions. Bears: a recent move to expand who/when may complain against a Session's action, the same accountability concern. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p1286)
- **GA51 O21** (2024, Approved & ratified 2025) — Amend BCO 43-1 to Clarify Prohibition on Complaints During Judicial Process. Most recent ratified change to the BCO 43-1 right-to-complain framework O28 builds upon. ⭐ [page](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p1165)
