# O82 — Amend BCO 12, 13, and 25 re Right of Congregation to Consent to those who Exercise Authority
**Targets:** BCO 12, 13, 25 (12-1, 13-9, 25-2) · **Source:** Rocky Mountain Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-82_RMP_1213.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-82_RMP_1213.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 13-9 so a Presbytery may assume original jurisdiction over a church "with the consent of the Session or the congregation" (and adds "with their consent" qualifiers throughout 13-9.f–g), and amends BCO 12-1 and 25-2 to create a constitutional mechanism by which a lone ruling elder (or the Presbytery, via new 13-9.i) may convene a congregational meeting of an elder-less/sessionless church to obtain its consent to appoint a temporary government under BCO 15-1 and 16-2. The stated aim is to bar a Presbytery from imposing a commission as an interim Session over a congregation without its consent.

**Cites in its own grounds:** Judicial Case #5, 13th GA (Preg v. Missouri Presbytery, 1985, on 13-9/11-4); SJC Case 90-4 (Chappell v. Eastern Carolina Presbytery, 19th GA, 1991); SJC Case 2001-8 (Lee v. Korean Capital Presbytery, 30th GA, 2002, citing BCO 25-11); SJC Case 2001-34 (Nichols & Couch v. James River Presbytery, 33rd GA, 2005). Also quotes Preliminary Principles 6 & 7, BCO 3-1, 3-4, 11-1/11-2, 16-2, 25-8/9/10/11, Morton Smith's Commentary, and Williamson & Cannada's Historic Polity of the PCA.

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[5 — TE S. Michael Preg, Jr., et al. v. Missouri Presbytery](../cases/ga13_1985__case1.md)** (13th GA/1985, sustained 16-0) — The Judicial Case #5 the overture quotes verbatim: the Commission held BCO 13-9 "contains no express provision … as would permit a presbytery to require" a visit "without a request by or a specific problem in the session or congregation," and warned against higher-court encroachment on lower courts. Directly construes the 13-9 power O82 narrows with "with their consent." [minutes](../markdown/ga13_1985.md#ga13-p128)
- **[1990-04 — Charles E. Chappell v. Eastern Carolina Presbytery](../cases/ga19_1991__1990-04.md)** (19th GA/1991, sustained) — The "SJC Case 90-4" the overture relies on: Presbytery determined a Session could not exercise its authority and assumed original jurisdiction by appointing a temporary Session over the church; SJC held the Presbytery erred, that presbyteries may not assume jurisdiction and act over a church without the congregation's consent. The precise scenario O82's 13-9 amendment codifies. [minutes](../markdown/ga19_1991.md#ga19-p488)
- **[2001-08 — TE Andy Lee v. Korean Capital Presbytery](../cases/ga30_2002__2001-08.md)** (30th GA/2002, sustained) — The "Case 2001-8" the overture quotes: SJC held a Presbytery erred in suspending a Session and appointing a commission to act for it, citing BCO 25-11 that congregational rights may never be taken away "without the express consent and affirmative action" of the congregation. Direct support for the consent requirement O82 writes into 13-9. [minutes](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p133)
- **[2001-34 — RE Leland Nichols & RE Sam Couch v. James River Presbytery](../cases/ga33_2005__2001-34_2002-02_2002-03.md)** (33rd GA/2005, sustained in part) — The "SJC 2001-34" the overture quotes for the "act on / act for / act against" framework: a higher court may not "act for" a lower court and may not force civil action against a congregation without a congregational vote. Frames the non-hierarchical principle O82 codifies. [minutes](../markdown/ga33_2005.md#ga33-p72)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **CCB advice, App. O II.E (CI ga39_2011)** (39th GA/2011) — On the very gap O82 fixes: the CCB reviewed a proposed overture on temporary governance for elder-less churches and warned it may conflict with Preliminary Principles 2 & 6 and BCO 12-1 if read as prescriptive. Most direct prior CCB treatment of the 12-1 temporary-government mechanism. [page](../inquiries/ga39_2011__ci01.md)
- **CI #15 (CCB ga14_1986)** (14th GA/1986) — Advised that a congregation cannot dissolve its Session, but the Presbytery may assume jurisdiction under BCO 13-9; construes the same 13-9 original-jurisdiction power O82 conditions on consent. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci18.md)
- **13-45 III, 65 (CCB ga13_1985)** (13th GA/1985) — Advised that BCO 13-9 grants Presbytery the prerogative to initiate regular visitation of its churches; the same-year counterpart to Judicial Case #5, and the very "visit … for the purpose of inquiring into and redressing the evils" clause O82 amends to add "with their consent." [page](../inquiries/ga13_1985__ci05.md)
- **II.F (CCB ga49_2022)** (49th GA/2022) — CCB found no conflict in amending BCO 25-2 to add a threshold for calling congregational meetings; bears on the 25-2 congregational-meeting machinery O82 also amends. [page](../inquiries/ga49_2022__ci06.md)
- **II.H (CCB ga51_2024)** (51st GA/2024) — CCB found a recent overture to amend BCO 13-9 (adding presbytery TE-care duties) not in conflict; recent CCB treatment of amending the same section. [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci08.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA39 O10** (2011, Approved but not ratified) — "Amend BCO 12 to Provide Temporary Governance for Churches Without Ruling Elders." The closest prior attempt at the same change O82 makes; passed the floor but failed ratification, leaving the gap O82 now targets. [page](../markdown/ga39_2011.md#ga39-p78)
- **GA27 O10** (1999, Adopted/final) — "Amend BCO to Authorize Interim Sessions." Created the interim-Session mechanism (BCO 15-1) that O82 presupposes and builds a consent-procedure around. [page](../markdown/ga27_1999.md#ga27-p50)
- **GA35 O10** (2007, Approved & ratified 2008) — "Amend BCO 12-1 and 12-2 to Revise Session Quorum Requirements." Amended the same 12-1 quorum sentence O82 edits; shows the section's drafting history. [page](../markdown/ga35_2007.md#ga35-p160)
- **GA49 O11** (2022, Answered in the negative) — "Amend BCO 25-2.e and Add BCO 25-2.f Regarding Threshold for Large Church Congregational Meetings." ⭐ A recent attempt to amend the same 25-2 congregational-meeting provision. [page](../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p1299)
- **GA51 O10** (2024, Answered in the negative) — "Add Presbytery TE Care to BCO 13-9." ⭐ The most recent attempt to amend BCO 13-9; bears on the appetite for and drafting of 13-9 changes. [page](../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p1270)
