# O20 — Add to BCO 5 a Section "C. Churches Without a Governing Body"
**Targets:** BCO 5 (new Section C, paragraphs 5-11 to 5-14) · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-20_Westminster_5C.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-20_Westminster_5C.pdf)

**What it does:** Adds a new Section C to BCO 5 for a particular church that, due to extraordinary circumstances, cannot provide government (BCO 12-1) from its own membership: the Presbytery may appoint a transitory Session of elders from within its bounds (5-11), which must seek the congregation's concurrence and move toward electing elders under BCO 24 or affiliation under BCO 2-2 / 25-11 (5-12); a particular church may be changed to a mission church only by a 3/4 Presbytery vote with members cited and given opportunity to concur or petition, and the reasons recorded (5-13); and each member retains the right to complain under BCO 43 (5-14).

**Cites in its own grounds:** No prior cases, overtures, or inquiries cited by name. Cites only constitutional text — Preliminary Principle 6, BCO 12-1, BCO 24, BCO 13-9, BCO 2-2, BCO 25-11, BCO 43 — and observes the issue "has arisen through the process of review and control."

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[2016-14 — Wills v. Metro Atlanta Presbytery](../cases/ga45_2017__2016-14.md)** (GA45/2017, denied) — Construes BCO 13-9(f): Presbytery has inherent authority to dissolve a church without a mandated procedure. The overture would supply exactly such a procedure for the cognate act of changing a particular church to mission status (3/4 vote, citation of members, recorded reasons, right to complain). [minutes](../markdown/ga45_2017.md#ga45-p543)
- **[1991-2 — Nickoley and Stephens v. Southwest Presbytery](../cases/ga20_1992__1991-02.md)** (GA20/1992, not sustained) — Held the Presbytery correctly determined it lacked constitutional authority to suspend a session or act as a "Session pro tem." Bears directly on the overture's novel "transitory Session" device, which would for the first time expressly authorize a Presbytery-appointed session. [minutes](../markdown/ga20_1992.md#ga20-p142)
- **[2022-7 — Harrell et al. v. Covenant Presbytery](../cases/ga50_2023__2022-07.md)** (GA50/2023, sustained) — Members of a mission church were censured by a provisional Session; SJC reversed, addressing provisional-Session authority and congregational rights (BCO 5). Bears on the protections the overture builds in for members under a Presbytery-appointed/transitory Session. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga50_2023.md#ga50-p852)
- **[2024-5 — Bigelow v. North Florida Presbytery](../cases/ga52_2025__2024-05.md)** (GA52/2025, granted) — Mission-church governance dispute involving a provisional session, dissolution of a call, and congregational rights under BCO 5/12-5; SJC found Presbytery erred. Illustrates the governance-gap friction (provisional/transitory session, member rights) the overture targets. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p981)
- **[1990-5 — Melton et al. v. Tennessee Valley Presbytery](../cases/ga19_1991__1990-05.md)** (GA19/1991, denied) — Members challenged a congregational vote dissolving their church and transferring property; SJC upheld the vote (BCO 24-4, 13-9, 11-4, 43). Bears on the member-protection question the overture answers: who may vote and complain when a church's status or existence is changed. [minutes](../markdown/ga19_1991.md#ga19-p530)

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **App. O II.E (GA39/2011)** — "Temporary governance for churches lacking ruling elders": CCB found a proposed overture on temporary governance for elder-less churches *may have conflicted* with the Constitution if read as prescriptive under Preliminary Principles 2 and 6 and BCO 12-1. Closest prior action; this overture revives that project and must navigate the same conflict caution. [page](../inquiries/ga39_2011__ci01.md)
- **12-53 III, 62 (GA12/1984)** — "Minimum ruling elders required to constitute a valid session": one RE plus a pastor is a valid session; a church without a pastor needs at least two REs (BCO 12-1). Defines the very "no governing body" threshold the overture's 5-11 presupposes. [page](../inquiries/ga12_1984__ci08.md)
- **CI #15 (GA14/1986)** — "Whether a congregation may dissolve the session": a congregation cannot dissolve its session, but Presbytery may assume jurisdiction under BCO 13-9. Bears on the overture's allocation of authority between congregation and Presbytery when a church loses its governing body. [page](../inquiries/ga14_1986__ci18.md)
- **16-77 III (GA16/1988)** — "Membership status and session authority upon dissolution of a congregation": on dissolution the session terminates and membership is dissolved for those not transferred; Presbytery may not unilaterally reassign members. Bears on the member rights the overture's 5-13/5-14 seek to protect on a change of status. [page](../inquiries/ga16_1988__ci08.md)
- **App. O II.G (GA44/2016)** — On a mission-church government overture, CCB found conflict with BCO 5-3.a (an evangelist has no minutes to submit). Shows CCB's exacting scrutiny of BCO 5 mission-church amendments; relevant to how 5-C will be reviewed. [page](../inquiries/ga44_2016__ci02.md)

### Prior overtures
- **GA41 O2 (2013, Referred)** — North Texas, "Amend BCO 5-1, 5-2, 5-9 and Add Sections 5-11 and 5-12 Regarding Mission Churches." A prior attempt to add new 5-11/5-12 to BCO 5; this overture occupies the same numbering space (5-11 to 5-14). [page](../markdown/ga41_2013.md#ga41-p816)
- **GA42 O2 (2014, Out of order)** — North Texas, renewed "Amend BCO 5-1, 5-2, 5-9 and Add Sections 5-11, 5-12 Regarding Mission Churches"; ruled out of order. Same provision real estate; cautionary precedent. [page](../markdown/ga42_2014.md#ga42-p253)
- **GA30 O25 (2002)** — Ascension, "Revise BCO 12-2, Add 12-11, Revise 25-6 & 15-1 Regarding Non-Functioning Sessions." Earlier attempt to solve the very "non-functioning session" problem — via Chapter 12 — that this overture now handles in Chapter 5. [page](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p76)
- **GA38 O15 (2010, Approved & ratified 2011)** — Potomac, "Revise BCO 5-2 Through 5-11 and Add New BCO 5-5 Regarding Church Organization." The restructure that set Chapter 5's current shape and the 5-x numbering; the framework into which 5-C must fit. [page](../markdown/ga38_2010.md#ga38-p368)
- **GA12 O33 (1984, Approved but not ratified)** — Covenant, "Amend BCO 11-4 to Clarify Presbytery Authority to Dissolve Sessions." A prior attempt to fix the same gap (presbytery power over a church's session) that failed ratification. [page](../markdown/ga12_1984.md#ga12-p56)
- **GA24 O28 (1996, Answered in the negative)** — Potomac, "Amend BCO 13-9f to Clarify Dissolving Churches." A rejected attempt to define how a church is dissolved or changed in status. [page](../markdown/ga24_1996.md#ga24-p54)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 13-9 / 5-3 (interim/temporary session)** — Recurrent friction where a presbytery improvises governance for a church without a session — the exact problem the overture addresses: **Potomac, 39th (2011)** — "appointed a commission to act as the interim session" (BCO 3-1, 13-9, 16-1, 25-8ff); **Eastern Canada, 40th (2012)** — "may not assign a temporary session without the concurrence of the church"; **Illiana, 22nd (1994)** — "no notation of congregational approval of a commission replacing the session"; **Korean Southwest, 52nd (2025)** — "received organized churches without sessions and without providing rationale." The overture's concurrence requirement (5-12) and recorded-reasons requirement (5-13) respond directly to these. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)
- **BCO 12-1** — **Grace, 51st (2024)** — Presbytery "approved REs to 'augment' church sessions apart from constitutional election"; shows presbyteries straining BCO 12-1 where a church cannot constitute its own session. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)
