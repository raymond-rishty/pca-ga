# O30 — Amend BCO 46-6 to Require Recording of Reception of One Dismissed to Another Presbytery
**Targets:** BCO 46-6 · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-30_Westminster_46-6.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-30_Westminster_46-6.pdf)
**What it does:** Adds a sentence to BCO 46-6 requiring a Presbytery to record in its minutes the date of reception of a minister, licentiate, or candidate who was dismissed to another Presbytery. The grounds reason that, because the man remains under the dismissing Presbytery's jurisdiction until actually received by the other, the court holding original jurisdiction must be clearly known and recorded.
**Cites in its own grounds:** None. The grounds rest only on the text of BCO 46-6 itself (jurisdiction continues until received by the other); no SJC case, prior overture, or CCB ruling is cited by number.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **17-82 III** (GA17/1989, advice given) — a letter from a presbytery committee chairman is insufficient evidence of dismissal; BCO 46-6 requires a certificate bearing the stated clerk's signature (per BCO 10-4). - **36-30 II.C** (GA36/2008, in conflict) — CCB found a Southeast Alabama overture adding membership-transfer procedures (touching 38-3a/46-5/46-6/57-6) in conflict over profession-of-faith language; shows a prior attempt to amend the 46-x transfer cluster and the CCB's cautions, on a different issue than recording. [page](../inquiries/ga36_2008__ci02.md)
- **App. O II.T** (GA40/2012, in conflict) — CCB again found proposed Southeast Alabama membership overtures (touching 46-6 among others) in conflict over the required profession for membership; parallel CCB review of the same provision, tangential to recording. [page](../inquiries/ga40_2012__ci03.md)

### RPR exceptions
- **BCO 46-6** — 50 citations, overwhelmingly the exact defect O30 fixes: "No record reflecting when a man who was transferred to another presbytery was received by that presbytery" and "No record regarding the reception of a certificate of dismission from transferring presbytery." Examples: Korean Southwest Orange County (50th/2023, raised), Tennessee Valley (50th/2023, raised), Missouri (51st/2024, outstanding), Chicago Metro / James River / Siouxlands / Chesapeake / Central Georgia / Northern California / Rio Grande (52nd/2025, many raised/open). Strong, current, real-world friction directly motivating O30. [by provision](../index/RPR-BY-PROVISION.md)

### Note
The RPR record is the dominant bearing evidence: 50 BCO 46-6 exceptions across the 49th–52nd Assemblies (2022–2025), many still unsatisfactory/open, document the precise problem — Presbyteries failing to record reception of a dismissed man — that O30 is written to cure, making this one of the most RPR-grounded overtures in this batch. The four judicial cases and the two CCB-advice items are high-recall spine hits that cite 46-6 but turn on jurisdiction/discipline or profession-of-faith questions rather than the recording requirement; they are retained as construing the same provision but flagged tangential. Inquiry 17-82 III is the closest non-RPR analogue (certificate/recording mechanics). Prior overtures GA36 O3 and GA40 O33 are genuine prior attempts to amend chapter 46 within the broader membership-transfer cluster. No GA48–52 prior overture targets this provision, so no star applies. No renumbering caveat: BCO chapter 46 is not in bco_renumberings.jsonl. Cross-link: the parallel provision BCO 46-8 (reception/jurisdiction) carries its own large RPR record; v1's references to cases 1990-8 and overtures GA6 O4 / GA24 O21 were not in the deterministic spine and have been pruned.
