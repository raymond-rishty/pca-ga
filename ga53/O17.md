# O17 — Amend BCO 5-2 Regarding the Dissolution of Mission Churches
**Targets:** BCO 5-2 (5-2.c) · **Source:** Westminster Presbytery · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-17_Westminster_5-2.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-17_Westminster_5-2.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends BCO 5-2.c so that a Presbytery dissolving a mission church must follow the procedures of **both** BCO 13-10 (incl. its 60-day notice) and BCO 25-10 (prohibition on seizing congregational property against its will), adding express cross-references to those member/congregation protections that 5-2.c currently omits.

**Cites in its own grounds:** None. (Grounds invoke BCO 13-10 and BCO 25-10 as the protective provisions, but cite no prior cases, overtures, or inquiries by number.)

### Judicial cases (SJC/CJB)
- **[Judicial Case 92-1 — pastoral care of members of dissolved Grace Church, Braintree](../markdown/ga21_1993.md#ga21-p196)** (GA21/1993, out_of_order) — Construes the very question 5-2.c addresses: a Presbytery's dissolution of a congregation and its duty to care for the enrolled members afterward (held Presbytery "does have the responsibility of providing for pastoral care by establishing a mission church or by providing pastoral care in some other way"). Directly bears on the overture's concern that members be cared for per BCO 13-10. [minutes](../markdown/ga21_1993.md#ga21-p196)
- **[Bigelow v. North Florida Presbytery (2024-5)](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p983)** (GA52/2025, granted) — Leading recent decision on BCO 5-2 mission churches: the rights of mission-church members and how a pastoral relationship is dissolved within a mission work. SJC distinguishes the (one-judge) *Harrell* concurrence and holds that BCO 5-5 assent does not give mission-church members the same congregational-vote protection as a particular church when a call is dissolved. Frames precisely the member-protection gap O17 seeks to close in 5-2.c. ⭐ [minutes](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p983)
