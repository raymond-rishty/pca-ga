# O85 — Add BCO 44 on Objections
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Refer back *(vote 102-28-0)*
**Assembly (2026) voted:** Refer back to the court of origin
**Targets:** BCO 44 (new chapter, refilling the "Vacated" slot); consequential edits to BCO 45-1, 45-4, 45-5 · **Source:** Northwest Georgia · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-85_NWGA_44.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-85_NWGA_44.pdf)

**What it does:** Moves the "objection" remedy — the right of a court member who lost the right to vote on an appeal/complaint under BCO 39-2 to have a recorded objection — out of BCO 45 ("Dissents, and Protests, and Objections") into a new, dedicated BCO Chapter 44 (currently "Vacated"), striking the objection language from BCO 45-1/45-4 and renumbering 45-5. Stated purpose: objections have a fundamentally different eligibility requirement than dissents/protests, and encouraging them offers a lighter-weight alternative to carrying a complaint/appeal upward, reducing the judicial load on the courts.

**Cites in its own grounds:** BCO 39-2 (loss of vote in appeal/complaint cases); BCO 45; Preliminary Principles 1 and 5; the SJC by way of example. No specific case/overture/inquiry numbers cited.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **GA50 II.H (ci08)** (GA50/2023) — CCB found that amending BCO 15-4, 45-1, and 45-4 to let GA commissioners register *objections* to SJC decisions is "not in conflict" with the BCO (Adopted 8-0-0). The most recent constitutional treatment of the very BCO 45-1/45-4 objection language O85 relocates (same matter as GA50 O11 below). ⭐ [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci08.md)
- **20-75 (ci04)** (GA20/1992) — A presbyter may complain against a court's decision to *receive* a protest alleged to be intemperate/disrespectful, since that reception is itself a court action. Construes the BCO 45-5 "temperate language … shall be recorded" clause that O85 carries verbatim into new BCO 44-3. [page](../inquiries/ga20_1992__ci04.md)
- **12-53 II (ci06)** (GA12/1984) — Alleged GA errors may be challenged under BCO 45, and later assemblies cannot annul prior actions. Bears on the function of the BCO 45 dissent/protest/objection remedy O85 reorganizes. [page](../inquiries/ga12_1984__ci06.md)
