# O78 — Amend RAO 4-21 to Authorize Electronic Meetings for GA Committees and Commissions
**Overtures Committee (2026) recommends:** Answer in the affirmative
**Targets:** RAO 4-21 (new), existing 4-21 renumbered → 4-22, conform cross-ref in RAO 14-11.a · **Source:** Southeast Alabama Presbytery (SEAL) · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-78_SEAL_RAO4-21.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Overture-78_SEAL_RAO4-21.pdf)

**What it does:** Inserts a new RAO 4-21 authorizing GA committees and commissions established by the BCO and RAO — except the Overtures Committee and Committees of Commissioners — to meet electronically (RONR 12th ed. 9:30-36) if they so provide in their own rules, and expressly authorizes ad hoc/ad interim committees to meet electronically at the call of the Chairman/Convenor; the existing 4-21 (Guidelines for Keeping Minutes) is renumbered 4-22. Purpose: regularize a practice many committees already follow by custom but which RONR 9:35 requires be expressly authorized by the appointing assembly.

**Cites in its own grounds:** No prior overtures, SJC cases, or CCB rulings by id. Its rationale cites BCO 12-6 (Sessions may meet electronically) and BCO 13-4 (Presbyteries may meet electronically) as "recently amended," OMSJC 4.1 (SJC may meet by telephone conference), and RONR (12th ed.) 9:30-36, 9:35, 2:8, 2:25.

### Constitutional inquiries / CCB advice
- **17-82 III, 8** (GA17/1989, advice given) — CCB construed BCO 13-4 (the Presbytery analogue O78 invokes) and ruled **telephone conference meetings are impermissible** absent authorization; the historical baseline the recent BCO 12-6/13-4 amendments reversed and that O78 now extends to GA committees — directly bears on the requirement that electronic meetings be expressly authorized rather than left to custom. [page](../inquiries/ga17_1989__ci02.md)
- **27-43 II.2** (GA27/1999, advice given) — CCB advised a presbytery may receive a candidate via "interactive electronic means" to satisfy BCO 18-3's appearance requirement; an early recognition of electronic participation's legitimacy in church courts, supporting the overture's claim that the Church's framework already accepts electronic meetings. [page](../inquiries/ga27_1999__ci02.md)
- **II.D** (GA50/2023, not in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0) — CCB found Southern New England's overture amending RAO 4-21.d (Committees of Commissioners reviewing compliance) not in conflict; bears as a prior amendment to the same RAO 4-21 paragraph O78 renumbers to 4-22. [page](../inquiries/ga50_2023__ci04.md)
- **II.I** (GA51/2024, not in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0) — CCB found the overture amending RAO 4-21.d (enrollment-data reporting from higher-ed institutions) not in conflict; another recent amendment to the same paragraph being renumbered. [page](../inquiries/ga51_2024__ci09.md)
