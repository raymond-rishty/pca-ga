# O33 — Amend RAO 16-7.h Regarding Minority Reports from RPR
**Targets:** RAO 16-7.h · **Source:** Westminster · **PDF:** [https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-33_Westminster_RAO16-7.pdf](https://pcaga.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Overture-33_Westminster_RAO16-7.pdf)

**What it does:** Amends RAO 16-7.h so that only members of the Committee on Review of Presbytery Records who voted in the minority on the Presbytery report as a whole may indicate an intention to file, or sign, an RPR minority report (currently "any member" may give notice of intent; at least six members must file). Aligns RPR practice with RONR 51:64–70, under which only those who did not concur with the majority may sign a minority report.

**Cites in its own grounds:** Robert's Rules of Order (RONR 12th ed., 51:64–70) — no PCA cases, overtures, or inquiries cited by number.

### Note
This is an RAO (not BCO) provision, so the provision-exact join returned no cases/inquiries/overtures/RPR rows; all bearing actions above were found by topical search (minority-report mechanics + the RPR/RAO 16 chapter). Pruned: every RPR-BY-PROVISION "RAO 16-7" string match was actually RAO **16-3** records-review content (executive-session minutes, recording of exceptions, etc.) — none concern RAO 16-7.h minority reports — so all such RPR rows are incidental and excluded. Strongest analogues are GA48 O9 (adopted) and O10 (failed), which regulate the same mechanic (member count + minority status), and GA50 O22 (failed) on constraining when minority reports may be filed. Renumbering caveat: the CRPR was historically RAO ch.14 (older actions cite RAO 14-3.e.5 / 14-6.c alongside the modern RAO 16-x), but the renumbering data has no 14→16 chapter-shift entry and none of the ch.14 actions touch the minority-report subsection. The mixed track record — GA adopted a numeric-threshold change (GA48 O9) but declined a "when permitted" restriction (GA50 O22) even after CCB cleared it — is the key signal for O33.
