## Response found satisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.532](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p532)*

**2024-01: Mar 14, 2023** ( _BCO_ 19-2.f; _RAO_ 16-3.e.5) —
Presbytery approved a candidate for licensure whom they
judged held a view that was hostile to the system or striking
at the vitals.

**Response:** We appreciate the RPR's diligence and
recognize Presbytery's action was irregular, though we
believed it to be appropriate in this case. We would offer the
following rationale as to why we made this decision: 1) The
candidate was already serving as a pastoral intern at one of
our churches and was seeking to fulfill all of the formal
internship requirements of _BCO_ 19-7ff, which includes the
testing of his preaching and teaching gifts. In order for the
intern to do this adequately, we believed licensure would be
necessary. 2) At our Presbytery meeting in March, members
of the Session where the candidate was serving as a pastoral
intern made clear that they agreed with Presbytery with
respect to the assessment of the candidate's particular view on
remarriage, and that they would be working with the candidate
to encourage him to reconsider his view in light of Scripture
and the standards and that they would ensure he not teach or
preach this view to the congregation. 3) The candidate seemed
humble and teachable and so we believed giving some
additional time to reconsider the view while working closely
with the Session of the church where he was serving and
fulfilling his internship requirements would be the kindest and
most reasonable approach. 4) At the end of the examination
on the floor of Presbytery, it was made clear to the candidate
by the chair of the Leadership Development Committee that
unless the candidate's view changed to be in accord with our
system of doctrine, he would not be able to be ordained in our
Presbytery. 5) As noted in the minutes, we also instructed the

candidate that he "not teach, preach, or counsel his exception
to the standards."
**Rationale:** _BCO_ 19-2.f is explicit that “The court
may grant an exception to any difference of doctrine ONLY
if in the court’s judgment the applicant’s declared difference
is not out of accord with any fundamental of our system of
doctrine because the difference is neither hostile to the system
nor strikes at the vitals of religion.” (emphasis added) This
language does not give Presbytery the authority to grant an
exception that is hostile to the system of doctrine or strikes at
the vitals, even if Presbytery believes there are special
circumstances.
