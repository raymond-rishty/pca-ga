## Response found satisfactory — 47th General Assembly (2019)
*[ga47_2019 p.463](../../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p463)*

**Exception: Jan 17, 2017** ( _BCO_ 21-4; _RAO_ 16.3.e.5) – Three
examined candidates gave identical, verbatim stated differences.
Our rules require that candidates express their stated differences “in
their own words.”
**Response:** Gulfstream acknowledges this mistake. The minutes of
January 17, 2017 have been amended to include the following

statements:

Item 9.b. TE [name omitted], [church name omitted], transfer exam
from Presbytery of Central Florida [PCA]

- When asked about changes in previous view of _Confession_,

_Catechisms_, or _Book_ _of Church Order_, TE [name omitted]
replied,
`o` [In reference to _WCF,_ Ch. XXI, Paragraph 8, & WLC

117, & WSC 60] “I do not believe that Scripture
excludes all forms of recreation on the Sabbath Day.
Recreation, especially when understood in the context
of the fellowship of God’s people, and not taken to
extremes, can be a means to enjoy both rest and
fellowship. (Gen. 2:2-3; Ex. 16:30; 31:15-17; Mark
2:27). I understand that this exception is more than
semantic, but not out of accord with any fundamental
of our system of doctrines
`o` [In reference to WLC 109] “Regarding sins forbidden

in the second commandment and specifically the
‘making of any representation of God, o fall or of any
of the three persons, either inwardly in our mind, or
outwardly in any kind of image or likeness of any
creature whatsoever;’: I believe that images of Christ,
either in one’s mind or used for pedagogical purposes,
do not in all cases violate the second commandment.
Scripture itself gives narratives of Jesus. It is difficult,
if not impossible, to avoid forming mental pictures of
Jesus regarding his humanity while reading those
narratives. I understand that this exception is more than
semantic, but not out of accord with any fundamental
of our system of doctrines.”

Item 9.c. TE [name omitted] [church name omitted], transfer exam
from Presbytery of Central Florida [PCA]

- When asked about changes in previous view of _Confession_,

_Catechisms_, or _Book_ _of Church Order_, TE [name omitted]
replied:
`o` [In reference to WCF, Ch. XXI. Paragraph 8 (likewise

WLC 117 and 119 and WSC 60) **] “** I do not agree that
the necessary implication of Scripture teaches the
unlawfulness of all forms of recreation on the Sabbath.

The Sabbath was initiated by God and also instituted
by him (Gen 2:2) primarily for the purpose of rest from
labor (Ex 20:9-11, 31:15-17). To the extent that such
recreations are indeed restful and moreover not sinful

in themselves, I find their exercise fully permissible on
the Sabbath. I believe this exception to be more than
semantic, but not out of accord with any fundamentals
of our system of doctrines.”
`o` [In reference to WLC 109] “The prohibition against

‘making any representation of God … inwardly in our
mind’ seems to me to be not only unreasonable but also
contrary to the pattern of Scripture, which itself
describes visible manifestations of God (Luke 3:22 and
Rev 1:13-16, for example). I believe the second
commandment would prohibit the making of any
mental images of our _own_ design apart from Scripture,
however we do not sin in creating mental representations
of that which Scripture describes to us, so long as such
images are not objects of our worship. I believe this
exception to be more than semantic, but not out of
accord with any fundamentals of our system of
doctrines.”

Item 9.d. Rev. [name omitted], [church name omitted] transfer exam
from Evangelical Presbyterian Church [EPC]

- When asked about differences with the _Confession_, _Catechisms_,

or _Book_ _of Church Order_, Rev. [name omitted] replied,
`o` [In reference to _WCF_ 21.8, WLC 117, WSC 60] **“** I do

not believe the Word of God demands we lay down all
“works, words, and thoughts” on the Lord’s Day.
These can be given to the Lord and done unto His
glory. I also believe that recreation can be done unto
the glory of God on the Sabbath and can in fact produce

rest that leads to the worship of God and
wholeheartedness.
`o` [In reference to WLC 109] “I believe the second

commandment prohibits us from creating mental
images of our own making. However, when Scripture
leads us to form mental images directly from the
descriptions of scripture, we do not sin or fall into
error. I believe this exception to be more than
semantic, but not out of accord with any fundamentals
of our system of doctrines.”
Said minutes were amended by action of Gulfstream Presbytery on
April 30, 2019.
Please note further that Gulfstream, during its January 17, 2017
Stated Meeting, gave full consideration of the stated differences,
with further questioning and amplification. In each instance,
Gulfstream Presbytery judged the stated difference to be “to be more
than semantic, but ‘not out of accord with any fundamental of our
system of doctrine’” [ _BCO_ 21-4, _RAO_ 16-3.e.5)c)]
Copies of the amended minutes are available for inspection
upon request.
