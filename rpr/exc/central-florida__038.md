*No record of whether Presbytery considers out of bounds labors to be a valid…*

## Raised — 34th General Assembly (2006)
*[ga34_2006 p.243](../../markdown/ga34_2006.md#ga34-p243)*

**Exception: General:** No record of whether Presbytery considers out
of bounds labors to be a valid Christian ministry. _BCO_ 20-1

## Response found satisfactory — 35th General Assembly (2007)
*[ga35_2007 p.172](../../markdown/ga35_2007.md#ga35-p172)*

**Exception: General:** No record of whether Presbytery considers out
of bounds labors to be a valid Christian ministry. _BCO_ 20-1

**Response:** We will make sure our record includes the “reasons why it
considers the work to be a valid Christian ministry.” Central Florida

Presbytery does not ordain men to out of bounds work if it has not
been examined and determined by both Examining Committee and
floor discussion to be a valid Christian ministry per the requirements
of _BCO_ 20-1. We join the Committee in its concern wish to assure
our bothers of diligence in this matter.
