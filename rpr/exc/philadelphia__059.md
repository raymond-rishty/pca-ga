# Philadelphia Presbytery — BCO 13-9.b

*Presbytery did not fulfill its constitutional duty to review session records…*

**Presbytery:** Philadelphia  ·  **First raised:** 51st (2024)  ·  **Final disposition:** unsatisfactory (outstanding)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 13-9.b

**Lifecycle:** unsatisfactory (51st) → unsatisfactory (52nd)

---

## Response found unsatisfactory — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.622](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p622)*

**2023-3: Jan 15, 2022** ( _BCO_ 13-9.b; 40-1) — Presbytery did not fulfill
its constitutional duty to review session records when it declined to
review 2019 and 2020 minutes.

**Response:** The Philadelphia Presbytery has been reviewing past
Session Records, and will approve a new process at the January 2024
meeting that will ensure Session Records are reviewed in an expedited

manner.

**Rationale:** No evidence the Presbytery has been reviewing these
minutes.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.678](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p678)*

**2023-03: Jan 15, 2022** ( _BCO_ 13-9.b; 40-1) — Presbytery did
not fulfill its constitutional duty to review session records
when it declined to review 2019 and 2020 minutes.

**Response:** The Philadelphia Presbytery has been
reviewing past Session Records, and will approve a new
process at the January 2024 meeting that will ensure Session
Records are reviewed in an expedited manner.

**Rationale:** No evidence the Presbytery has been
reviewing these minutes.

**Response:** That the Philadelphia Presbytery direct
the Stated Clerk to amend the minutes of the January 15, 2023
as previously adopted to include the following: The
Philadelphia Presbytery agrees that we have not yet read and
approved the 2019 and 2020 minutes, but our inaction does
not mean that we are refusing to approve them. We have been
overwhelmed with several time-consuming disciplinary cases
over the past year that has occupied our time and manpower.
The Presbytery is committed to processing these minutes as
directed by RPR. The Presbytery thus directs the Stated Clerk
to make a marginal notation at the point of error in the original
minutes, directing readers to where they may find the
correction. Also, at the March 15, 2025 Stated Meeting the
following motion was passed: Whereas the PCA General
Assembly has cited the Philadelphia Presbytery for failing to
review its Sessions’ Record for the years 2018 and 2020,
2021, and 2023 Whereas the Book of Church Order ( _BCO_ )
40-1 requires the Presbytery to review the records of every
session subject to its jurisdiction yearly, Whereas the
Philadelphia undertook a review of the records of its sessions
for the years 2019 and 2023 and that these reviews were made
by two persons for each session and considered matters of
form and substance, The Coordinating Team proposes that it
create a committee of elders to review the session records for

the years 2019, 2020, 2021 and 2022 in the following manner:
Each Session’s records for these years would be reviewed by
only one reviewer. The reviewers would focus on identifying
possible instances of gross non-compliance with our
constitutional standards, not on exceptions of form or minor
exceptions of substance which may have been reported in the
2023 review of Session records. The committee is asked to
issue its report to the Presbytery by October 31, 2025. The
committee would consist of TE[s] [Names Omitted]’s name
was added. M/s/a by unanimous vote.

**Rationale:** Committee recognizes the challenges
each Presbytery faces but timely and thorough review of the
records of church Sessions are required, essential and an

important distinction of the Presbyterian Church in America.
Presbytery must also record in their minutes that they
reviewed Session Minutes for years 2019 and 2020.
