## Raised — 28th General Assembly (2000)

Exception: January 19, 1999 (16.h): Theological paper approved in which candidate stated “I believe that God gives extraordinary gifts (miracles, healing, and tongues)”. WCF 18-3; WLC #80; BCO 7-1.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.352](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p352)*

**Exception:** January 19, 1999 (16.h): Theological paper approved in which candidate stated “I believe that God gives extraordinary gifts (miracles, healing, and tongues)”. WCF 18-3; WLC #80; BCO 7-1.

**Response:** Presbytery respectfully disagrees with the exception. The candidate was thoroughly examined by our Credentials Committee and on the floor of Presbytery. Both the Committee and Presbytery concluded that the views of the candidate were in complete conformity with the Constitution of the PCA. Presbytery is puzzled by the citations that were listed as justification for the exception: WCF 18 is of Assurance of Grace and Salvation and section 3 elaborates on assurance. WLC #80 asks, “Can true believers be infallibly assured that they are in the estate of grace, and that they shall persevere therein unto salvation.” BCO 7-1 ties extraordinary gifts to new revelation. No where in his Theological paper did the candidate make any mention of new or continuing revelation or challenge or question the PCA position on assurance of salvation. The exception should read in the full context in its section of “Views on Charismatic Movement.”

**Rationale with regard to the second and third exceptions:** While Presbytery is correct with regard to the second sentence of each exception (“no extraordinary clause stated as rational”), the response to

the first sentence of each (“BCO 13-6 requires thorough exam of transfers in all areas”) is not acceptable. When a man is received from outside the PCA BCO 13-6 requires more elements to the examination than are listed for these men. BCO 13-6 specifies that for men from other denominations all areas of examinations specified in BCO 21-4 were (and are) required.
