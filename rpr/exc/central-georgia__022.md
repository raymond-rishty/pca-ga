# Central Georgia Presbytery — exception of substance

*No record that members of the provisional Session were called by the…*

**Presbytery:** Central Georgia  ·  **First raised:** 51st (2024)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)

**Lifecycle:** raised (51st) → satisfactory (52nd)

---

## Raised — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.483](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p483)*

**2024-5: Jan 28, 2023** (PP 6) — No record that members of the
provisional Session were called by the congregation.

## Response found satisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.528](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p528)*

**2024-05: 28 Jan, 2023** (PP 6) — No record that members of
the provisional Session were called by the congregation.

**Response:** Presbytery confirms that the provisional
(temporary) session was not called by the congregation. The
church had particularized on October 4, 2020, with a session
comprised of a pastor and two Ruling Elders. As of the
January 28, 2023 Presbytery meeting, both of the Ruling
Elders had resigned and left the church. Since there were no
Ruling Elders, Presbytery’s judgment was that the church
reverted back to mission status. Therefore, per _BCO_ 5-3,
Presbytery appointed a temporary session.
Presbytery accepts RPR’s judgment and promises to
be more careful in the future. For corrective action, the
Presbytery will advise the church membership regarding a
congregational meeting and proceed accordingly.
