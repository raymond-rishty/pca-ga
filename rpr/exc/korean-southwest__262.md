## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.604](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p604)*

**2024-02: General 2023** ( _BCO_ 8-4) — Presbytery apparently
approves calls for men to work outside the bounds of
Presbytery, but does not record in the minutes why these are
needful works and valid Christian ministries, nor do the
minutes demonstrate that there is assurance the man will have

full freedom in preaching and teaching ( _BCO_ 8-4; 8-7; 20-1)
For each man received into Presbytery in 2023 on the basis of
a call to a work outside the jurisdiction of Presbytery,
Presbytery must now approve and include in its minutes a

statement of why the work is a needful work and a valid
Christian ministry, and how Presbytery has been assured the
man will have full freedom to preach and teach the doctrine
of our Church.

**Response:** TE [Name Omitted] was called as an
assistant pastor for family ministries at [Church Name
Omitted]. [Church Name Omitted], a swell as TE [Name
Omitted], has assured the presbytery that TE [Name Omitted]
will have full freedom to preach and teach the doctrine of our
Church. On that basis, presbytery has determined that the
families that TE [Name Omitted] will be serving will benefit
from his pastoral care and teaching, and for that reason,
presbytery judges this to be a needful work.
Regarding a “coaching ministry” call, Presbytery
deemed it a “needful work” because it dealt with the

coaching/counseling of other pastors to be better equipped as
pastors.
