# Warrior Presbytery — WCF 1-1, BCO 13-9f)

*Presbytery as a body has not taken action on the views of a TE regarding his…*

**Presbytery:** Warrior  ·  **First raised:** 25th (1997)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)  ·  **Provisions:** WCF 1-1, BCO 13-9f)

**Lifecycle:** raised (25th) → satisfactory (26th)

## Raised — 25th General Assembly (1997)
*[ga25_1997 p.220](../../markdown/ga25_1997.md#ga25-p220)*

Exception: April 16, 1996; July 16, 1996; October 15, 1996: Presbytery as a body has not taken action on the views of a TE regarding his belief in continuing revelation and in the continuation of all gifts of the Spirit (1 Corinthians 14, Romans 14, Ephesians 4) with the exception of apostleship (WCF 1-1 and BCO 13-9f). We recognize the extenuating circumstances which necessitated postponement at the July meeting.

## Response found satisfactory — 26th General Assembly (1998)
*[ga26_1998 p.254](../../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p254)*

Exception: April 16, 1996; July 16, 1996; October 15, 1996: Presbytery as a body has not taken action on the views of a TE regarding his belief in continuing revelation and in the continuation of all gifts of the Spirit (1 Corinthians 14, Romans 14, Ephesians 4) with the exception of apostleship (WCF 1-1 and BCO 13-9f). We recognize the extenuating circumstances which necessitated postponement at the July meeting.
Response: The Court respectfully disagrees with this finding.
Reason: According to BCO 13-9 a, it is the prerogative of the Presbytery to make such judgments as are in view in the exception stated above. Included within said prerogative would naturally be the determination of when such judgments should be made. Warrior Presbytery hears a report from its Membership Committee at each stated meeting regarding the Teaching Elder in question. He and the Committee are in frequent communication as he seeks to come to his position on the issues involved. He has honored his ordination vows by making known to his Presbytery the change in his views which are still in a state of transition. Presbytery continues to pray for and work with him in the hope that he will return with even greater conviction to the doctrines he subscribed to in his ordination. Presbytery feels that it is acting appropriately in this situation and believes that Presbyterian Polity in general and the BCO in particular leaves such determinations exclusively to the Presbytery.

Adopted
