## Raised — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.680](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p680)*

**2024-3: May 09, 2023** ( _BCO_ 20-1) — No record of the reason why
presbytery considers an out of bounds work to be a valid Christian
ministry.

## Response found satisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.769](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p769)*

**2024-03: May 9, 2023** ( _BCO_ 20-1) — No record of the reason
why presbytery considers an out of bounds work to be a valid
Christian ministry.

**Response:** The Presbytery agrees with the Exception
of Substance. The Minutes should have noted that the out of

bounds TEs are engaged in valid ministries because the
ministries allow full freedom to preach and teach the Word.
The Presbytery has noted in its record that the TEs are
engaged in ministries that are considered to be valid. The
Presbytery promises to be more careful in the future to state
in the minutes that it considers the out of bounds work to be a

valid Christian ministry.
