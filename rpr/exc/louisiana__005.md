# Louisiana Presbytery — exception of substance

*Expiration of license to preach. Then licentiate did not apply for renewal.…*

**Presbytery:** Louisiana  ·  **First raised:** 25th (1997)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)

**Lifecycle:** raised (25th) → unsatisfactory (27th) → satisfactory (28th)

---

## Raised — 25th General Assembly (1997)
*[ga25_1997 p.210](../../markdown/ga25_1997.md#ga25-p210)*

Exception: October 19, 1996: Expiration of license to preach. Then licentiate did not apply for renewal. Procedures for renewal not followed.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 27th General Assembly (1999)
*[ga27_1999 p.198](../../markdown/ga27_1999.md#ga27-p198)*

Exception: October 19, 1996: Expiration of license to preach. Then licentiate did not apply for renewal. Procedures for renewal not followed.

## Response found satisfactory — 28th General Assembly (2000)
*[ga28_2000 p.329](../../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p329)*

Exception: October 19, 1996: Expiration of license to preach. Then licentiate did not apply for renewal. Procedures for renewal not followed.

Response: Presbytery met only two times in Stated Meetings in 1996: in January and again in October. In the interval between the meetings TE Ron Davis of Bethel Presbyterian Church in Lake Charles died. Licentiate RE Ben Thompson, a member of Bethel Presbyterian Church whose license was still valid, was asked by the Session of Bethel Presbyterian Church to fill the pulpit. Before the Stated meeting in October RE Thompson’s license expired. When the Presbytery discovered the oversight in October 1996, the motion was made, citing BCO 19-2, to waive re-examination in this extraordinary case and to renew his license. RE Thompson had been preaching regularly under the supervision of the Bethel Session.
