# Heartland Presbytery — BCO 25-11

*Presbytery entered a judgmental statement against the church without and…*

**Presbytery:** Heartland  ·  **First raised:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 25-11

**Lifecycle:** raised (28th) → satisfactory (29th)

---

## Raised — 28th General Assembly (2000)
*[ga28_2000 p.325](../../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p325)*

Exception: August 20-21, 1999: Presbytery entered a judgmental statement against the church without and recording the matter of facts (see dissent 99-77 &amp; protest 99-109) BCO 25-11; 35-3.

## Response found satisfactory — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.329](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p329)*

Exception: August 20-21, 1999: Presbytery entered a judgmental statement against the church without and recording the matter of facts (see dissent 99-77 &amp; protest 99-109) *BCO* 25-11, 35-3.

Response: Heartland Presbytery apologizes for having “entered a judgmental statement against the church without recording the matter of facts.” In the future we will be more careful to document the facts of the case. The fuller detailing of the facts which should have been recorded are these: (1) Messiah Church was no longer a member of the PCA and therefore no formal process could be brought against it. (Shortly after Heartland Presbytery established a study committee to investigate full preterism, Messiah Church withdrew from the PCA under terms of *BCO* 25-11.) (2) The presbytery had already approved a motion that declared full preterism to be “heretical” and outside the catholic faith as defined in the ancient creeds. (See “Committee Paper Investigating Full Preterism,” adopted in action 99-71. Note action 99-70 was not concluded until after 99-71 had been voted upon and approved.) (3) Messiah Church had already publicly identified itself as being full preterist in many ways. This public identification was communicated to the presbytery by the witness of two teaching elders and documentation provided by a former ruling elder at Messiah Church. All three affirmed that Messiah Church had sponsored a full preterist conference, had announced it publicly, and one of the advertising bills was presented to presbytery. They also presented testimony that he (the TE) and Messiah Church were working to establish a full preterist denomination, for which he was writing the *BCO*. They (the witnesses) also affirmed before presbytery that the teaching elder of Messiah Church taught full preterism on radio in the name of the church. (4) Thus, by the standard of Deut. 19:15, we simply took the determination of 99-71 that full preterism was heretical and beyond the pale of the catholic creeds (a determination with which 28th General Assembly found no fault when it acted on our overture) and applied it to the self-declarations of Messiah Church. Thank you for bringing to our attention this oversight of failing to record the facts.
Adopted
