## Response found unsatisfactory — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.679](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p679)*

**2023-7: Aug 02, 2022** ( _BCO_ 23-1) — Presbytery did not act to
dissolve a TE’s call and place him on the rolls without call after his
church left the denomination and he left the field.

**Response:** The Clerk of SOLA Presbytery apologizes for failing
to include this action in our minutes. It was an oversight on the clerk’s
part. We will make every effort to include all actions of the Presbytery
in our minutes in the future.

**Rationale:** No record that Presbytery corrected its record.
Presbytery has not recorded the dissolution of the TE’s call.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.757](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p757)*

**2023-07: Aug 2, 2022** ( _BCO_ 23-1) — Presbytery did not act
to dissolve a TE’s call and place him on the rolls without call
after his church left the denomination and he left the field.

**Response:** The Clerk of SOLA Presbytery apologizes
for failing to include this action in our minutes. It was an
oversight on the clerk’s part. We will make every effort to
include all actions of the Presbytery in our minutes in the
future.

**Rationale:** No record that Presbytery corrected its
record. Presbytery has not recorded the dissolution of the TE’s
call.

**Response:** The Southern Louisiana Presbytery
acknowledges the exception of substance cited by the
Committee on Review of Presbytery Records (RPR) for its
August 2, 2022, stated meeting, noting that the presbytery
failed to act under _BCO_ 23-1 to dissolve the call of Teaching
Elder [Name Omitted] and place him on the rolls without call
after his congregation, [Church Name Omitted], withdrew
from the Presbyterian Church in America and he ceased
serving that field. We also recognize that previous responses
to this citation were deemed unsatisfactory by the General
Assembly, and we have reviewed the Clerk's Report to
understand the deficiencies identified.

To address this exception and correct past
shortcomings, the presbytery has taken the following steps:
1. Circumstances of the 2022 Omission: In June

2022, [Church Name Omitted] voted to leave the PCA, and
TE [Name Omitted] subsequently left his position as pastor of
that congregation. At the August 2, 2022, meeting, the
presbytery was informed of these events but failed to take
formal action to dissolve his call due to a lack of familiarity
with the process to handle these sorts of cases. The minutes of
that meeting do not reflect the required action under _BCO_ 231. We apologize for this failure.
2. Corrective Action: At our April 2025 stated
meeting, the presbytery will address this omission by voting
to dissolve TE [Name Omitted]'s call to [Church Name
Omitted], effective July 3, 2022. This action will be recorded
in the minutes at our April 2025 stated meeting

3. Verification of Status: TE [Name Omitted]
received a call to serve as the minister at [Church Name
Omitted], Louisiana and remains a Minister in Good Standing
in our presbytery. WE recognize the formal error of approving
this call without first dissolving TE [Name Omitted]'s call
with [Church Name Omitted] and we will not make the same
mistake again.
4. Procedural Safeguards: To prevent future lapses
under _BCO_ 23-1, the presbytery will make sure to
appropriately label TEs according to their status in their
church. In addition, we will revise our standing rules to clarify
which committee is responsible for overseeing the dissolution
of calls.

The SOLA Presbytery affirms its commitment to
upholding _BCO_ 23-1 and respectfully requests that the
General Assembly find this response satisfactory. We are
available to provide additional documentation or clarification
if requested.
