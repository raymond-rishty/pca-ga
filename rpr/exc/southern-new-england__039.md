# Southern New England Presbytery — BCO 38-3.a

*TE improperly permitted to withdraw from membership and removed from roll even…*

**Presbytery:** Southern New England  ·  **First raised:** 51st (2024)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 38-3.a

**Lifecycle:** raised (51st) → satisfactory (52nd)

---

## Raised — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.680](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p680)*

**2024-1: Sep 16, 2023** ( _BCO_ 38-3.a.) — TE improperly permitted to
withdraw from membership and removed from roll even though he
was not (1) affiliated with another branch of the church and (2) a
member in good standing.

## Response found satisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.768](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p768)*

**2024-01: Sep 16, 2023** ( _BCO_ 38-3.a.) — TE improperly
permitted to withdraw from membership and removed from
roll even though he was not (1) affiliated with another branch
of the church and (2) a member in good standing.
**Response:** Southern New England Presbytery does
respectfully disagree with the exception taken by General
Assembly to its September 2023 stated meeting minutes. The
matter taken exception to was Presbytery’s handling of the
removal of TE [Name Omitted]. The exception states that TE

[Name Omitted] “was not affiliated with another branch of the
church.” While the committee bringing the matter of TE

[Name Omitted] to the floor was not aware of any affiliation
TE [Name Omitted] had entered into, during the floor debate
it was discovered that, in fact, TE [Name Omitted] had
affiliated with another branch. We included the name of that

branch in the body of the minutes. Because he had affiliated
with another branch, Southern New England Presbytery does
not believe that it erred in passing the motion.
