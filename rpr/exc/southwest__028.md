## Response found satisfactory — 30th General Assembly (2002)
*[ga30_2002 p.336](../../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p336)*

Exception: June 30, 2000: The purpose of the called
meeting is not stated with the minutes. BCO 13-12.

Response: The purpose of this Called Meeting was
specified in the Call of May 17, 2000. A copy of which
is attached [with the minutes turned in this year]. There
were only two items of business to be conducted:
(1) Approve the Pastoral Call from Covenant
Presbyterian Church, Sun City West, AZ, to TE [name
deleted], (2) Conduct appropriate examination of
[name deleted] as the RUF Representative at the
University of Arizona and approve his moving to the
field.
As you will note from paragraphs 1 and 2 of
the June 30, 2000 minutes, these were the only two
items discussed and acted on. BCO 13-12 does not say
that the purpose of the Called Meeting has to be
explicitly restated in the minutes if it has been clearly
stated in the Call which in this case it was.
The moderator, RE [name deleted], explicitly
ruled that we would follow the order as the business
items were listed on the docket. This was done as
paragraphs 1 & 2 so indicate. It appears, therefore,
that the Southwest Presbytery fully complied with
BCO 13-12. However, we have retroactively changed
out June 30, 2000 minutes to specifically reflect the
two items of business mentioned in the May 17, 2000
Call. Adopted
