# Eastern Canada Presbytery — BCO 21-4.c

*Extraordinary clause “invoked” to waive theological and exegetical papers…*

**Presbytery:** Eastern Canada  ·  **First raised:** 51st (2024)  ·  **Final disposition:** unsatisfactory (outstanding)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 21-4.c

**Lifecycle:** raised (51st) → unsatisfactory (52nd)

## Raised — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.498](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p498)*

**2024-6: Jun 02, 2023** ( _BCO_ 21-4.c) — Extraordinary clause
“invoked” to waive theological and exegetical papers without a vote
by presbytery.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.543](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p543)*

**2024-06: Jun 2, 2023** ( _BCO_ 21-4.c) — Extraordinary clause
“invoked” to waive theological and exegetical papers without
a vote by presbytery.

**Response:** Presbytery respectfully disagrees with the
exception. The checklist for reception of a minister from
another denomination (041-A) does not reference theological
or exegetical papers. Therefore, we believe these papers are
not required based on the PCA Administrative Committee
guidelines and our precedent of receiving ordained ministers
from other denominations.

**Rationale:** _BCO_ 13-6; reception of ordained
ministers from other denominations requires all the trials for
ordination requirements of _BCO_ 21-4.c(2), and (3), except in
the limited use of the extraordinary clause ( _BCO_ 21-4.i.), by
3/4 majority vote of Presbytery. Presbytery has not recorded
appropriate supermajority action of Presbytery vote to waive
the theological and exegetical papers.
