## Response found satisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.766](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p766)*

**2024-03: Oct 28, 2023** ( _BCO_ 20-1) — No record of the
reasons why Presbytery considers an out-of-bounds work to
be a valid Christian ministry.
**Response:** The Southern Louisiana Presbytery
acknowledges the exception of substance cited by the
Committee on Review of Presbytery Records (RPR) for its
October 28, 2023, stated meeting (p. 34), noting that the
minutes lack a record of the reasons why the presbytery
considered Teaching Elder [Name Omitted]'s out-of-bounds
work a valid Christian ministry under _BCO_ 20-1.
To address this exception, the presbytery provides the
following:
1. Circumstances of the October 28, 2023, Action: At
the October 28, 2023, meeting, the presbytery approved a call
for TE [Name Omitted] to serve i nan out-of-bounds work as
Executive Director of Reformed Youth Ministries. The

presbytery evaluated the work and deemed it a valid Christian
ministry based on its alignment with the gospel priorities of
the PCA, its founding by PCA Teaching Elders and Members,
and its close collaboration with the PCA MNA Permanent

Committee. However, due to the assumption that approval
implied validity, these reasons were not explicitly recorded in
the minutes.

2. Corrective Action: The Commission of presbytery
amended the October 28, 2023, minutes to include the
rationale for approving TE [Name Omitted]'s call: "The
presbytery considers TE [Name Omitted]'s work at Reformed
Youth Ministries a valid Christian ministry because it
advances the same mission of the PCA and aligns with the
PCA's mission and vision." This amendment is recorded in

Minutes.

The SOLA Presbytery affirms its commitment to
properly approving and documenting calls under _BCO_ 20-1
and respectfully requests that the General Assembly find this
response satisfactory. We are prepared to provide additional
documentation, such as TE [Name Omitted]'s report or
organizational materials, if needed.
