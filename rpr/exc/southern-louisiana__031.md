## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.755](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p755)*

**2023-03: Jan 22, 2022** ( _BCO_ 19-2) — Incomplete record of
licensure exam: no statement of Christian experience and
inward call.

**Response:** The Clerk of SOLA Presbytery apologizes
for failing to include this information in the Minutes. This is
always the first part of the floor examination that we approve
and I simply left it out. We will make every effort to include
this information in the minutes in the future.

**Rationale:** No record that Presbytery corrected its
record. Presbytery has not recorded the receipt of a statement
of Christian experience and inward call from the licensee.

**Response:** The Southern Louisiana Presbytery
acknowledges the exception of substance cited by the
Committee on Review of Presbytery Records (RPR) for its
January 22, 2022, stated meeting, noting that the record of a
licensure examination is incomplete under _BCO_ 19-2 due to
the absence of statements regarding the candidate's Christian
experience and inward call. We also recognize that previous
responses to this citation were found unsatisfactory by the
General Assembly, and we have reviewed the Clerk's Report
to identify the deficiencies therein.
To address this exception and rectify past
shortcomings, the presbytery offers the following:
1. Details of the January 22, 2022, Examination:
Upon review, the presbytery confirmed that the Candidate
was examined for licensure at the January 22, 2022, meeting.
The examination included questions on his Christian
experience and inward call, as required by _BCO_ 19-2, and he
provided satisfactory responses. However, these components
were not explicitly recorded in the original minutes. To
correct this, the presbytery amended the January 22, 2022,
minutes at its meeting to include: "The presbytery examined
Candidate Charles Marchman on his Christian experience,
noting his testimony of conversion, and his inward call."
2. Addressing Prior Unsatisfactory Responses: We
understand from the Clerk's Report that prior responses were
deemed unsatisfactory due to lack of an amended record. The
presbytery has addressed this by amending the 2022 minutes
with specific details and establishing a clear process for future
licensure exams.

The SOLA Presbytery affirms its commitment to
conducting and documenting licensure exams in full
compliance with _BCO_ 19-2 and respectfully requests that the
General Assembly find this response satisfactory. We are
prepared to provide additional documentation or clarification
if needed.
