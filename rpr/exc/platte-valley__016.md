## Raised — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.688](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p688)*

**2025-01: Nov 4, 2023** ( _BCO_ 21-4.f; _BCO_ 21-4.g) —
Presbytery appears to have incorrectly ruled the following
stated difference as merely semantic. Candidate appears to
deny the spiritual presence of Christ in the Lord’s Supper,
holding only to a memorialist view of the sacrament, which is
hostile to our system of doctrine. In candidate’s own words:
“I do affirm that by the Spirit, Christ is present in and at the
observance of the Supper. I believe that the Spirit does
confirm and encourage the faith of those who receive the
elements by faith in following Jesus in a unique way. We are
to remember with love for Christ and sorrow for our sins his

body and blood which were sacrificed for us on the cross. But
I cannot say that we feed on his spiritual blood or spiritual
body in some literal fashion. The act, both physically and
spiritually, occurred once and for all time at Golgotha. We
rightly counter Roman Catholics who seem repeatedly to
crucify Christ when practicing transubstantiation that turns
the bread and wine into the corporeal body and blood to feed
upon. I am uncomfortable with the Reformed rite if it turns
the bread and wine into his spiritual body and spiritual blood
upon which we literally feed. The RCC bodily crucifies Christ
again and again through the Mass—or so it appears to many
Protestants, myself included. I don’t wish to appear to crucify
Christ spiritually again and again through a Lord’s Supper that
has recipients eating the spiritual body and spiritual blood.
When we “feed on him in our hearts by faith with
thanksgiving,” I interpret this to mean that we pause to
remember and celebrate that at the cross it was finished both

in body and in spirit for our salvation—hallelujah and amen.
We figuratively feed upon the accomplished work of Christ at
the cross as we apply again and again its saving graces to our
daily lives through the agency of the supper when
accompanied by faith through the Holy Spirit. Again, I may
be misunderstanding what the article is saying, in which case,

I welcome conversation, and this might not be an exception.
For more see the Larger Catechism 168-170 and the Shorter
Catechism 96.”.
