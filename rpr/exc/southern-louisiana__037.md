## Response found unsatisfactory — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.679](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p679)*

**2023-11: Oct 22, 2022** ( _BCO_ 21-9) — Presbytery installed a pastor
without having received or approved a call.

**Response:** The commission failed to note that the TE did receive
a call and it was placed in his hands. Please forgive this failure on our
part. We will make every effort to include this information going
forward.

**Rationale:** No record that Presbytery corrected its actions ( _RAO_
16-10.b.1). Presbytery has not approved the terms of call for the TE.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.759](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p759)*

**2023-11: Oct 22, 2022** ( _BCO_ 21-9) — Presbytery installed a
pastor without having received or approved a call.

**Response:** The commission failed to note that the TE

this failure on our part. We will make every effort to include

**Rationale:** No record that Presbytery corrected its

**Response:** The Southern Louisiana Presbytery
acknowledges the exception of substance cited by the
Committee on Review of Presbytery Records (RPR) for its
October 22, 2022, stated meeting, noting that the presbytery
installed Teaching Elder TE [Name Omitted] as pastor of

[Church Name Omitted] Church without having received or
approved a call, in violation of _BCO_ 21-9. We also recognize
that previous responses to this citation were deemed
unsatisfactory by the General Assembly, and we have
reviewed the Clerk's Report to address the deficiencies
identified.

To resolve this exception and correct past
shortcomings, the presbytery offers the following:
1. Circumstances: At the October 22, 2022, meeting,
it is noted that SOLA Presbytery approved the petition of

[Church Name Omitted] church to be particularized as a
congregation of the PCA. The Presbytery voted to receive

[Church Name Omitted] and a commission was appointed by
the Moderator for the threefold purpose of particularizing

[Church Name Omitted] Church, approving the call of TE

[Name Omitted] and examining the Ruling Elders to be
elected by the congregation. There was a discussion at that
time about the irregularity of approving the particularization
of [Church Name Omitted] without the pastoral call being
approved by Presbytery.
2. Corrective Action: At that time we understood that

since TE [Name Omitted] had already been approved to
organize the church plant and since he was already a member
in good standing of our presbytery that the irregularity of the
request could be rectified prior to the commission meeting on
December 18, 2022. Therefore, prior to the December 18,
2022 commission meeting, [Church Name Omitted] voted to
call TE [Name Omitted] as their pastor. At that meeting the
commission of SOLA Presbytery reviewed this call and
approved it as part of its actions on behalf of presbytery,
however, this action was not reflected in the minutes

presented to Presbytery. Therefore, we have corrected the
minutes to include the commission's approval of TE's Shaw's
call to [Church Name Omitted] Church.
3. Verification: The presbytery confirmed with

[Church Name Omitted] that the congregation indeed voted to
call TE [Name Omitted] on December 19, 2025, and TE

[Name Omitted] accepted the call. This supports the
retroactive approval and ensures compliance with _BCO_ 21-9.
4. Addressing Prior Unsatisfactory Responses: We
understand from the Clerk's Report that prior responses were
unsatisfactory due to "failure to document the call's approval"
and "lack of corrective action". The presbytery has addressed
this by retroactively approving the call, amending the
minutes, and establishing a preventive process.
The SOLA Presbytery affirms its commitment to
adhering to _BCO_ 21-9 and respectfully requests that the
General Assembly find this response satisfactory. We are
prepared to provide additional documentation or clarification
if needed.
