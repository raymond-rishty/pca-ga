## Raised — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.682](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p682)*

**2025-03: May 11, 2024** ( _BCO_ 21-4; _RAO_ 16-3.e.6) —
Presbytery incorrectly judged a candidate’s stated differences
as merely semantic.
First minute read: “I have a perceived difference with
the _WCF_ 4.1 (cf. also _WLC_ 15; _WSC_ 9) which states: “It
pleased God the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost, for the
manifestation of the glory of His eternal power, wisdom, and
goodness, in the beginning, to create or make of nothing the
world, and all things therein, whether visible or invisible, in
the space of six days, and all very good.” The Confession here
appears to teach that creation took place in the span of six,
literal 24 hour periods. I do not believe an orthodox view of
creation necessitates the “Calendar Day Interpretation” of
Genesis 1-2. That being said, I believe God called all things,
both invisible and visible, out of nothing (ex nihilo) into being
by his voice, as the Belgic Confession 12 states without
reference to the span of time: “We believe that the Father,
when it seemed good to him, created heaven and earth and all
other creatures from nothing, by the Word—that is to say, by
the Son.”

Second minute reads: “I have a perceived difference
with the _WCF_ 21.8 (cf. also _WLC_ 1117, 119; _WSC_ 60), which
states: “This Sabbath is to be kept holy unto the Lord when
men, after a due preparing of their hearts, and ordering of their
common affairs beforehand, do not only observe an holy rest
all the day from their own works, words, and thoughts about
their worldly employments and recreations; but also are taken
up the whole time in the public and private exercises of His
worship, and in the duties of necessity and mercy.” The
Confession here appears to teach that personal, private
activities for pleasure are not appropriate on the Sabbath. I
believe that New Covenant Christians keep the Sabbath holy
primarily by worshiping God weekly on the First Day (i.e.
Sunday). We rest from our work and ordinary labors in order
to facilitate our worship of God. Recreational activities that
do not hinder our ability to worship are still permissible. This
view is expressed in the Heidelberg Catechism 103: “Q. What
is God’s will for you in the fourth commandment? A. First,
that the gospel ministry and education for it be maintained,

and that, especially on the festive day of rest, I diligently
attend the assembly of God’s people to learn what God’s
Word teaches, to participate in the sacraments, to pray to God
publicly, and to bring Christian offerings for the poor.”” Both
statements depart from the Standards’ teaching on the relevant
topics, and thus are more than semantic.
