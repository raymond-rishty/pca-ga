# Gulfstream Presbytery — BCO 15-2, RAO 16-3.e.4

*Power of commission not constitutionally prescribed for Credentials Committee…*

**Presbytery:** Gulfstream  ·  **First raised:** 49th (2022)  ·  **Final disposition:** unsatisfactory (outstanding)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 15-2, RAO 16-3.e.4

**Lifecycle:** raised (49th) → unsatisfactory (50th)

---

## Raised — 49th General Assembly (2022)
*[ga49_2022 p.523](../../markdown/ga49_2022.md#ga49-p523)*

**Exception: Standing Rules 6-8; 6-5** ( _BCO_ 15-2, _RAO_ 16-3.e.4) –
Power of commission not constitutionally prescribed for Credentials
Committee to allow TEs to move onto field prior to presbytery
approval. Quorum of commission must be 2 more than half
membership AND at least 2 TEs and 2 REs. Criteria not met by rules
for committee and so cannot act with power of commission.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 50th General Assembly (2023)
*[ga50_2023 p.558](../../markdown/ga50_2023.md#ga50-p558)*

**Exception: Standing Rules 6-8; 6-5** ( _BCO_ 15-2, _RAO_ 16-3.e.4) – Power of
commission not constitutionally prescribed for Credentials Committee to
allow TEs to move onto field prior to presbytery approval. Quorum of
commission must be 2 more than half membership AND at least 2 TEs and 2
REs. Criteria not met by rules for committee and so cannot act with power of
commission.

**Response:** _BCO_ 15-2 notes the requirements of a commission “shall consist
of at least two teaching elders and two ruling elders, and the quorum shall be
one more than half its membership _unless otherwise determined by the_
_Presbytery._
By-law 6-5 is Gulfstream Presbytery of determining otherwise the quorum
and power of commission for its Credentials Committee
_BCO_ 15-2 also lists some matters that are among those which may be
properly executed, but it does not seem to restrict the power of commission
to those matters only.
