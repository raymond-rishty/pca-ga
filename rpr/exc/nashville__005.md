## Response found satisfactory — 28th General Assembly (2000)
*[ga28_2000 p.331](../../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p331)*

Exception: General: Standing rules on session records should comply
with BCO 40-1,2,3.
Response: nashville presbytery cannot detect the lack of compliance in
our Standing Rules with BCO 40-1,2,3, nor has this been pointed out
before. the sections o fours rs which appear to be germane to the
general exception, together wit hour adopted list of minutes rules, are
excerpted below:
3-1 Book of Church Order. Chapter 13 of the Book of Church Order, THE PRESBYTERY, shall govern Presbytery in all matters to
which this chapter speaks.
CHAPTER TEN: SESSIONAL RECORDS
10-1 RULES: Sessions shall keep their records according to rules
adopted by the Presbytery, securing from the stated clerk a copy of these
rules inserting the list of rules on the inside of the front cover of their
Sessional Records Book. a copy of the sheet listing the items by which
the annual check of sessional records is made, shall also be inserted in
the inside of the front cover of each sessional records Book. Sessions
are to inform themselves about the contents of both of these papers and to
seek to observe them in keeping Sessional Records Books,
10-2 EXAMINATION Sessional records shall be presented for
examination according to the schedule set by the Stated Clerk.
comments of approval or criticism concerning each set of records shall

be noted on the correction sheet for the help of those preparing the minutes.

**Guidelines for keeping Session Minutes consistent with the Book of Church Order and Rules of Assembly Operation.**

1. All Minutes should be typed or written legibly.
2. All Minutes are to be kept in one volume for permanent record.
3. The name of the church is to appear on the cover of the volume.
4. Pages are to be numbered consecutively.
5. No blank pages are to be left between records of meetings.
6. The minutes of the annual congregational meeting and all other called meetings are to be included in the book.
7. Every meeting is to be recorded as to date, place, time, name of those present, absent, and excused. The minutes are to show that the meeting was opened and closed with prayer and each meeting should be recorded so as to show an accurate record of the proceedings. **Only motions passed need to be recorded.**
8. Minutes should show the purpose of the meeting stated or called. If called, the exact call of the meeting must be stated, with evidence that only business was conducted.
9. The names of the elders and viewpoints expressed by them need not be recorded.
10. All meetings are to be attested by the clerk and the moderator and the minutes of each meeting are to be approved at that meeting or at subsequent meeting as read or corrected.
11. Joint meetings of the Session and Deacons are to be recorded.
12. Congregational meetings of the Session and Deacons are to be recorded.
13. The minutes should show that a quorum was present at any given meeting.
14. Records are to be examined as to whether the proceedings have been correctly recorded in the light of the Book of Church Order and the above guide of the Nashville Presbytery.
15. A copy of the NP Standing Rules and a copy of this sheet of guidelines should be included with the minutes.

Please show us how our rules fail to comply. The Nashville Presbytery is currently studying and changing its structure. This process will result in changes to the Standing Rules. We will take care to adopt rules that fully comply with the BCO.
