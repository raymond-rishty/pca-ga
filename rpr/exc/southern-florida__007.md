# Southern Florida Presbytery — BCO 13-6

*BCO 13-6 requires thorough exam of transfers in all areas. No extraordinary…*

**Presbytery:** Southern Florida  ·  **First raised:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 13-6

**Lifecycle:** raised (28th) → unsatisfactory (29th) → satisfactory (30th)

---

## Raised — 28th General Assembly (2000)
*[ga28_2000 p.341](../../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p341)*

Exception: July 20, 1999: BCO 13-6 requires thorough exam of transfers in all areas. No extraordinary clause stated as rationale.

## Response found unsatisfactory — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.353](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p353)*

Exception: July 20, 1999: BCO 13-6 requires thorough exam of transfers in all areas. No extraordinary clause stated as rationale.

Response: Presbytery respectfully disagrees with both exceptions. The men transferring were given a knowledge examination and were asked the questions put to candidates at their ordination which they answered in the affirmative (BCO 13-6). The examination followed the requirements of D-21, Check List For Reception of a Minister From Another Denomination (copy enclosed). This Check List is from the “Handbook For Presbytery Clerks – July 1999” prepared by the Office of the GA Stated Clerk. On page one of examiner David M. Smith’s worksheet in answer to Question he wrote, “Only partial – not complete” in answer to Question he wrote, Only partial – not complete.”

## Response found satisfactory — 30th General Assembly (2002)
*[ga30_2002 p.335](../../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p335)*

Exception: July 20, 1999: BCO 13-6 requires
thorough exam of transfers in all areas. No
extraordinary clause stated as rationale.
Response: Presbytery has discontinued using D-21,
Check List For Reception of a Minister From Another
Denomination, which is part of the “Handbook for

Presbytery Clerks.” Presbytery is now following BCO
13-6 in all such transfers.
