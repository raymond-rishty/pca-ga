**Lifecycle:** raised (29th) → satisfactory (30th)

## Raised — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.351](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p351)*

Exception: July 22, 2000: paragraph 31.10, concerning the ordination
examination of a man. BCO 21-4. there is no indication of completion
of internship. there is no indication of approval of educational
requirements and original languages. There was no sermon approved
and no documentation of his call. ashe was coming as a Licentiate,
there was no indication of his being asked if he changed his previous
views concerning the WCF, WSC, or the BCO. BCO 21-4.

## Response found satisfactory — 30th General Assembly (2002)
*[ga30_2002 p.334](../../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p334)*

Exception: July 22, 2000: paragraph 31.10,
concerning the ordination examination of a man. BCO
21-4. There is no indication of completion of
internship. There is no indication of approval of
educational requirements and original languages.
There was no sermon approved and no documentation
of his call. ashe was coming as a Licentiate, there
was no indication of his being asked if he changed his
previous views concerning the WCF, WSC, or the
BCO. BCO 21-4.

Response: Southeast Louisiana Presbytery agrees
with the exceptions, except for the preaching portion.
We will make every attempt to be more careful in
reporting all actions taken by Presbytery. All actions
cited in the exception were accomplished but not
recorded.

In reference to a sermon not being preached by one
who is licensed, the examination for licensure requires
a sermon to be preached and BCO 21-4 par. #2
specifically states that “If the Presbytery previously
approved all parts of the licensure examination, it need
not re-examine the intern in those areas at this time.”
