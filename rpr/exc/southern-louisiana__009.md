**Response:** My apologies for not including TE [name omitted]’s
differences in his own words in Southern Louisiana Presbytery’s
April 2018 minutes. Please find his written statements below
regarding his differences on the Sabbath and WLC #107.
Concerning the Sabbath:
“ _My view of the Sabbath or Lord’s day is that it is to be a day set_
_aside for rest and worship. We avoid normal weekly activities on the_
_Lord’s Day, but I do think the Confession and Catechisms are_
_unnecessarily restrictive regarding all types of recreation, which_
_would seem to include things like taking a walk with my family or_
_playing in the yard with my kids. This being said, I do embrace the_
_Biblical thrust of the Catechisms and Confession in that the Lord’s_
_day is to be set aside for worship and rest.”_
Concerning _WCF_ #107:
“ _My view concerning the second commandment is that I see no place_
_for images of God to be used in worship, but I believe the use of_
_images of Jesus for pedagogical purposes, such as ina children’s_
_storybook Bible or other education curriculum, does not violate the_
_second commandment.”_
