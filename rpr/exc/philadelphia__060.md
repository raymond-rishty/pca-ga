## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)

**2024-02: General 2023** ( _BCO_ 13.9.b; _BCO_ 40-1) — No
record of review of records of church sessions.
**Response:** From the March 15, 2025 Stated Meeting:

Whereas the PCA General Assembly has cited the
Philadelphia Presbytery for failing to review its Sessions’
Record for the years 2018 and 2020, 2021, and 2023 Whereas
the Book of Church Order ( _BCO_ ) 40-1 requires the Presbytery
to review the records of every session subject to its
jurisdiction yearly, Whereas the Philadelphia undertook a
review of the records of its sessions for the years 2019 and
2023 and that these reviews were made by two persons for
each session and considered matters of form and substance,
The Coordinating Team proposes that it create a committee of
elders to review the session records for the years 2019, 2020,
2021 and 2022 in the following manner: Each Session’s
records for these years would be reviewed by only one
reviewer. The reviewers would focus on identifying possible
instances of gross non-compliance with our constitutional
standards, not on exceptions of form or minor exceptions of
substance which may have been reported in the 2023 review
of Session records. The committee is asked to issue its report
to the Presbytery by October 31, 2025. The committee would
consist of TE[s] [Names Omitted]’s name was added. M/s/a
by unanimous vote.
**Rationale:** Each Presbytery is required under _BCO_
40-1 to review all Session minutes at least on a yearly basis.
The continued failure to fully review Session minutes for the
years 2019, 2020, 2021 and 2022 reflects a serious dereliction
of duty. The solution suggested by the Presbytery does not
appear to reflect the intent and spirit of the _BCO_ 40-2 which
requires a thorough examination of Session minutes and not
simply reviewing only for gross non compliance of
constitutional standards.
