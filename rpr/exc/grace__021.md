## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.550](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p550)*

**2024-05: Jan 10, 2023** ( _BCO_ 13-2; _BCO_ 34-10) — Process
of _BCO_ 13-2 and 34-10 not followed in cases of TEs without

call for three or more years.
**Response:** Presbytery disagrees with this exception.
In order to comply with _BCO_ 13-2 and 34-10, Presbytery
adopted the following Standing Rule: 2.8. The Stated Clerk
shall report at each Stated Meeting the names of any Teaching
Elders who have three consecutive un-excused absences from

Stated Meetings and any Teaching Elders who have been

without call to a particular work for 3 years (BCO13-2). 2.8.1
The Stated Clerk shall recommend the action, if any,
Presbytery should take. This Report was made for each of the
three (3) Stated Meetings in 2023 [p. 54, 95, & 135], noting
that all Teaching Elders, who are without call, regardless of
whether the three (3) year period has passed, are currently
filing our pulpits on a regular basis and meeting the needs of
our small congregations. Of our 39 churches, 17 do not have
full-time pastors. There is only 1 Teaching Elder who is past
the three (3) period, and he is filling one of these pulpits
almost every Lord’s Day. Accordingly, no recommendation
was made to proceed under _BCO_ 34-10 and there were no
motions from the floor to take any action.
**Rationale:** _BCO_ 13-2 states that _BCO_ 34-10 shall be

followed f orate retained o na presbytery’s rolls for more
than three years without call. Furthermore, _BCO_ 34-10 states
that the clerk shall deliver written notice to a TE without call

for more than three years that Presbytery will consider his
status at the next stated meeting. Neither a lack of
recommendation from the clerk nor the absence of a motion

from the floor constitutes compliance with _BCO_ 13-2 and 3410; Presbytery must correct its action by following _BCO_ 13-2
and 34-10.
