## Raised — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.695](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p695)*

**2025-29: Nov 12, 2024** ( _BCO_ 21-4.f) — Presbytery appears
to have incorrectly judged that a candidate’s stated difference
is not out of accord with any fundamental of our system of
doctrine because the difference is neither hostile to the system
nor strikes at the vitals of religion. “4) _WLC_ #177 (maybe
_WSC_ #97). I believe in paedocommunion (young child
communion). Our baptized children are ‘not only for the
solemn admission of the party baptized into the visible
Church, but also to be unto him a sign and seal of the covenant
of grace, of his ingrafting into Christ, of regeneration, of
remission of sins, and of his giving up unto God, through
Jesus Christ, to walk in newness of life’ ( _WCF_ 28.1). I believe
they should likewise be welcome at the Lord’s Table as part

of this family when they are welcomed to the kitchen table as
part of their families (i.e., not as suckling infants but as young
children). As described in the _WCF_ 31.4, ‘suspension from the
sacrament of the Lord’s Supper for a season’ is for those who
are being disciplined, and I do not believe it is biblical to treat,
our covenant children the same way as 89 ‘obstinate
offenders.’ ( _WCF_ 31.3) I agree with Dr. Robert S. Rayburn’s
position in the Minority Report to the Ad-Interim Committee
to Study the Question of Paedocommunion (16th GA).
However, and this is probably the most important aspect of
this, i will not nor have i ever practiced anything contrary
to that which is permitted in the Westminster Standards and
the _BCO_, as God’s Word and my ordination vows would
require me to submit in practice to the judgment of the church
in this matter.” ["I believe in paedocommunion."].
