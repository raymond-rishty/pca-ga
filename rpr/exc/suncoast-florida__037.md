**Response:Response:** The candidate coming for
ordination had been licensed at the previous Stated Meeting
of Presbytery where his stated differences were adjudged “as
more than semantic, but not striking at the vitals of the system
of doctrine as set forth in the Westminster Confession of Faith

and Catechisms.” A note was added to the February minutes

that the candidate’s stated differences had not changed since
he was licensed at the September 13, 2022 Stated Meeting.
1. _WSC_ 60 states that on the Sabbath day we are to
observe “a holy resting all that day, even from such worldly
employments and recreations as are lawful on other days; and
spending the whole time in the public and private exercises of
God’s worship.” While I largely agree with the Westminster
view of the Sabbath, I believe that lawful, restful recreational
activities are permissible on the Sabbath day.
2. _WLC_ 109, in discussing the Second
Commandment, describes this command as forbidding “the
making any representation of God, of all or of any of the three
persons, either inwardly in our mind, or outwardly in any kind
of image or likeness of any creature whatsoever.” I believe
that the formation of certain images of the three persons of the
Trinity in one’s mind is almost impossible to avoid,
particularly as Jesus took on flesh as a human person, and the
Holy Spirit is depicted variously as a dove, tongues of fire,
etc. I don’t find images such as these to be particularly
harmful in things like children’s books, granted that these
images are not to be worshiped nor are they to be perceived
as actual portrayals of the persons of the godhead.
