## Response found satisfactory — 26th General Assembly (1998)
*[ga26_1998 p.246](../../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p246)*

Exception: November 1-2, 1996: Candidate examined in theology
indicates a belief in continuing revelation. The minutes record no details
of his views.
Response: The sentence in 2410 which reads: “However, Candidate Dan
wile n sky h ada guarded reservation regarding the issue of continuing
revelation which was focused on heavily by the committee in their
meeting” represents an editorial comment which should not have been
included in the minutes of Presbytery’s actions.
The last sentence of paragraph 2410 makes this clear when it states
that the Candidates and Examinations Committee is satisfied that Mr
Wilensky’s views are compatible with the PCA standards. Furthermore,
Presbytery found Mr. Wilensky’s views acceptable as attested by the
action reported in 2410.3 wherein Mr. W ilensky’s examination was
sustained
The Presbytery deemed the candidate’s views not to represent an
exception to our standards The editorial comment raises a question where
there should have been none; however, we failed as a Presbytery to catch
this in our review and approval process.
The North Texas Presbytery begs the pardon of the Assembly for
the improper inclusion of this editorial comment in our minutes.
Adopted
