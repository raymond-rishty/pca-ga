**Response:** Regarding the dates cited by RPR, in the following
places, the minutes of Iowa Presbytery meetings indicate that the
commission reported to the presbytery on issues with which they
were dealing and actions they had taken.

- March 11, 2017 - p.26 (lines 14-23) show the report of Cono

Commission to the presbytery.

- May 6, 2017 - p.26 (beginning at line 32) and p.46 (lines 1-21)

and Appendix B of those minutes show the report of Cono
Commission to the presbytery.

- July 8, 2017 - p 52 (lines 19-26) record the report of that

commission to the presbytery.

- November 11, 2017 - p.72 (lines 25 and following) and p.73

(lines 1-18) show the final report of the commission to the
presbytery
Because you cited those dates, we point you to those portions of
minutes of those meetings.
But we suspect that your concern is not that of the commission
reporting regularly to the presbytery but the presbytery reviewing its
minutes. The final review of the minutes of the meetings of that
commission was done when the Session of that congregation (the
commission) submitted their final minutes. They were reviewed by
the Ad.Nom. Committee and the committee’s motion to approve
those minutes was approved by Iowa Presbytery at its meeting on
July 2, 2018.

We apologize for not recording such review of their records in
2017. Such review was planned to take place in the review of
Session records when the commission had completed its labors. This
was carried out by the Ad. Nom. Committee prior to and then
approved by Iowa Presbytery at is meeting on July 2, 2018.
