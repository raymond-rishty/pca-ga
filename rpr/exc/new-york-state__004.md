# New York State Presbytery — BCO 8-7

*No record of assurance of full freedom to maintain and teach the doctrine of…*

**Presbytery:** New York State  ·  **First raised:** 33rd (2005)  ·  **Final disposition:** unsatisfactory (outstanding)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 8-7

**Lifecycle:** raised (33rd) → unsatisfactory (34th)

---

## Raised — 33rd General Assembly (2005)
*[ga33_2005 p.295](../../markdown/ga33_2005.md#ga33-p295)*

**Exception** : **September 17-18, 2004:** No record of assurance of full
freedom to maintain and teach the doctrine of our church for a TE
whose call to an out of bounds work was approved by presbytery.
_BCO_ 8-7

## Response found unsatisfactory — 34th General Assembly (2006)
*[ga34_2006 p.278](../../markdown/ga34_2006.md#ga34-p278)*

**Exception** : **September 17-18, 2004:** No record of assurance of full
freedom to maintain and teach the doctrine of our church for a TE
whose call to an out of bounds work was approved by presbytery.
_BCO_ 8-7

**Response** : In this case it was so obvious to NYS Presbytery that the
minister in question would have full freedom that notation of this fact
was accidentally omitted from the minutes. This is a church that has
been in informal discussions with NYC Presbytery about joining the
PCA. Their previous minister was a PCA minister, and the one
before that OPC.
