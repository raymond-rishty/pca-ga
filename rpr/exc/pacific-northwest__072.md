## Response found satisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.664](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p664)*

**2024-03: May 19, 2023** ( _BCO_ 43.8) — Timely and orderly
complaint dismissed without a hearing.
**Response:** Respectfully, the Pacific Northwest
Presbytery does not agree that this is an exception of
substance. The complaint in question was ruled out of order
by the Session, and the Presbytery similarly ruled it out of
order. The matter was subsequently carried to the Standing
Judicial Commission (SJC). Presbytery does not believe this
constitutes “an apparent violation of Scripture, a serious
irregularity from the Constitution of the PCA, an action out of
accord with a deliverance of the GA, a matter of impropriety,
an important delinquency, or non-compliance with _RAO_ 163.e.5-6.

1. Timeliness and Order of Complaint:

RPR concluded that the complaint was “timely
and orderly” without reviewing the Record of the
Case. It is unclear how this determination could

have been made without access to the full record,
particularly when Presbytery ruled the complaint
out of order.

2. _BCO_ 43-8 Does Not Apply:

There is no constitutional requirement for a
higher court to hold a hearing i fit deems a
complaint out of order. Additionally, there is no

requirement to adopt a reason for ruling a
complaint out of order. Past SJC decisions have
demonstrated that complaints can be ruled out of
order without citing reasons. Even if the
complaint had been heard, _BCO_ 43-9 permits a
court to deny a complaint without providing
reasons. _BCO_ 43 does not require the higher court
to produce a decision equivalent to the standards
of the _SJC Manual_, §17.1.
Furthermore, _BCO_ 43-8 specifically applies only
after the higher court has determined that a
complaint is timely and in order for it to be heard.
Since the Presbytery deemed the complaint out of
order, _BCO_ 43-8 is not applicable in this case.
3. Judicial Matters Should Not Be Reviewed by
RPR:

According to _RAO_ 16-4.e.9, “Minutes of
presbytery dealing with judicial cases shall not be
dealt with by Committee on Review of
Presbytery Records when notice of appeal or
complaint has been given the lower court ( _BCO_
40-3).” This complaint was carried to the SJC as
Case 2023-11, and a decision was rendered by the
SJC on March 7, 2024. Members of the RPR who
serve on the SJC, as well as the Pacific Northwest
Presbytery representative, could have informed
the RPR of this fact in May 2024.
For these reasons, the Pacific Northwest Presbytery
maintains that the citation is unwarranted, and further
attention to this matter is unnecessary.
