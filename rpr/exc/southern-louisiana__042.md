# Southern Louisiana Presbytery — BCO 8-7

*No record of annual report(s) of TE(s) laboring out of…*

**Presbytery:** Southern Louisiana  ·  **First raised:** 51st (2024)  ·  **Final disposition:** unsatisfactory (outstanding)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 8-7

**Lifecycle:** unsatisfactory (51st) → unsatisfactory (52nd)

---

## Response found unsatisfactory — 51st General Assembly (2024)
*[ga51_2024 p.677](../../markdown/ga51_2024.md#ga51-p677)*

**2023-1: General 2022** ( _BCO_ 8-7) — No record of annual report(s) of
TE(s) laboring out of bounds.

**Response:** SOLA Presbytery apologizes for not including annual
reports from our TE’s laboring out of bounds in our Minutes. It is our

practice to hear reports from our ministers and churches at the end of
our meetings. At that designated time our ministers laboring out of
bounds give a report before presbytery. We will make an effort to make
note of the reports when they are delivered by our Ministers laboring
out of bounds going forward.

**Rationale:** No record that Presbytery corrected its record or
actions. Presbytery has not recorded its receipt of reports from out of
bounds ministers.

**Response:** The Southern Louisiana Presbytery
acknowledges the exception of substance cited by the
Committee on Review of Presbytery Records (RPR) for
General 2022, noting the absence of recorded annual reports
from Teaching Elders (TEs) laboring out of bounds, as
required under _BCO_ 8-7. We also recognize that previous
responses to similar citations were deemed unsatisfactory by
the General Assembly, and we have reviewed the Clerk's
Report to understand the rationale behind those findings.
To address this exception and rectify past
deficiencies, the presbytery has taken the following actions:
1. Identification of TEs Laboring Out of Bounds: We
compiled a comprehensive list of TEs currently laboring out
of bounds. These include: [Names Omitted].

2. Policy Implementation: To prevent future lapses,
the presbytery noted that our standing rules require all
ministers laboring out of bounds to present a report on their
work at our October stated meeting. The Stated Clerk has been
retasked with notifying TEs of this requirement annually and
ensuring the reports are presented for presbytery review.
3. Response to Prior Unsatisfactory Findings: We
understand from the Clerk's Report that prior responses lacked
specific detail regarding our actions to correct this deficiency.
The presbytery regrets these oversights and has addressed
them by ensuring that subsequent years include detailed
records of each TE's report and the presbytery's approval or
follow-up actions.
The SOLA Presbytery commits to ongoing
compliance with _BCO_ 8-7 and requests that the General
Assembly find this response satisfactory. We are available to
provide additional documentation or clarification if needed.
