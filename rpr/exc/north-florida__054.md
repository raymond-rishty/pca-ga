## Response found satisfactory — 47th General Assembly (2019)
*[ga47_2019 p.493](../../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p493)*

**Exception: Oct 13, 2016** ( _BCO_ 21-4) – No record of stated
differences or how the Presbytery judged them.
**Response:** Presbytery agrees with this exception and pledges to
properly record candidate’s differences and Presbytery’s judgment
in the future. The candidate’s differences in his own words, which
were approved as more than semantic but not out of accord with any
fundamental of the system of doctrine, will be recorded in the
minutes and were as follows:

**WCF 21-8:** This section, along with WSC 60 and
WLC 117, forbids acts of recreation on the Sabbath
day. i take exception to this statement because i believe
it is contrary to what the Bible teaches. Jesus says that
the Sabbath was made for man, not man for the
Sabbath. Ex. 23:12 states that the Sabbath is both for
rest and refreshment – spiritually and physically.
Finally, the WLC 121 helps us understand that one way
to practice “remembrance” on the Sabbath is through
thankful remembrance of creation. Thus, since the
Sabbath was made for humans, in part for their physical
refreshment, and for thankful remembrance of creation,
I believe non-organized recreation, such as hiking or
biking, is a practical way to observe the Sabbath. This
is because recreation can be very refreshing for most
humans and is a way to enjoy God’s creation.
**WLC 109** : Since Christ is the “image of the invisible
God” (Col 1:15), and the “exact imprint of his nature”
(Heb 1:3), one cannot help but picture Christ inwardly
when reading the Gospels or taking the Lord’s supper.
Thus, I take exception to the statement that we cannot
make any representations of God inwardly, as the
Confession states. Furthermore, while I affirm that we
cannot make any representations of God for the
purpose of worship, I believe we can use images of the
humanity of Christ for educational purposes – but not
for worship.

**WLC 151** : Though on one level all sins are equally
worthy of God’s wrath and require Christ’s atoning
blood, I affirm WLC 151 when it says that some sins
bring us more guilt and do us more damage. Thus, I
affirm most of WLC 151, such as when it says sins
against a weaker brother are greater (Rom 14:13);
however, there is an aspect of WLC that I disagree
with. I take exception to the assertion that the day in
which sin is committed (i.e., the Lord’s Day) makes it

worse.

**WLC 191:** While I affirm that Christians should submit

themselves to governing authorities (Rom 13:1), I do
not believe this implies that the authority of the church
is under the state. I therefore take exception to the idea
that the church is “countenanced and maintained by the
civil magistrate”; I believe that Christ alone is the one
who supports and maintains the church (Eph. 1:22).
