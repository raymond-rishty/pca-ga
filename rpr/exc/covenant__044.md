## Response found unsatisfactory — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.538](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p538)*

**2024-01: Feb 7, 2023** ( _BCO_ 38-3) — No record that upon an
attempt of a PCA TE to transfer to the PC(USA) that
Presbytery judged whether or not the PC(USA) is failing to
maintain the Word and Sacraments in their fundamental

integrity.
**Response:** Covenant Presbytery respectfully
disagrees that it erred in this citation. _BCO_ 38-3.a and _BCO_
38.3.b are taken in concert with one another. _BCO_ 38.3.a says,
in part,”if at the time of the attempt to withdraw he is in good
standing, the irregularity shall be recorded, and his new
membership acknowledged.” _BCO_ 38.3.b says in part, “when
a member or minister of the Presbyterian Church in America
shall attempt to withdraw from the communion of this branch
of the visible Church by affiliating with a body judged by the
court of original jurisdiction as failing to maintain the Word
and Sacraments in their fundamental integrity…”. The
acknowledgement of the irregularity (noted in the minutes as
“dismissed to” [note not “transferred”]” and his new

membership recorded in the minutes fulfills the requirement
of _BCO_ 38-3.a. Committee members may disagree with the
conclusion o fa pres byte r yon a certain matter, but that is not
a reason fora citation of substance.

**Rationale:** A determination must be made whether

the new ecclesiastical body is a branch of the visible church,
permitting irregular removal ( _BCO_ 38-3.a.), or whether the
new ecclesiastical body is a body failing to maintain the Word
and Sacraments in their fundamental integrity in order to warn
the TE of the danger of such attempted affiliation ( _BCO_ 383.b.).
