## Response found satisfactory — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.343](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p343)*

Exception: Man licensed who “holds exception in his view of the sabbath
(he does not believe that the Sabbath Day and Lord’s Day are the same),
the law (he is not absolutely sure that the Decalogue is the New Testament
Ethic), and on who may administer the Sacraments (he cannot see that the
administration of the sacraments is the sole prerogative of teaching elders;
he believes that ruling elders and others who have dem onstrated
knowledge, understanding, and maturity concerning the sacraments should
be allowed to administer them .)” (WCF2X-1, 27-4, 19-2).

Response: New River respectfully resubmits the same response to the
ongoing exception in the case of licentiate terry Roush, as follows:
“Terry Roush has rescinded his exception concerning the Decalogue as
the new testament ethic and expressed a view consistent with the
Reformed Faith. The Presbytery finds his other two exceptions (in the
area of only teaching elders being authorized to administer the
Sacraments and his view that the Sabbath Day and the Lord’s Day are
not the same) as allowable and therefore his license remains in effect.”
in response to the report of the cr pratt he 28th GA, the
Presbytery would note the following items.
as to reason a (“To teach these things at all is to disturb the
peace and purity of the church.”) the presbytery respectfully calls the
attention of the cr pr to the minutes of the 14th General Assembly,
Section 14-52, report of the committee of commissioners on judicial
Business, especially to the final paragraph concerning allowing
exceptions, as follows:
"This allowance of exceptions, however, does not warrant his
teaching or preaching of that matters oas to disturb the peace and
purity of the church. the court of jurisdiction must determine in each
situation whether such unwarranted actions have occurred. ”
(Emphasis added).
New River Presbytery continues to believe we alone have the
right to determine whether the teaching of these exceptions (if such
should occur) should be limited by the Presbytery. We believe that the
cr pr has gone beyond the bounds of their authority in recommending
an exception that would deny us this right without judicial process.
as to reason b (The courts of the church have declined to define the
“fundamentals of the system of doctrine” and they have never limited
them to the results of past judicial decisions.), the Presbytery respectfully

calls the attention of the cr pr to the minutes of the 10th General Assembly, page 103, Section 10-75, report of the committee of
Commissioners on Judicial Business, item 25 (Answer to Q.2 and Q.3,
quoted in part as follows:
"Also, as result of proper judicial processes, judgment may be
made which determinately interpret what may or may not be in accord
with our standards. any other procedure of set tine forth or compiling
a list of essential....doctrines would, in effect, am end the standards by
an unconstitutional method. ” (Emphasis added.)
New River Presbytery continues to believe the ONLY way to
determine that any doctrine is to be viewed throughout the General Assembly as a “fundamental of the system” and therefore requires all
Sessions and Presbyteries to deny license or ordination is through
‘proper judicial process.’ Until such time as these two doctrines have
been upheld by the standing judicial committee of the General Assembly as such ‘fundamentals of the system ’ and Session or
Presbytery is free to grant as they see fit. Again, we believe that the
cr pr has gone beyond the bounds of their authority in recommending
an exception that would declare these two (or any other doctrines) as
“fundamentals of the system” without judicial process.
as to reason c (GA has determined that only te ’s can administer
the Sacraments), New River Presbytery never has allowed anyone but a
TE to administer the sacraments and will not in the future.
Since this is the third year in a row that we have submitted this
same response, New River Presbytery hereby forwards this response
through the Committee on Constitutional Business in accordance with
RAO Section 7-2 as a non-judicial reference, asking that their non
binding advice be forwarded to the cr prof the 29th General Assembly.

Rationale with regard to the second exception: No response received.
