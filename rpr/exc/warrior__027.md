## Response found satisfactory — 32nd General Assembly (2004)
*[ga32_2004 p.244](../../markdown/ga32_2004.md#ga32-p244)*

**Exception: August 27, 2002:** Candidate for ordination not examined
in original languages. _BCO_ 21-4.a.2 and RAO 14-3.e.7. Also, no record
of ordinand’s signing Ministerial \Obligation book. _BCO_ 13-7.

## Response found satisfactory — 34th General Assembly (2006)
*[ga34_2006 p.302](../../markdown/ga34_2006.md#ga34-p302)*

**Exception: August 27, 2002:** Candidate for ordination not examined
in original languages. _BCO_ 21-4.a.2 and RAO 14-3.e.7 Also, no
record of ordinand’s signing ministerial obligation book. _BCO_ 13-7

**Response:** It is the practice of Warrior Presbytery to accept an
ordination candidate’s grade record of acceptable performance from
his seminary of graduation in lieu of exams in the original languages.
That was the action of the presbytery and should have been duly
recorded in the minutes. We apologize for this oversight and will
make all due effort to see that all the details of ordination exams are
duly recorded in our minutes in the future. Presbytery did fail to have
the candidate sign the Book of Ministerial Obligation. The oversight
was later discovered by the current clerk. The then TE did sign the
BMO at the stated meeting of the presbytery on April 20, 2004.
