## Raised — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.340](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p340)*

Exception: June 9, 2000: approval of the examination for ordination
of a candidate who states as part of his exceptions tot hew CF, WLC,
and WSC that “I also believe the Sabbath rest to mean any one day in
seven.” WCF 21-7; WLC 116; WSC 59.

## Response found satisfactory — 30th General Assembly (2002)
*[ga30_2002 p.321](../../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p321)*

Exception: June 9, 2000: Approval of the
examination for ordination of a candidate who states as
part of his exceptions to the WCF, WLC, and WSC that
“I also believe the Sabbath rest to mean any one day in
seven.” WCF 21-7; WLC 116; WSC 59

Response: In the examination of Candidate [name
deleted] for ordination, Presbytery regrets allowing the
candidate’s own statement “I believe the Sabbath rest
to mean any one day in seven,” to appear in our
minutes with all its ambiguity. We understand how
that statement might be interpreted as the view that we
might choose another day of the week besides Sunday
as the day for corporate worship. The Committee’s
references to WCF 21-7 and to WLC 116 imply that
they so interpreted [name deleted]’s statement. But
that is not his view. His intention is to affirm that
ministers, unable to rest on the Lord’s Day due to their
official duties, are to choose another day of the week
on which to rest. Presbytery does not consider this
view an unacceptable exception to our Standards and
trusts the Committee agrees. Adopted
