# Tennessee Valley Presbytery — BCO 20-11

*Presbytery granted a TE permission to continue laboring out of bounds as a…*

**Presbytery:** Tennessee Valley  ·  **First raised:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 20-11

**Lifecycle:** raised (28th) → satisfactory (29th)

---

## Raised — 28th General Assembly (2000)
*[ga28_2000 p.342](../../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p342)*

Exception: January 9, 1999 (Minister and His Work Committee): Presbytery granted a TE permission to continue laboring out of bounds as a pastor of an independent reformed church in Michigan. 1) The record doesn't include a call or Presbytery's approval of a call, 2) There is no assurance that the TE will be allowed to teach and preach the whole council of God's word, and 3) There is no record that the TE had contacted Great Lakes Presbytery for approval to work within the bounds of that Presbytery. BCO 20-11 &amp; 21-1.

## Response found satisfactory — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.354](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p354)*

Exception: January 9, 1999 (Minister and His Work Committee): Presbytery granted a TE permission to continue laboring out of bounds as a pastor of an independent reformed church in Michigan. 1) The record doesn't include a call or Presbytery's approval of a call, 2) There is no assurance that the TE will be allowed to teach and preach the whole council of God's word, and 3) There is no record that the TE had contacted Great Lakes Presbytery for approval to work within the bounds of that Presbytery. BCO 20-11 &amp; 21-1.

Response: In January of 1999 the Tennessee Valley Presbytery (TVP) improperly granted TE Steve Simmons permission to labor out of bound of the TVP because of his call to serve a non-PCA congregation in Michigan.

The TVP should have corresponded with the Great Lakes Presbytery concerning TE Simmons' pending move. Furthermore, they should have encouraged TE Simmons to transfer his credentials to the Great Lakes Presbytery that he might receive their permission to labor out of bounds within the Presbytery.

The TVP regrets its error in this matter.

In the Lord's gracious providence, TE Simmons has, as of October 2000, been received as a member of the Great Lakes Presbytery, thus correcting the TVP's error.

Thank you for calling our attention to this error.

Adopted
