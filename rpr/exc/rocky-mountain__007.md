# Rocky Mountain Presbytery — exception of substance

*Installed a man who is not a pastor of a particular…*

**Presbytery:** Rocky Mountain  ·  **First raised:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Final disposition:** satisfactory (closed)

**Lifecycle:** raised (28th) → satisfactory (29th)

---

## Raised — 28th General Assembly (2000)
*[ga28_2000 p.339](../../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p339)*

Exception: January 21-22, 1999: Installed a man who is not a pastor of a particular church.

## Response found satisfactory — 29th General Assembly (2001)
*[ga29_2001 p.350](../../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p350)*

Exception: January 21-22, 1999: installed a man who is not a pastor
of a particular church.

Response: Rocky Mountain Presbytery agrees with the exception and
promises to be more careful in the future. (It does not seem feasible or
appropriate to attempt to correct this action, a sit is now a matter of
history. BCO 8-7 describes the provision for a Presbytery approving the
call of a teaching elder to work wi than organization outside the
jurisdiction of the PCA. it has been the practice of this presbytery to
provide an installation service for our teaching elders called to such other
ministries, if they desire and request such a service. by action of the
Presbytery as its Winter stated meeting on January 26, 2001, by a motion
duly made, seconded, debated and carried, adopted the policy of
conducting commissioning services for men who desire and request such
after the presbytery has approved a call to a teaching elder to work with
an organization outside the jurisdiction of the PCA.
