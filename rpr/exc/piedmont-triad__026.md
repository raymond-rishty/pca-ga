## Raised — 52nd General Assembly (2025)
*[ga52_2025 p.683](../../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p683)*

**2025-04: May 11, 2024** ( _BCO_ 21-4; _RAO_ 16-3.e.5) —
Presbytery incorrectly judged a candidate’s stated differences
as merely semantic. Both stated differences actually differ
from the Confession of Faith.

The first difference reads: “I have a perceived
difference with the _WCF_ Chapter 21, Section 8: “The Sabbath
is then kept holy unto the Lord, when men, after a due
preparing of their hearts, and ordering of their affairs
beforehand, do not only observe an holy rest, all the day, from
their own, works, words, and thoughts about their worldly
employments, and recreations, but also are taken up the whole
time, in the public and private exercises of his worship, and in
the duties of necessity, and mercy.” though i do believe it is
important to keep the Sabbath as the Lord’s Day and to
worship on the first day of the week, when Christ rose. As the
pattern laid out in scripture. I do not think we have to literally
rest from all worldly matters to keep this. As humans, we are
to set aside a day of the week to worship and rest in the Lord.
I think the proper way to observe the Sabbath day is to gather
together as the body and worship our God and King. (Matt.
28, Acts 2) I do think it is important to set aside the Lord’s
Day to be purposeful in your reflection on God and gather
with the body in your local church. This way you hear the
Word proclaimed, pray/reflect on the week. And partake of
the Lord’s Supper in order to nourish your faith. Though I do
not think per the confession, you can do nothing on the
Sabbath. I do think doing things with your family, recreation,
yardwork, etc. can all be means of rest and bring glory to God.
This is part of our living our lives daily as Christians seeking
to honor and serve Christ daily. In other words, as Christians
we shouldn’t only think about Christ and his word on Sunday.
Rather we are to live our daily lives praising him and serving

him daily in our various callings. The Sabbath is the day set
aside to prepare to live out this calling.”
The second difference reads: “I have perceived
difference with _WCF_ chapter 4 section 1: “It pleased God the
Father, Son, and Holy Ghost, and for the manifestation of the
glory of his eternal power, wisdom, and goodness in the
beginning, to create, or make of nothing, the world, and all
things therein whether visible or invisible, in the space of six
days; and all very good.” My view of creation would be the
standard day age view. The ‘six days’ are understood in the
same sense as “in that day” of Isaiah 11:10-11 —that is, as
periods of indefinite length and not necessarily of 24 hours
duration. There are other similar uses of the Hebrew word for

“day” (yôm) in Scripture to support this view of periods
longer than 24 hours including that in the very context of
Genesis 2:4. Another argument for this approach is that the
seventh day in Genesis 1 is not concluded with the boundary
phrase, “and there was evening, and there was morning” as
with the other days, and therefore it continues, as indicated by
Hebrews 4:1-11's quotation of Psalm 95:11.”
