# GA47 O31 — Form Ad Interim Committee on Domestic Abuse & Sexual Assault

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 31 from Eastern Pennsylvania Presbytery (to AC, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 662](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p662)*

---

“Form an Ad Interim Committee to Address Matters Related to Domestic Abuse, Domestic Oppression, and Sexual Assault” (Version 6)

**Be it resolved:** 1. That the 47th General Assembly create a seven-member Ad Interim Committee (AIC) tasked to accomplish the following. a. Prepare an annotated bibliography of recommended pastoral resources available on the nature of domestic abuse, domestic oppression, [15] and sexual assault, as well as pastoral resources for the care of victims of these sins; b. Explore these matters from an historical perspective, giving special consideration of how they have been conceived of and addressed within the Reformed tradition and provide a summary of their findings. c. Describe "best practices" and policy guidelines that could be helpful for elders, Sessions, Presbyteries, and agencies for protecting against these sins and for responding to them; d. If the Committee deems it prudent, it may include in the report any revisions or additions to _BCO_ Rules of Discipline that might more appropriately protect victims of these sins within a biblical process, which changes may be issued as an overture to a subsequent General Assembly by any Presbytery that would wish to do so; e. If the Committee deems it prudent, it may include in the report any statement the Committee might recommend for the GA to consider adopting regarding any PCA repentance related to these matters, which statement may be issued as an overture to a subsequent General Assembly by any Presbytery that would wish to do so. 2. The 47th GA authorizes the Moderator of the 47th GA to appoint the seven voting members of this Committee. ( _RAO_ 9-4) a. These members shall be PCA ministers or PCA ruling elders, and the Committee shall include at least two of each. (Presbyteries, Sessions, and individuals may contact the Moderator with suggested names.) b. The 47th GA authorizes the Moderator to make all of his appointments after the 47th GA adjourns. ( _RAO_ 9-4) 3. The 47th GA authorizes the Moderator to appoint others to serve the Committee as advisors, regardless of gender or denominational affiliation, within the confines of its budget. The sins of domestic abuse and sexual assault are expressly forbidden by Scripture and the Westminster Standards. (For example, see 2 Timothy 3:2-5; Jude 7; Exodus 21:15; Deuteronomy 22:25-27; also, _WLC_ 135 and 139.) 2. These sins are especially heinous as they are “against the express letter of the law,” “break many commandments,” “admit of no reparation,” and often involve various other aggravations ( _WLC_ 151). 3. Sadly, these sins are prevalent in our society, and in the church. [16] 4. These sins often produce in the victims a complex response of denial along with immense fear and shame that persist with varying severity throughout life (e.g., 2 Samuel 13:1-22). 5. It is the responsibility of the elders of the Church to exercise their authority, severally and jointly, to shepherd those who are under their care by guarding against such sinful behavior as much as possible, by wisely caring for those victimized by the sins of others, and by appropriately disciplining those guilty of such sins, with an aim toward a full repentance. 6. these sins have been a concern and a subject of attention not only in other denominations but even in the proceedings of the 46th General Assembly. 7.
