# GA28 O13 — Amend BCO 21 to Allow Modified Re-Ordination Procedures for Former PCA Ministers

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 13 From Susquehanna Valley Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 289](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p289)*

---

“Re-ordination of former PCA Ministers” whereas the Book of Church Order rightly calls fora presbytery to divest a minister if he continues on the rolls without call for over three years (BCO 34-10, 13-2), and

Whereas, this procedure is rarely followed, and

Whereas, this leads to men not being as closely connected to sessions as is good for them or as is good for the work of the church, and

Whereas, this often puts pressure on presbyteries to approve “calls” which are questionable, and

Whereas, one reason for this failure of presbyteries to act is that both the man and his presbytery are hoping a call will come and if it does after he is divested he would have to go through the entire ordination process again, Therefore, the Presbytery of Susquehanna Valley hereby overtures the General Assembly to add a new section 5 as follows to chapter 21 of the BCO and renumber the following sections: NEW 21-5: Modified procedures may be used for the re-ordination of a former PCA minister who receives a call to a particular church after he was divested without censure at his request (38-2) or was divested of his office without censure against his will (34-10). The presbytery in which he is being called shall carefully investigate the circumstances under which he was divested. If the presbytery decides that the reason he was divested was simply a lack of call, it may proceed to give him the trials for ordination outlined in 21-4 without going through the normal candidate, licensure, and internship procedures. At its discretion, the presbytery may accept his previous ordination in lieu of examining him in the Greek, and Hebrew languages, church history, and the history of the PCA. Adopted by the Presbytery of Susquehanna Valley on February 18, 2000. Attested by: /s/ David F. Rundle, Stated Clerk 12. That OVERTURE 4 from James River Presbytery (“Clarify Use of Professional in Cases of Process”) be answered in the affirmative with the following substitute. That BCO 32-19 be amended by deleting the current language (indicated by strikeouts) and replacing it with the following new language. 32-19. No professional counsel shall be permitted as such to appear and plead in cases of process in any court; but an accused person may, if he desires it, be represented before the Session by any communing member of the same particular church, or before any other court, by any member of that court. A member of the court so employed shall not be allowed to sit in judgment in the case. 32-19. The accused shall be entitled to be represented before the courts of this church by counsel, who shall have the right to be heard by oral and/or written argument. Any such counsel shall be a communing member of the Presbyterian Church in America. Any member of a court so engaged shall not be allowed to sit in judgment of the case. No person shall accept any fee or other remuneration for any service rendered as counsel, but reimbursement may be made to cover any personal expenses incurred or to be incurred by such counsel in the course of representation. Adopted Grounds: The overture identifies important defects in the provision as it stands, i.e., the possibility of discontinuity of counsel in the appeals process, and the uncertainty of the church with respect to the meaning of “professional counsel”. The substitute language provides improved economy, consistency, and logical progression in the proposed amendment, while making plain the otherwise implied rights of counsel, and broadening the membership criteria for counsel.
