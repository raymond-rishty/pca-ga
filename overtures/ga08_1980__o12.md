# GA8 O12 — Reaffirm the GA's original statement on abortion

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Evangel Presbytery

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 35](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p35)*

---

Whereas, the General Assembly, as a court of jesus Christ, should speak with a united voice in affirming the sanctity of human life under the protection of the Sixth Commandment, Therefore Be It Resolved, that Evangel Presbytery overture the Eighth General Assembly to reaffirm its original statement on abortion: "That because Scripture clearly affirms the sanctity of human life and condemns its arbitrary destruction, we affirm that the intentional killing of an unborn child between conception and birth, for any reason at any time, is clearly a violation of the Sixth Commandment. " Added to this statement are the following affirmations: "Concerning crisis pregnancies in which it appears that the premature removal of the unborn child from the mother is a necessary consequence of medical procedures essential to preserving the mother's life, we affirm that: I The Sixth Commandment not only forbids the taking of innocent life for whatever reason, but also requires the preserving of innocent life wherever possible, 2. The life of the mother as well as her child comes under the full protection of the Sixth Command ment and all moral medical wisdom and skill must be used to preserve her life as well as the life of her child. 3. To say that we are ever placed in the position of choosing between two sins (killing the child or neglecting the preservation of the mother's life) is to surrender our belief in the Sovereign overruling providence of our All-wise and All-holy God (1 Cor. 10:13) and to allege that we must ' do evil that good may come” . . a philosophy clearly condemned by Scr
