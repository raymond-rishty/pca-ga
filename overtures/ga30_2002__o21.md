# GA30 O21 — Adopt Mutual Forbearance on Women in Military and Dismiss WIM Committee

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 251](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p251)*

“Mutual Forbearance on Women in Military Military & B&O) and Dismiss Women in Military Committee With Thanks”

Whereas PCA members facing such concerns require sound pastoral counsel as regards their duty before God; and

Whereas “The duty which God requireth of man, is obedience to his revealed will (WSC 39);” and

Whereas “The moral law is summarily comprehended in the Ten Commandments (WSC 41);” and

Whereas “The fifth commandment requireth the preserving the honor, and performing the duties, belonging to every one in their several places and relations, as superiors, inferiors, or equals (WSC 64);” and, “The fifth commandment forbiddeth the neglecting of, or doing any thing against, the honor and duty which belongeth to every one in their several places and relations (WSC 65);” and

Whereas concerns related to women serving in the military touch upon the various responsibilities of superiors, inferiors and equals in the military chain of command and among the citizenry of the commonwealth; and

Whereas “The sixth commandment requireth all lawful endeavors to preserve our own life, and the life of others (WSC 68);” and “The sixth commandment forbiddeth the taking away of our own life, or the life of our neighbor unjustly, or whatsoever tendeth thereunto (WSC 69);”

Whereas concerns related to woman serving in the military understandably may involve concerns over potential increased loss of life due to reduced combat effectiveness with mixed sex troop arrangements; and

Whereas “The seventh commandment requireth the preservation of our own and our neighbor’s chastity, in heart, speech, and behavior (WSC 71);” and “The seventh commandment forbiddeth all unchaste thoughts, words, and actions (WSC 72);” and

Whereas potential increased sexual immorality and violation is an understandable concern as regards both men and women serving in the military; and

Whereas these teachings of Scripture as summarized in our Constitution provide sound basis for objection to such mixed troop arrangements; and

Whereas nevertheless “men of good character and principles may differ” with each other concerning such an application of these teachings, so that some out of conscience may object to women serving in the military, while others may not object; and

Whereas some, however, may object to what they understand to be an unscriptural prohibition concerning women in the military that binds conscience to something other than God’s revealed will, and that runs the risk of intermeddling in civil affairs; and

Whereas given such disagreement we are all required “to exercise mutual forbearance toward each other;” Therefore let it be resolved that the 30th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America formally go on record as affirming that our constitution provides sound basis for any of our members to object conscientiously to women serving in the military in various circumstances, including the conscription of women for military service, without requiring others to maintain the same objections; and finally Let it be further resolved that the Ad Interim Committee on Women in the Military be dismissed with thanks.
