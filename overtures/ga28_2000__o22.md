# GA28 O22 — Condemn the SJC Decision in the John Wood Case

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 22 From Louisiana Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 278](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p278)*

---

“Condemn the SJC Decision in the John Wood Case”

Whereas, Presbyterianism historically has allowed for one General Assembly to condemn the opinion of another General Assembly (M 12GA, p. 140: "A subsequent General Assembly may take a contrary position and condemn the action of a previous Assembly."); and

Whereas, the decisions of the Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) are those of the previous General Assembly (BCO 15-4: "and each subsequent Assembly should declare the Standing Judicial Commission as a whole to be is commission”), and

Whereas, the General Assembly is bound "to bear testimony against error in doctrine ., injuriously affecting the Church.” (BCO 14-6.a ); and

Whereas, the decision oft hes jc in RE Presbyterian Church in America vs. John Wood is evidently flawed, and

Whereas, because of the peculiar nature of current PCA judicial process, the General Assembly as such never was able directly to review this decision of the SJC; and

Whereas, the decision is flawed in numerous ways, especially in its interpretation of the Constitution; and

Whereas, among the errors in Constitutional interpretation are the following: (1) the SJC assumes that a corporate responsibility by a session means that individual members cannot also be held responsible for the action of the session; (2) the SJC assumes that there were no doctrinal issues involved, but merely the action of inviting a woman to fill the pulpit, when there is evidence that Mr. Wood advocates female preaching; (3) the SJC assumes that action by Tennessee Valley Presbytery to vindicate the session of cedar springs presbyterian Church, Knoxville, Tennessee, automatically clears Mr. Wood, (4) the SJC forcibly recused members oft hes jc from Ascension, Calvary, and Western Carolina Presbyteries, based on the allegation that these Presbyteries were parties to the case, whereas in point of fact, ina case of original jurisdiction, the "original and only parties ina case of process are the accuser and the accused. The accuser is always the Presbyterian Church in America" (BCO 31-3), and

Whereas, this situation of having a seriously-flawed decision in this judicial case is troubling to the church and is potentially divisive, and

Whereas, the god who has called us to peace is not the author of confusion, and

Whereas, the Ninth Commandment requires "from the heart, sincerely, freely, clearly, and fully, speaking the truth, and only the truth, in matters of judgment and justice" (WLC, Q/A 144), and Now Therefore, Be it Resolve, that Louisiana Presbytery hereby overtures the 28th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to condemn and to bear testimony against the judgment of the 27th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America regarding the judicial case with respect to the Rev. Mr. John Wood Adopted by Louisiana Presbytery at the 63rd Stated Meeting, Opelousas, Louisiana, on April 15, 2000.
