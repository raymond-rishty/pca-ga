# GA28 O22 — Condemn the SJC Decision in the John Wood Case

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 22 From Louisiana Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 278](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p278)*

---

“Condemn the SJC Decision in the John Wood Case”

Whereas, Presbyterianism historically has allowed for one General Assembly to condemn the opinion of another General Assembly (M 12GA, p. 140: "A subsequent General Assembly may take a contrary position and condemn the action of a previous Assembly."); and

Whereas, the decisions of the Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) are those of the previous General Assembly (BCO 15-4: "and each subsequent Assembly should declare the Standing Judicial Commission as a whole to be is commission”), and

Whereas, the General Assembly is bound "to bear testimony against error in doctrine ., injuriously affecting the Church.” (BCO 14-6.a ); and

Whereas, the decision oft hes jc in re Presbyterian Church in America vs. John Wood is evidently flawed, and

Whereas, because of the peculiar nature of current p ca judicial process, the General Assembly as such never was able directly to review this decision of the SJC; and

Whereas, the decision is flawed in numerous ways, especially in its interpretation of the Constitution; and

Whereas, among the errors in Constitutional interpretation are the following: (1) t hes jc assumes that a corporate responsibility by a session means that individual members cannot also be held responsible for the action of the session; (2) the SJC assumes that there were no doctrinal issues involved, but merely the action of inviting a woman to fill the pulpit, when there is evidence that Mr. Wood advocates female preaching; (3) the SJC assumes that action by Tennessee Valley Presbytery to vindicate the session of cedar springs
