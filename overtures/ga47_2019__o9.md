# GA47 O9 — Amend BCO 42-4, 43-2, & 43-3 on Filing Deadlines for Cases

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 9 from Platte Valley Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **BCO:** 42-4

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 610](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p610)*

---

“Amend _BCO_ 42-4; 43-2; 43-3 regarding Method and Deadlines of Filing Cases”

**Whereas**, the Standing Judicial Commission ruled in Case 2016-02 ( _M45GA_, 2017, pp. 510–14), that notice of complaints must be made by hard copy, and that electronic filing of complaints are administratively out of order; and

**Whereas**, the language of both _BCO_ 42-4 (filing appeals) and _BCO_ 43-3 (filing complaints) confusingly refer to two kinds of notification: the notification of the lower court’s original decision, and the notification of appeal/complaint by the appellant/ complainant; and

**Whereas** _,_ _BCO_ 42-4 and _BCO_ 43-3 explicitly provide that the “notification” (that is, the notification of the lower court’s original decision) may be made electronically, but do not explicitly forbid notification of appeals and complaints from being made electronically; and

**Whereas**, _BCO_ 43-2 does not describe multiple types of “notification,” but still does not explicitly define that complainants may not file a complaint electronically; and

**Whereas** _,_ the _Operating Manual_ (18.10.b) of the Standing Judicial Commission much more clearly defines proper, timely filing: Documents required or permitted to be filed by a party shall be filed with the Stated Clerk. Such filing shall not be timely unless the documents are received in the office of the Stated Clerk within the time fixed for such filing, except that papers shall be deemed filed on the day of mailing if certified, registered or express mail of the United States Postal Service or any private service where verifying receipt
