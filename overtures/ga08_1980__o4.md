# GA8 O4 — Amend BCO 15-1 on permanent-committee alternates

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of the Western Carolinas  ·  **BCO:** 15-1

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 39](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p39)*

---

Whereas, each year there are elected alternate members of the Permanent Committees of the General Assembly, one Teaching and one Ruling Elder, and Whereas, these alternate members have an annual cost of a full and regular member, and Whereas, by the present rule of BOCO 15-1-11 these alternate members are voting members only in the event of a lack of full committee quorum, and Whereas, no specific ruling determines whether this shall be a quorum among the Ruling Elders on the committee, or among the Teaching Elders, or the quorum of the committee as a whole, and Whereas, by their regular attendance these duly-elected members acquire expertise in the committee's field of business, and should be able to vote wisely. Therefore, The Presbytery of the Western Carolinas overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to change section 15-1-11 to replace the last sentence of that paragraph to read: "Each alternate should attend each meeting and fill any vacancy necessary to take the place of any absentee in the particular elder-category to which the alternate belongs, and vote only if this substitution is being made.' 1 In this manner, it will not be necessary for an absence of 3-5 regular committee members for an alternate to become useful to the General Assembly who elected him, but he may begin to vote whenever just one regular member in his elder-category is absent. i fa teaching elder is absent, the Teaching Elder alternate takes his place; i fa ruling elder is absent, the Ruling Elder alternate makes the substitution.
