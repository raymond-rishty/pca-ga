# GA30 O1 — Add 'and Unity' to Sixth Ordination Vow in BCO 21-5

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 1 from Western Carolina Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)  ·  **Disposition:** Approved & ratified (2003)  ·  **Ratified**  ·  **BCO:** 21-5

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 246](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p246)*

---

“Add ‘and unity’ to Sixth Ordination Vow in BCO 21-5”

Whereas, the theme of the 29th General Assembly was “Unity of the Spirit in the Bond of Peace”; and

Whereas, in Ephesians 4:3 Paul commands the people of God to “Make every effort to keep the unity of the Spirit through the bond of peace,” going on to declare that Christ has specifically given pastors and teachers to the Church so that the body of Christ may reach “unity in the faith (vs. 13); and

Whereas, the sixth question proposed to Ruling Elders and Deacons at their ordination and installation calls them to promise to strive for the purity, peace, and unity of the Church (Book of Church Order 24-5): Therefore Be It Resolved, that the Western Carolina Presbytery petition the General Assembly to amend the Questions for Ordination of Teaching Elders found in Book of Church Order 21-5, by adding the words “and unity” to the sixth question, so that the question would now read: “Do you promise to be zealous and faithful in maintaining the truths of the Gospel and the purity and peace and unity of the Church, whatever persecution or opposition may arise unto you on that account?” 7. That Overture 2 from Western Carolina and Metropolitan New York Presbyteries (“Diversity of views on women in Military and Dismiss WIM Committee with Thanks - Version 1”) be answered in the negative and that Overture 21 from New River Presbytery (“Mutual Forbearance on Women in Military and Dismiss Women in Military Committee With Thanks”) and Overture 26 from Potomac Presbytery (“Diversity of Views on Women In Military and Dismiss WIM Committee with Thanks - Version 2”) be answered by reference to the Assembly’s action on Overture 2. [See also 30-57, p. 287.] Adopted Grounds: This recommendation is proposed without prejudice to the merits of the matter under consideration. The overtures have been referred to both Bills and Overtures and to the Ad Interim Committee. To have the committees possibly report conflicting recommendations would lead to confusion. The Assembly will be better served by taking this matter up in the docketed report of the Ad Interim Committee, which will include minority recommendations similar to those proposed in the overtures.
