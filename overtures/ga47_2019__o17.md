# GA47 O17 — Amend BCO 32-8 to Allow Video Testimony by Witnesses

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 17 from Houston Metro Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Approved & ratified (2021)  ·  **Ratified**  ·  **BCO:** 32-8

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 631](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p631)*

---

“Amend _BCO_ 32-8 to Allow for Video Testimony by Witnesses” **Whereas,** an important aspect of any case of process is the gathering and presenting of evidence, including testimony produced by witnesses; **Whereas**, testimony by witnesses before the court can be inconvenient due to geographic distance or other circumstances; **Whereas**, it is important that witnesses be examined with an opportunity to be cross-examined by both parties ( _cf._ _BCO_ 32-13); and **Whereas**, technology has made available the possibility of having a virtual presence with video and audio interaction even when a person is geographically distant from a meeting or court room, and the courts of the civil magistrate allow for video testimony by witnesses; **Therefore, be it resolved**, that the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America amend _BCO_ 32-8 with the following (additions are underlined, deletions are struck through): **32-8** . When the offense with which an accused person stands charged took place at a distance, ~~and~~ or it is inconvenient for the witnesses to appear before the court having jurisdiction, that court may either (a) appoint a commission of its body, or (b) request the coordinate court contiguous to the place where the facts occurred, to take the testimony for it, or (c) have the testimony taken by videoconference, which shall employ technical means that ensure that all persons participating in the meeting can see and hear each other at the same time, and which allows for live cross-examination by both parties.
