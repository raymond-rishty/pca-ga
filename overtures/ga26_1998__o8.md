# GA26 O8 — Cease Publishing Ministers' Private Information in the PCA Yearbook

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 8 From Heritage Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Referred

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 65](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p65)*

---

“Cease Publishing Private Information in Yearbook”

Whereas, the PCA Yearbook annually contains enough personal information about PCA ministers to potentially severely compromise privacy of its ministers to persons of ill will,

Whereas, in years past such information was gathered prior to modem privacy problems.

Whereas, material gathered from the PCA Yearbook together with other reasonably available information could significantly compromise the financial integrity of ministers by persons o fill will,

Whereas, this publishing this information provides no significant useful legitimate purpose,

Therefore be it resolved, that the General Assembly instruct the Stated Clerk’s Office to cease gathering or publishing mother’s maiden name and location of birth of ministers, And to review other data gathered for the Yearbook and cease publishing information about churches, agencies or ministers that is of little use and may be potentially damaging. And that the General Assembly instruct the other committees and agencies to review their gathering of and distributing of personal information No personal information should be gathered unless there is significant legitimate purpose. If so gathered, it must be kept confidential. Attested by: /s/ Bruce B Howes, Stated Clerk 12.
