# GA26 O16 — Deny Expanding Northern California Presbytery into Nevada and Utah

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 16 From Central Georgia Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 173](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p173)*

---

“Deny Enlarging Northern California Presbytery”

Whereas, from its beginning, the Presbyterian Church in America has sought to reach out to the entire nation, and

Whereas, the PCA’s philosophy in doing so has been to restrict geographical boundaries of presbyteries to a reasonable size and to allow all of the presbyteries and churches to have responsibility for spreading the gospel in the destitute areas of the church, and

Whereas, the 1984 General Assembly stated: “That our presbyteries be encouraged to limit their boundaries to those geographic areas for which they are able to take meaningful responsibility in evangelism and church development, leaving other areas for orchestrated action of all presbyteries working together through the General Assembly”; and

Whereas, Central Georgia Presbytery ordained a man in March 1998 to be a church planter in the Provo, Utah, area, and

Whereas, Central Georgia Presbytery, in ordaining this man to undertake this work, was acting in full accord with encouragement of the General Assembly and the examples of other Presbyteries, and

Whereas, Northern California Presbytery has overtured the General Assembly to extend its boundaries so that Nevada and all of Utah would be included within its bounds, thus adding close to 200,000 square miles to its territory, and

Whereas, if this overture from Northern California were approved, no other presbytery could lawfully plant churches in this vast area, and

Whereas, Northern California is a small presbytery numerically, as is evident from the latest available denominational statistics (1,166 communicant members, thus ranking 43rd out of 49 Anglo presbyteries in communicant strength; total giving to the presbytery from the churches was $21,247.00, thus ranking 39th out of 49 Anglo presbyteries), and

Whereas, as adopted by the General Assembly, the minimal ideal for a presbytery is to have at least three churches with 125 communicant members, and according to the latest denominational statistics, Northern California has only two such churches; and

Whereas, the ideal boundaries for a presbytery, as set forth by the General Assembly, include driving distances of no more than two and a half hours, and

Whereas, the driving distance across Nevada and Utah along Interstate 80 is more than 600 miles, and

Whereas, the driving distance between the Nevada-California border and San Francisco is another 200 miles; and

Whereas, if the Northern California overture were adopted, Northern California’s boundaries would stretch about 4,000 miles, from the international dateline in the middle of the pacific ocean to the rocky Mountains, and

Whereas, Utah has more in common with Colorado than with California, as is evident culturally and geographically and as reflected in the fact that the old skyline synod of the presbyterian church in the united states of America encompasses Colorado and Utah.

Now therefore, be it resolved, that Central Georgia Presbytery hereby overtures the 26th General Assembly to do the following: Deny the request from Northern California Presbytery to extend its boundaries so that the denomination may continue to bring its entire resources to be aron the destitute regions of the Nation. Adopted at the Spring Meeting of Central Georgia Presbytery on April 14, 1998 Attested by: Julian H.
