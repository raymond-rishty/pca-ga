“Amend _BCO_ 35-9 to Require Recording All Parts of a Trial” **Be it resolved** that the 52 [nd] General Assembly revise _BCO_ 35-9 as shown below (underlining for additions; strikethrough for deletions): **35-9.** All testimony parts of a trial other than deliberations shall be recorded (by transcription, audiotape, videotape, or some other electronic means) and witnesses informed of such prior to testifying. Such recording becomes part of the Record of the Case. However, in order to be referenced in written or oral briefs, such recording must be transcribed and the transcription authenticated by the trial court. The court of final appeal may assess the cost of transcription equitably among the parties So that _BCO_ 35-9 as amended would read: **35-9.** All parts of a trial other than deliberations shall be recorded (by transcription, audiotape, videotape, or some other electronic means) and witnesses informed of such prior to testifying. Such recording becomes part of the Record of the Case. However, in order to be referenced in written or oral briefs, such recording must be transcribed and the transcription authenticated by the trial court. The court of final appeal may assess the cost of transcription equitably among the parties **Rationale** 1. This clarifies, for example, that objections raised by the parties during a trial and the court's ruling on those objections, are recorded and available for transcription in the event of an appeal _Adopted by the Southeast Alabama Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 22,_ _2025_ _Attested by TE Kevin Corley, stated clerk_
