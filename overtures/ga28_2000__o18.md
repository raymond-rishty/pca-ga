# GA28 O18 — Amend BCO 13-1 to Increase Ruling Elder Commissioners in Presbyteries

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 18 From Ellisville PCA, Ellisville, MS  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 13-1

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 287](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p287)*

---

“Amend BCO 13-1 to Allow Additional Ruling Elders as Presbytery Commissioners”

Whereas, the formation of the Presbyterian Church in America was the result of the Lord, in His Providence, convicting elders of the need to remain true to the Biblical principles and teachings upon which our faith is based, and

Whereas, our elder fathers in the faith were the first to point out and take a stand against errors being made and taught in the old Southern church, and

Whereas, ruling elders have played an integral part in the organization and growth of the Presbyterian Church in America, serving diligently in all courts of the church, and

Whereas, the parity of the elders hi pis compromised when the composition of church courts contain substantially more teaching elder commissioners than ruling elder commissioners, and

Whereas, ruling elders serve the church generally, when called thereunto (BCO 8-3; Acts 20:28f), as well as the local congregation, and

Whereas, the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America recognized the need for more ruling elder involvement and participation in said court by amending the Book of Church Order to increase the number of ruling elder commissioners from each church from one ( 1) to two (2), with additional commissioners for larger congregations, and

Whereas, there is a similar need for more ruling elder involvement and participation in the church presbyteries. Now Therefore, Be it Resolved, that the undersigned Session overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to amend the Book of Church Order, 13-1, to increase the number of ruling elder commissioners to presbytery meetings from each church from one ( 1) to two (2), with additional commissioners for larger congregations, and Be it Further Resolved, that the Book of Church Order 13-1 be amended by deleting the present paragraphs and substituting the following, to wit: the presbytery consists of all the teaching elders and churches within its bounds that have been accepted by the Presbytery. When the Presbytery meets as a court it shall comprise all teaching elders and ruling elders as elected by their Session. Each congregation is entitled to two ruling elder representatives for the first 350 communing members or fraction thereof, and one additional ruling elder for each additional 500 communing members or fraction thereof. Adopted by the Session of the undersigned Session at a called meeting on the 5th day of December, 1999. Attested by: Is/ Charles Probst, clerk of Session, Ellisville Presbyterian Church NOTE: This Overture was rejected by Grace Presbytery (RAO 10-10) 10. That OVERTURE 6 from Cherokee Presbyterian Church (“Amend BCO 16-4 to Allow that Exceptions May Not Be Taught”) be answered in the negative. Adopted grounds the powers of the presbytery with respect to this issue are well defined in the Constitution (e.g., BCO 13-9.d, -9,f.) and have been carefully and consistently construed by the General Assembly in judicial cases (e.g., M14GA, pp. 125-126, M18GA, p. 205, M20GA, pp. 163-164). Further, the proposed amendment introduces, without definition, terminology unknown to our Constitution, and thus liable to misinterpretation.
