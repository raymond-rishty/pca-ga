In judicial cases, the Session may elect one of its members to preside as moderator or invite a minister of the Presbytery to which the church belongs to preside as moderator. **RATIONALE:** Sessions should have discretion to elect a moderator in judicial cases. Some Sessions may have ruling elders who are highly experienced in church discipline and cases of process, and they should have the liberty to elect such a ruling elder to preside.
