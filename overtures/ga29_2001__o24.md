# GA29 O24 — Set Conditions for Use of Executive Session in Committee Meetings

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 24 From Grace Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 223](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p223)*

---

(to B&amp;O) "Set Conditions for Use of Executive Session"

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church in America was founded as a “grass roots” denomination; and

Whereas, one of the manifestations of the “grass roots” concept is the fact that the meetings of the committees of the church were assumed to be open to all members of the church (M13GA, p. 90); and

Whereas, this openness, which has been mandated by the General Assembly, is consonant with the fact that in the PCA we operate with subservient “committees” rather than semi-autonomous “boards”; and

Whereas, the election of men to the various committees is an act by which the Assembly selects representatives; and

Whereas, the meetings of church committees ought to be open to all the members of the denomination with the possible exception of certain highly sensitive matters, the knowledge of which being widespread might be detrimental; and

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church (USA) has adopted strict guidelines for when its boards may go into executive session, so as to guarantee openness in the deliberations; and

Whereas, we in the PCA should not be less open and honest than our liberal counterpart; and

Whereas, this is especially the case since we self-professedly operate with committees rather than boards, as is done in the PC(USA); and
