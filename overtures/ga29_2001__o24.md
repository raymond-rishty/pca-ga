# GA29 O24 — Set Conditions for Use of Executive Session in Committee Meetings

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 24 From Grace Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 223](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p223)*

---

(to B&amp;O) "Set Conditions for Use of Executive Session"

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church in America was founded as a “grass roots” denomination; and

Whereas, one of the manifestations of the “grass roots” concept is the fact that the meetings of the committees of the church were assumed to be open to all members of the church (M13GA, p. 90); and

Whereas, this openness, which has been mandated by the General Assembly, is consonant with the fact that in the PCA we operate with subservient “committees” rather than semi-autonomous “boards”; and

Whereas, the election of men to the various committees is an act by which the Assembly selects representatives; and

Whereas, the meetings of church committees ought to be open to all the members of the denomination with the possible exception of certain highly sensitive matters, the knowledge of which being widespread might be detrimental; and

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church (USA) has adopted strict guidelines for when its boards may go into executive session, so as to guarantee openness in the deliberations; and

Whereas, we in the PCA should not be less open and honest than our liberal counterpart; and

Whereas, this is especially the case since we self-professedly operate with committees rather than boards, as is done in the PC(USA); and

Whereas, we want to preserve this openness which was set forth as the ideal in the PCA from early in its existence: Now Therefore, Be It Resolved, that Grace Presbytery hereby overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to take the following actions: 1. to reaffirm that all General Assembly committees, both permanent and ad-interim, committees of commissioners and agencies will operate in open session. 2. to require that said bodies may go into executive session only for the following reasons: property negotiations, discussion of active or pending criminal matters or civil litigation, discussion of personnel performance, personal safety, or approval of reports if required to be done in executive session. 3. to declare that whenever a committee or agency enters executive session, only the elected members and ex-officio members of that committee or agency may remain in the room, the only exception to this policy being that a person may be invited to stay if he has specific information, is otherwise involved in the matter under consideration, or not otherwise available to the committee or agency. 4. To determine that any decisions made in executive session must be reported in public and access to said decisions guaranteed to any member of the church, and that all members are guaranteed access to the minutes and official records of all committees and agencies. 5. To amend the Rules for Assembly Operation by adding a new section (4-19) to read as follows: All General Assembly committees, both permanent and ad-interim, committees of commissioners, and agencies will operate in open session. Said bodies may go into executive session only for the following reasons: property negotiations, discussion of active or pending criminal matters or civil litigation, discussion of personnel performance, personal safety, or approval of reports if required to be done in executive session. Whenever a committee or agency enters executive session, only the elected members or ex-officio members of the committee or agency may remain in the room, the only exception to this policy being that a person may be invited to stay if he has specific information, is otherwise involved in the matter under consideration, or not otherwise available to the committee or agency. Adopted by the 96th Stated Meeting of Grace Presbytery on the 11th day of January, 2001. Attested by: /s/ Eugene C. Case, Stated Clerk
