# GA29 O7 — Require Exceptions for Views Other Than Six 24-Hour Creation Days

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** Calvary Presbytery: Require Exceptions for Views Other Than Six 24-Hour Creation Days  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 141](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p141)*

---

It is the opinion of the CCB that Overtures 7, 20, 23, and 30 touch on issues that are not properly before the CCB since they do not seek constitutional amendment or constitutional advice. a. The Creation Study Committee Report has the status of any other study committee report, such as those on the gifts of the Holy Spirit and Freemasonry, that is, it is the thinking of a particular General Assembly on a specific subject. b. The Creation Study Committee Report, as with all other study committee reports adopted by a General Assembly, does not amend the constitution (BCO Preface, III. “The Constitution Defined” and BCO 26-1); only amendments effected by the proper constitutional procedures are actual and lawful amendments (BCO 26-2, 26-3 and 26-6). c. The Creation Study Committee Report has the status of being among those decisions of the General Assembly that are to be given “due and serious consideration by the Church and its lower courts when deliberating matters related to such actions” (BCO 14-7). d. Overtures relating to the Creation Study Committee Report would have the same status as the report, i.e. of being among those decisions of the General Assembly that are to be given “due and serious consideration by the Church and its lower courts when deliberating matters related to such actions” (BCO 14-7). e. It is the purview of the lower courts to determine the suitability of men applying for reception/ordination with regard to the men’s views on the matters raised in *in thesi* statements, subject always to proper review and/or judicial process.
