# GA8 O11 — Reconsider the statement on alcoholic beverages

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Eastern Carolina  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 34](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p34)*

---

Whereas, an overture brought before the 1979 General Assembly on the subject of alcoholic beverages was not received by the Assembly; (Minutes, p. 92); and Whereas, the General Assembly of 1979 rejected the ruling of the Sub-committee on Judicial Business that the overture was in order; (Minutes, p. 181); and Whereas, the Book of Church Order 15-6 authorizes the General Assembly to "recommend measures for the promotion of charity, truth, and holiness through all the churches under its care"; and Whereas, the consumption of alcoholic beverages is one of the greatest hindrances to holiness in our land; and Whereas, the Scripture view of Christian liberty is clearly stated in Romans 14:21, 1 Corinthians 8:13, and especially in 1 Peter 2:16; and Whereas, the consumption of alcoholic beverages continues to be one of the most serious stumbling blocks in our country today; Be It Resolved, by the General Assembly in an in ihesi deliverance that total abstinance from alcoholic beverages, except in medicinal use, be the approved position of our denomination in this matter. Adopted by Eastern Carolina Presbytery on Jan 26, 1980. Attest: C. Gene Pjrks Stated Clerk Answered in the negative, with comment. § 8-69, 111, 4; p. 98.
