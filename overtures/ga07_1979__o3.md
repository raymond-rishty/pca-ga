# GA7 O3 — Acknowledge All New Congregations Equally During Assembly Recognition

**Assembly:** 7th (1979)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Westminster  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga07_1979 p. 35](../markdown/ga07_1979.md#ga07-p35)*

---

Whereas, it has been the practice of the past General Assemblies to recognize publicly certain congregations which have joined the PCA during the year prior to the meeting of the General Assembly, and

Whereas, these congregations are usually ones with large memberships and much wealth, and

Whereas, smaller congregations with less financial means are usually not recognized, and

Whereas, the Scriptures warn against showing favoritism on the basis of such things as wealth, James 2:1-4, Therefore, Westminster Presbytery overtures the Seventh General Assembly that if time is taken during an Assembly to recognize congregations which have united with the PCA during the year prior to the Assembly, that every effort be made to acknowledge every congregation so that no congregation be overlooked. Adopted at the stated meeting, October 21, 1978. Attest: Larry E. Ball Stated Clerk Answered in the affirmative as amended. § 7-37, III, 2; p. 97.
