# GA29 O25 — Forbid Women to Preach or Teach in Corporate Worship

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 25 From Grace Presbytery (to B&amp;O &amp; CCB) “Forbid Women to Preach or Teach in Corporate Worship”  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 224](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p224)*

---

Whereas, the Word of God and the Confessional Standards of the Presbyterian Church in America declare without ambiguity that a woman shall not teach or exercise authority over a man; and

Whereas, this prohibition is proclaimed as a result of the creation ordinance and not for the reason of first century cultural expediency; and

Whereas, no ecclesiastical body of the Presbyterian Church in America (be it congregation, session, presbytery, or assembly) may, under any assumed authority, set aside the clear teaching of God’s Word or the Westminster Standards; and

Whereas, a contrary view to both Scripture and the Standards is being taught and adopted, specifically, that session may allow the instruction of men by women in ecclesiastical assembly under the “authority and oversight” of the elders; Therefore the Presbytery of Grace, meeting this 12th day of September, 2000, overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America: 1. to affirm that a woman may not preach or teach in the corporate worship of any church or in the corporate worship conducted by any church court or by any committee, board, and/or agency of a church court; and 2. to reaffirm that the Presbyterian Church in America believes that the Scripture teaches that the offices of Elder and Deacon are open to men only. Adopted at the 98th Stated Meeting of Grace Presbytery on the 12th day of September, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Eugene C. Case, Stated Clerk 18. That Overture 27 from the Presbytery of Philadelphia (“Amend BCO 12-5e to Prohibit Women from Preaching”) be answered in the negative. Grounds: Our constitution addresses the issues sufficiently. Adopted
