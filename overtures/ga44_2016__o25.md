“Amend _BCO_ 15-1; 15-2; Add new 15-4 and Renumber Subsequent Paragraphs”

**Whereas**, a commission differs from an ordinary committee;

**Whereas** a commission can be an efficient way to conduct matters on behalf of a larger body;

**Whereas** Sessions may also benefit from the use of Commissions to conduct matters on behalf of the Session;

**Whereas** there is no provision in the _Book of Church Order_ for commissions appointed by a Session,

**Therefore be it resolved**, that _The Book of Church Order_ be amended by amending _BCO_ 15-1 and 15-2, adding a new _BCO_ 15-4, and renumbering _BCO_ 15-4 to 15-6 accordingly. Proposed additions are underlined. Paragraphs 15-4 through 15-6 would be renumbered to 15-5 to 15-7. [Note: there is no change to 15-3.] **15-1.** A commission differs from an ordinary committee in that while a committee is appointed to examine, consider and report, a commission is authorized to deliberate upon and conclude the business referred to it, except in the case of judicial commissions of a Presbytery appointed under _BCO_ 15-3 or of judicial commissions of a Session appointed under _BCO_ 15-4. A commission shall keep a full record of its proceedings, which shall be submitted to the court appointing it.
