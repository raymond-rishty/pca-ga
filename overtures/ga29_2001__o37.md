# GA29 O37 — Divide Potomac Presbytery and Form New Chesapeake Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 37 From Potomac Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 177](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p177)*

---

“Divide Potomac Presbytery and Form Chesapeake Presbytery.” Now Therefore, Be it Resolved, that Potomac Presbytery overtures the 29th General Assembly to form – effective January 1, 2002 – a new “Chesapeake” Presbytery by: 1. establishing the boundary of the continuing potomac presbytery to include: • the city of w ashington, D.C. • the maryland counties of Calvert, Charles, Frederick, Montgomery, Prince George’s, Saint Mary’s, and Washington. • the west virginia counties of Berkeley, Jefferson, and Morgan. • the virginia counties of Alexandria, Arlington, Clarke, Fairfax, Fauquier, Frederick, Prince William, and Loudoun, including the cities within their borders. 2. establishing the boundary of the new chesapeake presbytery to include: • the city of baltimore and the maryland counties of Baltimore, Carroll, Harford, Howard, Anne Arundel; and Kent Island in Queen A nne’s County. approved at the called meeting of potomac presbytery held on march 31, 2001. adopted at the stated meeting of potomac presbytery held on may 15, 2001. Attested by: Richard R. Larson, Stated Clerk 24. that overture 38 from the Westminster Presbytery be answered in the affirmative; and further, Defeated That the 29th General Assembly declare that the New Presbytery described in the Overture 38 be constituted as follows: 1. its geographical boundaries shall bethe same as those of the current Westminster Presbytery. 2. the organizing meeting of the new presbytery shall beheld on the date of the january stated meeting of the current westminster Presbytery, in the year of 2002. the stated clerk of the current presbytery shall notify all churches in the current presbytery of the time and location of that meeting. 3. the name of the new Presbytery, election of the committees, standing rules and all other operational procedures of the new presbytery shall be established by the presbytery at the January 2002 meeting, or at later more opportune times, as determined by the New Presbytery at its January 2002 meeting. 4. all those churches and teaching elders whoa re currently member of the westminster presbytery shall be enrolled as members of the new Presbytery, unless they have certified to the stated clerk of the current westminster presbytery that they wish to remain in westminster Presbytery. 5. The current Stated Clerk of Westminster Presbytery, TE Steve Meyerhoff, shall convene the January 2002 meeting of the New Presbytery, and preside until the election of a Moderator. The Committee adopted the following as a substitute motion: Proposed Response of the 29th General Assembly to Communication 5 and Overture 38 From Westminster Presbytery Dear Fathers and Brothers of Westminster Presbytery, Greetings in the Name of the Lord. We lament the occasion of this communication, but testify to our trust that the Lord of the Church is working in all things, even these unhappy circumstances, for His glory and the good of our branch of His Church. With respect to your letter May 16, 2001 (Communication 5), this Assembly raises no question concerning your ecclesiastical and civil right as a presbytery to withdraw from the Presbyterian Church in America. However, this Assembly would be profoundly remiss in its duty to "recommend measures for the promotion of charity, truth and holiness through all the churches under its care" (BCO 14-6.k.) were it to fail to challenge Westminster Presbytery's judgment in this action. We urge you to consider again your action, and reverse your judgment. As we understand your communication, you have, by majority vote, determined to withdraw from the Presbyterian Church in America. At the same time, you ask this Assembly to make provision for erecting a new presbytery composed of the Teaching Elders and Churches that do not wish to leave the PCA. Such a request might be above reproach, if the Teaching Elders and Churches in question were just those that voted against the action of presbytery to withdraw, and who were convinced in conscience that submission to the Presbytery in this matter was sin. However, we are reliably informed that in fact a portion of those voting with the majority in favor of withdrawing from the PCA, did so with the undisguised intention of joining the new PCA presbytery. We are further informed that another portion of those voting with the majority, actually intending to depart as a presbytery, and thus claim the name and traditions of Westminster Presbytery, knew of the true intention of the aforementioned portion, and acquiesced in the matter. This circumstance, fathers and brothers, raises serious questions about the integrity of this vote, questions which, as your brothers in the Lord, we press upon you with the utmost urgency. First, with respect to the portion of the majority voting to withdraw, who did so with no intention of in fact withdrawing—please consider the following. You have apparently voted on a proposal of great moment, a proposal crying out for a principled consideration, out of mere expediency. Surely this is unworthy of the solemn privilege and responsibility of voting. Further, you apparently voted knowing that the resultant continuing Westminster Presbytery, composed of those Teaching Elders and Churches that actually do intend to withdraw from the PCA, will proclaim to the world that she, unlike the proposed new PCA presbytery, is the true, faithful presbytery, sincerely committed to the Westminster standards. Your affirmative vote makes possible this circumstance, while at the same time you cannot possibly in conscience support this allegation—for in your intention to be restored to the PCA, you profess yourselves desirous of being members of a body sincerely committed to those same Standards. Finally, you are duty bound, by your vow to submit to the government of the church, to submit to *this* decision of Westminster Presbytery, and withdraw from the PCA.
