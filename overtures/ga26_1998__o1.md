# GA26 O1 — Amend BCO 13-5 to Clarify Presbytery Membership for Minister Without Call Who Needs to Relocate

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 1 From Potomac Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Approved & ratified (1999)  ·  **Ratified**  ·  **BCO:** 13-5

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 200](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p200)*

---

“Amend BCO 13-5 to Clarify Membership of One Honorably Retired” [Should be entitled “to clarify membership of a teaching elder without call Who Needs to Relocate”]

Whereas, the requirement of BCO 13-2, “A minister shall be required to hold his membership in the Presbytery within whose geographical bounds he resides...” appears in some cases to be in tension with the direction offered in BCO 13-5, “Ordinarily, only a minister who receives a call to a definite ecclesiastical work within the bound sofa particular presbytery may be received as a member of that Presbytery except in cases where the minister is already honorably retired, or in those cases deemed necessary by the Presbytery ”; Therefore, Potomac Presbytery overtures the Twenty-Sixth General Assembly to amend the Book of Church Order 13-5 by adding a new sentence after the words ‘review of the General Assembly.’, as follows: 'In such cases deemed necessary, which may include the case of a minister without call whose circumstances appear to require relocation within the bounds of that Presbytery, the time allotment of BCO 13-2 shall be counted from the day the minister was first continued on the roll without call in any Presbytery. ’ So that the amended paragraph reads, 13-5 ‘Ordinarily, only a minister who receives a call to a definite ecclesiastical work within the bounds of a particular Presbytery may be received as a member of that Presbytery except in cases where the minister is already honorably retired, or in those cases deemed necessary by the Presbytery, subject to the review of the General Assembly. In such cases deemed necessary, which may include the case of a minister without call whose circumstances appear to require relocation within the bounds of that Presbytery, the time allotment of BCO 13-2 shall be counted from the day the minister was first continued on the roll without call in any Presbytery’’ [Note: material in italics is the proposed new language in the BCO.]
