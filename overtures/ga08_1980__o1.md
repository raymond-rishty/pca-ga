# GA8 O1 — Add BCO 24-2 on ministers compelled to leave office

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Grace Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Approved → sent to presbyteries; ratification not located  ·  **BCO:** 24-2

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 38](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p38)*

---

> *Answered in the negative. § 8-88, III, 11; p. 118. (ga08_1980.md, line 2045) — confirmed: '11. That Overture 1 be answered in the negative. Adopted.' (line 6828); proposal never sent to presbyteries.*

(Clerk’s note: This overture was received too late for consideration by the Seventh General Assembly, and is thus carried over to this Assembly .) Whereas, troubles and difficulties may arise which make it impossible or undesirable that a minister continue to serve his congregation, even though these troubles and difficulties are not of such a nature that demand the institution of judicial process against the minister; and Whereas, the minister may feel compelled to tender the resignation of his pastoral charge, or the church shall desire to be relieved of its pastor, resulting in the dissolution of the pastoral relation; Therefore, Grace Presbytery overtures the Seventh General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to institute procedures to amend the Book of Church Order by the addition of anew paragraph 24-2: When a minister may feel compelled to tender the resignation of his pastoral charge or the church shall desire to be relieved of its pastor because of an unresolvable situation existing between him and his church, and the minister is not eligible for retirement nor worthy of discipline, the church shall provide for the support of the minister subsequent to the dissolution of the pastoral relation in such away and for such a time ai shall receive the approval of the Presbytery, for a period of not less than 90 days, unless he shall receive a Call to another work prior to the expiration of this period. Attest: Rev. Norman A. Bagby, Jr. Stated Clerk May II, 1979 Answered in the negative. § 8-88, III, 11; p. 118.
