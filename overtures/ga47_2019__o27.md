# GA47 O27 — Study Possibility of Remote Voting at General Assembly

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 27 from Westminster Presbytery (to AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 659](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p659)*

---

“Study Possibility of Remote Voting at General Assembly”

**Whereas**, the Presbyterian Church in America was founded as a “grass roots” denomination with power and authority flowing from the bottom up; and

**Whereas**, the expense of sending a commissioner to the General Assembly has become prohibitive for many small churches in the PCA; and

**Whereas**, many commissioners would attend the General Assembly, yet are unable because of health restrictions; and

**Whereas**, the General Assembly meetings now have a small representation by ruling elders; and

**Whereas**, it is in accordance with the founding principles of the PCA to have parity of the teaching and ruling elders in authority; and

**Whereas**, it is in accordance with the founding principles of the PCA and our _BCO_ to have parity of teaching and ruling elders on General Assembly committees ( _BCO_ 14-1; Principles for the Organization of the Assembly, item 10) and to have at least parity of teaching and ruling elders at General Assembly court meetings ( _BCO_ 14-2); and

**Whereas**, the technology exists to have commissioners vote on issues of the General Assembly from a distance while they watch the proceedings of the General Assembly online; and

**Whereas**, the General Assembly as presently structured would continue with the exception of a distance voting commissioner; **Therefore**, Westminster Presbytery overtures the 47 [th] General Assembly to instruct the PCA Administrative Committee to investigate and report at the 48 [th] general assembly a plan to create adv c (Distance Voting Commissioner) which would entail the ability of regularly elected commissioners to vote electronically at a distance from the site of the General Assembly. _Adopted by Westminster Presbytery at its stated meeting, March 9, 2019_ _Attested by /s/ TE Mark J. Blalack, stated clerk_
