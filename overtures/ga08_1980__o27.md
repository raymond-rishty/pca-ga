# GA8 O27 — Reaffirm the personhood of the unborn (abortion)

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of the Ascension

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 35](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p35)*

---

Whereas, the Sixth General Assembly was of one mind in recognizing that the unborn child is a person from the moment of conception; and

Whereas, such persons, being created in the image of God, are entitled to the full protection of the law of God in the Sixth Commandment; and

Whereas, the Westminster Larger Catechism. Question 136. rightly specifies the only Scriptural excep tions to the prohibition of taking life, namely capital punishment, lawful war, and necessary defense; and

Whereas, an unborn child is (a) incapable of committing a capital offense, (b) incapable of the aggression which legitimates killing in self-defense (unless [a] is denied), and (c) abortion is not a proper activity of lawful war, that is, it is senseless to declare war on a fetus or view it as a warring enemy; and

Whereas, the action of the Seventh General Assembly which erected a new, fourth exception to the Sixth Commandment (a) was not given on Scriptural grounds but rather reasons of emotion spd expediency and (b) implicitly denies the unborn child's status as a person created in God's image by denying him the God-given protection to all persons, thus contradicting the Scriptural foundation of the Ad-Interim Committee Report and thereby contradicting the basis of prohibiting abortion for other reasons as well; and

Whereas, the pretext for such a revision was that the assembly was divided, a position which if consistently followed would annul virtually all other assembly actions since they ordinarily carry some negative vote; and

Whereas, some who voted with the majority did not realize they were altering the General Assembly position but thought they were merely observing that the vote was divided; and

Whereas, the revision in no way strengthened our testimony to the obligation to use all legitimate means to preserve the life of the mother as well as the child; and

Whereas, the last sentence which was added to the Seventh General Assembly’s action contradicts what precedes it, since one cannot take "all possible efforts" to preserve the life of the child when abortion is permitted in some cases; and

Whereas, the blatant devaluing of the life of unborn children is so rampant in our society, pointing to the need for strict adherence to Scripture alone;

Therefore, be it resolved that the Presbytery of the Ascension overtures the Eighth General Assembly to affirm without equivocation the action of the Sixth General Assembly, namely, "That because Scripture clearly affirms the sanctity of human life and condemns its arbitrary destruction, we affirm that the intentional killing of an unborn child between conception and birth, for any reason at any time, is clearly a violation of the Sixth Commandment. Adopted the eighth day of January, 1980, at Akron, Oh Frank D. Moser, Stated Clerk Answered by reference to answer to Overture 12. § 8-69, III, 3; p. 98.
