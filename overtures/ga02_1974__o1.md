# GA2 O1 — Call Sessions to Prayer for Laborers and Appoint a Day of Prayer for Missions

**Assembly:** 2nd (1974)  ·  **Source:** Rev. William Rose  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga02_1974 p. 25](../markdown/ga02_1974.md#ga02-p25)*

---

FATHERS AND BRETHREN: To the General Assembly meeting in Macon, Ga. September 17, 1974

Whereas: One of the greatest needs facing the National Presbyterian Church is the need for laborers for the great white harvest field of over 3 billion people in the world: THE majority of whom do not know CHRIST, and multitudes have never heard HIS name:

Whereas: One of the greatest needs in the “National Presbyterian Church” is for laborers for the whitened harvest field.

Whereas: CHRIST said in Luke 10:2 THEREFORE SAID HE UNTO THEM. THE HARVEST TRULY IS GREAT. BUT THE LABOURERS ARE FEW: PRAY YE THEREFORE THE LORD OF THE HARVEST THAT HE WOULD SEND FORTH LABOURERS INTO HIS HARVEST. Clearly letting us know that the way for believers to secure the necessary laborers is to PRAY TO THE LORD OF THE HARVEST TO SEND FORTH LABOURERS INTO HIS HARVEST. Therefore: The General Assembly is overtured to: 1. Call to the attention of every session this great need of our fellowship. 2. Set aside a day, when the Sessions can bring Luke 10:2 before their people in any way that HE should lead them, the end result being that our people would begin to pray for Labourers, and GOD would call out labourers to work for CHRIST in the harvest field of the world. William H. Rose, Jr.
