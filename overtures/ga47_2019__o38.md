# GA47 O38 — Establish Study Committee on Domestic Violence & Sexual Assault

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 38 from Georgia Foothills Presbytery (to AC, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 691](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p691)*

---

“Establish a Study Committee on Domestic Violence and Sexual Assault” (Version 7)

**Whereas**, the sins of domestic abuse and sexual assault are expressly forbidden (2 Timothy 3:2-5; Jude 7; Exodus 21:15; Deuteronomy 22:2527; also _WLC_ 135 and 139); and

**Whereas**, these sins are especially heinous because they are “against the express letter of the law,” “break many commandments,” “admit of no reparation,” and often involve various other aggravations ( _WLC_ 151); and

**Whereas**, these sins are grievously prevalent in our society; [17] and

**Whereas**, these sins often produce in the victims a complex response of denial along with immense fear and shame that persist with varying severity throughout life (e.g., 2 Samuel 13:1-22); and

**Whereas**, it is the responsibility of the elders of the church to exercise their authority, severally and jointly, to shepherd those who are under their 17 For example, the National Domestic Violence Hotline reports: “More than 1 in 3 women (35.6%) and more than 1 in 4 men (28.5%) in the United States have experienced rape, physical violence and/or stalking by an intimate partner in their lifetime” (available at http://www.thehotline.org/resources/statistics/, accessed 6/15/18). For further sobering statistics on sexual violence see the National Center for Injury Prevention and Controls report entitled _Sexual Violence Surveillance: Uniform Definitions and Recommended Data_ _Elements_ (Atlanta: Center for Disease Control, 2014; available online at [http://www.cdc.gov/violenceprevention/pdf/sv_ surveillance_definitionsl-2009-a.pdf).](http://www.cdc.gov/violenceprevention/pdf/sv_%20surveillance_definitionsl-2009-a.pdf) care by guarding against such sinful behavior as much as possible, by wisely caring for those victimized by the sins of others, and by appropriately disciplining those guilty of such sins toward a full repentance; and

**Whereas,** these sins have been a concerning subject of attention not only in other denominations but even in the proceedings of the 46th General Assembly; and

**Whereas**, the 42nd General Assembly previously exhorted the PCA and its courts to respond boldly against one form of these sins, namely the sexual abuse of children; and

**Whereas**, no standardized resources for pastoral care on these sins and their complex consequences exist for officers, church courts, and agencies in the PCA; and

**Whereas**, no clear guidelines for policy or practice with regard to these sins exist for the officers, church courts, and agencies in the PCA that would protect individuals and congregations; and

**Whereas**, the Rules of Discipline make no consideration for the unique heinousness of these sins and their practical effect on victims;

**Therefore, be it resolved** that the Georgia Foothills Presbytery hereby overtures the 47th General Assembly of the PCA to form a committee to study: (1) the nature of domestic abuse and sexual assault; (2) the pastoral resources available for the care of survivors of these sins; (3) the best practices and policy guidelines for elders, church courts, and agencies for protecting against these sins and for responding to them; (4) the possibility of changes to the Rules of Discipline that would appropriately protect victims of these sins within a biblical process; and (5) the possible need for repentance as a church; and

**Be it further resolved** that the committee shall be appointed by the moderator, and shall include among its members or advisory members at least two subject-matter experts and at least two women; and

**Be it further resolved** that the budget for the study committee be set at $15,000/year and that funds be derived from gifts to the AC designated for that purpose, with the Georgia Foothills Presbytery committed to offer $1,000; and

**Be it further resolved** that the committee shall present their findings and recommendations for adoption by the 48th General Assembly. _Adopted by Georgia Foothills Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 9, 2019_ _Attested by /s/ TE Don Aldin, stated clerk_
