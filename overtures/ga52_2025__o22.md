“Amend _BCO_ 20-3, 24-3 and 25-1 to clarify "Regular Standing" re minimum voting age” **A.**

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 20-3, 24-3, and _BCO_ 25-1 be amended as shown below. Proposed additions are indicated by underlining. 20-3. when a congregation is convened for the election of a pastor ... All communing members in good and regular standing, but no others, are entitled to vote in the churches to which they are respectively attached. (cf. _BCO_ 25-1) 24-3. All communing members in good and regular standing, but no others, are entitled to vote in the election of church officers in the churches to which they respectively belong. A majority vote of those present is required for election. (cf. _BCO_ 25-1) 25-1. The congregation consists of all the communing members of a particular church, and they only are entitled to vote. A congregation may establish a rule setting a minimum voting age (regular standing), but it must be adopted by a two-thirds (2/3) majority at a congregational meeting that is called with at least 30 days' notice. (cf. _BCO_ 20-3, 24-3) **B.** **Be it also resolved** that if GA adopts this, or an amended version, the Stated Clerk will make this full Rationale available when the Amendment Ballot is sent to Presbyteries. **Rationale** - The need for this Overture arises from an SJC Decision in March 2024, where, by a vote of 15-5, the SJC ruled that the _BCO_ does not permit a congregation to set a minimum voting age. [7] This Overture seeks to permit such. It argues that such has historically been common in American Presbyterianism. It's part of what was, and is, meant by "regular" standing. The _Psiaki_ Decision did not define what the word "regular" means in the phrase "good and regular standing" and this Overture seeks to treat it as it has historically been understood. When the PCA was formed in 1973 it adopted the PCUS _BCO_ of 1925. Minor communicants did not vote in 1925, as they were not regarded as being in "regular" standing. Our _BCO_ 25-1 and 24-3 remain unchanged from 1925. The sentence above in _BCO_ 20-3 remains unchanged from the PCUS _BCO_ of 1879. [ 8] This Overture’s rationale provides many and varied arguments for why the PCA should allow such freedom, which is generally described below in Robert's Rules.
