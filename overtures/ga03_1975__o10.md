# GA3 O10 — Vest Foreign Mission Oversight in Presbyteries Rather Than Assembly Committees

**Assembly:** 3rd (1975)  ·  **Source:** New River Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga03_1975 p. 37](../markdown/ga03_1975.md#ga03-p37)*

---

New River Presbytery (by unanimous vote) respectfully overtures the Third General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America as follows: 1. That whereas evangelism in our native land is a very vital part of the “Great Commission” as well as evangelism throughout all the world, 2. And whereas preaching and personal evangelism need to be stimulated among our people, 3. And whereas this matter is of sufficient importance to merit a special committee to give thought, planning and inspiration to this matter, as well as earnest prayer. New River Presbytery (by unanimous vote) overtures the General Assembly to instruct the Committee on Mission to the U. S. that they appint a special subcommittee to promote this aspect of evangelism in obedience to the “Great Commission”; And, further, 1. Whereas fulltime evangelists are certainly in order in the Church today to lend encouragement, assistance and inspiration to congregations and pastors, 2. And whereas the office of evangelist is definitely indicated in the Scripture, 3.
