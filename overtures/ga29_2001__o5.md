# GA29 O5 — Divide Heartland Presbytery to Create Iowa Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 5 from Heartland Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 167](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p167)*

---

“Divide Heartland Presbytery” Whereas, the geographical boundaries of heartland presbytery are among the largest in the PCA; and, Whereas, the churches within the boundaries of iowan ow number 6 organized churches numbering 813 members; two mission; and one possible church transferring from Independency; and, Whereas, stewardship of the local churches relates to time, talent and tithes; and, with the current distances, it is difficult to m aintain close fellowship due to the travel time required; and, Whereas, requests from other Presbyteries with fewer churches and members have been approved (recognizing the same difficulties as Heartland); THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that Heartland Presbytery be divided so that the State of Iowa would become a separate Presbytery of the PCA effective January 1, 2002. Adopted at the 36th Stated Meeting of Heartland Presbytery, on November 11, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Lawrence N. Lunceford, Stated Clerk 10. That Overture 12 from Central Carolina Presbytery requesting to remove the eleven county area known as the “Piedmont Triad” of North Carolina from the Central Carolina Presbytery to form a new, Piedmont Triad Presbytery, be answered in the affirmative. Adopted
