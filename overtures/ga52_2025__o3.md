# GA52 O3 — Erect Ad Interim Committee on Christian Nationalism

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 3 from Arizona Presbytery (to OC, AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1169](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1169)*

---

“Erect Ad Interim Committee on Christian Nationalism”

**Whereas**, the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) has adopted the American Revision (1788) to the Westminster Confession of Faith, which represents a substantial change from the Historic Text (1646) as it relates to the relationship between the church and the civil magistrate; and

**Whereas**, there has been recent teaching, among those who subscribe to or have adopted certain aspects of the Reformed Tradition, as to the nature of this relationship between the church and the civil magistrate, which have caused confusion, division, and dissension even among the congregants of PCA churches and affected PCA pastors and officers; and

**Whereas**, these teachings are from a particular type of Christian Nationalism which professes conformity to the Reformed Tradition; and

**Whereas**, church courts have the responsibility to determine questions of doctrine seriously, and in general to maintain truth and righteousness, condemning erroneous opinions which tend to bring injury to the peace, purity, or progress of the Church ( _BCO_ 11-4);

**Therefore, be it resolved** that the 52 [nd] PCA General Assembly erect and convene an Ad Interim Study Committee on Christian Nationalism ( _RAO_ 9-1), to study the relationship between Christian Nationalism, Theonomic Reconstructionism, and similar viewpoints deemed necessary by the committee. Further, the committee shall advise on whether these viewpoints and formulations are in conformity with the system of doctrine taught in the Westminster Standards or where they may diverge from the system of doctrine. Further, the committee shall write a report that gives pastoral guidance when addressing congregations, new members, and future officers of the PCA.

**Be it further resolved** that the budget for the study committee be set at $10,000/year and that funds be derived from gifts to the AC designated for that purpose, with General Assembly appealing to the various Presbyteries to fund this initiative via such gifts ( _RAO_ 9-3).

**Be it further resolved** that the Arizona Presbytery is committed to offer a $2,500 gift to assist in funding the work of this Ad Interim Study Committee. _Adopted by the Arizona Presbytery at its stated meeting, September 6, 2024._ _Attested by /s/ RE Richard Wolfe, stated clerk._
