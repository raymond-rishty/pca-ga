# GA30 O18 — Amend BCO 33-1 & 34-1 to Require Greater Consensus for Original Jurisdiction

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 18 from Potomac Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference  ·  **BCO:** 33-1

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 217](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p217)*

---

“Amend Both BCO 33-1 and 34-1 to Require Greater Consensus to Assume Original Jurisdiction Over Members and Ministers”

Whereas the history of the Presbyterian Church in America, with respect to the former denominational connection of many of its officers and congregations, indicates the great importance of the voice of a minority in attempts to preserve the faithfulness of a denomination; whereas the present wording of the Book of Church Order wisely makes provision for the correction of one court through the efforts of others of like grade, through an appeal to a more inclusive court;

Whereas the grassroots history of the Presbyterian Church in America indicates her desire to act in unity with the consensus of her churches; whereas the present wording of Book of Church Order of 33- 1 allows two Sessions to require a Presbytery to assume original jurisdiction injudicial process against a member in another congregation, and BCO 34-1 allows two presbyteries to require the General Assembly to assume original jurisdiction in judicial process against a minister in another Presbytery;

Whereas, as the Presbyterian Church in America grows, this standard, expressed in a fixed number, will allow an increasingly smaller representation of the denomination to challenge the ordinary rights of a Session or Presbytery to fulfill its Constitutional duties;

Whereas the ordinary rights of a court to fulfill its constitutional responsibilities should not be liable to challenge unless there is a significant proportion in the church that is persuaded that a court has failed to act properly; and

Whereas the parallel provisions (BCO 33-1 and BCO 34-1) have slightly different language, which may lead to confusion, and any change introduced in the standard of one section should reasonably be introduced in the other on similar grounds; Therefore, to ensure that a disproportionate representation of churches or presbyteries does not exercise undue influence over other churches, presbyteries and the General Assembly in pursuing discipline; and, to ensure that there is a significant indication of the need for a higher court to assume original jurisdiction in such a case for the sake of the purity and testimony of the church, Potomac Presbytery overtures the 30th General Assembly to amend The Book of Church Order as follows: 1. In BCO 33-1, strike the words “two other” and add in that place “at least five percent, but not less than two, of all of the” so that the section reads: and at least five percent, but not less than two, of all of the Sessions of churches in the same Presbytery request the Presbytery of which the church is a member to initiate proper or appropriate action in a case of process and thus assume jurisdiction and authority, the Presbytery shall do so. and 2. In BCO 34-1, strike the words, “and two other Presbyteries request the General Assembly to assume original jurisdiction (to first receive and initially hear and determine), the General Assembly shall do so.” and add in that place: and at least five percent of all of the Presbyteries request the General Assembly to initiate proper or appropriate action in a case of process and thus assume jurisdiction and authority, the General Assembly shall do so.
