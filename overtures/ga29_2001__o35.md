# GA29 O35 — Remove Concurring Opinions from SJC Report in Judicial Matter 99-1

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 35 From Western Carolina Presbytery (to B&amp;O)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 245](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p245)*

---

"Remove Concurring Opinion in Judicial Matter 99-1"

Whereas, the Western Carolina Presbytery has issued a call to the Presbyterian Church in America to bring the John Wood matter to closure and to endeavor to pursue together both the purity, peace and unity of the church; and

Whereas, we question the propriety of the Standing Judicial Commission attaching concurring opinions to a report that they were not going to proceed to judicial process, since concurring opinions are provided for only in cases of judicial process and to include these concurring opinions tend to disturb the peace of the Church, after the decision has been reached; and

Whereas, to allow the personal allegations against individual members of the Assembly to be placed in the Minutes of the General Assembly can only bring hurt to the individuals concerned, and cause resentments that are destructive of the purity, peace and unity of the Church; and

Whereas, this is not the proper venue for such allegations, since TE's who have acted improperly should be charged in the courts of original jurisdiction, where they can have a fair trial; and

Whereas, we do not believe that the provision for concurring opinions was ever intended to provide a forum for personal attack as occurs in this report; Therefore, Western Carolina Presbytery urges that all of the concurring opinions be removed from the SJC report, and that they not appear in the Minutes of the General Assembly. Adopted by Western Carolina Presbytery at a called meeting on May 8, 2001. Attested by: /s/ Skip Gillikin, Stated Clerk 23. That Personal Resolution #1 from TE H. Timothy Miessler be answered in the negative. Grounds: The phrase “under any circumstance” in lines 9 of the resolution prohibits women from teaching other women and children. (Titus 2:2-4; 2 Timothy 1:5 with 2 Timothy 3:15) Adopted

Whereas, Scripture teaches that a woman is not to teach or exercise authority over a man, I Timothy 2:12 “But I do not allow a woman to teach or exercise authority over a man, but to remain quiet,”; and

Whereas, Scripture states why this prohibition on women is true in 1 Timothy 2:13-15, viz., (1) Adam was created prior to Eve; (2) Adam was not deceived but Eve was; and (3) women have the management of the home and the rearing of the children as their assigned roles (Titus 2:3-5; 1 Timothy 5); and

Whereas, it is an impossibility for a woman to teach “non-authoritatively” while remaining quiet; and

Whereas, Scripture is authoritative in its very nature; and

Whereas, a woman that expounds, exhorts, preaches, applies, lectures on, or gives an address from or based in part on Scripture, with men present, is using Scripture authoritatively over a man; and

Whereas, to allow a woman to do this violates Scripture; and

Whereas, the PCA is a confessional church and upholds the inerrant, infallible Word of God, the Old and New Testaments, as our “only rule of faith and obedience,” WLC Q. 3; and

Whereas, we want to respect positions taken by previous General Assemblies; and

Whereas, the 28th General Assembly (2000) declared that “godly men and women [may] give presentations even though they are not elders, but who in God’s providence may have insights and experience which may prove helpful and instructive to those who lead in ministry” (M28GA, pp. 101-02); and

Whereas, we do not want anyone to think that the Presbyterian Church in America has taken a position that would allow women, in the presentation of their insights and experience, to expound, exhort, preach, apply, lecture on, or give an address from or based in part on Scripture, particularly to a mixed audience; and

Whereas, recent events may have given that impression; and

Whereas, it is important that we be as clear as possible in our positions; and

Whereas, in the past, General Assemblies have clarified positions taken by previous Assemblies (cf. M8GA, p. 97); Therefore Be It Resolved, that the 29th General Assembly clarify the position taken by the 28th General Assembly by declaring that Scripture prohibits a woman, under any circumstance, from publicly expounding, exhorting, applying, or preaching the Word of God. 24. That Personal Resolution #3 from TE Frank J. Smith be answered in the negative. Grounds: We believe the preferred procedure for submitting a change to the BCO is outlined in RAO 10, and we see no compelling reason to deviate from that procedure in this case. Adopted

Whereas, Central Carolina has wisely proposed amending BCO 43-1 with regard to the relationship between a complaint and an appeal; and

Whereas, it would seem prudent to handle other concerns regarding the relationship between a complaint and an appeal at the same time; Now Therefore Be It Resolved, that the General Assembly authorizes its Bills and Overtures Committee to consider other perfecting language to BCO 43-1 dealing with the relationship between a complaint and an appeal. commissioners Present: Presbytery commissioner Ascension TE Carl W.
