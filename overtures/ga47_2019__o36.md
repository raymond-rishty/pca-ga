# GA47 O36 — Amend BCO 40-4 & 40-5 to Clarify Meaning of 'Credible Report'

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 36 from Savannah River Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Approved → sent to presbyteries; ratification not located  ·  **BCO:** 40-4

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 686](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p686)*

---

> *That Overture 36 from Savannah River Presbytery ('Amend BCO 40-4 and 40-5 To Clarify the Meaning of Credible Report') be answered in the negative. Adopted. (ga47_2019 vote tally: Negative 112-10-6)*

“Amend _BCO_ 40-4 and 40-5 To Clarify the Meaning of ‘Credible Report’”

**Whereas,** there has been various interpretations regarding the meaning of “credible report” in _BCO_ 40-5; and

**Whereas**, these various interpretations have contended either that (1) a credible report may arise from any credible source presented to the court having appellate jurisdiction with respect to the court next below; or (2) a credible report may arise only upon referral of a matter from the General Assembly according to _RAO_ 16-10.c; and

**Whereas**, _BCO_ 40-4 allows that: “Courts may sometimes entirely neglect to perform their duty, by which neglect heretical opinions or corrupt practices may be allowed to gain ground; or offenders of a very gross character may be suffered to escape; or some circumstances in their proceedings of very great irregularity may not be distinctly recorded by them.” and

**Whereas**, _BCO_ 40-4 also states that: “If, therefore, the next higher court be well advised that any such neglect or irregularity has occurred on the part of the lower court, it is incumbent on it to take cognizance of the same, and to examine, deliberate and judge in the whole matter as completely as if it had been recorded, and thus brought up by review of its records.” and

**Whereas**, consequently when a court next below neglects to perform its duty under the Constitution; and further, fails to record its actions distinctly, the remedy is for the court with appellate jurisdiction to “be well advised that any such neglect or irregularity has occurred;” and

**Whereas**, since it is not clearly defined by what means the appellate court may “be well advised that any such neglect or irregularity has occurred;” and

**Whereas**, when the lower court fails to distinctly record its actions as required, and further does not inform members who may be affected and harmed by these actions with notice in a timely manner, it results in members losing their right to file an Appeal ( _BCO_ 42) or a Complaint ( _BCO_ 43) in a timely manner; and

**Whereas**, courts can fail in the proper exercise of their responsibility to distinctly record their actions in such a manner that it is not discerned or discovered by the higher court during the process of the regular review of the lower court’s records, the higher court during its review of records is not able to determine or judge the validity of the record; and

**Whereas**, _BCO_ 40-4 and _BCO_ 40-5 grant the higher court the right to review the record again once the higher court has received credible reports of “any important delinquency or grossly unconstitutional proceedings of such court;” and these credible reports can come from any credible source, which then requires the higher court to review “any important delinquency or grossly unconstitutional proceedings of such court.” **Therefore**, to clarify the meaning of “credible reports” this overture recommends that _BCO_ 40-4 and _BCO_ 40-5 be amended with the following (additional wording underlined): 40-4. Courts may sometimes entirely neglect to perform their duty, by which neglect heretical opinions or corrupt practices may be allowed to gain ground; or offenders of a very gross character may be suffered to escape; or some circumstances in their proceedings of very great irregularity may not be distinctly recorded by them. In any of these cases their records will by no means exhibit to the higher court a full view of their proceedings. If, therefore, the next higher court be well advised, through a credible report as defined in _BCO_ 40-5, that any such neglect or irregularity has occurred on the part of the lower court, it is incumbent on it to take cognizance of the same, and to examine, deliberate and judge in the whole matter as completely as if it had been recorded, and thus brought up by review of its records. 40-5. When any court having appellate jurisdiction shall receive a credible report, with respect to the court next below of any important delinquency or grossly unconstitutional proceedings of such court, the first step shall be to cite the court alleged to have offended to appear before the court having appellate jurisdiction, or its commission, by representative or in writing, at a specified time and place, and to show what the lower court has done or failed to do in the case in question. A credible report may arise either from a review of the records of the lower court as required under _BCO_ 40-1; or from other credible means that make known to the court having appellate jurisdiction of the court next below of any important delinquency or grossly unconstitutional proceedings; and/or where the right of members to file a timely Appeal ( _BCO_ 42) or Complaint ( _BCO_ 43) was abridged by a lower court’s failure to conduct proper proceedings or to provide timely notices. When a court acts on any credible report, the parties shall be the lower court and the court having appellate jurisdiction. The court thus issuing the citation may reverse or redress the proceedings of the court below in other than judicial cases; or it may censure the delinquent court; or it may remit the whole matter to the delinquent court with an injunction to take it up and dispose of it in a constitutional manner; or it may stay all further proceedings in the case; as circumstances may require. As amended the provision would read: 40-4. Courts may sometimes entirely neglect to perform their duty, by which neglect heretical opinions or corrupt practices may be allowed to gain ground; or offenders of a very gross character may be suffered to escape; or some circumstances in their proceedings of very great irregularity may not be distinctly recorded by them. In any of these cases their records will by no means exhibit to the higher court a full view of their proceedings. If, therefore, the next higher court be well advised, through a credible report as defined in _BCO_ 40-5, that any such neglect or irregularity has occurred on the part of the lower court, it is incumbent on it to take cognizance of the same, and to examine, deliberate and judge in the whole matter as completely as if it had been recorded, and thus brought up by review of its records. 40-5. When any court having appellate jurisdiction shall receive a credible report, with respect to the court next below of any important delinquency or grossly unconstitutional proceedings of such court, the first step shall be to cite the court alleged to have offended to appear before the court having appellate jurisdiction, or its commission, by representative or in writing, at a specified time and place, and to show what the lower court has done or failed to do in the case in question. A credible report may arise either from a review of the records of the lower court as required under _BCO_ 40-1; or from other credible means that make known to the court having appellate jurisdiction of the court next below of any important delinquency or grossly unconstitutional proceedings; and/or where the right of members to file a timely Appeal ( _BCO_ 42) or Complaint ( _BCO_ 43) was abridged by a lower court’s failure to conduct proper proceedings or to provide timely notices. In these cases, the parties shall be the lower court and the court having appellate jurisdiction. The court thus issuing the citation may reverse or redress the proceedings of the court below in other than judicial cases; or it may censure the delinquent court; or it may remit the whole matter to the delinquent court with an injunction to take it up and dispose of it in a constitutional manner; or it may stay all further proceedings in the case; as circumstances may require. _Adopted by Savannah River Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 16, 2019_ _Attested by /s/ RE William Hatcher, stated clerk_
