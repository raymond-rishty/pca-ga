# GA30 O22 — Form Blue Ridge Presbytery from Division of New River Presbytery

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 22 from New River Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 181](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p181)*

---

“Form Blue Ridge Presbytery” Whereas the churches of New River Presbytery located within the Commonwealth of Virginia have for some time prayed about, considered, and most recently held planning meetings (including several churches in James River and Potomac Presbyteries) concerning the establishment of a new Presbytery in western Virginia, and Whereas the demographic, sociological and economic connections of churches throughout western Virginia - principally along and to the west of the Blue Ridge Mountains - make working together as a Presbytery highly desirable, and Whereas the churches currently located in western Virginia have discovered that they are of one mind in their desire to do church planting in this region, as well as develop an enlarged work of Reformed University Ministries among the many colleges and universities in this region (in addition to the other normal functions of a Presbytery), and, Whereas, while the remaining churches located in West Virginia fall just short of the minimum membership and attendance goals, they have a special cohesiveness that their leadership believes will enable them to overcome their somewhat small size, Therefore, New River Presbytery, meeting at Nitro, West Virginia in its stated meeting of March 9th, 2002, hereby overtures the 30th General Assembly that, effective September 1, 2002 a new Presbytery of the Blue Ridge be formed by the division of New River Presbytery (along with churches from James River Presbytery and Potomac Presbytery - see separate overtures) as follows: a.
