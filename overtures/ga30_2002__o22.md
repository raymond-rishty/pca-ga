# GA30 O22 — Form Blue Ridge Presbytery from Division of New River Presbytery

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 22 from New River Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 181](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p181)*

---

“Form Blue Ridge Presbytery”

Whereas the churches of New River Presbytery located within the Commonwealth of Virginia have for some time prayed about, considered, and most recently held planning meetings (including several churches in James River and Potomac Presbyteries) concerning the establishment of a new Presbytery in western Virginia, and

Whereas the demographic, sociological and economic connections of churches throughout western Virginia - principally along and to the west of the Blue Ridge Mountains - make working together as a Presbytery highly desirable, and

Whereas the churches currently located in western Virginia have discovered that they are of one mind in their desire to do church planting in this region, as well as develop an enlarged work of Reformed University Ministries among the many colleges and universities in this region (in addition to the other normal functions of a Presbytery), and,

Whereas, while the remaining churches located in West Virginia fall just short of the minimum membership and attendance goals, they have a special cohesiveness that their leadership believes will enable them to overcome their somewhat small size, Therefore, New River Presbytery, meeting at Nitro, West Virginia in its stated meeting of March 9th, 2002, hereby overtures the 30th General Assembly that, effective September 1, 2002 a new Presbytery of the Blue Ridge be formed by the division of New River Presbytery (along with churches from James River Presbytery and Potomac Presbytery - see separate overtures) as follows: a. New Boundaries of New River Presbytery: “All of West Virginia except for the counties of Morgan, Berkeley and Jefferson.” b. Boundaries of the newly formed Blue Ridge Presbytery: “All churches and missions in Virginia, bounded on the north and east and including the counties (and contained independent cities) of Frederick, Clarke, Fauquier, Culpeper, Orange, Louisa, Fluvanna, Albemarle, Nelson, Appomattox, Campbell, and Pittsylvania and bounded on the west and including the counties of Giles, Pulaski, and Carroll (east of 1-77). c. Revised boundaries of James River Presbytery will be as follows: “All of Virginia south and east of and including the following counties, except for the counties of Accomac and Northampton on the Eastern Shore: Northumberland, Westmoreland, King George, Stafford, Spotsylvania, Hanover, Goochland, Cumberland, Buckingham, Prince Edward, Charlotte, and Halifax.” d. Revised Virginia boundaries of Potomac Presbytery will be as follows: “The Virginia counties of Arlington, Fairfax, Prince William, and Loudoun, including the cities within their borders.” 9. That Overture 8 from James River Presbytery be answered by referring to Recommendation 8. Adopted
