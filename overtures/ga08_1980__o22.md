# GA8 O22 — Affirm sessions' role in nominating officers (BCO 25-1)

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Southern Florida  ·  **Disposition:** Approved → sent to presbyteries; ratification not located  ·  **BCO:** 25-1

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 41](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p41)*

---

Whereas, the BCO 25-1 calls for the congregations to responsibly submit names to the Church Sessions of those who are "active male members who qualify . . . etc" and Whereas, the BCO no where defines what it means by "active" and Whereas, every male member of congregations of the Presbyterian Church in America, while assumed to be active because there exists no "inactive list" and yet every church session knows some men to be so infrequent in the worship and work of the church as to disqualify them from the sacred offices and Whereas, regular faithful attendance is both a commando f the lord of the church and necessary fora good example to the church, and Whereas, different congregations offer varying degrees of activity within the various church programs, Now Therefore Be It Resolved, by the Presbytery of Southern Florida: ' 'That the Presbytery of Southern Florida believes it to be the responsibility of the local Church Sessions to determine whether names submitted to it of prospective church officers are indeed "active male members who meet the qualifications of the Word. " And that local sessions should consistently apply their interpretation of what it means to be "active" from year to year or so inform the congregations of any changes. Furthermore that men should not be nominated with the hopes that the high offices of Elder or Deacons will make them more faithful or interested in the Church but because they have shown unfailing interest in the worship and work by their past diligence. " (See minutes of G.A. 1979 Page 80:3) Attest Earl Mizell Stated Clerk Amended and
