That **Overture 27** from Northwest Georgia Presbytery, “Erect Ad Interim Committee to Receive Suggestions for Permanent Committee and Agency Operations, Resources, and Expenses,” be answered in the **NEGATIVE** . Grounds: Pursuant to _RAO_ 9-1 ad interim committees may be erected solely to “handle particular matters of business as designated by the Assembly.” The ad interim committee proposed in the overture only provides generalized directives, not specific deliverables or outcomes, and does not speak to “particular matters of business.” The proposed committee would be instructed to solicit and receive recommendations with no criteria for the source of those recommendations, how they will be solicited, evaluated, or reported. It is also already the case that any presbytery, session, officer of the church, or congregant who has suggestions for a Permanent Committee or Agency is already welcomed to send suggestions to said Committee or Board. Further, per _RAO_ 4-11, the budget of MNA is reviewed and approved by the Permanent Committee and the Administrative Committee, whose members have been elected by the General Assembly. That budget is then reviewed by the MNA Committee of Commissioners, along with the annual audit (per _RAO_ 14-7) before being presented for approval by the General Assembly. The constitutional checks and balances are already in place, and the proposed ad interim committee would add inefficiency and confusion. 10. That **Overture 28** from Northwest Georgia Presbytery, “Demand Mission to North America (MNA) to Apologize and Terminate Personnel,” be answered in the **NEGATIVE** . Grounds: 1. We unanimously reaffirm the public apology of our Coordinator Irwyn Ince from Feb 12, 2025 which said: Mission to North America (MNA) serves the PCA for the advancement of Christ’s kingdom through our churches in the U.S. and Canada. We do this through a two-pronged focus on church planting and church vitality. Our church planting efforts facilitate starting and supporting new PCA churches. Our church vitality efforts provide ministry resources that enable our churches to grow in their outward facing missional engagement in their communities, loving neighbors as our Lord commands (Matt 22:39). One such long-standing MNA ministry is our Refugee and Immigrant Ministry (RIM). Recently, we posted links on our webpage to external organizations offering a wide range of recommendations and frequently asked questions on immigration. MNA’s leadership erroneously allowed the posting of content that advised undocumented persons on ways to avoid being detained by authorities. We affirm that it is our Christian duty to obey the lawful commands of the civil magistrate and be subject to their authority (WCF 23.4; Romans 13:14; 1 Peter 2:13-14). to counsel otherwise is a sin. We confess that we fell short of our Biblical and Confessional standard. We repent and apologize. We also apologize for causing confusion and consternation in our church. We have removed all the previously posted information from our website. MNA’s Refugee & Immigrant Ministry, along with other MNA ministries, seeks to serve our churches with resources that enable them to faithfully follow our Lord’s call to love the stranger. However, MNA does not engage in providing legal advice, political campaigning or partisan positions. The information we post going forward will provide ecclesiastical resources in line with our Confession for guidance on the matter. 2. Toward the end of online postings consistent with our standards, an internet conduct policy was unanimously adopted by the MNA Permanent Committee at the 2025 MNA spring meeting. This new policy will be reviewed for ratification by the 52 [nd] General Assembly in June 2025. 3. With a new internet conduct policy and expectations for MNA staff coming, the MNA Permanent Committee still desires to express our deep appreciation for MNA leadership, MNA staff and ministry leaders, and their commitment to kingdom and mercy ministries on behalf of the Presbyterian Church in America. 11.
