“Amend _BCO_ 24-1 To Require REs and Deacons to State Confessional Differences”

**Whereas,** the Presbyterian Church in America is a confessional denomination, as defined by its Constitution ( _BCO_, Preface, III) and the vows that its Teaching Elders, Ruling Elders, and Deacons are required to take concerning that Constitution ( _BCO_ 21-5, Q.2; 24-6, Q.2); and,

**Whereas,** the PCA clarified this confessional position to be “good faith subscription” at its 30 [th] General Assembly (2002) [6] by adding to the examination of Teaching Elder candidates what is now _BCO_ 21-4.f,g, which includes: “While our Constitution does not require the candidate’s affirmation of every statement and/or proposition of doctrine in our _Confession of Faith_ and _Catechisms_ ” and that the Presbytery “shall require the candidate to state the specific instances in which he may differ with the _Confession of Faith_ and _Catechisms_ in any of their statements and/or propositions.”; and,

**Whereas,** _BCO_ 24-1 is the parallel description of required examinations for Ruling Elder and Deacon nominees, but does not contain the clarifications concerning good faith subscription of _BCO_ 21-4.f,g; and,

**Whereas,** the vow pertaining to the Confession and Catechisms that Ruling Elders and Deacons take is identical to the vow for Teaching Elders ( _BCO_ 21-5, Q.2; 24-6, Q.2), suggesting that their relationship to the _Confession_ and _Catechisms_ is the same as that of Teaching Elders;
