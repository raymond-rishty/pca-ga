# GA28 O24 — Amend BCO to Address Improper Dissemination of Judicial Case Information

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 24 From Southeast Alabama Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 296](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p296)*

---

“Improper Dissemination of Information Regarding Judicial Cases and Information Detrimental to Reputations”

Whereas, the Lord, through the Apostle Paul, urges believers to live “with all humility and gentleness, with patience, showing forbearance to one another in love, being diligent to preserve the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace” (Ephesians 4:2-3 – NASB); and

Whereas, the authors of the Westminster Larger Catechism explain that the Ninth Commandment requires of us “the preserving and promoting of truth between man and man, and the good name of our neighbor, as well as our own;…a charitable esteem of our neighbors; loving, desiring, and rejoicing in their good name; sorrowing for, and covering of their infirmities;…a ready receiving of a good report, and unwillingness to admit of an evil report, concerning them;” (WLC 144); and

Whereas, the Book of Church Order (PCA) in 27-1 says, “Discipline is the exercise of authority given the Church by the Lord Jesus to instruct and guide its members and to promote it’s purity and welfare”; and

Whereas, the Book of Church Order (PCA) 27-5 says, “Scripture law is the basis of all discipline because it is the revelation of God’s Holy will. Proper disciplinary principles are set forth in the Scriptures and must be followed. They are: - **a.** Instruction in the Word; - **b.** Individual’s responsibility to admonish one another; - **c.** If the admonition is rejected, then the calling of one or more witnesses; - **d.** If rejection persists, then the Church must act through her court unto admonition, suspension, excommunication and deposition. Steps (a) through (d) must be followed in proper order for the exercise of discipline”; and

Whereas, the Presbyterian and Reformed News which is published primarily by members of the PCA has printed articles about judicial cases which are in process and therefore it circumvents the constitutionally ordained church courts, and may prejudice the court by the information it disseminates; Therefore Be it Resolved, that Southeast Alabama Presbytery overture the General Assembly to instruct all members of the Presbyterian Church in America not to publish information about a judicial case which is in process; and Be it Further Resolved, that General Assembly instruct all members of the Presbyterian Church in America to follow the procedures set down in the Book of Church Order when there is a grievance and to refrain from improperly disseminating information detrimental to the reputation of others, and Be it Finally Resolved, that the General Assembly instruct its Stated Clerk to include in his next pastoral letter to all churches in the PCA a copy of this overture. adopted unanimously by the presbytery of southeast Alabama, April 28, 2000, at Millbrook, Alabama. Attested by: Is/ Henry Lewis Smith, Stated Clerk 15. That OVERTURE 5 from Louisiana Presbytery (“Amend BCO 38-4 by Deletion”) be answered in the negative. Adopted Grounds: The third and fourth “Whereas” clauses misconstrue the proper interpretation of the provision in question. Rightly construed, no remedy is needed. It cannot be “reasonably inferred that administrative removal from the membership roll may NOT occur under this provision if the member were regularly worshipping and participating in another church other than the one in which he took his membership vows” because the sentence is qualified by the opening sentence: “When a member of a particular church has willfully neglected the church fora period of one year, or has made it known that he has no intention of fulfilling the church vows. . . Clearly mere participation at another church does not exempt one from consideration under this standard. The provision is further qualified in the second paragraph by the phrase “will not fulfill his membership obligations” (emphasis added) which obligations are summarized in the parenthetical in the first paragraph, i.e., “(BCO 57-5, nos. 3- 5)” . Again, clearly mere participation in another church does not satisfy this standard. On the other hand, the sentence in question (“in this or any other branch of the visible Church”) is important to keep in the provision because it makes clear that if a person is willing to fulfill the vows of membership (not just attend or participate) in another church, the Session should not remove the person from the roll in an act of “pastoral discipline”, but dismiss the person to that branch of the visible Church.
