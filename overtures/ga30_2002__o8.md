# GA30 O8 — Revise Boundaries of James River Presbytery

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 8 from James River Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 183](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p183)*

---

“Revise Boundaries of James River Presbytery”

Whereas, having been appraised by formal overture of the desire of several of the churches in our Presbytery located in the western area of Virginia desiring to unite with the newly forming Presbytery of the Blue Ridge, and

Whereas, having reviewed the proposed overture from New River Presbytery asking the General Assembly to establish this new Presbytery, and

Whereas, having reviewed the proposed new boundaries of James River Presbytery contained in this proposed overture from New River Presbytery,

Therefore be it resolved that James River Presbytery, voting at Hopewell, Virginia in the One Hundred and Fifth Stated Meeting of January 19, 2002 does hereby approve of the following change to our boundaries effective September 1, 2002: Revised boundaries of James River Presbytery will be as follows: “All of Virginia south and east of and including the following counties, except for the counties of Accomac and Northampton on the Eastern Shore: Northumberland, Westmoreland, King George, Stafford, Spotsylvania, Hanover, Goochland, Cumberland, Buckingham, Prince Edward, Charlotte, and Halifax.” 10. That the General Assembly re-elect James C. Pakala and TE Douglas E. Lee to another term on the Presbyterian and Reformed Joint Commission on Chaplains and Military Personnel (PRJC) as members of the Class of 2006. Adopted 11. The 2003 GA/MNA Budget is deferred to the Committee on Administration. Deferred to AC report [See 30-62, III, 7, p. 295.] 12. That we recommend to the GA that we defer action on Recommendation 12 of the Permanent Committee’s report until the MNA Committee has consulted with the Korean Language Presbyteries regarding the integrating of 1.5 and 2nd generation Koreans fully into the life of the PCA. Adopted 13. That the General Assembly give thanks to the Lord for the generous response of the churches and members of the PCA to the special needs resulting from the September 11, 2001, terrorist attacks in New York City and Washington, D.C. And that the General Assembly give thanks for God’s grace through the network of PCA churches in the metropolitan cities who were able and willing to minister to their communities in significant ways. Further, that churches be encouraged: • To develop plans for ministry in case disasters should occur in their communities, including ministry not only to PCA families, but to other members of the Body of Christ, as well as ministries proclaiming Christ’s mercy in Word and deed to those outside the Body of Christ. • To respond with generous giving when appeals for disaster response are issued through MNA for disasters occurring in the United States and Canada. Churches and individuals are encouraged to give through MNA, in order to reduce accounting and recipient costs for recipient churches and presbyteries, noting that MNA charges no administrative fees for the channeling of these gifts .Adopted 14. That Overture 13 from Session of Faith Reformed Presbyterian Church be answered in the negative. Adopted Rationale: • This overture originated from the Session of Faith Reformed Church in Fairmont, W. Virginia. It was rejected by New River Presbytery. Per RAO 10-10, the Session forwarded the overture to General Assembly. • Two other PCA churches are located in the counties which this Session asks to have moved to Pittsburgh Presbytery. These two churches are opposed to the change. Thus, of the three churches directly affected by the change, two are opposed. • Pittsburgh Presbytery has not taken any action on this change.
