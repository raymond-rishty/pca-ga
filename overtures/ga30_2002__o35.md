# GA30 O35 — Clarify Relationship of Separately Incorporated Agencies to Assembly

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 35 from Ascension Presbytery (to AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Referred

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 303](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p303)*

---

“Clarify Relationship of Separately Incorporated Agencies to Assembly”

Whereas, denominational management personnel openly participated in and led a meeting of an organization billing itself as “Presbyterian Pastoral Leadership Network (PPLN)” on April 10, 2002; and

Whereas, participation by denominational management personnel in such a group is in apparent violation of the Conflict of Interest Policy adopted by the General Assembly (M21GA, pp. 174-77); and

Whereas, although the Conflict of Interest Policy was adopted in light of questions of propriety with regard to financial transactions, the Policy itself is quite broad, and includes involvement with non-profit organizations; and

Whereas, among the examples of conflict of interest are the following: “Using the PCA’s personnel, equipment, supplies, or good will for other than formally approved activities, programs, and purposes” and “Expending unreasonable staff time during normal business hours for ... other organization to the detriment of work performance for the PCA”; and

Whereas, assistants to one or more management personnel have been used to create a website, to e-mail others, and to assist in the April 10, 2002 meeting; and

Whereas, the purposes of this organization include the violation of the regulative principle of worship and the Presbyterian ideal of uniformity of worship as evidenced in the PPLN’s “Our Vision” viz. “We want our church’s future to be characterized by the following Gospel priorities ... Appreciating different forms of worship within the Reformed tradition”; and

Whereas, it is inappropriate for denominational servants to lend their power, prestige, and influence to such endeavors, especially when they are aiming to overturn established denominational doctrine and policy, whether through Constitutional amendment and/or other means such as publicly supporting and promoting overtures to change the PCA’s historic understanding of subscription to the Westminster Confession of Faith (see PPLN’s “Our Vision”), and

Whereas, no teaching or ruling elder may legitimately undermine denominational doctrine; and

Whereas, this is especially true when such teaching or ruling elders are using General Assembly resources; and

Whereas, whenever denominational servants engage in such activity, they undermine the confidence of the church as a whole in her committees and agencies, and could threaten financial giving to them;

Now therefore, be it resolved, that Westminster Presbytery hereby overtures the General Assembly to counsel denominational management personnel that the use of denominational resources and/or personnel to advocate and/or promote organization such as the Presbyterian Pastoral Leadership Network is inappropriate. And

be it further resolved, that Westminster Presbytery hereby overtures the General Assembly to counsel all paid employees on the General Assembly level that such involvement in leadership positions with PPLN or any similar entities are considered to be incompatible with continued occupation of denominational posts.
