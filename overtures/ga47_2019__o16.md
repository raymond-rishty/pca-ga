# GA47 O16 — Amend RAO to Provide for Moderator-Elect and Vice-Moderator

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 16 from Georgia Foothills Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 629](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p629)*

---

“Amend the _RAO_ to Provide for a Moderator-Elect and Vice-Moderator by Revision of _RAO_ 1-4, Addition of _RAO_ 1-6, 1-7, and 1-8, and Revision of _RAO_ 2-1, 2-3” **Whereas**, the role of Moderator of the General Assembly is a complicated responsibility. i twill be helpful fora man to have a year’s notice in order to prepare for his duties, and **Whereas**, some PCA presbyteries have a Moderator-In-Nomination rule in their Standing Rules, and **Whereas**, other Presbyterian denominations, such as the Associate Reformed Presbyterian Church and the Evangelical Presbyterian Church elect a Moderator-Elect or Vice-Moderator, and **Whereas**, the Assembly subsequent to the election of a Moderator-Elect or Vice-Moderator still would have the option of electing another man as Moderator, and **Whereas**, since we use electronic voting rather than voting cards or paper ballots, it is no longer necessary to specify that paper ballots be used, and **Whereas**, it is customary that the Moderator yield the chair at times. The revision to _RAO_ 2-1 specifies who the Moderator _pro tempore_ may be, and **Whereas**, under _Robert’s Rules of Order_ the term “privileges of the floor” only means that one is allowed to be present during a meeting and does not mean that one has the right to speak ( _RONR_ [11 [th] ed.] p. 29, footnote). The original purpose of _RAO_ 2-3 was to give a former Moderator of the General Assembly the right to participate in debate. One must be a commissioner in order to vote. **Therefore, be it resolved** that Georgia Foothills Presbytery overtures the Forty-seventh
