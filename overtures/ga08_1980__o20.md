# GA8 O20 — Clarify the boundaries of Central Florida Presbytery

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Presbytery of Central Florida

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 43](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p43)*

---

Whereas, the Fifth General Assembly in the matter of the erection of the Presbytery of Central Florida did adopt boundaries which are ambiguous and incomplete, to wit: ( 1) Southern boundary is defined as the southern borders of Sarasota and Manatee counties. Manatee County lies north of Sarasota County, therefore boundary should be the southern boundary of one or the other of these counties, not both. (2) Southern boundary as defined omits the eastern border of Sarasota County, the southern portion of the eastern border of Manatee County, the eastern border of Hardee County, the eastern portion of the southern border of Polk County, and the southern portion of the eastern border of Osceola County.

Whereas, the Mission To The United States Committee of the General Assembly has published a map which defines properly the southern boundary. Therefore, the Presbytery of Central Florida does overture the 8th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to correct the southern boundary description of the Presbytery of Central Florida to read as follows: Southern boundary be the southern and eastern borders of Sarasota County, the southern portion of the eastern border of Manatee County, the southern and eastern borders of Hardee County, the eastern portion of the southern border of Polk County, the southern border and the southern portion of the eastern border of Osceola County and the southern border of Brevard County. Adopted at the Winter meeting of Presbytery. January 15, 1980. Attest: Charles W. Ambler. Jr. Stated Clerk Answered in the affirmative. § 8-50, III, 7;
