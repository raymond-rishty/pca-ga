# GA47 O24 — Add Constitutional Precedence Statement to Agency Policy Manuals

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 24 from Philadelphia Metro West Presbytery (to AC, CDM,  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 653](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p653)*

---

“Add Constitutional Precedence Statement to MNA, MTW, RUF Policy Manuals” (Revised) CC, CTS, PCAF, PCA-RBI, RH) [Note: An earlier version of this overture appeared online in early March. At its May 4 meeting Philadelphia Metro West Presbytery revised the first “Whereas” clause to clarify the Committees and Agencies involved. There is no other change to the original overture.]

**Whereas** the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) currently has five Permanent Committees (Administrative Committee, Committee on Discipleship Ministries, Mission to North America, Mission to the World, and Reformed University Fellowship) and five Agencies (Covenant College, Covenant Seminary, PCA Foundation, PCA Retirement & Benefits, Inc., and Ridge Haven), and may in the future have other or additional Permanent Committees and Agencies (all such current and future Permanent Committees and Agencies, collectively, the “Permanent Committees and Agencies”) [ _BCO_ 14.1.12; _RAO_ 14.1]; and

**Whereas** it is necessary for the Permanent Committees and Agencies to function at all times under the authority of the Constitution (as defined in _BCO_ Preface III, and which includes the _Westminster Confession of_ _Faith,_ the _Larger Catechism_ and _Shorter Catechism,_ and the _Book of_ _Church Order_, collectively, the “Constitution”); [ _BCO_ 14.1.7–8; 14.6; Article V.G of the Corporate Bylaws]; and

**Whereas** it is standard operating procedure for organizations to write policy manuals; and

**Whereas** the Permanent Committees and Agencies rightly and necessarily have at times produced their respective policy manuals; and

**Whereas** it is essential for every policy manual of the Permanent Committees and Agencies to remain subject to the Constitution; and

**Whereas** it is possible for unanticipated conflicts, potential conflicts, or ambiguities to arise between Permanent Committee and Agency policy manuals and the Constitution; and

**Whereas** it is a well-recognized best practice for policy manuals of organizations to explicitly state their subordination to organizational constitutional documents and by-laws; **Therefore, it is resolved** that the Philadelphia Metro West Presbytery hereby overture the 47 [th] General Assembly to add the following statement to the preface of all policy manuals of the Permanent Committees and Agencies: “At every point, this entire manual is subordinate to the entire Constitution of the Presbyterian Church in America—the _Westminster_ _Standards_ and the _Book of Church Order_ . If there exists any conflict between the PCA’s constitutional documents and the content and stipulations of this manual, the Constitution carries final authority.” **Further resolved**, that any policy manual produced or amended by any of the Permanent Committees and Agencies shall, before becoming operational, be approved by its respective board and submitted to the General Assembly for ratification through the usual process of the reports of the Permanent Committees and Agencies. **Further resolved**, that each of the Permanent Committees and Agencies report back on the implementation of these actions to their respective Committee of Commissioners at the 48 [th] General Assembly. **Rationale** _**:**_ In order to carry out their responsibilities faithfully, Permanent Committees and Agencies must be able to develop and propose policies to the General Assembly for ratification. _BCO_ 14.1.7 indicates that “The Assembly's committees are to serve and not to direct any Church judicatories. They are not to establish policy, but rather execute policy established by the General Assembly.” The only effective method to ensure Permanent Committee and Agency policies yield to the PCA constitution, and the only way to ensure that constitutional due process for staff of these permanent Committees and Agencies in relation to written policies, is (1) to give the PCA constitution explicitly stated priority in all policy manuals, and (2) to make policy changes according to _BCO_ 14.1.7, which grants the General Assembly full visibility into proposed policies (and any wording changes to policies) in order to exercise its constitutionally-appointed powers of ratification. The action of the 5 [th] General Assembly, for example, delivers a clear process in relation to the Committee on Mission to the World (CMTW). This GA action (1977, p. 72, 5–64, IV, 9) differentiated between the MTW policy manual and the MTW handbook, when it determined “the policy manual to be revised only by General Assembly procedures while changes in the handbooks (which unavoidably need continual changes to meet changing situations with regard to such things as insurance, annuity, travel, etc.) be reported annually to the MTW Committee of Commissioners (CoC) at the General Assembly for their approval.” Usual process for policy changes then requires that changes/additions be submitted by CMTW to the CoC for ratification by the General Assembly, with a CMTW minute to this effect in each instance. The CoC should then include these as recommendations in their report to GA (including any minority reports).
