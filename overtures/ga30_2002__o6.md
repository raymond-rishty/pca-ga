# GA30 O6 — Ask AC to Provide Options for Lower Costs and Greater Ruling Elder Participation at GA

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 6 from Westminster Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)  ·  **Disposition:** Other

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 257](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p257)*

---

“Revise BCO 15-4 and 15-5 to Abolish the Standing Judicial Commission”

Whereas, the Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) was established for the noble goal of seeking to guarantee consistency of Constitutional interpretation; and

Whereas, throughout its relatively brief history the SJC has been the object of serious criticism of its procedures, policies, and judicial conclusions; and

Whereas, the seriousness of earlier criticism led to the appointment of an Ad Interim Committee on Judicial Procedures (AICJP), which gave its final report at the 1996 General Assembly, and whose recommendations were adopted by the denomination; and

Whereas, we believe that it is time to re-visit the question of the wisdom of having a Standing Judicial Commission; and

Whereas, among the abuses of the system which we have recently witnessed include the admittance to record of statements by members of the SJC which clearly crossed the bounds of propriety; and

Whereas, a significant portion of the 29th General Assembly clearly was frustrated by the inability to rectify the situation; and

Whereas, under our present rules, there are no Assembly- wide checks and balances on the SJC; and

Whereas, placing that much power in the hands of a relatively few individuals is not wise; and

Whereas, there is increasing frustration among many in the PCA arising from a perception that the decisions of the SJC do not reflect predictable justice; and

Whereas, we believe there is a better, more economical, and more efficient way of adjudicating cases;

Now therefore, be it resolved, that Westminster Presbytery hereby overtures the 30th General Assembly to begin the process of amending the BCO by replacing 15-4 and 15-5 with the following: 15.4 Every General Assembly shall commit all judicial cases within its jurisdiction to one or more commissions which shall be appointed in the following manner: 1. The General Assembly shall divide the presbyteries into no less than six geographical regions. 2. when a case arises from a Presbytery, the commissioners from the previous General Assembly who are from the other presbyteries in the same region as that Presbytery shall be put into a pool, from which shall be drawn, at random, alternation between ruling elders and teaching elders, the names of fourteen commissioners. 3. if a persons o selected declines to serve or is ineligible to serve, then another name will be selected in his place. 4. Those who serve on such a commission shall be ineligible to serve on another commission until the next General Assembly, unless the pool of eligible commissioners within that region has been exhausted. A quorum shall consist of at least ten members, at least five of whom must be ruling elders. 15.5 Eveiy General Assembly shall declare each of the commissions appointed under BCO 15-4 to be its own. Each of these commissions, when it has completed its work, shall report to the next General Assembly, which shall, without debate, vote on said report. If the General Assembly approves the commission report, it shall be admitted to record as the action of the General Assembly. If the General Assembly does not approve the commission report, then the court shall appoint a commission to readjudicate the matter or shall itself adjudicate it. Any request to the General Assembly under BCO 34-1 or BCO 41 for adjudication of a matter shall be decided by the General Assembly itself. 9. That Overture 9 from Louisiana Presbytery (“Forbid Freemasons from Being Officers in PCA”) be answered in the negative. Adopted Grounds: The General Assembly cannot determine abstractly, apart from regular process, how the courts, which under our Constitution are charged with the duty of judging the qualifications of candidates, are to interpret the Constitution in the regular discharge of their own functions. to attempt to do so would be in effect to amendthe Constitution by extra constitutional methods. The remedy for what failures there may be onthe part of courts of original jurisdiction must be found in the various provisions of the BCO designed for that end (e.g., BCO chapters 33- 1, 34-1, 40 and 43). Further, see the report of the Ad Interim Committee on Freemasonry presented to the 15th and 16th General Assemblies, and recommendation 3 of that report, adopted by the 16th General Assembly.
