“Amend _BCO_ 57-5 to Add Vow Regarding Trinity"

**Whereas**, there is but one only, living, and true God, who exists as three persons—God the Father, God the Son, and God the Holy Spirit—of one substance, power, and eternity (WCF 2; WLC 9; WSC 6); and

**Whereas**, the doctrine of the Trinity is foundational, essential, and vital to the Christian faith; indeed, presbyteries have been instructed to give due and serious consideration to “Appendix W,” which includes the affirmation: “the confession of the Trinity is the heartbeat of the Christian religion” (as adopted by the 45 [th] General Assembly, _Minutes,_ 29 and 624); and

**Whereas**, the visible church is made up of those who profess faith in the Lord Jesus Christ ( _BCO_ 2-1), and such profession of faith is a profession of the whole Christ, who is both truly God and truly man; and

**Whereas**, we live ina pluralistic age, where there is confusion over the essential being of God, illustrated by the fact that the vast majority of self-professing evangelicals in America affirm that “Jesus is the first and greatest being created by God” (Ligonier Ministries and LifeWay Research, “The State of Theology” 2018); and

**Whereas**, the doctrine of the Trinity has been and is currently under assault by the unbelieving world, by cults, by unitarians, and by various religions; and
