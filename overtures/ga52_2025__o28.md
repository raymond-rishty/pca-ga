# GA52 O28 — Demand MNA to Apologize and Terminate Personnel

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 28 from the Northwest Georgia Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1246](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1246)*

---

“Demand Mission to North America (MNA) to Apologize and Terminate Personnel”

**Whereas** in recent weeks certain employees and/or other representatives of Mission to North America (“MNA”) provided online counsel to undocumented persons on ways to avoid being detained by authorities of the United States Government; and

**Whereas** in providing this information to undocumented persons the responsible employees and/or other representatives of MNA violated the following legal provisions: (1) Title 18 United States Code (U.S.C.) §2 paragraph (a), which states that “Whoever commits an offense against the United States or aids, abets, counsels, commands, induces or procures its commission, is punishable as a principal;” and (2) Title 8 U.S.C. § 1324 Paragraph (iv) which states that, “Any person who – encourages or induces an alien to come to, enter, or reside in the United States, knowing or in reckless disregard of the fact that such coming to, entry, or residence is or will be in violation of law.”

**Whereas** we find that appropriate action must be taken to remedy the unlawful conduct of the employees and/or other representatives of MNA who participated in or otherwise permitted the posting of the inappropriate information; and

**Whereas** on February 12, 2025, MNA released a statement offering an apology for the “previously posted information” on their website which “erroneously allowed the posting of content that advised undocumented persons on ways to avoid being detained by authorities;” and

**Whereas** we find the statement released by MNA on February 12, 2025, to be insufficient action in light of the grievous nature of the conduct of the employees and/or other representatives of MNA who participated in or otherwise permitted the posting of the inappropriate information; and

**Whereas** we are commanded by Scriptures to “be subject to the governing authorities,” knowing their existence is instituted by God, and that “whoever resists the authorities resists what God has appointed” (Romans 13:1, 2), including a warning of likely judgment for such conduct; and

**Whereas** we are commanded to “pay to all what is owed to them: … respect to whom respect is owed, honor to whom honor is owed” (Romans 13:7); and

**Whereas** the respect and honor due to the United States Government remains clearly unrestored with the general apology offered by MNA; and

**Whereas** the Lord Jesus commands, “So if you are offering your gift at the altar and there remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there before the altar and go and be reconciled to your brother…” (Matthew 5:23-24); and

**Whereas** _Westminster Confession of Faith_ 15.5 states that, “Men ought not to content themselves with a general repentance, but it is every man’s duty to endeavor to repent of his particular sins particularly;” and

**Whereas** _Westminster Confession of Faith_ 20.4 states that, “the liberty which Christ hath purchased, are not intended by God to destroy, but mutually to uphold and preserve one another; they who, upon pretense of Christian liberty, shall oppose any lawful power, or the lawful exercise of it, whether it be civil or ecclesiastical, resist the ordinance of God;” and

**Whereas** _Westminster Confession of Faith_ 23.4 states that, “It is the duty of people to pray for magistrates, [and] to obey their lawful commands, and to be subject to their authority for conscience’ sake;” and

**Whereas** MNA rightly issued a general statement to “repent and apologize,” but has not publicly offered apologies and repentance to all particular parties aggrieved, nor has it provided notification holding appropriate staff members accountable. **Therefore, be it finally resolved** that the 52 [nd] General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America hereby directs the MNA to take the following actions within thirty (30) days of the adoption of this Overture: (a) Write and submit a statement to the Secretary of Homeland Security, the Attorney General of the United States, and the Director of U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services which apologizes for the offence of encouraging illegal immigrants to evade lawful authority; and (b) Write and submit a statement of repentance to the Presbyterian Church in America for permitting to exist within the ranks of MNA an environment among its staff in which a statement of the nature described herein would be released online for distribution, thus dishonoring the name of Christ and bringing shame upon our denomination; and (c) Terminate the employment of any individual on the MNA staff who participated in any manner in the preparation and release of the online statement which counseled undocumented persons on ways to avoid being detained by authorities of the United States Government. _Adopted by the Northwest Georgia Presbytery at its 57_ _[th]_ _stated meeting,_ _April 1, 2025_ _Attested by TE Robby Baxter, stated clerk_
