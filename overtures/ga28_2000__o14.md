# GA28 O14 — Erect Committee to Study Funding of General Assembly Attendance

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 14 From New Jersey Presbytery (to AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 268](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p268)*

---

“Erect committee to develop funding of General Assembly”

Whereas, the General Assembly consists of all teaching elders (TE) who are in good standing in their Presbyteries and Ruling Elders (RE) elected by their Sessions (BCO 14-2), and

Whereas, congregations are entitled to two RE representatives for the first 350 communing members or fraction thereof, and one additional RE for each additional 500 communing members or fraction thereof, (BCO 14-2), and

Whereas, at the 27th General Assembly the ratio of TE stores was over 2 to 1 (854 to 374), and it has long been the concern of the PCA that there be more RE representation from the congregations, and

Whereas, small congregations often lack the financial ability to send one representative let alone two or three, and

Whereas, representatives of small congregations often use creative ways to save money and still get to General Assembly meeting, (i.e. sharing rides, using buddy fares on planes, sharing rooms with representatives from other congregations, staying in inexpensive second-class motels, etc.), and

Whereas, small congregations may be able to find creative ways to send more than one representative to General Assembly without large increases in travel and lodging costs, (i.e. sharing rides, using buddy fares on planes, sharing rooms, having representatives pay their own travel and meal costs, etc.), and

Whereas, the increases in the registration fee for attending the General Assembly is making it more and more difficult for small congregations to send representatives, while large congregations are less impacted by the increases on a per capita basis, and

Whereas, this dynamic may lead to an imbalance in representation at the General Assembly, not only between TEs and REs (as already seen), but between large and small congregations, and

Whereas, the expenses of representatives to the General Assembly is to be defrayed by the bodies which they represent, (BCO 10-6), and

Whereas, the Administrative Committee does incur significant expense in arranging and facilitating the meeting of the General Assembly, and

Whereas, the Administrative Committee is to set a registration fee, subject to the approval of the General Assembly, (RAO 9-4), and

Whereas, ape rca pita division of the cost is more equitable and in the spirit of Christian love, than a flat rate which does not take into consideration the increased financial liability this causes per capita for small congregations when the flat rate is increased, (i.e. the ratio of increase per ca pita is much greater on small congregations than on large congregations), and

Whereas, the bible teaches us to take into consideration the needs of our brethren (cf. Acts 11:29). Therefore Be it Resolved that the 28th General Assembly of the PCA be asked to erect a committee to develop a more equitable method of funding the cost of the Assembly. adopted by the presbytery of new jersey on february 19, 2000.
