**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 12 from the Providence Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Approved but not ratified  ·  **BCO:** 58-5

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1191](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1191)*

> *RECOMMENDATION 12 as substituted to answer Overture 12 in the negative was adopted (1080-922). BCO 58-5 amendment was rejected by the 52nd General Assembly itself and not sent to presbyteries.*

“Amend _BCO_ 58-5 to Specify Only Officers May Distribute the Lord’s Supper” **Whereas** our _Confession of Faith_ (29.3), _Catechisms_ ( _Larger Catechism_ 169, 173, and 176) and the declarations of our own denomination* call for the Supper to be administered by ministers, **And whereas** the keys of the Kingdom and the sacraments are to be in the hands of the ordained offices of the church (Matthew 16:19), **And whereas** the role of the Session in discipline impinges upon the serving of the Supper ( _BCO_ 30-3; cf. _WCF_ 30:1-4) **And whereas** not since the beginning of Presbyterianism has the administering of the sacraments been in the hands of the non-ordained membership (see also the Scots Confession, Chapter 22**), **And whereas** in the Presbyterian church the passing of the elements by the laity has historically been done while seated, while those standing in front of the congregation represent the administering and serving role, and having non-ordained people standing with the minister and serving the Supper confuses the role of ordained office, **And whereas** there are no positive Scriptural mandates to allow non-ordained members to administer and serve communion that would encourage us to change our practice, **And whereas** the Directory of Worship (58-5) only implies (or assumes) the role of the officers in the serving of the Supper, not foreseeing the cultural trends in our contemporary society, **Be it resolved** that we overture the 52 [nd] General Assembly to amend the Directory of Worship in _BCO_ 58-5, third paragraph as follows (underlining for additi
