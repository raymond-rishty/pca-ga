> *RECOMMENDATION 12 as substituted to answer Overture 12 in the negative was adopted (1080-922). BCO 58-5 amendment was rejected by the 52nd General Assembly itself and not sent to presbyteries.*

“Amend _BCO_ 58-5 to Specify Only Officers May Distribute the Lord’s Supper”

**Whereas** our _Confession of Faith_ (29.3), _Catechisms_ ( _Larger Catechism_ 169, 173, and 176) and the declarations of our own denomination* call for the Supper to be administered by ministers, **And whereas** the keys of the Kingdom and the sacraments are to be in the hands of the ordained offices of the church (Matthew 16:19), **And whereas** the role of the Session in discipline impinges upon the serving of the Supper ( _BCO_ 30-3; cf. _WCF_ 30:1-4) **And whereas** not since the beginning of Presbyterianism has the administering of the sacraments been in the hands of the non-ordained membership (see also the Scots Confession, Chapter 22**), **And whereas** in the Presbyterian church the passing of the elements by the laity has historically been done while seated, while those standing in front of the congregation represent the administering and serving role, and having non-ordained people standing with the minister and serving the Supper confuses the role of ordained office, **And whereas** there are no positive Scriptural mandates to allow non-ordained members to administer and serve communion that would encourage us to change our practice, **And whereas** the Directory of Worship (58-5) only implies (or assumes) the role of the officers in the serving of the Supper, not foreseeing the cultural trends in our contemporary society,

**Be it resolved** that we overture the 52 [nd] General Assembly to amend the Directory of Worship in _BCO_ 58-5, third paragraph as follows (underlining for additions): Here the bread is to be distributed by the minister or to other officers for distribution. After having given the bread, he shall take the cup, and say: **In the same manner, He also took the cup, and** **having given thanks as has been done in His name, He gave** **it to the disciples, saying, “This cup is the new covenant in** **My blood, which is shed for many for the remission of** **sins. Drink from it, all of you.”** While the minister is repeating these words, let him give the cup directly to the people or to the officers for distribution. _Adopted by the Providence Presbytery at its stated meeting, February 4,_ _2025._ _Attested by TE Adam Tisdale, Stated Clerk_ Sources Referenced _**WCF**_ **29.3** The Lord Jesus hath, in this ordinance, appointed his ministers to declare his word of institution to the people; to pray, and bless the elements of bread and wine, and thereby to set them apart from a common to an holy use; and to take and break the bread, to take the cup, and (they communicating also themselves) to give both to the communicants; but to none who are not then present in the congregation. _**WLC**_ **169** . There is no definite suspension from the Sacraments. Suspension from office is the exclusion of a church officer from his office. This may be definite or indefinite as to its duration. With respect to church officers, suspension from Sacraments shall always be accompanied by suspension from office. But suspension from office is not always necessarily accompanied with suspension from Sacraments. Definite suspension from office is administered when the credit of religion, the honor of Christ, and the good of the delinquent demand it, even though the delinquent has given satisfaction to the court. Indefinite suspension is administered to the impenitent offender until he exhibits signs of repentance, or until by his conduct, the necessity of the greatest censure be made manifest.
