# GA30 O4 — Amend BCO 21-4 to Require Recording of Presbytery-Allowed Exceptions to Standards

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** Ohio Valley Presbytery: “Handling Exceptions to the  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 34-1

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 102](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p102)*

Westminster Standards ” It is the opinion of the CCB that the proposed constitutional language creates constitutional conflicts with the BCO Preliminary Principles. Furthermore, the proposed language creates constitutional ambiguity. 1. The proposed overture language “either in his private life” is in conflict with BCO Preliminary Principle II. 1 and II.7. 2. The proposed overture language requiring Presbyteries to record every man’s views disagreeing with the Constitution will erode the Presbyteries’ exclusive authority to determine if a candidate receives and adopts the Confession of Faith and the Catechism of the PCA as containing the system of doctrine taught in the Holy Scriptures. See BCO 21-5. This is in conflict with BCO Preliminary Principle II.2 that recognizes the right of each court to determine its membership. (BCO Preface II.2, BCO 21-1 through 7; BCO 13-1; BCO 13-9 a,c,d; BCO 11-3 & 4). 3. An ambiguity created by the proposed language is the introduction of the phrase: “exception to the standards.” This phrase is nowhere else found in our constitution. The phrase elicits a plethora of interpretations, many of which go beyond our present Constitutional parameters. 4. It has not normally been the mission of the CCB to decide on the placement of Constitutional language. [For text and action, see 30-50, III, 3, p. 238.] Adopted by CCB
