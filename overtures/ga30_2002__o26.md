“Diversity of Views on Women In Military Military & B&O) and Dismiss WIM Committee with Thanks - Version 2”

Whereas, “it pleased the Lord, at sundry times, and in divers manner, to reveal Himself, and to declare that His will unto His Church; and afterwards, for the better preserving and propagating of the truth, and for the more sure establishment and comfort of the Church against the corruption of the flesh, and the malice of Satan and of the world, to commit the same wholly unto writing: which maketh the Holy Scripture to be most necessary; those former ways of God’s revealing His will unto His people being now ceased (WCF 1.1);” and

Whereas, “The whole counsel of God concerning all things necessary for His own glory, man’s salvation, faith and life, is either expressly set down in Scripture, or by good and necessary consequence may be deduced from Scripture: unto which nothing at any time is to be added, whether by new revelations of the Spirit or traditions of men (WCF 1.6);” and

Whereas, “God alone is Lord of the conscience, and hath left it free from the doctrines and commandments of men, which are, in any thing, contrary to His Word; or beside it, if matters of faith, or worship. So that, to believe such doctrines, or to obey such commands, out of conscience, is to betray true liberty of conscience (WCF 20.2);” and

Whereas, “Synods and councils are to handle, or conclude nothing, but that which is ecclesiastical: and are not to intermeddle with civil affairs which concern the commonwealth, unless by way of humble petition in cases extraordinary; or, by way of advice, for satisfaction of conscience, if they be thereunto required by the civil magistrate (WCF 31.4);” and

Whereas, “Christ, as King, has given to His Church officers, oracles and ordinances; and especially has He ordained therein His system of doctrine, government, discipline and worship, all of which are either expressly set down in Scripture, or by good and necessary inference may be deducted therefrom; and to which things He commands that nothing be added, and that from them naught be taken away (BCO Preface, I.);” and

Whereas, “Godliness is founded on truth;” and, “No opinion can be more pernicious or more absurd than that which brings truth and falsehood upon the same level {BCO Preface, II, 4.);” and

Whereas, “While, under the conviction of the above principle, it is necessary to make effective provision that all who are admitted as teachers be sound in the faith, there are truths and forms with respect to which men of good character and principles may differ. In all these it is the duty both of private Christians and societies to exercise mutual forbearance toward each other (BCO Preface, II, 5.);” and

Whereas, concerns related to women serving in the military have been before this Assembly for several years without resolution; and

Whereas, particular concern has been raised as to whether “either expressly... or by good and necessary consequence” the Scriptures prohibit women from serving in various military capacities; and

Whereas, some have questioned whether speaking to this issue would be “to intermeddle with civil affairs” or if circumstances are sufficiently “extraordinary” to warrant speaking thus; and

Whereas, PCA members facing such concerns require sound pastoral counsel as regards their duty before God; and

Whereas, “The duty which God requireth of man, is obedience to his revealed will (fVSC 39);” and

Whereas, “The moral law is summarily comprehended in the Ten Commandments (WSC 41);” and

Whereas, “The fifth commandment requireth the preserving the honor, and performing the duties, belonging to every one in their several places and relations, as superiors, inferiors, or equals (WSC 64);” and, “The fifth commandment forbiddeth the neglecting of, or doing any thing against, the honor and duty which belongeth to every one in their several places and relations (WSC 65);” and

Whereas, concerns related to women serving in the military touch upon the various responsibilities of superiors, inferiors and equals in the military chain of command and among the citizenry of the commonwealth; and

Whereas, “The sixth commandment requireth all lawful endeavors to preserve our own life, and the life of others (WSC 68);” and “The sixth commandment forbiddeth the taking away of our own life, or the life of our neighbour unjustly, or whatsoever tendeth thereunto (WSC 69);” and

Whereas, concerns related to women serving in the military understandably may involve concerns over potential increased loss of life due to reduced combat effectiveness with mixed sex troop arrangements; and

Whereas, “The seventh commandment requireth the preservation of our own and our neighbour’s chastity, in heart, speech, and behavior (WSC 71);” and “The seventh commandment forbiddeth all unchaste thoughts, words, and actions (WSC 72);” and

Whereas, potential increased sexual immorality and violation is an understandable concern as regards both men and women serving in the military; and

Whereas, these teachings of Scripture as summarized in our Constitution provide sound basis for objection to such mixed troop arrangements; and

Whereas, nevertheless “men of good character and principles may differ” with each other concerning such an application of these teachings, so that some out of conscience may object to women serving in the military, while others may not object; and

Whereas, some, however, may object to what they understand to be an unscriptural prohibition concerning women in the military that binds the conscience to something other than God’s revealed will, and that runs the risk of intermeddling in civil affairs; and
