# GA28 O5 — Amend BCO 32-19 to Clarify Use of Counsel in Cases of Process

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 5 From Louisiana Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 32-19, 38-4

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 297](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p297)*

---

“Amend BCO 38-4 by Deletion”

Whereas, BCO 38-4 creates a method for administrative removal of a member from the church of the lord jesus christ without formal judicial process under certain specified circumstances.

Whereas, the first sentence of the second paragraph of BCO 38-4 reads thusly: “If after diligently pursuing such pastoral discipline, and after further inquiry and due delay, the session is of the judgment that the member will not fulfill his membership obligations in this or any other branch of the visible church (cf. BCO 2-2), then the Session shall erase his name from the roll.”

Whereas, the highlighted words create serious constitutional problems for a Session faithfully attempting to exercise its duties under BCO 38-4, inasmuch as it might be reasonably inferred that administrative removal from the membership roll may NOT occur under this provision if the member were regularly worshipping and participating in another church other than the one in which he took his membership vows.

Whereas, the highlighted words may be reasonably interpreted to require a Session to exercise formal discipline rather than administrative removal, if a member ignored his membership vows and began attending another congregation.

Whereas, it is the intent of scripture and our constitutional standards that decisions pertaining to the election of officers and well-being of particular congregations should be decided by those members who are faithful to their membership vows.

Whereas, the Session has the constitutional authority to receive, remove and dismiss members. BCO 12-5(a).

Whereas, the Session should reasonably and equitably have the authority under BCO 38-4 without instituting formal process to administratively remove a member who has failed to keep his membership vows, regardless of whether the member is attending another congregation. Therefore, Be it Resolved that Louisiana Presbytery respectfully moves that the first sentence of the second paragraph of BCO 38-4 be amended to delete the highlighted language above so that the sentence will read thusly: “If after diligently pursuing such pastoral discipline, and after further inquiry and due delay, the session is of the judgment that the member will not fulfill his membership obligations, then the Session shall erase his name from the roll.” Presbytery respectfully requests that the 28th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America concur in this proposed amendment and that there be the initiation of constitutional amendment procedures in accordance with our Book of Church Order. Adopted by Louisiana Presbytery on October 16, 1999. Attested by: /s/ George Thompson, Stated Clerk 16. That OVERTURE 27 from Gulf Coast Presbytery (“Amend RAO 14-7-3 by Adding Another Sentence”) be answered in the negative. Adopted Grounds: A rule change adopted so recently by the Assembly ought not to be reviewed again until the Church has had time to practically assess its effect. There is a presumptive virtue in settled law.
