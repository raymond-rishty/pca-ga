# GA29 O12 — Divide Central Carolina Presbytery to Form Piedmont Triad Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 12 From Central Carolina Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 168](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p168)*

---

“Divide Central Carolina Presbytery into Two Presbyteries” Whereas, the Central Carolina Presbytery exists for the Glory of God and the furtherance on His Kingdom here on Earth; Whereas, the Central Carolina Presbytery currently consists of Forty-Two (42) particular and mission congregations comprising more than Six Thousand (6,000) communing members within a geographical area requiring substantially more than three hours to cross; Whereas, the 16th General Assembly adopted guidelines for the multiplication of existing presbyteries into new presbyteries, which guidelines provide a set of ideals governing the development of new presbyteries; Whereas, pursuant to those guidelines, the Central Carolina Presbytery committed itself to a plan to grow into three separate presbyteries several years ago, which plan included developing three sub-regions (Metrolina, Triad, and Sandhills) within the presbytery and focusing church planting resources on developing new churches within each of these regions; Whereas, the Triad Region of the Central Carolina Presbytery has grown to Six (6) particular congregations and Two (2) mission congregations comprising more than One Thousand communing members and these churches have developed a strong sense of mutual care and potential for shared ministry and joint ministry projects; Whereas, Five of the particular congregations located in the Triad Region have communing membership in excess of One Hundred Twenty-Five, with strong prospects for continued growth in the future; Whereas, there is a commonly recognized regional identity within the Eleven
