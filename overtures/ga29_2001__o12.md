# GA29 O12 — Divide Central Carolina Presbytery to Form Piedmont Triad Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 12 From Central Carolina Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 168](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p168)*

---

“Divide Central Carolina Presbytery into Two Presbyteries”

Whereas, the Central Carolina Presbytery exists for the Glory of God and the furtherance on His Kingdom here on Earth;

Whereas, the Central Carolina Presbytery currently consists of Forty-Two (42) particular and mission congregations comprising more than Six Thousand (6,000) communing members within a geographical area requiring substantially more than three hours to cross;

Whereas, the 16th General Assembly adopted guidelines for the multiplication of existing presbyteries into new presbyteries, which guidelines provide a set of ideals governing the development of new presbyteries;

Whereas, pursuant to those guidelines, the Central Carolina Presbytery committed itself to a plan to grow into three separate presbyteries several years ago, which plan included developing three sub-regions (Metrolina, Triad, and Sandhills) within the presbytery and focusing church planting resources on developing new churches within each of these regions;

Whereas, the Triad Region of the Central Carolina Presbytery has grown to Six (6) particular congregations and Two (2) mission congregations comprising more than One Thousand communing members and these churches have developed a strong sense of mutual care and potential for shared ministry and joint ministry projects;

Whereas, Five of the particular congregations located in the Triad Region have communing membership in excess of One Hundred Twenty-Five, with strong prospects for continued growth in the future;

Whereas, there is a commonly recognized regional identity within the Eleven (11) County area known as the "Piedmont Triad" in North Carolina which the Triad Region of Central Carolina Presbytery was formed to build upon and which does not partition any existing metropolitan areas;

Whereas, the Central Carolina Presbytery believes it is consistent with the guidelines of the 16th General Assembly to form a new presbytery in the Eleven County area of North Carolina known as the "Piedmont Triad" and that this multiplication of ministry would further the advance of God's Kingdom and the growth of His Church on Earth; NOW, THEREFORE the Central Carolina Presbytery hereby petitions and overtures the General Assembly pursuant to 14-6(e) of the Book of Church Order of the Presbyterian Church in America as follows: (1) To remove the Eleven County area commonly known as the "Piedmont Triad" of North Carolina and consisting of Surry, Yadkin, Davie, Davidson, Stokes, Forsyth, Rockingham, Guilford, Randolph, Caswell and Alamance Counties from the existing bounds of the Central Carolina Presbytery; (2) To erect the "Piedmont Triad Presbytery of North Carolina," establishing the boundaries of such presbytery to include those Eleven counties and the particular and mission congregations currently located within them; (3) To authorize the conduct of business by this newly established church court effective July 1,2001, consistent with Chapter 13 of the Book of Church Order and such other provisions as may apply. It is so moved and approved by the Assembled Court of the Central Carolina Presbytery this 27th day of January, 2001. Attested by: /s/ Howie Burkhalter, Moderator, Central Carolina Presbytery This Overture was moved, seconded and approved at a duly constituted meeting of the Central Carolina Presbytery at which a quorum was present. Attested by: /s/ David M. Frierson, Stated Clerk
