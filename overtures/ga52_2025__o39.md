# GA52 O39 — Amend BCO 34-8 & Add BCO 33-5 to Clarify Process for Elevating Suspension to Deposition

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 39 from the Southeast Alabama Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference  ·  **BCO:** 33-5, 34-8

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1280](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1280)*

---

“Amend _BCO_ 34-8 and add _BCO_ 33-5 to Clarify the Process for Elevating Suspension from Office to Deposition”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 34-8 be amended as follows (underlining for additions; strikethrough for deletions): **34-8.** A minister under indefinite suspension from his office or deposed for scandalous conduct shall not be restored, even on the deepest sorrow for his sin, until he shall exhibit for a considerable time such an eminently exemplary, humble and edifying life and testimony as shall heal the wound made by his scandal. A deposed minister shall in no case be restored until it shall appear that the general sentiment of the Church is strongly in his favor, and demands his restoration; and then only by the court inflicting the censure, or with that court’s consent. The removal of deposition requires a three-fourths (3/4) vote of the court inflicting the censure, or a three-fourths (3/4) vote of the court to which the majority of the original court delegates that authority. Likewise, if it shall appear in time that any minister suspended from office fails to satisfy the requirements of _BCO_ 34-8 and/or _BCO_ 37-8, Presbytery may depose him from office, a majority of three-quarters (3/4) being necessary for this purpose. In such a case, the clerk shall under the order of the Presbytery forthwith deliver to the minister concerned a written note that, at the next stated meeting, the question of his being so dealt with is to be considered. This notice shall distinctly state the grounds for this proceeding. The party thus notified shall be heard in his own defense; and if the decision pass against him he may appeal, as if he had been tried after the usual forms.

**Be it further resolved** that a new _BCO_ 33-5 be added as follows (underlining for new wording): **33-5.** When a ruling elder or deacon is indefinitely suspended from office after a case of process or a BCO 38-1 case without process, he shall not be restored, even on the deepest sorrow for his sin, until he shall exhibit for a considerable time such an eminently exemplary, humble and edifying life and testimony as shall heal any wound made by his scandal. The removal of deposition requires a three-fourths (3/4) vote of his Session. If it shall appear in time that any ruling elder or deacon suspended from office fails to satisfy the above requirements, the Session may depose him from office, a majority of threequarters (3/4) being necessary for this purpose. In such a case, the clerk shall under the order of the Session forthwith deliver to the Ruling Elder or Deacon concerned a written note that, at the next stated meeting, the question of his being so dealt with is to be considered. This notice shall distinctly state the grounds for this proceeding. The party thus notified shall be heard in his own defense; and if the decision pass against him he may appeal, as if he had been tried after the usual forms. So that _BCO_ 34-8 as amended would read: **34-8.** A minister under indefinite suspension from his office or deposed for scandalous conduct shall not be restored, even on the deepest sorrow for his sin, until he shall exhibit for a considerable time such an eminently exemplary, humble and edifying life and testimony as shall heal the wound made by his scandal.
