# GA52 O7 — Amend BCO 34-1 Regarding Advisors & Original Jurisdiction in Process Against a TE

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 7 from the Missouri Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 34-1

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1175](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1175)*

---

“Amend _BCO_ 34-1 Regarding Advisors and Original Jurisdiction in Process Against a TE” **Whereas** the issue of maintaining high moral and theological standards for ministers in the PCA continues to be of utmost importance; and **Whereas** the _Book of Church_ order speaks to how these standards are to be upheld by Presbyteries; and **Whereas** the Church of the Lord Jesus Christ and her ministers are under greater public scrutiny than at any time in recent memory; and **Whereas** the officers of the church are charged with devising methods and strategies that enable us to maintain high standards for our ministers while also protecting the rights of the accused; and **Whereas** every Presbytery could benefit from the voluntary input, engagement and concerns of their fellow Presbyteries, especially in controversial cases; and **Whereas** it remains important to maintain the principle of original jurisdiction; and **Whereas** removal of original jurisdiction should only take place when it can be grounded in the constitution; and **Whereas** the PCA has grown considerably since the time _BCO_ 34-1 was last amended; and **Whereas** the age of the internet has significantly impacted the way we share and receive information; **Therefore, be it resolved** that _BCO_ 34-1 be amended as follows (underlining for additions, strikethrough for deletions): **34-1.** Process against a minister shall be entered before the Presbytery of which he is a member and may include Teaching or Ruling Elders from other Presbyteries as non-voting advisors.
