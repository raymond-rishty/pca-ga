# GA47 O41 — Direct MTW to Restrict Team Leader Roles to Ordained Elders

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 41 from Heritage Presbytery (to MTW)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 695](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p695)*

---

“Direct CMTW to Amend its Guidelines to Implement CMTW’s ‘Statement on Valuing Women in MTW,’ so that only Ordained Elders Will Be Allowed to Serve in the Roles of Team Leader, Regional Director, and International Director within MTW.”

**Whereas** on January 9, 2019, the Committee on Mission To the World (CMTW) adopted a “Statement on Valuing Women in MTW”; and

**Whereas** MTW subsequently proposed guidelines to implement the “Statement on Valuing Women in MTW” that were distributed to missionaries on January 11, 2019; and

**Whereas** the MTW guidelines affirm “that women may serve in leadership roles within MTW, recognizing that only ordained elders exercise ecclesiastical authority over church plants or churches” [See the attached Statement with Guidelines, page 3, paragraph 3.]; and

**Whereas** the MTW guidelines also affirm “there has been no MTW policy regarding women serving in these leadership roles…” [See the attached Statement with Guidelines, page 4, paragraph 1.]; and

**Whereas** the MTW guidelines initiate a new policy that allows women to serve in authority over men within MTW in the roles of team leader, regional director, and international director [See the attached Statement with Guidelines, page 8, #3.]; and

**Whereas** Heritage Presbytery and other presbyteries of the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) are charged to provide spiritual oversight for ordained elders who come from within our bounds, but who serve under MTW [See _BCO_ 8-3 &13-2.];

**Therefore be it resolved** that Heritage Presbytery hereby overture the 47th General Assembly of the PCA to direct CMTW to ensure that MTW amend its guidelines to implement CMTW’s “Statement on Valuing Women in MTW,” so that only ordained elders will be allowed to serve in the roles of team leader, regional director, and international director within MTW. **Rationale:** While CMTW’s intention of “supporting and equipping our sisters in Christ as they exercise their gifts and abilities throughout MTW’s global ministry…” [see the attached Statement with Guidelines, p. 698] is commendable, the MTW guidelines actually hinder some of our women from serving in MTW by creating an untenable crisis of conscience for them. Therefore, the MTW guidelines discourage some of the very sisters in Christ that CMTW’s “Statement on Valuing Women in MTW” intends to support in their missionary service. Secondly, them tw guidelines allow a woman to hold a position of authority within MTW in which she cannot exercise ecclesiastical authority over a man. And the MTW guidelines concede this would require an ordained man to fulfill some of her position’s responsibilities. [See the attached Statement with Guidelines, p. 700, #3.] But an obvious consequence of such an awkward arrangement would be questions about what a woman in such a position is and is not allowed to do. For example, MTW’s Policy Manual currently indicates the team leader position has very broad authority, including “spiritual care” over those on the missionary team. Yet the MTW guidelines leave the team leader position potentially open to non-elders. Therefore, the MTW guidelines are unwise because they can lead to placing women in difficult positions, while causing unnecessary confusion and conflict within a missionary team. Lastly, the MTW guidelines cite the Report of the Ad Interim Committee on Women Serving in the Ministry of the Church to the 45th General Assembly of the PCA, as a primary reason MTW is allowing women to have authority over men within MTW – even potentially over ordained elders. [See the attached Statement with Guidelines, p. 698.] But the 46th General Assembly rejected two overtures that would have allowed women to serve on PCA agency boards. [See Overtures 13 and 26.] _Adopted by Heritage Presbytery at its stated meeting, May 14, 2019_ _Attested by /s/ RE Conrad W Judy Jr., Stated Clerk_ **Attachment** **Statement on Valuing Women in MTW** Adopted by the Committee on Mission to the World January 9, 2019 Statement Adopted by Committee on Mission to the World: MTW values all women as created in the image of God. Under Scripture's authority and in our doctrinal view of ordination, we affirm that female disciples of Christ are co-heirs of the kingdom, co-laborers in the work of the gospel, gifted by the Spirit as members of the body of Christ and called to use these gifts for the building up of the Church and for the extension of the Kingdom of God throughout the world. Furthermore, the MTW community deeply needs the gifts and glory of God uniquely expressed in women. Therefore, MTW is committed to supporting and equipping our sisters in Christ as they exercise their gifts and abilities throughout MTW's global ministry as together we show and tell the gospel of God's grace. In keeping with this statement, we affirm that women may serve in leadership roles within MTW, recognizing that only ordained elders exercise ecclesiastical authority over church plants or churches. **Reasons for Creation of the Statement:** First, the growing desire from our denomination to encourage women to use their gifts for the service of the church are reflected in the recommendation passed at the 45 [th] General Assembly of the PCA, stating “that sessions, presbyteries, and the General Assembly strive to develop, recognize, and utilize the gifts, skills, knowledge and wisdom of godly women in the local, regional, and national church …” The spirit of this recommendation is to actively look for ways in which women can utilize their gifts in the local and larger church context. Second, women have been serving in various leadership capacities in MTW on different fields, in the home office, and in specific roles. In each of these contexts, those who made these appointments ensured that their leadership is in accord with our complementarian view of men and women’s roles in the church. The problem that emerged, however, was when women leaders were invited to global leadership meetings, questions arose from other MTW leaders and missionaries as to whether women should attend those meetings, and about the legitimacy of female leadership roles in light of passages such as 1 Timothy 2:12. What has complicated the matter is the fact that there has been no MTW policy regarding women serving in these leadership roles, nor has there been an organization-wide discussion on these issues. What has resulted is that faithful women serving in leadership are put in difficult positions, having to defend their leadership positions and presence at leadership meetings. It is in this context that we have initiated a discussion on women serving in leadership in MTW, and on deploying the God-given gifting of our MTW women, for the service of the Church. Field leadership has participated in this discussion in various ways, including writing of papers expressing a variety of opinion submitted to Committee on Mission to the World (CMTW). These discussions and papers were used by CMTW in their deliberations as they sought how to best articulate a policy for MTW on women serving in MTW. **Purpose of the Statement:** _“So God created man in His own image, in the image of God he created him;_ _male and female he created them. And God blessed them.”_ **Genesis 1:27-** **28a** _“In accordance with Scripture and the Reformed Confession, (is) the idea_ _that a human being does not bear or have the image of God, but that he or_ _she is the image of God”_ – Herman Bavinck, _Reformed Dogmatics: God and Creation_ CMTW’s statement calls our missional community to value all women, and to recognize and deploy women’s gifting in building the Church and extending the Kingdom of Jesus Christ around the world. In addition, our General Assembly through the Ad Interim Committee report has encouraged us to consider applicable recommendations from their report on “Women Serving in the Ministry of the Church,” amended and adopted by the 45 [th] General Assembly of the PCA. One aspect of the CMTW statement focuses on women in leadership roles in MTW. This leadership affirmation is part of CMTW’s encouragement to our missionary community to be “supporting and equipping our sisters in Christ as they exercise their gifts and abilities throughout MTW’s global ministry.” As part of any discussion on leadership, we also affirm that all MTW leadership roles are based on a biblical model of servant leadership that does not seek to wield power over others, whether leadership is exercised by a man or a woman. MTW further recognizes that application of this portion of the statement regarding women serving in leadership roles in MTW may lead to specific and local questions of implementation, because of our shared desire to ensure that ecclesiastical authority over churches or church plants is reserved for men.
