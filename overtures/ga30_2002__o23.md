# GA30 O23 — Redraw Western Boundary Between New York State and Ascension Presbyteries

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 23 from New York State Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 187](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p187)*

---

“Redraw New York State Presbytery Boundary”

Whereas, the current western boundary of the New York State Presbytery includes central New York east and including Monroe, Ontario, Livingston and Steuben counties; and,

Whereas, the current eastern boundary of the Ascension Presbytery includes western New York west and including Orleans, Genesee, Wyoming, and Allegany counties; and,

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church of Wellsville and its teaching elder desires to leave the Ascension Presbytery and affiliate with New York State Presbytery; and

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church of Wellsville is located in Allegany county which is currently within the boundary of the Ascension Presbytery; and,

Whereas, the New York State Presbytery desires to receive the Presbyterian Church of Wellsville and its teaching elder; and

Whereas, the New York State Presbytery desires to initiate and oversee the planting of healthy and reproducing PCA churches in every major city in upstate New York including Buffalo; and Therefore, Be It Resolved that the New York State Presbytery hereby overtures the 30th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to redraw the western boundary of New York State Presbytery and the northeastern New York boundary of Ascension Presbytery, to wit, that the counties currently belonging to the Ascension Presbytery which are in New York State be transferred to New York State Presbytery, thus having all the churches and counties within the geographical bounds of New York State, excepting those churches and counties belonging to Metropolitan New York Presbytery, within New York State Presbytery to be effective July 1, 2002 . COMMUNICATION 5 from Ascension Presbytery (to MNA) “Ascension Concurs with New York State Presbytery on Boundaries” Dear Dr. Taylor, This is to inform you that the Presbytery of the Ascension hereby concurs with the request of the New York State Presbytery that those counties in the state of New York currently within the bounds of the Presbytery of Ascension be ceded to the New York State Presbytery; and that the church (the Presbyterian Church of Wellsville) and teaching elder (TE Kenneth Thompson) currently situated within those counties be transferred to the New York State Presbytery pending their reception by that Presbytery. /s/ Frederick R. Neikirk, Stated Clerk 17. That Communication 9 from Potomac Presbytery be answered by referral to Recommendation 8. Adopted COMMUNICATION 9 from Potomac Presbytery (to MNA) “Potomac Presbytery Opinion on Proposed Blue Ridge Presbytery” Dear Dr. Taylor, Potomac Presbytery, voting at McLean Presbyterian Church, in the Stated Meeting of June 4, 2002, agreed to communicate to the 30th General Assembly the following recommendations concerning the proposed Blue Ridge Presbytery: 1) that the Committee on Mission to North America, and the committee of commissioners appointed with respect to the same, carefully review the boundaries of the proposed Blue Ridge Presbytery in light of the General Assembly Guidelines for Presbytery Boundaries (PCA Digest, 1973-1993, p. 215) [see below], with the understanding that in Potomac’s judgment a presbytery including counties divided by the Blue Ridge Mountains, with such an extensive expanse north and south, appears inconsistent with Guidelines 1, 5, 7, 8, and 10; 2) that it be understood that Potomac agrees to dismiss the Virginia counties of Clarke and Frederick to the proposed Blue Ridge Presbytery, should its creation be approved; 3) that it be understood that Potomac does not agree to dismiss the Virginia county of Fauquier, on the grounds that the county in question is clearly a part of the Washington Metropolitan Area (e.g., see Unites [sic] States Census Bureau definitions), and therefore under Guidelines 4, 5, 7, 8 and 10 ought properly to be within the boundaries of Potomac Presbytery; and 4) that, should the General Assembly approve the creation of the proposed Blue Ridge Presbytery, the General Assembly approve as well the following as the Virginia portion of Potomac Presbytery’s boundaries, effective September 1, 2002: “Virginia counties, together with the cities contained in their border: Arlington, Fairfax, Fauquier, Loudoun, and Prince William;” 5) that the Vice Chairman of the Boundaries Committees, TE David Silvernail, and committee member, TE David Coffin, be appointed by Potomac Presbytery to represent the views of Potomac Presbytery before the General Assembly and its relevant committees. Sincerely, /s/ Richard R. Larson, Clerk Guidelines for Presbytery Boundaries 15th General Assembly, 1987 (1) a presbytery should have a radius of iv z hours maximum driving distance. (2) a presbytery should have a minimum of 10 churches. (3) a presbytery should have a total communicant membership of at least 1000. (4) Presbytery boundaries should not partition metropolitan areas. (5) A presbytery should have regional cohesiveness. (6) A presbytery should have at least 3 churches each having a membership of at least 125 communicant members. (7) Presbytery boundaries should be such that its member churches have the potential for shared ministries. (8) Presbytery boundaries should be such that its member churches have a common commitment to the region within the boundaries and sense their shared responsibility to cover the region with the gospel. (9) When a presbytery reaches 30 churches, it should consider whether subdivision would lead to more effective ministry. (10) A presbytery should limit its boundaries to that geographic area for which it is able to take meaningful responsibility for evangelism and church development. 18.
