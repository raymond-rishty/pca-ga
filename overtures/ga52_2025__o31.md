> *SENT DOWN TO PRESBYTERIES — Proposed amendments to BCO: 8-4, 20-1, 21-1, 21-12, and 23-1; 12-3; 15-5.c; 18-2; 24-1 (two separate amendments); 31-10; 35-9; 36-4; 36-5; 36-8 and 42-2; 41-3; 57-2 [ratifying GA 53rd scheduled June 22-26 2026, not yet convened as of June 17 2026]*

“Amend _BCO_ 31-10 to Require Vote of the Entire Court to Suspend an Officer’s Official Functions during Process”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 31-10 is amended as follows (underlining for additions): **31-10** . when a member of a church court is under process, all his official functions may be administratively suspended at the court’s discretion; but this shall never be done in the way of censure, and this requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the entire court, even if the process is being conducted by a commission. So that _BCO_ 31-10 as amended would read: **31-10** . when a member of a church court is under process, all his official functions may be administratively suspended at the court’s discretion; but this shall never be done in the way of censure, and this requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the court itself, even if the process is being conducted by a commission. **RATIONALE:** There are occasions when a church officer (that is, “a member of a church court”) comes under process. Such process could potentially take a period of time to conclude. At the same time, it may be necessary for a number of reasons, including the honor of religion, the health of the court, and even the well-being of the accused, to suspend the accused from his official functions. Current _BCO_ 31-10 rightly points out that this is not a censure but is only an administrative suspension. Often process is conducted by a _BCO_ 15-3 commission rather than the entire court. Such a commission may be relatively small when compared to the entire court: a Presbytery commission must be at least four ( _BCO_ 15-2), but a Session commission could be as few as two members. In the case of a Teaching Elder, suspending him could result in a significant effect on both the Teaching Elder and his congregation. For example, the church could be required to find pulpit supply during the Teaching Elder’s suspension, or a church could be unable to celebrate the sacraments if another Teaching Elder is unavailable to them. Because a commission is acting _as the court_ ina case of process, the provision of _BCO_ 31-10 can currently be enacted by the members of the commission itself, rather than the larger court. in the case of a six-member commission, for example, that would mean that four members of the larger court could suspend a man from “all his official functions.” This could occur even in a Presbytery which had membership of more than 100 (Teaching Elders and Ruling Elder commissioners). If a court is going to take the step of suspending an officer from “all his official functions,” this should be done by a greater number of officers of the court than a 2/3 majority of a commission. _Adopted by Houston Metro Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 14, 2025_ _Attested by RE /s/ Eric Manthei, stated clerk_
