“Amend _BCO_ 31-10 to Require Vote of the Entire Court to Suspend an Officer’s Official Functions during Process”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 31-10 is amended as follows (underlining for additions): **31-10** . when a member o fa church court i sunder process, all his official functions may be administratively suspended at the court’s discretion; but this shall never be done in the way of censure, and this requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the entire court, even if the process is being conducted by a commission. So that _BCO_ 31-10 as amended would read: **31-10** . when a member o fa church court i sunder process, all his official functions may be administratively suspended at the court’s discretion; but this shall never be done in the way of censure, and this requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the court itself, even if the process is being conducted by a commission. **RATIONALE:** There are occasions when a church officer (that is, “a member of a church court”) comes under process. Such process could potentially take a period of time to conclude. At the same time, it may be necessary for a number of reasons, including the honor of religion, the health of the court, and even the well-being of the accused, to suspend the accused from his official functions. Current _BCO_ 31-10 rightly points out that this is not a censure but is only an administrative suspension. Often process is conducted by a _BCO_ 15-3 commission rather than the entire court.
