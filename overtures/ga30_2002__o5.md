# GA30 O5 — Amend BCO 34-1 to Require Ten Percent of Presbyteries to Assume Original Jurisdiction

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 5 from Ohio Valley Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)  ·  **Disposition:** Approved but not ratified  ·  **BCO:** 34-1

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 216](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p216)*

---

> *ITEM 1 did not receive the concurrence of the necessary number of presbyteries (2/3rds)...34-1...at least ten percent of all the Presbyteries...Defeated FOR: 40 AGAINST: 24*

and Metropolitan New York Presbytery, Uliana Presbytery, Missouri Presbytery, Piedmont Triad Presbytery, North Georgia Presbytery, Evangel Presbytery, Fellowship Presbytery, Tennessee Valley Presbytery, Rocky Mountain Presbytery, North Texas Presbytery, Nashville Presbytery, Susquehanna Valley Presbytery, Northern California Presbytery, North Florida Presbytery, Heartland Presbytery, Central Florida Presbytery, Northern Illinois Presbytery, South Texas Presbytery and Southeast Alabama Presbytery “Require Ten Percent of Presbyteries to Assume Original Jurisdiction Over a Minister in BCO 34-1 - Version 1”

Whereas, the grassroots history of the Presbyterian Church in America indicates her desire to act in unity with the consensus of her churches; and

Whereas, the present wording of Book of Church Order 34-1 allows only two presbyteries to require the General Assembly to decide whether to assume original jurisdiction in judicial process against a minister in another Presbytery; and

Whereas, this standard can allow presbyteries of a particular perspective, and potentially quite few churches, to challenge the ordinary rights of a Presbytery to determine its own membership according to the men and circumstances best known by the local Presbytery; and

Whereas, this standard, in effect, can allow two presbyteries and a few churches, to bring charges against one or many presbyters across Presbytery boundaries and potentially to flood the General Assembly with charges that express the concerns of very few; and

Whereas, such narrow concerns should not be allowed to challenge the ordinary rights of any Presbytery to determine its own membership unless there is a wide consensus in the church that a Presbytery has failed to act properly; Therefore, to ensure that a small minority of presbyteries or churches does not exercise inordinate influence over other churches, presbyteries and the General Assembly in pursuing the discipline of ministers; and to ensure that there is a significant indication of a likely consensus in the denomination that the GA must assume original jurisdiction in such a case for the sake of the purity and testimony of the church, Ohio Valley Presbytery / Metropolitan New York Presbytery / Illiana Presbytery / Missouri Presbytery / Piedmont Triad Presbytery / North Georgia Presbytery / Evangel Presbytery / Fellowship Presbytery / Tennessee Valley Presbytery / Rocky Mountain Presbytery / North Texas Presbytery / Nashville Presbytery / Susquehanna Valley Presbytery / Northern California Presbytery / North Florida Presbytery / Heartland Presbytery / Central Florida Presbytery / Northern Illinois Presbytery / South Texas Presbytery / Southeast Alabama Presbytery overtures the General Assembly to amend the Book of Church Order by changing the words “two other” in BCO 34-1 to “at least ten percent of all the Presbyteries.”
