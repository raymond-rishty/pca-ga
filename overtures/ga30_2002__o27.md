# GA30 O27 — Add to BCO 26-1 a Procedure for Alleged Conflicts Between Scripture and the Constitution

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 27 from Eastern Canada Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 26-1

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 275](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p275)*

---

“Add to BCO 26-1 Procedure for Dealing with Alleged Conflicts Between Scripture and the Constitution of the PCA”

Whereas, the 29th General Assembly found itself in the position that its rules compelled it to distribute in its permanent records material harmful to the reputation of certain people and groups; and

Whereas, the Constitutional Business Committee reported to the 29th General Assembly that we had no option but to follow BCO, even if it appeared to conflict with Scripture, with the only remedy being to amend BCO after the fact to avoid such action in the future; and

Whereas, this was not a matter of judicial process against these people; and

Whereas, this is disobedience to God’s command, as our church understands it (“The ninth commandment requires ... the preserving and promoting of ... the good name of our neighbor, ... a charitable esteem of our neighbors; ... sorrowing for, and covering of their infirmities” [Larger Catechism 144] and forbids “... all prejudicing ... the good name of our neighbors, ... unnecessary discovering of infirmities ...” [Larger Catechism 145], based on Scriptures such as: Prov 17:9 “He who covers a transgression seeks love, but he who repeats a matter separates friends”; 1 Pet 4:8 “And above all things have fervent love for one another, for ‘love will cover a multitude of sins’”; Prov 10:12 “Hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all sins”; Prov 12:16 “A fool’s wrath is known at once, but a prudent man covers shame”); and

Whereas, according to our confessional understanding, this would be an offence even if the statements should be true; and

Whereas, Christ warns us against even the least sin, saying, “if your eye causes you to sin, pluck it out and cast if from you. It is better for you to enter into life with one eye, rather than having two eyes, to be cast into hell fire” (Matthew 18:9); and

Whereas, it is critically important that the PCA keep the Scripture as our supreme authority under God in practice a swell a sin theory; Therefore, the Presbytery of Eastern Canada overtures the 30th General Assembly to add to BCO 26-1: “The Scripture is above the Constitution, and the Westminster Confession and Catechisms as our uniting, agreed interpretation of the Scriptures are above the Book of Church Order; and overrule the Book of Church Order; in case of conflict. a) If a majority of the General Assembly agrees that following the BCO appears to bring the General Assembly, its agencies or any of its members into conflict with the Scriptures, the matter shall be referred to the Constitutional Business Committee. Action shall be stayed on all matters relating to the presumed conflict until the Committee reports. The Committee’s report shall include a proposal for dealing with the matter in a way that eliminates the alleged conflict and a recommendation as to whether or not there is conflict. A three-quarters majority of the Assembly is required at this point to determine there is conflict. If a constitutional amendment is required, the action or part of an action which is deemed to be in conflict with Scripture shall be stayed until the constitutional process is complete. The actual judgment of a judicial commission may not be changed or delayed by this process, but any other proposed action of the Assembly or which appears in the minutes or appendices of the Assembly, including reasons for judicial commission judgments, may be modified under this procedure. b) In the lower courts, similar procedures shall be followed. The court may appoint a constitutional committee, or choose to act as such committee itself. However, if it appears that amendment of the Book of Church Order is necessary, the issue shall be referred to the General Assembly and its Constitutional Business Committee for its action.”
