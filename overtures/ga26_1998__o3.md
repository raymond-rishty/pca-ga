# GA26 O3 — Amend RAO 4 by Adding New Section 18 Allowing Nominations from Committees and Agencies

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 3 From New River Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 192](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p192)*

---

“Amend RAO 4, Adding New Section 18, Allowing Nominations From Committees and Agencies”

Whereas, Article VI of the Bylaws of the PCA permits the Boards of Trustees of Insurance, Annuities & Relief, Covenant College; Covenant Theological Seminary and the PCA Foundation to make requests to the Presbyteries to nominate specific men to their respective boards, and

Whereas, article vi of the bylaws of the PCA permits the boards of trustees of Covenant College, Covenant Theological Seminary and the PCA Foundation to submit to the General Assembly Nominating Committee letters of recommendation concerning particular nominees for their respective boards, and

Whereas, past actions of the general assemblies of the PCA in approving the procedures mentioned above presumes that these Boards have good and valid reasons to be given such permission, and

Whereas, the Bible clearly teaches that there should be no partiality shown in the work of the church (e.g. Deut. 1:17, 10:17; Mark 10:35-45; 1 Timothy 5:21), and

Whereas, the Book of Church Order (14-1-3) spells out this Biblical principal when it states: “The work of the Church as set forth in the Great Commission is one work, being implemented at the General Assembly level through equally essential committees” (emphasis added), and

Whereas, other Committees and Boards of the PCA may feel that permission to communicate with Presbyteries and with the General Assembly Nominating Committee would better enable them to carry out the duties assigned to them by the General Assembly, Therefore Be it Resolved that NEW RIVER PRESBYTERY, meeting at New Martinsville, West Virginia on the 15th day of November, 1997, does hereby overture the 26th General Assembly to add a new Section 4-18 to the rules of assembly Operation, as follows: “Any Committee or Agency of the PCA whose members are elected by the General Assembly through the report of the assembly Nominating Committee may, at its own discretion, make requests to the Presbyteries to nominate specific men to said Committee/Agency and may submit to the Assembly Nominating Committee letters of recommendation concerning particular nominees from the Presbyteries. such requests and recommendations must be made by vote of the full committee/board of trustees and signed by the Chairman or his designee. Notwithstanding these requests and recommendations, the Presbyteries remain free to nominate men of their own choice and the nominating committee is free to present nominees to the General Assembly of their own choice.”
