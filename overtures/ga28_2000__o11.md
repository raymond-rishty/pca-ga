From the Presbytery of Mississippi Valley “Authorize Formation of a Provisional Presbytery in Russia”

**Whereas**, TE Blake Purcell is a member of Mississippi Valley Presbytery, and

**Whereas**, Mississippi Valley Presbytery has authorized TE Blake Purcell to serve out of bounds as an evangelist in St. Petersburg, Russia, and

**Whereas**, Mississippi Valley Presbytery has entrusted to TE Blake Purcell (BCO 8-6) "the power to organize churches, and, until there is a Session in the church so organized, to instruct, examine, ordain, and install ruling elders and deacons therein, and to receive or dismiss members;" **Therefore, Be it Resolved** that Mississippi Valley Presbytery overture the 28th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America, according to BCO 15-6, to exercise its "power to commit to a commission, consisting of not less than three elders, the task of forming a provisional Presbytery in a foreign country [Russia] where there exists no compatible indigenous presbyterian and reformed church. Such a commission shall have authority to act as the Presbytery in all matters pertaining to the establishment and ordering of a national church and shall report annually to the General Assembly. The commission shall be dissolved when there are at least three national teaching elders and three organized churches under its care, and these shall then constitute a separate national church."
