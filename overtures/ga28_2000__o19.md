# GA28 O19 — Dissolve Northeast Presbytery and Form Three New Presbyteries

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 19 From Northeast Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 114](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p114)*

---

“Dissolve Northeast Presbytery; Form Three New Presbyteries” For the 28th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to form three new presbyteries out of the existing northeast presbytery (thereby dissolving the existing Northeast Presbytery) as follows: 1. Southern New England Presbytery— Geographical Boundaries: Connecticut (excluding the CT "panhandle" that is currently in Metro Presbytery), Rhode Island and Massachusetts. 2. New York State Presbytery— Geographical Boundaries: The boundaries include all the Churches, ministers and territories in New York State, excluding those churches, ministers and territories under the jurisdiction of metropolitan new york presbytery and ascension Presbytery ; and further, subject to the approval of ascension Presbytery, to include the churches, ministers and territories within New York State but are currently under the jurisdiction of ascension Presbytery. 3. Northen New England Presbytery— Geographical Boundaries: Maine, New Hampshire, and Vermont Rationale: 1. Geographical-

Whereas, the formation of the above constituted presbyteries would significantly ease the burden of vast geographical distances between churches and would have access to the major travel corridors that move through the region; and

Whereas, the above stated boundaries are reflective of natural travel corridors as follows: Southern New England—A Southern New England Presbytery would significantly ease the burden of vast geographical distances between churches and would have access to the major travel corridors that move through the tri-state region (1-95,1-91,1-84). Northern New England— (1-95,1-93,1-89) New York State— There are some cities and towns in upstate New York which are not presently under any presbytery jurisdiction (Utica, Syracuse, Watertown, Elmira, Coming) and having no PCA churches therein, and these same cities and towns are connected by major interstates (1-90, 1-81 and 1-17) providing opportunities for population growth; and furthermore, there are other cities (Rochester and Buffalo) under the jurisdiction of Ascension Presbytery but have no PCA churches. 2. Mission— whereas the great commission of our savior mandates that the church of jesus christ is to make disciples of all nations and that church planting is the most effective way of obeying this commandment, and

Whereas church planting is best accomplished as from local and regional initiatives that will be further facilitated by more frequent and more regionally based meetings; and whereas the demographic realities of church planting are better understood and accounted for by those closest to that demographic reality, and

Whereas it is more likely for churches if they sense a mission in their own shared regional context to work toward the planting of new churches through a coordinated strategy that is informed by a relatively precise consensus about the demographic realities, and whereas the formation of the above constituted presbyteries would move us closer to a "reasonable size" that would allow for greater participation and cooperation in mission, prevenient shepherding, leadership development and worship 3 History--

Whereas the Northeast Presbytery has spent the last two years working in regional committees in preparation for the creation of three new presbyteries Explanation: in the process leading to the formation of the metro new york Presbytery in 1997, a general consensus was formed about the meaning of presbytery in order to reflect a more authentic regional church model the notion of "Presbytery" as a "regional church" pushed us to think of the Presbytery not simply in judicial terms but also in ministry and mission terms such as to more truly reflect the theology of an organically connected church subsequent to the formation of the metro new york Presbytery, a series of "vision meetings" were held (some in New Haven, others on the floor of presbytery in special session) A consensus was formed for a regional church model that more authentically expressed the prophetic, priestly and kingly dynamics of church ministry (as related then to the ministry of word, worship/prayer and diaconal/shepherding kinds of activities). In order to better approximate these kinds of ministry dynamics in our presbytery, it was decided at the September, 1998 presbytery meeting to restructure the Northeast Presbytery into three sub-regional committees (New York State, Northern New England, and Southern New England) The restructuring resulted in a committee structure which was less "task" based and more "regional" based which was intended to encourage a more holistic approach to doing the work of the gospel as a regional church it is therefore the judgment of the northeast presbytery that the time has now come for these three regional committees to make our new working relationship official with the creation of three new presbyteries,

Whereas, the consent of ascension presbytery is being sought for the release of various churches and territories toward the goal of forming anew york Presbytery. RECOMMENDATION:

Therefore, be it resolved that the 28th General Assembly create three new presbyteries, after the September, 2000 meeting of northeast Presbytery, with the following boundaries: Northern New England Presbytery: including the states of Maine, New Hampshire and Vermont, and Southern New England Presbytery: including the states of Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and that portion of connecticut not included in the Metro Presbytery, and new york state presbytery including that portion of new york state not included in Metro Presbytery, And

be it further resolved that the current Northeast Presbytery be dissolved, after the September 2000 meeting of northeast presbytery information : Current Churches, Attendance, and Teaching Elders by Proposed New Presbyteries 1.
