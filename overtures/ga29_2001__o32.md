# GA29 O32 — Join and Receive Mid-America Presbytery into North Texas Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 32 From Mid-America Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 174](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p174)*

---

"J &amp; R of Mid-America Presbytery into North Texas Presbytery"

Whereas, Mid-America Presbytery, at times in its history having boundaries encompassing all of Oklahoma and parts of Kansas, Missouri, and Arkansas, and as many as ten churches, now, by the departure of churches and territory on several occasions to other expanding presbyteries, has been reduced to only part of the state of Oklahoma and to only four remaining churches with a combined communing membership of between four and five hundred, and

Whereas, Mid-America Presbytery has the fewest churches of any of the contiguous PCA presbyteries, and

Whereas, Mid-America Presbytery does not believe it currently has the resources of churches, presbyters, funds and morale most effectively to staff appropriately presbytery committees and commissions and move toward further growth, and

Whereas, Mid-America Presbytery desires to gain encouragement towards future growth by being a part of a presbytery experiencing growth by church planting and the establishment of campus ministries, and

Whereas, Oklahoma City and a corridor of counties connecting it to Texas are already part of North Texas Presbytery where currently, with the help of the Southwest Church Planting Network, two new Oklahoma City area church plants and a Reformed University Fellowship at the University of Oklahoma are being established, and

Whereas, being received into North Texas Presbytery would immediately provide our churches with the option of participating in the three-presbytery, Southwest Church Planting Network (North Texas, South Texas and Southwest); and

Whereas, our four remaining churches joining with North Texas Presbytery seems the best pathway for an eventual reestablishing of a larger Oklahoma area presbytery as soon as enough particular churches are formed, THEREFORE, BE IT

RESOLVED, that Mid-America Presbytery – pending the consent of North Texas Presbytery to receive us – petitions the 29th General Assembly meeting in Dallas, Texas, in June, 2001, to be allowed to be joined and received, with our four particular churches, their officers and members of the presbytery, and all remaining Oklahoma counties into North Texas Presbytery, effective July 1, 2001, this action effectively dissolving Mid-America Presbytery. Adopted at the 40th Stated Meeting of Mid-America Presbytery, on April 20, 2001. Attested by: /s/ John Owen Butler, Stated Clerk COMMUNICATION 1 from North Texas Presbytery May 3, 2001 L. Roy Taylor, Stated Clerk RE: Mid-America Presbytery Overture to 29th GA to Join NTP Fathers and Brothers: At the 66th stated meeting of the North Texas Presbytery held April 27-28, 2001 at Park Cities Presbyterian Church, the Presbytery passed the following motion: “M&amp;P that the North Texas Presbytery inform the 29th General Assembly that it concurs with the overture of Mid-America Presbytery to dissolve and to join the NTP.” This effectively places all Indian Territory within the boundary of the NTP. Yours in Christ, /s/ T. John Mulkey, Stated Clerk North Texas Presbytery
