# GA28 O6 — Amend BCO 38-4 to Clarify Administrative Removal from Membership Roll

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 6 From Cherokee Presbyterian Church  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 16-4, 38-4

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 288](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p288)*

---

“Amend BCO 16-4 to Allow that Exceptions May Not Be Taught” the session of the cherokee presbyterian church respectfully requests that the North Georgia Presbytery nevertheless forward for consideration by the PCA ’s General Assembly in June, 2000, the following overture (to be accompanied by notice of the Presbytery’s negative vote on the overture at its fall meeting): We hereby request the 2000 General Assembly of the PCA proceed with the process to add to the BCO, Chapter 16 on Church Orders the following Paragraph 16-4: “When the process of examination reveals any exceptions to the constitutional standards that the relevant court judges, if taught, to be harmful to the peace and well-being of the church (though not serious enough to disqualify the candidate for office), it may by a majority vote direct the candidate for office not to teach the exception.” Rationale: this clarifies by making explicit to all what is understood by many to bean implicit power of the courts of the church. thank you for your consideration of this request. Letter dated January 7, 2000 and signed by Rev. Ted Lester, Moderator, for the session of cherokee presbyterian church of Woodstock, Georgia. NOTE: North Georgia Presbytery had previously rendered a negative vote upon its earlier consideration. at the last meeting of the presbytery of north Georgia, on January 15, 2000, the Committee on Bills and Overtures in their report, instructed the clerk to forward this overture to the office of the stated Clerk, Presbyterian Church in America, for handling. 11. That OVERTURE 13 from Susquehanna Valley Presbytery (“Re-Ordination of Former PCA Ministers”) be answered in the negative Adopted Grounds: The procedure, if desired, is already available to the Presbytery via the “extraordinary” clause of BCO 21-4. NOTE: the premise of the first “Whereas” is not well founded. A presbytery may do as Potomac Presbytery in such cases, e.g., adopt a resolution as follows: “That, upon review as required by BCO 13-2 and 34-10, and finding no culpable dereliction, nor evident lack of acceptance by the church, the following Teaching Elders be continued on the roll w/o call: — — ----------. This continuance is approved with the understanding that it shall be reviewed annually, and with the understanding that each candidate shall have the responsibility to demonstrate that a continued absence of call is not the result of “breach of his covenant engagement”, or “lack of acceptance to the church” (cf. b pp ,.A.2.i.).”
