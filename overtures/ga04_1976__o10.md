# GA4 O10 — Establish Annual Special Offering and Increase Funding for Christian Education and Publications

**Assembly:** 4th (1976)  ·  **Source:** Covenant Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga04_1976 p. 34](../markdown/ga04_1976.md#ga04-p34)*

---

The following overture to the 4th General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America was adopted by Covenant Presbytery on July 6, 1976.

Whereas the First General Assembly of the National Presbyterian Church chose to place itself in the historic tradition of Old School Presbyterianism by its adoption of a Message To All The Churches Of Jesus Christ and in that document specifically set forth its intention to be the agent rather than merely the superintendent of every aspect of the Lord’s work; and

Whereas the subsequent development of the Church shows the consistent application of that principal to foreign missions, home missions, and Sunday schools; Therefore, Covenant Presbytery respectfully overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to appoint a special committee to make recommendations to the next Assembly concerning the following: 1. That the General Assembly study the propriety of training men for the ministry in institutions which are not under the discipline of the Presbyterian Church in America 2. That the General Assembly reconsider its approval of any college or seminary now recognized or endorsed which may permit women to enter degree programs designed to equip men for ordination to the Gospel ministry. 3. That the General Assembly require formal agreements of co-operation similar to those developed by the Mission to the World Committee from any college or seminary recommended to our churches for the preparation of men for the ministry. 4.
