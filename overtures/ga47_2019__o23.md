# GA47 O23 — Withdraw from the National Association of Evangelicals

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 23 from Central Carolina Presbytery (to IRC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 651](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p651)*

---

“Withdraw from the National Association of Evangelicals”

**Whereas** the Presbyterian Church in America is a member of the National Association of Evangelicals (NAE); and

**Whereas** the label “evangelical” has come to designate political distinctives as much or more than theological commitments and distinctives; [8] and

**Whereas** Chapter 31 of the _Westminster Confession of Faith_ states that “Synods and councils are to handle, or conclude nothing, but that which is ecclesiastical: and are not to intermeddle with civil affairs which concern the commonwealth, unless by way of humble petition in cases extraordinary; or, by way of advice, for satisfaction of conscience, if they be thereunto required by the civil magistrate.”; and

**Whereas** PCA _BCO_ 3-3 states that “The sole functions of the Church, as a kingdom and government distinct from the civil commonwealth, are to proclaim, to administer, and to enforce the law of Christ revealed in the Scriptures.”; and

**Whereas** the Scriptures clearly teach that Christians should be stewards of God’s creation, care for sojourners and aliens, and promote matters of civil justice, and members of the PCA may have legitimate differences of opinion on the most effective political policies to address these matters; and

**Whereas** the NAE has stated that it intends not only to advocate for biblical values and ethics in general, but that “we must advocate for political policies;” [9] and 8 Benjamin Huskinson. “The Missing Link between Evangelicals and Trump.” The[American-Interest.com. (https://www.the-american-interest.com/2018/11/23/the-missing-](https://www.the-american-interest.com/2018/11/23/the-missing-link-between-evangelicals-and-trump/) [link-between-evangelicals-and-trump/)](https://www.the-american-interest.com/2018/11/23/the-missing-link-between-evangelicals-and-trump/) 9 National Association of Evangelicals. “For the Health of a Nation: An Evangelical Call to [Civic Responsibility.” NAE.net (https://www.nae.net/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/For-the-](https://www.nae.net/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/For-the-Health-of-the-Nation_spreads.pdf) [Health-of-the-Nation_spreads.pdf)](https://www.nae.net/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/For-the-Health-of-the-Nation_spreads.pdf)

**Whereas** the NAE has frequently intermeddled in civil affairs, by publicly pushing for action on the environment [10], immigration [11], and has changed its original stance on the death penalty [12] ; and

**Whereas** the NAE has recently passed a motion entitled “Fairness for All” [13] which, in advocating for a political compromise regarding sexual orientation, gender identity, and religious freedom, goes beyond Chapter 31 of the _Westminster Confession of Faith_ ; and

**Whereas** the NAE claims to speak for all of its members and to be their voice in Washington; and

**Whereas** these are only a few of many examples of the NAE’s continuing practice of intermeddling in civil affairs; and

**Whereas** our sister denomination the RPCNA earlier withdrew from the NAE in 2009 citing “President Leith Anderson’s participation in the meeting between Christians and Muslims where the document ‘Loving God and Neighbor Together: A Christian Response to “A Common Word Between Us and You”’ was approved and signed,” and noting that “the document is clearly based on an unbiblical premise. It falsely assumes that Christianity and Islam approach the same God, but in different ways.” [14] ; and`

**Whereas** no other NAPARC denominations are members of the NAE; the Southern Baptist Convention is not a member of the NAE; the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod is not a member of the NAE; no Anglican bodies are members of the NAE; no predominantly African American denominations are members of the NAE; no mainline denominations are members of the NAE; the Roman Catholic Church is not a member of the NAE; and no Orthodox communions are members of the NAE; and
