The 24th and 25th General Assemblies received overtures from at least four presbyteries requesting the separation of reformed university ministries from mission to north america for the purpose of establishing reformed university ministries as a permanent committee of the PCA. Included in the General Assembly’s responses to these overtures is this motion, approved by the 24th General Assembly: The General Assembly believes that constituting Reformed University Ministries as a new permanent committee may have significant advantages and disadvantages for Reformed University Ministries, GA/MNA and for the General Assembly of the PCA. Further, the change proposed has never been formally considered by GA/MNA or by General Assembly. Therefore, the General Assembly recognizes that GA/MNA Committee has appointed a study committee to study the relationship of reformed university Ministries and GA/MNA, and awaits the report of its findings and recommendations to the 25th General Assembly, by normal process, through the GA/MNA Committee. For the following reasons, the GA/MNA Committee believes it is expedient that the GA/MNA committee be released from responsibility for the oversight of reformed University Ministries: ■ the rapid growth of reformed university ministries has been nurtured by the protection and wisdom of GA/MNA during its critical formative years. Reformed University Ministries will continue to mature as it assumes full responsibility under the Lord for its future. ■ The size, scope and budget of reformed university ministries have grown to a place of parity with that of GA/MNA, which legitimizes a move from a subordinate ministry relationship to a full ministry partnership. ■ Reformed University Ministries’ distinctive mission, philosophy of ministry, and leadership have matured to the place where further expansion (both national and international), require that Reformed University Ministries fully accept the privileges and responsibilities of a permanent program committee, accountable to the PCA General Assembly. ■ The 24th and 25th General Assemblies received overtures from PCA Presbyteries urging that Reformed University Ministries be permitted to become a permanent program committee of the Presbyterian Church in America.
