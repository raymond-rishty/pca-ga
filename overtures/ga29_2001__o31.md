# GA29 O31 — Amend RAO 15-1 to Bar Denominational Employees from Serving on the SJC

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 31 From Mid-America Presbytery (to B&amp;O)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 233](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p233)*

---

“Declare Denominational Employees Not Eligible For Membership On SJC”

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church in America has prided itself on being a "grass roots" denomination; and

Whereas, the PCA has put this principle into effect with respect to the Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) by prohibiting anyone from serving on the SJC and a Permanent Committee of the General Assembly simultaneously (BCO 15-4); and

Whereas, the same principle is seen to be at work in that "Employees of the Assembly's committees or agencies are not eligible for office on an Assembly's committee or agency which administers matters directly related to their area of employment" (RAO 4-7); and

Whereas, the work of the SJC covers the denomination as a whole; and

Whereas, at present, four of the twenty-four members of the SJC are denominational employees; and

Whereas, it seems prudent that denominational employees should not serve simultaneously on the SJC; Now Therefore, Be It Resolved, that Mid-America Presbytery hereby overtures the 29th General Assembly to declare that no denominational employee is eligible to serve on the SJC, excepting those already elected before General Assembly’s ruling on this overture; **And Be It Further Resolved** that Mid-America Presbytery hereby overtures the 29th General Assembly to amend the Rules for Assembly Operations by adding to RAO 15-1 a sentence, immediately after the first sentence, as follows: &gt; "No one who is employed by a denominational committee or agency may serve on the Standing Judicial Commission." Adopted at the 40th Stated Meeting of Mid-America Presbytery, on April 20, 2001. Attested by: /s/ John Owen Butler, Stated Clerk
