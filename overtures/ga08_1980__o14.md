# GA8 O14 — Amend BCO and RAO on calling a special Assembly

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Session of Westminster Presbyterian Church, Fort Walton Beach, Florida

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 40](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p40)*

---

(Note: This overture was presented to Gulf Coast Presbytery, but was not adopted by Presbytery.)

Whereas, the Book of Church Order 15:5 now allows for forty (40) men from seven (7) Presbyteries to constitute a quorum for General Assembly;

Whereas, this could allow a General Assembly to be called by a special interest minority;

Whereas, the Book of Church Order 26:3 requires a minimum of 1/6th of the total congregation for a congregational meeting; Therefore, the Session of Westminster Presbyterian Church, Fort Walton Beach, Florida, proposes that the Book of Church Order and the Operating Rules for General Assembly be amended as follows: 1. That the number of Presbyteries required to call a General Assembly be at least 1/4th of the total number of Presbyteries; 2. That the number of Commissioners required to call a General Assembly be the same as the required number fora congregational meeting in the Book of Church Order 26:3, at least 1/6th of the total number of Commissioners. 3. That the total number of Teaching and Ruling Elders who are eligible to attend should be the whole number upon which the 1/6th fraction is based. 4. That if or when the fraction of Commissioners on the floor of General Assembly drops below the number required for a called Assembly, that the General Assembly suspend business until a true quorum can be obtained, not just a percentage of those enrolled. If this true quorum cannot be obtained, General Assembly should be declared adjourned by the Moderator. Adopted by the Session of Westminster Presbyterian Church, Fort Walton Beach, Florida on November 11,1979. Attested: M.C. Hull Clerk of Session C. Don Darling Moderator of Session Answered in the negative. § 8-88, III, 16; p. 118.
