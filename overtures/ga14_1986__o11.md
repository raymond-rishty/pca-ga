# GA14 O11 — Require Parties in Judicial Cases to Appear Personally Before the Commission

**Assembly:** 14th (1986)  ·  **Source:** Eastern Carolina Presbytery

*Source: [ga14_1986 p. 51](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p51)*

---

Whereas, there are many Judicial Cases that come annually to the General Assembly, and Whereas, in many cases those directly involved in those Judicial Cases are often not present at the Assembly, and Whereas, it is difficult for the Judicial Commission to seek to render a decision in such a case, and Whereas, it is unfair to the parties involved for the Commission to seek to render a decision in such cases, Therefore, be it resolved that Eastern Carolina Presbytery overtures the Fourteenth General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America that in each Judicial Case the General Assembly cite the parties involved to appear and present their cases personally. Motion approved at Stated Meeting, July 20, 1985. Attest: James B. Moore, Stated Clerk Answered in the negative, 14-52, 26, p. 126.
