# GA8 O31 — Reconsider the site of the 1982 General Assembly

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Central Florida  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 33](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p33)*

---

Whereas, the Committee on Administration of the Presbyterian Church in America has decided against pursuing the possibility of a meeting of the General Assembly in Pittsburgh in 1982; and

Whereas, the-specific reason for this decision was the potential embarrassment to the dignity and the integrity of the PCA's relationship to the Reformed Presbyterian Church of North America; a church which shares with the PCA membership in the North American Presbyterian and Reformed Council and whose facilities the PCA would have been using at the very time that Church would have been meeting in Grand Rapids, Michigan, with the other Churches ofNAPARC;and

Whereas, both proper decorum and courtesy would suggest that the PCA honor its commitment to practice fellowship with the Churches in NAPARC; and

Whereas, new developments have surfaced in the relationships enjoyed between the PCA and the Reformed Presbyterian Church, Evangelical Synod, the Orthodox Presbyterian Church and the Reformed Presbyterian Church of North America, as a result of which a concurrent meeting of the general assemblies and General Synods would seem to be most useful at this time; and

Whereas, the travel distance does not seem to be a prohibitive consideration, it being farther from Jackson, Mississippi, to Fort Lauderdale, Florida, where the Assembly is scheduled to meet in 1981, than it is from Jackson to Grand Rapids; now therefore. Be It Resolved, that the Central Florida Presbytery does overture the General Assembly to reconsider its decision on the site of the 1982 General Assembly meeting, with a view of joini
