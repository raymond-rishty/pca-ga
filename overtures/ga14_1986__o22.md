# GA14 O22 — Amend BCO 14-2 to Establish a Delegated General Assembly

**Assembly:** 14th (1986)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Western Carolina  ·  **Disposition:** Approved → sent to presbyteries; ratification not located  ·  **BCO:** 14-2

*Source: [ga14_1986 p. 76](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p76)*

---

> *All BCO 14-2 overtures at the 14th GA (including Overture 22 from Western Carolina Presbytery proposing a delegated assembly) were answered by reference to Ad Interim Committee Recommendation 2 (See 14-31, 2, p. 106), which was itself recommitted rather than adopted. The 15th GA subsequently rejected the delegated assembly concept outright: 'proposing a delegated assembly was defeated by vote of 3*

Whereas, the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America as presently constituted bears the potential of a gathering of some 2,500 commissioners; and

Whereas, the work of a deliberative body is more efficient and productive when it is of a manageable size; and

Whereas, a limited number of commissioners elected by each Presbytery will reduce the cost of otherwise large and expensive gatherings; and

Whereas, we are called and committed to responsible stewardship in our oversight of God's people; and

Whereas, a limited number of commissioners elected by a Presbytery shall bring forth the selection of those best qualified to serve; Now

Therefore, be it resolved, that the Presbytery of Western Carolina overturn the General Assembly to re-draft Chapter 14-2 of the Book of Church Order to provide for a delegated General Assembly. Attest: W. Donald Munson, Jr., Stated Clerk See 14-31, 2, p. 106. 12-10, B, p. 51
