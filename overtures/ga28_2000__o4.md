# GA28 O4 — Divide Westminster Presbytery into Two Presbyteries

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 4 From James River Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 291](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p291)*

---

“Clarify use of professional counsel in cases of Process”

Whereas, BCO 32-19 forbids the use of professional counsel “as such” in cases of process but permits an accused to be represented before the session of the church of the accused by a member of that church, and before any other court by a member of that court; and

Whereas, the phrase “as such” is subject to different interpretations; and

Whereas, the limitation on representation before a court higher than the Session may destroy the continuity of representation of the accused and otherwise prejudice his defense, Therefore, Be it Resolved that the James River Presbytery respectfully overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to clarify, amend, and restate BCO 32-19 so that it will read in its entirety as follows: 32-19 An accused person may be counseled and represented before the Session or any other court by any communing member of the same particular church of which the accused is a member, or by any member o for commissioner to a court before which the accused appears, provided, however, that the individual serving as counsel may not serve for a fee or other remuneration Notwithstanding the foregoing, payments may be made to reimburse or cover any direct expenses incurred or to be incurred by such counsel in the course of representation. If such counsel is a member o for commissioner to the court before which the accused appears, he may not sit in judgment in the case. adopted at the spring stated meeting of james river presbytery on april 8, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Eugene H. Friedline, Stated Clerk
