# GA28 O4 — Divide Westminster Presbytery into Two Presbyteries

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 4 From James River Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 291](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p291)*

“Clarify use of professional counsel in cases of Process”

Whereas, BCO 32-19 forbids the use of professional counsel “as such” in cases of process but permits an accused to be represented before the session of the church of the accused by a member of that church, and before any other court by a member of that court; and

Whereas, the phrase “as such” is subject to different interpretations; and

Whereas, the limitation on representation before a court higher than the Session may destroy the continuity of representation of the accused and otherwise prejudice his defense, Therefore, Be it Resolved that the James River Presbytery respectfully overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to clarify, amend, and restate BCO 32-19 so that it will read in its entirety as follows: 32-19 An accused person may be counseled and represented before the Session or any other court by any communing member of the same particular church of which the accused is a member, or by any member o for commissioner to a court before which the accused appears, provided, however, that the individual serving as counsel may not serve for a fee or other remuneration Notwithstanding the foregoing, payments may be made to reimburse or cover any direct expenses incurred or to be incurred by such counsel in the course of representation. If such counsel is a member o for commissioner to the court before which the accused appears, he may not sit in judgment in the case. adopted at the spring stated meeting of james river presbytery on april 8, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Eugene H. Friedline, Stated Clerk 13. That OVERTURE 7 and COMMUNICATION 1 from North Florida Presbytery (“Decry contentious practices of the “Presbyterian and Reformed News”) be answered in the negative. Adopted Grounds: The Overture would improperly have the Assembly conclude as grounds for the requested action censurable offenses without due process, or would restrain improperly and unwisely dissemination of information in the Church. Further the Overture appears to unwisely call into question the supreme value of an independent press to a church whose authority is grounded in the willing compliance of an informed conscience and whose testimony to the truth is, humanly speaking, enabled by full and free debate. The overture appears to neglect entirely the essential place such an independent press has had in the history of the conservative movements in both northern and southern Presbyterian Churches, and the similar language of condemnation employed by those who found the views then expressed disagreeable. The wisest and most profitable response of presbyters to publications with which they disagree is to publish their own views, setting forth the truth, correcting errors, and evidencing both formally and materially the virtues that are rightly lauded in the Overture. Minority Report on OVERTURE 7 and COMMUNICATION 1 That Overture 7 and Communication 1 from the presbytery of north florida be answered as follows: The Presbyterian Church in America affirms that the only official publications authorized to record the story of the PCA are those sanctioned by the General Assembly. All other publications represent only the views and opinions of individuals within the denomination. Christian forbearance requires that these publications and the people responsible for them should be treated with the respect due to any minority view within the church. Moreover, we affirm the great value of an independent press in assuring full and free debate within the church and the role such publications have played in the history of the conservative movements in both the Northern and Southern Presbyterian Churches. The issues raised by the Overture from North Florida are serious, especially the allegations of possible violations of scripture and the subordinate standards However, the General Assembly is not the proper forum in which to investigate these reports. Consequently, the Assembly encourages the Presbytery of north florida to bring the charges to the courts of original jurisdiction to investigate and take whatever action is deemed appropriate. TE Craig Childs TE Stephen Estock TE Paul Frick TE Bill Lamkin RE Bill McClay TE Charles McGowan
