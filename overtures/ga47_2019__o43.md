“Formation of a Study Committee on Domestic Abuse and Sexual Assault” (Version 8)

**Whereas**, the sins of domestic abuse, including physical, emotional, and verbal abuse, and sexual assault are expressly forbidden in scripture (Deuteronomy 22:25-27; Eph. 5:25-29; 2 Timothy 3:2-5; 1 Peter 3:7; Jude 7; Exodus 21:15; also WLC 135 and 139); and

**Whereas**, these sins are especially heinous because they are “against the express letter of the law,” “break many commandments,” “admit of no reparation,” and often involve various other aggravations (WLC 151); and

**Whereas**, these sins are grievously prevalent in our society as well as the Church; and

**Whereas**, these sins cause a complex set of physical, emotional, spiritual, and psychological responses—including immense shame, fear and anxiety, depression, trauma, isolation, anger, and denial—that persist with varying levels of severity throughout life; and since these heinous sins do lasting damage to every aspect of a victims life (e.g., 2 Samuel 13:1-22); and

**Whereas**, it is the responsibility of the elders of the church to exercise their authority, severally and jointly, to shepherd those who are under their care by guarding against such sinful behavior as much as possible, by wisely caring for the vulnerable and those victimized by the sins of others, and by appropriately disciplining those guilty of such sins toward a full repentance in each case in accordance with applicable law (Ezekiel 34:1-24; James 1:27; BCO 8-3; 27-4); and
