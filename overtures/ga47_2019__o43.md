# GA47 O43 — Form Study Committee on Domestic Abuse & Sexual Assault

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 43 from Metro Atlanta Presbytery (to AC, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 705](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p705)*

---

“Formation of a Study Committee on Domestic Abuse and Sexual Assault” (Version 8)

**Whereas**, the sins of domestic abuse, including physical, emotional, and verbal abuse, and sexual assault are expressly forbidden in scripture (Deuteronomy 22:25-27; Eph. 5:25-29; 2 Timothy 3:2-5; 1 Peter 3:7; Jude 7; Exodus 21:15; also WLC 135 and 139); and

**Whereas**, these sins are especially heinous because they are “against the express letter of the law,” “break many commandments,” “admit of no reparation,” and often involve various other aggravations (WLC 151); and

**Whereas**, these sins are grievously prevalent in our society as well as the Church; and

**Whereas**, these sins cause a complex set of physical, emotional, spiritual, and psychological responses—including immense shame, fear and anxiety, depression, trauma, isolation, anger, and denial—that persist with varying levels of severity throughout life; and since these heinous sins do lasting damage to every aspect of a victims life (e.g., 2 Samuel 13:1-22); and

**Whereas**, it is the responsibility of the elders of the church to exercise their authority, severally and jointly, to shepherd those who are under their care by guarding against such sinful behavior as much as possible, by wisely caring for the vulnerable and those victimized by the sins of others, and by appropriately disciplining those guilty of such sins toward a full repentance in each case in accordance with applicable law (Ezekiel 34:1-24; James 1:27; BCO 8-3; 27-4); and

**Whereas**, these sins have been a concerning subject of attention not only in other denominations (cf. the Roman Catholic Church and the Southern Baptist Convention) but even in the proceedings of the 46th General Assembly of the PCA; and

**Whereas**, the 42nd General Assembly previously exhorted the PCA and its courts to respond boldly against one form of these sins, namely the sexual abuse of children; and

**Whereas**, the PCA report on “Divorce and Remarriage” was limited in its focus and does not adequately define the scope and nature of domestic abuse and sexual assault nor adequately address many pastoral concerns regarding the care of those victimized by such sins; and

**Whereas**, no standardized resources for pastoral care on these sins and their complex consequences exist for officers, church courts, and agencies in the PCA; and

**Whereas**, no clear guidelines for policy or practice with regard to these sins exist for the officers, church courts, and agencies in the PCA that would protect individuals and congregations; and

**Whereas**, the Rules of Discipline protect against unjust accusation ( _BCO_ 31 8), they also make no consideration for the unique heinousness of these sins and their practical effect on victims;

**Therefore, be it resolved** that Metro Atlanta Presbytery hereby overtures the 47th General Assembly of the PCA to form a committee to study: (1) the best practices and policy guidelines for elders, church courts, and agencies for justly and lawfully protecting against these sins, for providing safety for potential victims who come forward, and for responding to those guilty of these sins; and (2) the possibility of changes to the Rules of Discipline that would appropriately protect victims of these sins within a biblical process; and (3) the pastoral resources available for the care of survivors of these sins as well as ministry and counsel for those guilty of such sins; (4) the possible need for repentance as a church; and (5) to include in their report a definition of the scope and nature of domestic abuse and sexual assault; and

**Be it further resolved** that the committee shall be appointed by the moderator, and shall include among its members or advisory members at least two subject-matter experts and at least two women; and

**Be it further resolved** that the budget for the study committee be set at $15,000/year and that funds be derived from gifts to the AC designated for that purpose, with **Metro Atlanta** Presbytery committed to offer $ **1,000** ; and

**Be it further resolved** that the committee shall present their findings and recommendations for consideration by the 48th General Assembly. _Adopted by Metro Atlanta Presbytery at its stated meeting, May 7, 2019_ _Attested by /s/ TE Randy Schlichting, stated clerk_
