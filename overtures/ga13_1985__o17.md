# GA13 O17 — Commit BCO Chapter 58 to Study for Refinement & Clarity

**Assembly:** 13th (1985)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Missouri  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga13_1985 p. 49](../markdown/ga13_1985.md#ga13-p49)*

---

Whereas, questions have been raised about exceptions to the Book of Church Order; and

Whereas, the 1984 General Assembly began to formulate answers to this question on the floor by adopting two separate motions, (1) the first of which indicated that exceptions to the BCO are to be handled in a different manner from exceptions to the Confession of Faith because Ordination Question 3 asks “merely” approval of the form of government and discipline, and (2) the second of which indicated that exceptions to the BCO dealing with a matter spoken to in the Westminster Confession or Catechisms shall be dealt with as exceptions to the Confession or Catechisms; and

Whereas, these motions did not directly address the heart of the Ordination Question 3 (BCO 21-5), namely, “the general principles of Biblical polity” which are not dealt with in The Confession of Faith or Catechisms;

Therefore, be it resolved that the answers of these two adopted motions be further clarified by the addition to these motions of the following motion: “Further, when an exception to the BCO is dealing with a matter that is itself one of the ‘general principles of Biblical polity,’ the exception shall be dealt with as the presbytery deals with an exception to the Westminster Confession of Faith or Catechisms.” Adopted at the Winter Meeting on January 18-19, 1985. Attest: Albert F. Moginot, Jr., Stated Clerk Answered in the affirmative, 13-45, III, 42, p. 107.
