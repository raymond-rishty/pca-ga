# GA8 O17 — Change the boundaries of Pacific Presbytery

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Pacific Presbytery

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 42](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p42)*

---

Whereas, the boundaries of Presbyteries are determined by the General Assembly (BCO 15-6) and

Whereas, the present boundaries of Pacific Presbytery include the states of Arizona, California, New Mexico, Hawaii, Oregon, and Washington;

Whereas, the overture of the churches of Arizona and New Mexico to the Seventh General Assembly meeting, June, 1979, to be established as a separate Presbytery was denied primarily because ‘to remove some of the stronger churches of the Presbytery would further hinder resolutions of problems presently existing in Pacific Presbytery"

Whereas, in the time since the Seventh General Assembly, Pacific Presbytery has been strengthened with the organization of three churches in California and the reception of a large congregation in Washington; and Arizona and New Mexico churches have been strengthened with the resolving of internal problems; and with the developing of two new works. And

Whereas, the cost of travel is still a major problem with the great distances involved. Not merely to get to Presbytery meetings to have a quorum, but for committees to function properly; and the assistance provided by Mission to the United States is inadequate to cover these needs. Therefore, the session of Prescott Presbyterian Church in America respectfully overtures the January. 1980 Stated Meeting of Pacific Presbytery to overture the Eighth General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church In America meeting in June. 1980 to form the states of Arizona and New Mexico as a separate Presbytery effective at the conclusion of the 1980 General Assembly. Adopted by Pacific Presbytery January 26. 1980. Attest: William F. Woodhall Stated Clerk Answered in the affirmative. § 8-50, I II, 6; p. 86.
