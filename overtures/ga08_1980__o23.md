# GA8 O23 — Limit the Judicial Committee to properly appealed cases

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Southern Florida

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 41](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p41)*

---

Whereas, the Presbyterian Church in America holds to the form of Presbyterian government in which Church Sessions, Presbyteries and the General Assembly are courts of the Church with defined powers. And whereas, it has come to our attention that the Judicial Committee of the General Assembly has attempted to give advice and interpretation to individuals or congregations prior to seeking the advice of the Presbyteries which would have original jurisdiction, and Whereas, the General Assembly has at times not agreed with the interpretation or advice of the Judicial Committee making the committee’s efforts futile and their advice incorrect as expressing the mind of the Assembly, Now Therefore Be It Resolved, that the Presbytery of Southern Florida request the General Assembly to instruct the Judicial Committee to handle only those cases which come properly by way of appeal after first coming before the courts of original jurisdiction, namely the Church Sessions, and the Presbyteries, and that churches or individuals with grievances should first proceed through proper channals of the Presbyterian Church system before going to the committees of the General Assembly! Attest Earl Mizell Stated Clerk Answered in the negative. § 8-88, III, 21; p. 119.
