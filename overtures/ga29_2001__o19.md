# GA29 O19 — Ask CCB to Examine SJC Minutes in John Wood Matter

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** Presbytery of the Ascension to ask CCB to Examine the Minutes of the SJC  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 143](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p143)*

---

In response to the Overture from the Presbytery of the Ascension, the CCB understands the SJC to have acted in accord with GA’s directive to proceed according to the provisions of BCO 31-2 which required the SJC to enter an “investigation” phase of the matter before determining if there was sufficient evidence that “should result in raising a strong presumption of the guilt of the party involved” before instituting process and proceeding to trial. Presbyteries were informed by letter (dated August 29, 2000) of : (1) their right to submit evidence supporting their allegations to the SJC panel during this investigative phase; and (2) the time and place to submit evidentiary material related to the investigation. The understanding of the SJC was that further theological argument was appropriate for a trial, should an indictment have been rendered. Since no indictment was made, however, on the basis of the evidence presented by all notified parties it would have been inappropriate to try John Wood on the basis of theological arguments against matters neither he, nor the SJC, felt he supported. Had the matter moved to trial, it would have been proper for the presbyteries to make arguments for their allegations of theological error. The CCB acknowledges that, according to “Whereas #7-9” in Ascension Presbytery’s Overture, it is unfortunate that there was no formal, written response from the SJC to Ascension Presbytery’s inquiry following the panel’s initial contact and prior to its hearing.
