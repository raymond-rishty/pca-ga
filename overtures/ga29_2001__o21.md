# GA29 O21 — Amend BCO 13-1 to Allow Two Ruling Elders Per Congregation in Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 21 From Ellisville Presbyterian Church (to B&amp;O &amp; CCB)  ·  **Disposition:** Approved & ratified (2002)  ·  **Ratified**  ·  **BCO:** 13-1

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 221](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p221)*

---

“Amend BCO 13-1 to Allow at Least Two Ruling Elders to Represent Each Congregation in Presbytery Meetings”

Whereas, the formation of the Presbyterian Church in America was the result of the Lord, in His Providence, convicting elders of the need to remain true to the Biblical principles and teachings upon which our faith is based, and

Whereas, our elder fathers in the faith were the first to point out and take a stand against errors being made and taught in the old Southern church, and

Whereas, John Edward Richard, in his History of the Presbyterian Church in America (1986, p. 131) posits, “There seems to be a desire in the pre-birth movement of the new church to give right concern and positions to all officers, whether lay or ministerial. We may even hope to return to decentralized polity so wisely advocated by James H. Thornwell near the birth of the Southern church,” and

Whereas, the Scriptures of the Old Testament in 1 Kings 12:1-5, 18, which record King Rehoboam’s fateful rejecting and discarding of the wisdom of “the elders (who) had stood before his father Solomon while he still lived” and how Rehoboam instead consulted and followed only the advice of “the young men who had grown up with him, who stood before him,” clearly demonstrate the tragic results of rejecting and discarding the wisdom of old and wise elders, and

Whereas, the Scriptures of the New Testament in 1 Timothy 3:1-7, especially require that the elder be a man who “is not a novice” plainly declaring the desirability and advantage of those elders who are men not only of demonstrable godliness but of rich and lengthy experience in walking with the Lord, that they may wisely serve in the courts of the church, and

Whereas, ruling elders have played an integral part in the organization and growth of the Presbyterian Church in America, serving diligently in all courts of the church, and

Whereas, the parity of the eldership is compromised when the composition of church courts contain substantially more teaching elder commissioners than ruling elder commissioners, and

Whereas, in 1999, 2873 teaching elders held membership in the 58 presbyteries numbering 1206 churches (Minutes, 28th General Assembly), and

Whereas, in presbytery meetings, 2873 teaching elders might dominate the business of the church brought from the Sessions of the 1206 churches, and could exert clergical [sic] influence at the presbytery court level that may disregard the needs of grass roots Session courts, and

Whereas, Proverbs 11:14 and 24:6 make it clear that the more counsel (wisdom) brought to the issues before the church, the greater the probability the building of the kingdom will be achieved, and

Whereas, the more participation by ruling elders at the lower court levels (Session and Presbytery), the greater will be the probability that there will be interest in and counsel provided by ruling elders on the issues and problems that confront the churches at the grass roots level of the denomination, and

Whereas, ruling elders serve the church generally, when called thereunto (BCO 8-3; Acts 20:28f), as well as the local congregation, and

Whereas, the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America early recognized the need for more ruling elder involvement and participation in said Court by amending the Book of Church Order so as to increase the number of ruling elder commissioners from each church from one (1) to two (2), with additional commissioners for larger congregations; and

Whereas, there is a similar need for more ruling elder involvement and participation in the church presbytery court,

Now therefore Be it Resolved that the undersigned Session overtures the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to amend to Book of Church Order, 13-1, to increase the number of ruling elder commissioners to presbytery meetings from each church from one (1) to two (2), with additional commissioners for larger congregations, and Be It Further Resolved, that the Book of Church Order 13-1 be amended by deleting the present paragraphs and substituting the following, to wit: The Presbytery consists of all the teaching elders and churches within its bounds that have been accepted by the Presbytery. When the Presbytery meets as a court it shall comprise all teaching elders and ruling elders as elected by their Session. Each congregation is entitled to two (2) ruling elder representatives for the first 350 communing members or fraction thereof, and one additional ruling elder for each additional 500 communing members or fraction thereof. Adopted by the Session at a stated meeting on the 12th day of November, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Charlie H. Probst, Clerk of Session of Ellisville Presbyterian Church This overture was submitted at the 90th Stated Meeting of Grace Presbytery on January 9, 2001. Grace Presbytery answered the overture in the negative. Attested by: /s/ Charlie H. Probst, Clerk of Session Ellisville Presbyterian Church 16. That Overture 24 from Grace Presbytery (“Set Conditions for Use of Executive Session”) be answered in the negative. Grounds: It is imprudent to restrict the use of executive session to the cases enumerated in this overture. Adopted
