“Amend BCO 13-1 to Allow at Least Two Ruling Elders to Represent Each Congregation in Presbytery Meetings”

Whereas, the formation of the Presbyterian Church in America was the result of the Lord, in His Providence, convicting elders of the need to remain true to the Biblical principles and teachings upon which our faith is based, and

Whereas, our elder fathers in the faith were the first to point out and take a stand against errors being made and taught in the old Southern church, and

Whereas, John Edward Richard, in his History of the Presbyterian Church in America (1986, p. 131) posits, “There seems to be a desire in the pre-birth movement of the new church to give right concern and positions to all officers, whether lay or ministerial. We may even hope to return to decentralized polity so wisely advocated by James H. Thornwell near the birth of the Southern church,” and

Whereas, the Scriptures of the Old Testament in 1 Kings 12:1-5, 18, which record King Rehoboam’s fateful rejecting and discarding of the wisdom of “the elders (who) had stood before his father Solomon while he still lived” and how Rehoboam instead consulted and followed only the advice of “the young men who had grown up with him, who stood before him,” clearly demonstrate the tragic results of rejecting and discarding the wisdom of old and wise elders, and
