# GA52 O34 — Amend RAO 16 to Require Session & Diaconate Membership Reporting

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 34 from the Savannah River Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1272](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1272)*

---

“Amend _RAO_ 16 to Require Reporting on Session and Diaconate Membership and Duties”

**Whereas** the 45 [th] General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America adopted the 4th recommendation from the Study Committee on the Role of Women in the Ministry of the Church which, in its final form stated: “That sessions, if possible, establish a diaconate of qualified ordained men.”; and

**Whereas** the rationale in the body of the report explaining this recommendation stated that “There is at least anecdotal evidence that some PCA churches are choosing not to establish an ordained diaconate, even with qualified candidates, because the church wishes to be free to establish a body of unordained servants, both male and female ( _Book of Church Order_ 9-7)”, and similar reports of this practice persist within our denomination; and

**Whereas** _BCO_ 9-7 recognizes an unofficial and unordained role within the church called “assistants to the deacons” comprised of “godly men andS women of the congregation to assist the deacons in the caring for the sick, the widow, the orphans, the prisoners, and other who may be in distress or need”; and

**Whereas** at its 50 [th] General Assembly, the PCA approved an amendment to the _Book of Church Order_ ( _BCO_ ) 7-3 and affirmed it at its 51 [st] General Assembly which prohibits assigning the titles connected to the ecclesial offices of pastor, elder, or deacon to non-ordained members;

**Therefore be it resolved** the Savannah River Presbytery respectfully overtures the 52 [nd] General Assembly of the PCA to protect the constitutional practice
