# GA52 O44 — Transition byFaith to a Press-Release-Based Publication

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 44 from the Pee Dee Presbytery (to AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1295](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1295)*

---

“Transition byFaith to a Press-Release-Based Publication”

**Whereas** byFaith magazine was established as the official publication of the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) to serve as a voice for the denomination; and

**Whereas** the PCA is committed to maintaining a clear distinction between denominational communications and editorial commentary, ensuring that official communications remain impartial and representative of the church as a whole; and

**Whereas** the purpose of denominational communication should be to provide clear and accurate information regarding the actions, policies, and ministries of the PCA without editorializing or presenting particular viewpoints that may not reflect the consensus of the denomination (1 Corinthians 14:40); and

**Whereas** byFaith magazine has, at times, functioned as more than a source of official denominational news, engaging in commentary and editorial perspectives that may not always represent the collective voice of the General Assembly ( _BCO_ 14-1.5; _WCF_ 31.3); and

**Whereas** many churches and Presbyteries would benefit from an official PCA publication that focuses on disseminating essential information, press releases, and updates from ministries and Presbyteries in a way that prioritizes clarity, transparency, and denominational unity (Ephesians 4:3); and

**Whereas** transitioning byFaith into a press-release-based platform would enhance trust in denominational communications by ensuring that its content remains neutral, factual, and reflective of the entire PCA rather than selective viewpoints; and

**Whereas** such transition and narrowing of focus would be accompanied by a decrease in expenditures for byFaith, which currently is ~$400,000 ( _M51GA_, 2024, Appendix A, 139), assisting the Administrative Committee in meeting its budget; and

**Whereas** the reputation of Christ and His Church should not be compromised by unnecessary controversies or unbalanced representation in denominational communications (2 Corinthians 8:21);

**Therefore, be it resolved** that the 52 [nd] General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America directs the Administrative Committee to transition byFaith magazine from its current editorial format to a press-releasebased publication, limited to the following types of content: 1. Official press releases from the Administrative Committee and other denominational agencies regarding actions taken by the General Assembly, significant initiatives, and denominational decisions. 2. Ministry updates from PCA churches, Presbyteries, and denominational agencies, highlighting the work of the church in missions, discipleship, and service. 3. Announcements and reports from the General Assembly and Presbyteries, ensuring transparency and accessibility of denominational proceedings.

**Be it further resolved** that the Administrative Committee develop and implement a plan to restructure byFaith within one year of the adoption of this overture, ensuring that the new format aligns with the PCA’s commitment to truth, transparency, and denominational unity _Adopted by the Pee Dee Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 26, 2025_ _Attested by TE Mark Horne, Stated Clerk_
