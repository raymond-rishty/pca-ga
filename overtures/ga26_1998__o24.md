# GA26 O24 — Amend BCO 58-4 to Clarify Fencing of the Lord's Table

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 24 From New Life Presbyterian Church  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 58-4

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 209](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p209)*

---

“Amend BCO 58-4 to Clarify the Fencing of the Lord’s Table”

Whereas, the Scriptures teach that the Lord’s Supper is open to those who profess true faith in the Person and work of Jesus Christ alone for the forgiveness of their sins; and

Whereas, there is confusion among numerous churches concerning the meaning of BCO 58-4, resulting in a significant number of our churches practicing a “face value” reading and application of the last paragraph of BCO 58-4; and

Whereas, the historic interpretation of the language of BCO 58-4 goes beyond the requirements of both the Scriptures and the Westminster Standards concerning admission to the Lord’s Table; and

Whereas, there are certain rare and extraordinary cases where a Session would be prudent in fencing the Lord’s Table to admit profession believers who at the time of the celebration are not on the membership roll of an evangelical church. Therefore we recommend that the third paragraph of BCO 58-4 be revised as follows: **Current BCO 58-4**: Since, by our Lord’s appointment, this Sacrament sets forth the Communion of the Saints, the minister, at the discretion of the Session, before the observance begins, may either invite all those who profess the true religion, and are communicants in good standing in any evangelical church, to participate in the ordinance; or may invite those who have been approved by the Session, after having given indication of their desire to participate. It is proper also to give a special invitation to non-communicants to remain during the service. **Proposed Change**: Since, by our Lord’s appointment, this Sacrament sets forth the Communion of the Saints, the minister, at the discretion of the Session, before the observance begins, may either invite: (1) all those who profess the true religion, and are communicants in good standing in any evangelical church to participate in the ordinance; or may invite those (2) the communicant members of this church, and those communing members of other churches who have been approved by the Session, after having given indication of their desire to participate. In addition, the minister may invite those who profess the true religion and are baptized, but due to extraordinary circumstances acceptable to the Session are not currently on the roll of any evangelical church, who have been approved by the Session after having given indication of their desire to participate. It is also proper to give a special invitation to non-communicants to remain during the service. 14. That Overture 25 from Central Carolina Presbytery be answered in the affirmative with the following substitution (see also Committee on Constitutional Business, p. 230) Adopted as amended and sent down to presbyteries for advice and consent
