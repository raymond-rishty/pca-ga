# GA30 O33 — Appoint Study Committee to Investigate Today's New International Version of the Bible

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 33 from Central Carolina Presbytery (to B&O & AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 280](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p280)*

---

“Erect Study Committee Regarding ‘Today’s New International Version of the Bible’”

Whereas, the International Bible Society and Zondervan Publishing Company has published “Today’s New International Version” (TNIV) of the New Testament; And whereas the stated translation approach for the TNIV is to be “gender neutral;” And whereas this approach adds words and phrases that are not found in any of the Hebrew and Greek manuscripts of the Bible (see examples below); And whereas, many PCA churches use the 1984 NIV version for public worship, curriculum, and private devotional readings, And whereas many will confuse the NIV with the TNIV;

Be it resolved that Central Carolina Presbytery overtures the 30th GA of the PCA to appoint a study committee with a budget of $5,000 to investigate the TNIV and to make recommendations concerning this version in the interest of the peace and purity of the Church at large and our own denomination in particular. Examples: Dr Wayne Grudem, Research Professor of Bible and Theology at Phoenix Seminary in Scottsdale, Ariz., and a past president of the Evangelical Theological Society, pointed out to WORLD Magazine some changed meanings in the TNTV New Testament: Compare NIV Hebrews 2:6 (What is man that you are mindful of him, the son of man that you care for him?) with TNIV Hebrews 2:6 (What are mere mortals that you are mindful of them. human beings that you care for them?). The TNIV mistranslates the singular Greek words huios ("son") and anthropos ("man"). It removes the connection of this phrase to Jesus, who called Himself "the Son of Man." Compare NTV Hebrews 12:7 (Endure hardship as discipline; God is treating you as sons. For what son is not disciplined by his father?) with TNTV Hebrews 12:7 (Endure hardship as discipline; God is treating you as his children. For what children are not disciplined by their parents?). The TNTV mistranslates the Greek terms huios ("son") and pater ("father"), which in their singular forms cannot mean "child" or "parent." It obscures the parallel with God as Father and ignores the special nature of fatherly discipline. Compare NTV James 1:12 (Blessed is the man who perseveres under trial, because when he has stood the test, he will receive the crown of life ...) with TNTV James 1:12 (Blessed are those who persevere under trial, because when they have stood the test, they will receive the crown of life ...). The TNIV mistranslates the Greek word aner. which means a male human being, and in doing so loses the allusion to the "blessed man" in Psalm 1:1, 32:2; 40:4, etc. Compare NTV Revelation 3:20 (I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in and eat with him, and he with me) with TNIV Revelation 3:20 (I stand at the door and knock. If anyone hears my voice and opens the door, I will come in and eat with them, and they with me). The TNTV mistranslates the Greek masculine singular pronoun autos in a way that loses the emphasis on fellowship between Jesus and an individual believer. The cumulative effect of hundreds of such changes is not only poor grammar but a loss of the Bible's emphasis on individual responsibility and individual relationship with God.
