# GA30 O33 — Appoint Study Committee to Investigate Today's New International Version of the Bible

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 33 from Central Carolina Presbytery (to B&O & AC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 280](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p280)*

---

“Erect Study Committee Regarding ‘Today’s New International Version of the Bible’” Whereas, the International Bible Society and Zondervan Publishing Company has published “Today’s New International Version” (TNIV) of the New Testament; And whereas the stated translation approach for the TNIV is to be “gender neutral;” And whereas this approach adds words and phrases that are not found in any of the Hebrew and Greek manuscripts of the Bible (see examples below); And whereas, many PCA churches use the 1984 NIV version for public worship, curriculum, and private devotional readings, And whereas many will confuse the NIV with the TNIV; Be it resolved that Central Carolina Presbytery overtures the 30th GA of the PCA to appoint a study committee with a budget of $5,000 to investigate the TNIV and to make recommendations concerning this version in the interest of the peace and purity of the Church at large and our own denomination in particular. Examples: Dr Wayne Grudem, Research Professor of Bible and Theology at Phoenix Seminary in Scottsdale, Ariz., and a past president of the Evangelical Theological Society, pointed out to WORLD Magazine some changed meanings in the TNTV New Testament: Compare NIV Hebrews 2:6 (What is man that you are mindful of him, the son of man that you care for him?) with TNIV Hebrews 2:6 (What are mere mortals that you are mindful of them. human beings that you care for them?).
