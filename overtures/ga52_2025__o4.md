“Erect Ad Interim Study Committee on Christian Nationalism”

**Whereas,** there has been debate among churches in the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) concerning the relationship of church and state that has occurred under the broad term of Christian Nationalism; and

**Whereas,** Christian Nationalism has been promulgated theologically by Moscow Theology and Postmillennial Theonomic Reconstructionism; and

**Whereas,** there has been disagreement and confusion regarding what these various viewpoints actually teach; these variations have caused confusion, division, and dissension among the congregants of PCA churches and disturbed PCA pastors and officers; and

**Whereas,** many members of PCA churches continue to encounter, be influenced by, and be disturbed by these teachings from various online sources; and

**Whereas,** questions have been raised regarding current conformity of these viewpoints to the system of doctrine taught in the Westminster Standards as held by the PCA; and

**Whereas,** the above mentioned viewpoints have brought confusion on theological definitions and concepts that have disrupted the peace, purity, and progress of a significant number of families, churches, and sessions within the PCA; and

**Whereas,** church courts have the responsibility to determine questions of doctrine seriously, and in general to maintain truth and righteousness, condemning erroneous opinions which tend to bring injury to the peace, purity, or progress of the Church ( _BCO_ 11-4);
