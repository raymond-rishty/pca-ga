# GA26 O11 — Repudiate the IRC's Response to the 1997 Investigation Overtures

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 11 From Northeast Presbytery

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 84](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p84)*

---

“Repudiate response of irc to investigation of 1997”

Whereas, our Constitution reminds us that the requirements of the Ninth Commandment include “the preserving and promoting of truth between man and man, and the good name of our neighbor, as well as our own, appearing and standing for the truth, love and care of our own good name, and defending it when need requireth, . . (Larger Catechism, Q/A 144), and that among those things which are forbidden by the Ninth Commandment are “undue silence in a just cause, and holding our peace when iniquity calleth for either a reproof from ourselves, or complaint to others . (Larger Catechism, Q/A 145), and

Whereas, Northeast Presbytery joined with three sister presbyteries in overturing the 25th General Assembly to investigate fully the actions of the Interchurch Relations Committee (IRC); and

Whereas, the reason behind the call for said investigation was that, primci facie, the IRC had failed to carry out the directive of a previous General Assembly, and

Whereas, in issuing this call for investigation, none of the presbyteries questioned the motives of the IRC in its actions, and

Whereas, in responding to these overtures, the IRC did attack the motives of the presbyteries which had expressed this concern, and

Whereas, the IRC employed harsh language in describing the presbyteries and their overtures (“The IRC frankly is offended by these overtures. . Not only do they contain factual errors but also what could be described as deliberate distortions of the truth We maintain that Christian charity, the command of love to ‘believe all things’ (1 Corinthians 13:7), a concern for the effects of false accusations, for ‘the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity, and it is set on fire by hell’ (James 3:6), a recognition that the works of the flesh include ‘contentions, . . dissentions, factions’ (Galatians 5:20), and a reminder of the admonition to ‘avoid foolish disputes, contentions, for they are unprofitable and useless’ (Titus 3 :9), ought to have tempered our brothers’ actions”); and

Whereas, the IRC’s report alleges that there were numerous factual errors in at least one of the overtures to the General Assembly; and

Whereas, there were no substantive errors in any of these overtures, and

Whereas, to the contrary, there were numerous factual errors which the IRC committed in its report to the 25th General Assembly (see attached sheets), and

Whereas, having taken umbrage when the fraternal delegate of the christian Reformed Church used the same terminology of the IRC report (“factual errors deliberate distortions of the truth. . .”) to express profound disagreement with the IRC’s evaluation of his denomination, the 25th General Assembly defended its reputation by spreading on the record a response to the delegate’s remarks, and

Whereas, the 25th General Assembly did not likewise protect the reputations of four of its constituent presbyteries; and

Whereas, Northeast Presbytery frankly is offended by having been castigated by a Committee of the General Assembly; Now

Therefore be it resolved, that Northeast Presbytery hereby overtures the 26th General Assembly to repudiate the IRC’s response to these overtures to the last Assembly, and to request the resignation of any current members of their c who approved o for helped to write the 1997 report. EVALUATING THE INTERCHURCH RELATIONS REPORT TO THE 1997 GENERAL ASSEMBLY There are discrepancies between the IRC Report to the 1997 General Assembly and statements made at and actions taken at NAPARC [NOTE: unless otherwise noted, the references are to the 1997 Commissioners’ Handbook]. IRC Report The report states that a letter was received from the Christian Reformed Church (CRC), and says: “The Committee did not respond to this letter At their meeting on September 1996, the letter from Dr. Engelhard was viewed as the response of the CRC to the action of the 23rd General Assembly, the letter did not seem to need a reply Later, it became clear that Dr Engelhard expected a response. . .” [p 807, lines 26-28], FACT: Immediately after the NAPARC meeting, Dr Bill Edgar, a member of the Interchurch Relations Committee, stated that “we never received” the letter from the CRC Synod, “probably because of the US mail” failing to deliver it [P&R News, Fall 1996], IRC Report The report says that “[l]ater on in the Assembly [sic], under new business, the PCA raised the question with regard to the CRC” [p. 813, lines 4- 5] FACT: The delegation first raised the issue under Communications on the previous day [Minutes, 1996 NAPARC meeting], IRC Report The Committee says that “[t]he matter was put to a vote as to whether the matter was properly before the Council since the Bylaws had just been amended to permit suspension before termination” [p. 813, lines 25-27], FACT There was never any discussion at NAPARC as to the propriety of the motion to suspend in contrast to a motion to terminate membership. The question raised had to do with whether this new business came under new Bylaws which deal with how to handle new business, including its timeliness [Minutes, 1996 NAPARC meeting]. There are discrepancies between the IRC Report to the 1997 General Assembly and the account gleaned from the 21s1 General Assembly minutes IRC Report Dealing with “some history,” the report notes that the 20th General Assembly (1992) “instructed the IRC to prepare documentation regarding the CRC’s actions in such areas as ‘the view of Scripture, the toleration of beliefs in evolution and homosexuality, and the treatment of brethren who have stood against these modernist trends’. . This was a far greater task than perhaps the GA understood when it ordered the report and consequently the IRC was unable to bring its report to the 21s1 General Assembly: [pp. 814-815], FACT: In 1993, the Committee noted that the action of the 20th GA regarding the CRC had been taken before the Assembly had heard from the cr c regarding the actions of the 1992 Synod. The Committee recommended that the 21st GA communicate to the CRC its thanksgiving to God that it did not open the church offices of minister, elder and evangelist to women. The Committee then stated: “It is the strong feeling that it would be inappropriate and even offensive to the CRC, for the PCA to initiate an investigation into the internal affairs of another denomination, especially when that denomination is in the process of seeking to resolve their problems and debates. We wish them well” [M21GA, p. 392], IRC Report This year’s report states that the effect of adopting an overture which called the CRC to repentance “sent the ICR in a search to verify the alleged deviations listed in the overture” [p 815, lines 11-12], FACT: The 1993 General Assembly voted that the IRC should “comply with instructions given by the 20th General Assembly” to “prepare documentation with regard to the Christian Reformed Church” [M21GA, p. 68], The IRC’s rationale with regard to its action regarding the CRC’s relationship to NAPARC is dubious at best IRC Report: This year’s report states that at the 1996 meeting of NAPARC, “the PCA delegation brought a motion to suspend the CRC and thus began the process toward eventual expulsion of the CRC.” It then lists several reasons why it took this action. “First, while suspension is not necessary for removal of a denomination, it was widely assumed that this was the place to begin” [p 816, lines 7ff|. FACT: The vast majority of the members on the 1995 IRC committee of Commissioners, which brought the recommendation to the floor, have expressed that there was no such assumption on their part [P&R News, Fall, 1996], The Committee of Commissioners had decided to ask the Assembly to wait until the 1996 NAPARC meeting, so as to give the CRC one more year to repent. But apart from that repentance, the definite intention was to terminate the CRC’s membership, swiftly. IRC Report “Our own processes of discipline in the PCA anticipate a progression of judgments which might lead to repentance on the part of the accused” [p. 816, lines 11-13] FACT: It is not true that PCA discipline mandates that suspension precede excommunication. Although that may usually be the case, a church court may proceed to inflict the highest censure immediately upon trial [for example, see BCO 37-9], IRC Report The Committee gave its second reason: “the CRC is due to revisit the issue [of women’s ordination] in the year 2000. . [A] motion to remove (skipping suspension) would run into strong arguments that in fairness to the CRC, their own review process must be honored before the women in office decision can be considered irrevocable” [p. 816, lines 14-19], FACT This reason appears to many to be a mere excuse and no reason at all. It documents that the IRC did not carry out the intention of the General Assembly. Moreover, the IRC’s own report stated that “no one expects the CRC to return to its earlier position forbidding women in office” [p 816, lines 14-15] - which reality could very well answer any argument about the CRC not having completed its review process IRC Report The third reason given in the report was that “the IRC felt that a ‘grand gesture’ of a motion to remove the CRC from NAP ARC would almost certainly fail, as at the moment the OPC and the CRC are in the process of discussing the issue. Thus it was felt that a motion to suspend could in fact gather enough support to pass Then, when practical, certainly no later than the 2000 meeting, the process could be completed with a vote to remove the CRC” [p 816, lines 19-23] FACT: There is a significant change in verb tense: the Committee “felt” (past tense), because the OPC and CRC “are in the process of discussing” (Present tense) the matter in point of fact, the OPC and CRC were NOT discussing the matter when the 1996 NAP ARC meeting took place. Therefore, it is questionable how much effect their discussions could have had on the action of the IRC delegation. there are problems with the account by their co fits processing of the question of CRC membership in NAP ARC. IRC Report The Committee, in dealing with several overtures, contends that the “allegation” that the IRC did not make a motion to remove the CRC from NAP ARC is “misleading” [p 817, line 4], FACT: As their own report demonstrates, they admit that they did NOT make such a motion [p. 816, lines 7ff| There might be any number of reasons why they did not - but, as an historical fact and a matter of record, the delegation did NOT make that motion. it is not an “allegation” - it is simply a statement of fact. This placed the committee in a temporary state of confusion and some matters were not carried out as well as they might have been” [p. 817, lines 1Off], FACT: This matter was not on the NAPARC agenda.
