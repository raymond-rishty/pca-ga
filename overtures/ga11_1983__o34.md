# GA11 O34 — Register Opposition to the Proposal for a Representative Delegated Assembly

**Assembly:** 11th (1983)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of Oklahoma  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga11_1983 p. 40](../markdown/ga11_1983.md#ga11-p40)*

---

Whereas, the Oklahoma Presbytery meeting in Lawton, Oklahoma, March 11-12, 1983, respectfully responds to the Committee on Administration proposal concerning representative assemblies. This presbytery wishes to record its opposition to this proposal for the following reasons: 1. That since many former Reformed Presbyterians are still not familiar with the operation of General Assembly, nor acquainted with its commissioners that this proposal is perhaps for the future but not now. 2. That the making of this proposal seems to be in conflict with the recommendations and reports of the Tenth General Assembly to wit: p. 132 — ‘It appears that the church is not ready to make a change from the present ‘grass roots’ representation’, and p. 134 — ‘That study questions of delegated assemblies be discontinued for the present.’ Attest: PAUL E. DE LONG, STATED CLERK Answered by action §11-30, III, 10, 21, pp. 80-81.
