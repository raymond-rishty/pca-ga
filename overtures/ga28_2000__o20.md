# GA28 O20 — Reject Covenant College Board's Response to Protest Regarding Roman Catholic Mass

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 20 From Westminster Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 253](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p253)*

---

“Reject Covenant College’s Response to Protest”

Whereas, our high calling extends beyond the local congregation to the regional and national levels of the church (Deuteronomy 13:12-18), and

Whereas, part of this responsibility involves the maintaining of truth and righteousness in all of the agencies of the General Assembly; and

Whereas, the Covenant College Board of Trustees has acknowledged that a choir from Covenant College sang during the observance of the Roman Catholic mass; and

Whereas, the Board has also responded to a protest which was lodged during the 27th General Assembly with regard to this matter; and

Whereas, in making response to this protest, the Board has tried to make a distinction between singing in a mass and participating in it; and

Whereas, this is a distinction without a difference; and

Whereas, no competent church historian or theologian would recognize such a distinction; and

Whereas, the Roman Catholic Church itself does not regard this distinction as valid; and

Whereas, our Confessional Standards are clear, that the doctrine of transubstantiation “is repungnant, not to Scripture alone, but even to common sense, and reason; and overthroweth the nature of the sacrament, and hath been, and is, the cause of manifold superstitions; yea, of gross idolatries” (Confession of Faith, XXIX, 5); and

Whereas, the doctrine of transubstantiation is integral ly tied with the celebration of the mass, and

Whereas, our Confessional Standards also declare the truth that “the popish sacrifice of the mass is most abominably injurious to Christ’s one, only sacrifice, the alone propitiation for all the sins of his elect” 0Confession of Faith, XXIX, 2), and

Whereas, as a Presbyterian denomination, we have a solemn obligation to maintain our historic doctrine, which is also taught in Holy Scripture (Hebrews 7:27), and

Whereas, the singing ina roman catholic mass by a choir which is under the auspices of our denomination has subjected the Presbyterian Church in America to ridicule and suspicion, and W'hereas, we have an obligation to protect our church’s own good name as well as the good name of covenant college (1 Corinthians 14:40, 1 Thessalonians 5:23); and

Whereas, the explanation given by the Covenant College Board does not excuse the participation in this false and superstitious worship (Romans 6 :1-2); Now Therefore Be it Resolved, that Westminster Presbytery overtures the 28th General Assembly to reject the Board’s proposed response to the protest; and that the 28th General Assembly direct the board of covenant college not to permit any musical group from the college to participate in any service where a Roman Catholic mass is held, And Be It Further Resolved, that since this matter is broader than merely the affairs of covenant College, that Westminster Presbytery hereby requests that if this overture is referred to a committee of Commissioners, that it be sent to the Bills & Overtures Committee, and that otherwise, if the response to the protest comes directly to the floor of the Assembly (in accord with precedent - M20GA, pp 78-79), that this Overture be considered at the time that the proposed response to the protest comes to the floor of the Assembly.
