# GA26 O26 — Erect an Ad Interim Committee to Study the Role of Women in Combat

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 26 From Philadelphia Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 212](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p212)*

---

“Erect an ad interim committee to study the role of women in Combat”

Whereas, God, the creator of male and female (Genesis 1, 2, is the Author of their distinctive roles (Gen. 2, 1 Cor. 11), as well as the distinctive afflictions they face in light of man’s fall into sin (Gen. 3); and

Whereas, war is the result of fallen man’s sinfulness (Gen. 4; James 4:1-2), and thus affects men, women, and children with its horror and devastation (Jos. 6:20-21; 2 Kings 25); and

Whereas, the Church which is to be salt and light (Matt. 5) is to proclaim the Good News that God is reconciling men, women, and children to Himself in Christ, not counting their sins against them (2 Cor. 5:19), and to speak clearly to the world as to how we are to live both personally and as a society (Psa. 119:46, 138:4, Acts 9:15), and

Whereas, men are called by God in Scripture to protect their daughters and wives not only as fathers and husbands (Num. 30:1-16), Ruth 2 & 3, 1 Cor. 11, Eph. 5) but also as military combatants (Deut. 20; Jos 6:20-21; Psa 144:1), and

Whereas, governing officials of both church and state are instituted by god as His ministers and therefore are accountable to God by the light of nature and as His will is expressed in Holy Scripture (Rom 13; 1 Cor 4, 1 Tim 3); and

Whereas, our American government has implemented an unwise military policy of deploying men and women together in intimate quarters for military combat and immediate engagement with hostile lethal force; and

Whereas, Christians are forbidden by the sixth commandment from “all taking away the life of ourselves, or of others, except in case of public justice, lawful war, or necessary defense,” ( WLC, Q 136:1-5); and

Whereas, some PCA Sessions have begun to counsel their women that service in combat roles is morally wrong (January 1998 Minutes, Presbytery of Philadelphia), I. THEREFORE BE IT NOW

RESOLVED, that the 26th General Assembly of the PCA reaffirm its moral commitment to the following truths of God’s eternal and unchangeable Law: 1. That “the honor that inferiors owe to their superiors ‘includes’ defense, and maintenance of their persons and authority, according to their several ranks, and the nature of their places;” (WLC, Q. 127:30,31)’ 2. That it “is required of superiors toward their inferiors” to provide for the “protecting, and providing for them all things necessary for soul and body;” (WLC, Q. 129:54-56), 3. That “the sins of superiors” include “commanding things unlawful, or not in the power of inferiors to perform; counseling, encouraging, or favoring them in that which is evil; dissuading, discouraging, or discountenancing them in that which is good, careless exposing, or leaving them to wrong, temptation, and danger,” (WLC, Q 130:64-69, 71), 4 That the “duties required in the sixth commandment” include “avoiding all occasions, temptations, and practices, which tend to the unjust taking away the life of any, by just defense thereof against violence,” (WLC, Q. 135:89-92), 5. That the “duties required in the seventh commandment are, chastity in body, mind, affections, words, and behavior, and the preservation of it in ourselves and others,” (WLC, Q. 137:22-25); 6. That the seventh commandment also requires “marriage by those that have not the gift of continency, conjugal love, and cohabitation,... shunning all occasions of uncleanness, and resisting temptations thereunto.” ( WLC,Q 138:30-34), and 7, That the sins forbidden in the seventh commandment include “unchaste company; .. and all other provocations to, or actions of uncleanness, either in ourselves or others ” ( WLC, Q. 139:52, 54). II. AND MAY IT BE FURTHER

RESOLVED, that the 26th General Assembly of the PCA establish a six-person committee composed of two representatives elected from and by the Philadelphia Presbytery, Chaplain Stephen W Leonard, and two others elected by the 26th General Assembly, as well as a national leader of the PCA Women In the Church to serve as an additional nonvoting consultant, to report back to the 27th General Assembly of the PCA with answers to the following questions along with their recommendations: 1 Does the right and duty that women have to self-“defense” that may involve physical violence in a time of “just war” (e.g. Jud. 9:53) justify women’s service in immediate combat roles and/or military service? 2. Does the intimate “cohabitation “ of men and women required by combatants in the current army and navy violate: A The principle of “cohabitation” for only marriage? B The duty to avoid “unchaste company. . and all other provocations to, or acts of uncleanness, either in ourselves or others”? C. The duty of superiors to inferiors to “provide for them all things necessary for soul and body?” 3. Do the intimate conditions required by current army and navy policy constitute any of the following: A A “careless exposing” of inferiors by superiors to “wrong, temptation and danger?” BAn impediment to perform the duty to have “chastity in body, mind, affections, words, and behavior; and the preservation of it in ourselves and others9” C. An impediment to perform the duty of “shunning all occasions of uncleanness, and resisting temptations thereunto”? 4. Inasmuch as the Scriptures declare that husbands are to honor their wives as the “weaker partners” (1 Peter 3:7), does the placing of women in combat conditions and/or military service: A Constitute “commanding” what is “unlawful”, or “not in the power of inferiors to perform9” B Constitute a breach of the duty of superiors toward their inferiors to provide for their “protecting,” and “providing for them all things necessary for soul and body” {WLC, Q. 129:54-56)? 5. May a Christian woman refuse to be drafted for military service for a “just war” on conscientious grounds (should such an eventuality arise as is predicted by all involved in the current debate)? May this be done by appeal to the Westminster Larger Catechism Q. 135 - “the duties required in the sixth commandment are the avoiding all occasions, temptations, and practices, which tend to the unjust taking away the life of any, by just defense thereof against violence,” since the potential of women dying in combat is an “unjust taking away the life of any” a swell as not a “just defense”? 6. Does the PCA’s provision of chaplains to the military in spite of its current policies on women in combat roles and/or military service constitute the sin of superiors toward inferiors of “counseling, encouraging, or favoring them in that which is evil”? 7. Is the PCA’s current silence on the issue of women in combat roles tantamount to the sins of superiors against inferiors by “dissuading, discouraging, or discountenancing them in that which is good”9 8 Should the conclusion be reached that the current military policy regarding women in combat roles and/or military service is not only unwise but morally wrong, should the church be satisfied to exercise its general discipline against its members who teach or practice women in combat roles, or should it seek to institute judicial process against such (BCO 27:1)? 9. Any other relevant questions that come before the committee III. AND MAY IT BE FINALLY

RESOLVED: 1 That the 26th General Assembly urge our male and female members who are considering or presently engaged in military service to take counsel of their sessions as to the teaching of God’s Word in the matter of the role of men and women in military combat roles, also referring them to the Minutes of the Twenty-Fifth General Assembly, pp, 185ff, 2. That the Stated Clerk of the General Assembly send a copy of this overture to the North American Presbyterian and Reformed Council (NAPARC), to the national associations of evangelicals (NAE), and to our military chaplains, asking them for their advice and concerns on these matters, 3. That the Stated Clerk of the General Assembly send a letter to those governmental and military officials and military chaplains who are currently in the policy debates regarding the relationship of men and women in combat roles, expressing the PCA’s commitment to the unchanging moral law of God as expressed through our Larger Catechism as cited above in Resolution 1.1-7. This letter shall urge them to honor God’s law in their policy decision, and shall further inform them that the PCA is discussing this matter and intends to provide further moral guidance in subsequent Assembly decisions. 4. That the PCA’s Committee for Christian Education and Publications, in consultation with the Women In the Church and the PCA’s chaplains, develop guidelines and instructional materials that will encourage and equip military personnel for growth in spiritual holiness in the context of current military policy on men and women in combat roles.
