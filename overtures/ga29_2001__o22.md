# GA29 O22 — Divide Great Lakes Presbytery & Form Ohio Valley Presbytery

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 22 from Great Lakes Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 169](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p169)*

---

"Divide Great Lakes Presbytery and Form Ohio Valley Presbytery" Now Therefore, Be it Resolved, that Great Lakes Presbytery unanimously overtures the 29th General Assembly to form a new “Ohio Valley” Presbytery by: Establishing the boundary of the continuing Great Lakes Presbytery to include: All the state of Michigan; All of Indiana north and east of and including the counties of Porter, Jasper, Pulaski, Fulton, Wabash, Huntington, Wells, and Adams; and All of Ohio currently in Great Lakes Presbytery north and east of and including the counties of Mercer, Shelby, Logan, Union, Madison, Pickaway, Hocking, Vinton, and Meigs. Establishing the boundary of the new Ohio Valley Presbytery to include: All of Indiana that is currently in the Great Lakes Presbytery and is south and west of but not including the counties of Porter, Jasper, Pulaski, Fulton, Wabash, Huntington, Wells, and Adams; and All of Ohio south and west of but not including the counties of Mercer, Shelby, Logan, Union, Madison, Pickaway, Hocking, Vinton and Meigs. All of the counties of Kentucky currently in Great Lakes Presbytery. This action is to be effective July 1, 2001. Adopted unanimously at the Stated Meeting of the Great Lakes Presbytery held on October 13, 2000 12. That the General Assembly approve the 2002 budget of Reformed University Ministries, and note the budget including the affiliated campus ministries is provided to the General Assembly for its information; and further, that the Assembly note with thanksgiving the opportunities and challenges represented by the entire 2002 budget. Deferred to the AC Report, p. 254-255 13. That the General Assembly re-elect TE Kennedy Smartt to another term and elect TE D. Charles (Chuck) Frost to the Presbyterian and Reformed Joint Commission on Chaplains and Military Personnel (PRJC) as members of the Class of 2005. Adopted 14. That the General Assembly adopt the 2002 GA/MNA budget and commend it to the churches for their support. Deferred to the AC Report, p. 254-255 15. That the General Assembly pause now for a 15 minute season of prayer for the advancement of the Gospel in North America – praying for the following “renewed commitments”: - A renewed realization that the church is built by our Lord; - A renewed commitment to prayer; - A renewed commitment to unity in the church; - A renewed commitment to reaching the many cultures, ethnic/racial groups and socio-economic groups in North America; - A renewed commitment to mercy ministries – preaching the Gospel in Word and showing the mercies of Christ in deed; - A renewed commitment to the kind of stewardship of the Lord’s resources that will give greater priority to the advancement of the Gospel; - A greater commitment to functional teamwork. Adopted 16. That the General Assembly call upon our presbyteries to set aside a significant portion of their meetings to pray for the advancement of Gospel through the revitalization of our existing churches and through the planting of new churches – throughout North America and throughout the world. Adopted 17. That the General Assembly encourage each congregation and presbytery of the General Assembly to seek with a greater zeal the Lord’s calling for them in the advancement of the Gospel. I Corinthians 12:7 and Ephesians 4:7 promise that the Spirit of God has given each a gift for the common good and the building of the church. Just as there is a calling to every local church in reaching the local community and the world with the Gospel, so there is a calling for every congregation in church planting – reaching our “Judea and Samaria,” as well as our “Jerusalem” and the “uttermost parts.” Adopted 18. That Overture 26 from the New York State Presbytery be answered in the affirmative (see also Communication #6). Adopted
