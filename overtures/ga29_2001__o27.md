# GA29 O27 — Amend BCO 12-5e to Prohibit Women from Preaching

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 27 From the Presbytery of Philadelphia (to CCB &amp; B&amp;O) "Amend BCO 12-5e to Prohibit Women from Preaching"  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 12-5

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 226](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p226)*

---

Whereas, there are some among us who believe that Scripture explicitly prohibits women from teaching and having authority over men in the church (1 Cor. 14:34, 35; 1 Tim. 2:11, 12); and

Whereas, women may encourage each other and the children under them (Titus 2:3-5); and

Whereas, our Confession explicitly and implicitly envisioned the ordinance of preaching and teaching, or "the ministry of the Word", to be limited to ordained male ministers of the Word (WCF 27.3, 4 [Scripture proofs]; LC 54 [Scripture proofs], 156, 158, 159, 169, cf. 176); and

Whereas, the BCO teaches that candidates for the ministry of the Word (18-1), those licensed to the ministry of the Word (19-1), and interns who are to be ordained to the ministry of the Word (19-7) are to be males; and

Whereas, BCO 19-1 teaches that "no man" can preach without licensure in the presbytery "where he will preach"; and

Whereas, there are some who believe that there is considerable confusion within our church as to the proper and biblical role of women in regards to the ordinance of preaching and teaching during stated worship services; and

Whereas, there are some who believe that Paul's prohibition was primarily related to the activity of teaching itself and not to the office of teaching (though it is necessarily implied, "good and necessary consequence", WCF 1:vi); and

Whereas, it is impossible for women to remain silent (1 Cor. 14:34; 1 Tim. 2:12) while teaching and preaching to men during stated worship services; and

Whereas, it is impossible for females to instruct men and women, to exhort from Scripture before men, or to teach or explain passages of the Bible from the pulpit during ordinary stated worship services before men and still fulfill the Biblical command of 1 Tim. 2:12 which states, "But I do not allow a woman to teach or exercise authority over a man, but to remain quiet."; and

Whereas, our denomination has historically seen clearly on the issue of the role of women in the public ministry of the Word; Therefore Be It Resolved, that the General Assembly amend the BCO, par. 12-5.e d to read: To call congregational meetings when necessary; to establish and control Sunday schools and Bible classes with special reference to the children of the church; to establish and control all special groups in the church such as Men in the Church, Women in the Church and special Bible Study groups; to encourage the women of the church to hold meetings and give instruction, appropriate to their calling (e.g. Titus 2:3-5), while prohibiting women from expounding or preaching the Word of God as an ordinance of public worship (cf. BCO 4-4; 53-2), as this is clearly forbidden in Scripture (1 Tim. 2:11-12); to promote World missions; to promote obedience to the Great Commission in its totality at home and abroad; to order collections for pious uses; To exercise in accordance with the Directory for Worship, authority over the time and place of the preaching of the Word and the administration of the Sacraments, over all other religious services, over the music in the services, and over the uses to which the church building and associated properties may be put; to take the oversight of the singing in the public worship of God; to ensure that the Word of God is preached only by such as are sufficiently gifted, and also duly approved and called to that office (cf. WLC 158) and in accordance with this to prohibit women from preaching the Word of God as part of the corporate worship of the church (cf. BCO 4-4; 53-2), as this is forbidden in Scripture (1 Tim. 2:11-12); to assemble the people for worship when there is no minister; to determine the best measures for promoting the spiritual interests of the church and congregation; Adopted at the March 10, 2001, stated meeting of Philadelphia Presbytery. Attested by: /s/ Frank D. Moser, Stated Clerk 19. That Overture 28 from the Presbytery of Philadelphia (“Pray for Religious Liberty”) be answered in the affirmative, as amended. Grounds for changes: The phrase “a summary of” has been added to reduce mailing costs. The overture has been edited to make the language applicable to all members of the PCA regardless of their national origin. Adopted as amended
