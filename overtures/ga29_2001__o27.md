# GA29 O27 — Amend BCO 12-5e to Prohibit Women from Preaching

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 27 From the Presbytery of Philadelphia (to CCB &amp; B&amp;O) "Amend BCO 12-5e to Prohibit Women from Preaching"  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 12-5

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 226](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p226)*

---

Whereas, there are some among us who believe that Scripture explicitly prohibits women from teaching and having authority over men in the church (1 Cor. 14:34, 35; 1 Tim. 2:11, 12); and

Whereas, women may encourage each other and the children under them (Titus 2:3-5); and

Whereas, our Confession explicitly and implicitly envisioned the ordinance of preaching and teaching, or "the ministry of the Word", to be limited to ordained male ministers of the Word (WCF 27.3, 4 [Scripture proofs]; LC 54 [Scripture proofs], 156, 158, 159, 169, cf. 176); and

Whereas, the BCO teaches that candidates for the ministry of the Word (18-1), those licensed to the ministry of the Word (19-1), and interns who are to be ordained to the ministry of the Word (19-7) are to be males; and

Whereas, BCO 19-1 teaches that "no man" can preach without licensure in the presbytery "where he will preach"; and

Whereas, there are some who believe that there is considerable confusion within our church as to the proper and biblical role of women in regards to the ordinance of preaching and teaching during stated worship services; and

Whereas, there are some who believe that Paul's prohibition was primarily related to the activity of teaching itself and not to the office of teaching (though it is necessarily implied, "good and necessary consequence", WCF 1:vi); and

Whereas, it is impossible for women to remain silent (1 Cor. 14:34; 1 Tim. 2:12) while teaching and preaching to men during stated worship services; and

Whereas, it is impossible for females to instruct men and women, to exhort from Scripture before men
