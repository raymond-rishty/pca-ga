# GA52 O49 — Erect an Ad Interim Committee on AI

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 49 from the Pacific Northwest Presbytery (to OC, AC, CC,  ·  **Disposition:** Carried over

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1303](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1303)*

---

CDM, CTS, Geneva, MNA, MTW, PCAF, RH, RUF) “Erect ana d interim committee on AI”

**Be it resolved** that the General Assembly erect a study committee of seven members, consisting of teaching or ruling elders, with an annual budget of $15,000 (funded by gifts to the Administrative Committee), tasked to: a. Write a report by the 54th General Assembly (June 2027) on the teaching of Scripture and the _Westminster Standards_, with a particular reference to our anthropology ( _WCF_ 4, 7) and ecclesiology ( _WCF_ 25), and suggest general principles for the use or limits of artificial intelligence technology (AI) in the Church; b. Then apply these general principles to the following suggested areas: i. _Pastoral Ministry_ : AI use in preaching, teaching, and pastoral care ii. _Church Administration_ : AI use in local churches, Presbyteries, and the General Assembly, also addressing security concerns iii. _Church Discipline_ : suggest criteria for assessing AIgenerated evidence in church courts, especially in disciplinary cases iv. _Christian Vocation and Diaconal Ministry_ : a Reformed approach to work and service amid AI-driven changes in the workplace and economy, with diaconal strategies for affected church members v. _Christian Education:_ implications for PCA institutions (e.g., Covenant College, Covenant Seminary), especially on education philosophy and practice vi. _Medical Ethics_ : boundaries for AI use in medical practice with particular emphasis in recovering natural function vs. enhancing human ability beyond natural limitations vii. _Evangelism and Apologetics_ : strat
