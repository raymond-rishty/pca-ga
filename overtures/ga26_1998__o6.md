# GA26 O6 — Permit Vendors to Sell Goods at General Assembly Booths

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 6 From Eastern Carolina Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 66](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p66)*

---

“Permit vendors to sell at ga Booths”

Whereas, the General Assembly, in addition to its work as a court, also provides a forum for the encouragement of the commissioners in attendance;

Whereas, the General Assembly from the beginning has permitted vendors to set up booths to display their materials,

Whereas, this practice provides a valuable service in giving commissioners an opportunity to see materials and talk to company representatives which might otherwise be difficult to do;

Whereas, these vendors pay a significant fee to have such booths,

Whereas, only the PCA Bookstore is allowed to sell materials at the Assembly and other vendors are not permitted to sell their goods and services;

Whereas, the PCA bookstore does not carry many of the goods which vendors would be willing to make available;

Whereas, there have been vendors who no longer display their goods and services because this policy greatly hinders their recouping their costs in having such a booth,

Whereas, the present policy works to the disadvantage and inconvenience of the commissioners at the General Assembly; It is moved that the General Assembly direct the Permanent Committee on Administration to allow vendors to sell those goods and services at the General Assembly which are either not available through the PCA Bookstore booth or having been available at the booth have sold out. Adopted at the Winter Stated Meeting of Eastern Carolina Presbytery, January 24, 1998. Attested by: /s/ Austin Leake, Stated Clerk
