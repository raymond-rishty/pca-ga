# GA8 O33 — Invite the RPCES to join the PCA

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Mid-Atlantic Presbytery

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 38](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p38)*

---

Whereas, the Reformed Presbyterian Church, Evangelical Synod is a sister of the Presbyterian Church in America, and

Whereas, there is a need today fora united presbyterian voice for the historic christian faith, a viable alternative to theologically liberal Presbyterianism on the North American continent, and

Whereas, the Mid-Atlantic Presbytery is one of three PCA presbyteries most affected by our Lord's mandate to build His church within the same geographical boundaries of those within which the RPCES is at work, sometimes to the confusion of the world and even other parts of Christ's body. Therefore, the Mid-Atlantic Presbytery overtures the Eighth General Assembly to humbly extend an invitation to the Reformed Presbyterian Church, Evangelical Synod to join the Presbyterian Church in America, thereby creating one church, one united witness in the nation and to the world and one fellowship as true brethren in Christ. Furthermore, should the Eighth General Assembly act upon the above overture in the affirmative, that Mid-Atlantic Presbytery be authorized immediately to discuss with Delmarva Presbytery of the RPCES our mutual concerns and possible redrawing of boundaries should our presbyteries be joined. Adopted at the Spring meeting. May 9-10, 1980. Attest: Daniel B. Ice Stated Clerk Answered by letter of invitation. See § 8-43, IV, 5; p. 80ff. To the Committee of Commissioners on Judicial Business
