# GA8 O7 — Study committee on dispensationalism vs. covenant theology

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Grace Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 34](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p34)*

---

Whereas, the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America, a court of the Church of Jesus Christ, should speak with a clarion voice in affirming the truth of the Word of God and the application of that Word to life; and

Whereas, the Seventh General Assembly did enact an amendment to the statement on abortion adopted by the Sixth General Assembly, which, as adopted, is ambiguous. The ambiguity exists because the amendment admits that the "intentional killing of an unborn child between conception and birth (abortion)" in one excepted specified circumstance may not be a violation of the Sixth Commandment and, yet, directs that "all possible efforts are to be made to preserve the lives of both the mother and the child" (emphasis added); and

Whereas, the General Assembly is agreed that the Scriptures affirm the sanctity of life; Therefore, Be It Resolved, that Grace Presbytery overture the Eighth General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to rescind the amendment adopted by the Seventh General Assembly; to reaffirm the statement on abortion adopted by the Sixth General Assembly; and to amend that statement by the addition of the following: In those cases in which it is the judgment of competent medical authorities that the continued presence of the unborn child within the mother's body is a certain threat to the life of the mother, the premature removal of the unborn child may be justified provided that the procedure shall not involve intentional aborticide and that all possible medical procedures are used to preserve the life of the child as well as the li
