# GA8 O26 — Redraw the boundaries of New River Presbytery

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Session of Covenant Church

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 43](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p43)*

---

(Note: This Overture vias presented to New River Presbytery, but not adopted by Presbytery.)

Whereas, the boundaries of the New River Presbytery are very broad and the nine churches distantly scattered; thereby, making fellowship and cooperation difficult, and

Whereas, the /97# yearbook of the Presbyterian Church in America records avery poor financial report of total contributions to the Presbytery by its churches of $6.164 of which approximately 80% of that amount goes to one mission work in Princeton; thereby, making it financially impossible to begin other new works or support other causes, and

Whereas, two of the nine churches have only reported 17 and 34 members respectively and have not had any reported additions in 1978. one has not had a pastor in several years and the other is ina two field situation, and

Whereas, three of the churches (Lebanon. Little River, and Westminsterl and the mission, (Princeton (are closer to the bounds of other Presbylerys, and

Whereas, several Presbytery meetings in the past have shown very serious divisions, doctrinal disagree ment, and a disregard for the Book of Church Order and the Westminster Confession of Faith, and

Whereas, it is apparent to this session that the New River Presbytery does nothing but meet four times a year, supports a struggling mission work with most of its money, and shows no viability or effectiveness in starting new churches, nor is financially able to start any churches, and

Whereas, the New River Presbytery has been cited by the General Assembly for improper action, and

Whereas, the New River Presbytery has shown its inability to deal with one of its churches and has seen a division take place, which has possibly reduced the number of churches in the Presbytery and has definitely affected the financial base of the Presbytery, and.

Whereas, the Presbytery failed to deal with one of its ministers, who had written slanderous remarks about another minister in the New River Presbytery; Therefore, because the New River Presbytery has proven itself not to be viable and effective, due to financial weaknesses, the number of churches, geographical locations, serious divisions and doctrinal differences, the session of the Covenant Presbyterian Church, believing that the New River Presbytery is not viable as a Reformed Presbyterian witness in the Presbyterian Church in America hereby overtures the New River Presbytery to overture the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to dissolve the New River Presbytery and approve the following plan: I. That the following ministers and. or their churches due to their geographical locations, seek to labor in the bounds of the Mid-Atlantic Presbytery: Rev. Wayne Good Rev. William Hill Rev Lee Trinkle Rev. Linwood Wilkes 2. That the Rev. David Williams, due to his geographical location, seek to labor in the bounds of the North Georgia Presbytery. 3. That the following ministers and, or their churches due to their geographical locations, seek to labor in the Westminster Presbytery: Rev. George Alder Rev. Norman Evans Rev Michael Bolus Rev. James Fishel Rev. John Dodd Rev. Rod Mays South Ruffner Presbyterian Pliny Presbyterian Church Church Approved unanimously by the Session of the Covenant Presbyterian Church February 18. 1980. TE Rod S. Mays. Moderator RE Mark George RE L. Paige Melton, Clerk RE Jim Eades RE Ralph Martin RE Jim Jarvis RE John Handley RE Jack Simpson Answered in the negative. § 8-50, III, 8; p. 86.
