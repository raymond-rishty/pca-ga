# GA52 O25 — Amend BCO 46-4 to Add Associate Membership for Honorably Retired TEs

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 25 from the Northwest Georgia Presbytery (to CCB, OC)  ·  **BCO:** 46-4

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1240](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1240)*

---

“Amend _BCO_ 46-4 to Add a Class of Associate Membership for Honorably Retired TEs”

**Whereas** our current _BCO_ 46-4 defines an associate member as those believers temporarily residing in a location other than their permanent homes;

**Whereas** the aim of this overture is pastoral and not for the purpose of changing our polity; or the needs of TEs called to works which are not to a local church. (Those differing calls as far as a defined relationship to a local church require a more complex study);

**Whereas** ministers of the gospel who have served the church well and received Honorably Retired (HR) status may need ongoing pastoral care and vital relationships that local churches can give but is not practical for Presbytery Shepherding committees or Pastoral Care Committee to implement. The pastoral care of an HR minister would be a good application of I Timothy 5:10 coupled with the charge of John 13:14 and more suitable for deacons and elders of a local church to tend to their needs, Paul’s Partnership with the Philippian church was vital to his wellbeing and sanctification. (Phil.1:1-4);

**Whereas** we already have a provision to assign ministers under discipline to a local church ( _BCO_ 46-8). We hold to two types of discipline, one being the regular preaching of the Word, and the other which is discipline for the unrepentant/repenting brother or sister. The assigning of an HR TE as an Associate member of a local church for what we describe as good discipline and retaining membership in a Presbytery would give to an HR what the _WCF_ speaks of “so as to conduce to their mutual good, both in the inward and outward man… and saints are bound to maintain a holy fellowship and communion in the worship of God.” ( _WCF_ 26.1-2);

**Whereas** for an HR minister they may need to locate close to children and grandchildren for ongoing support and not be able to travel to Presbytery several times during the year. The gifted men on a church Session and diaconate may treat him and his wife pastorally (I Corinthians 12, Proverbs 24:17) without the awkwardness of an undefined relationship;

**Whereas** the HR minister should not have to give up his ministerial privileges and financial tax status by becoming demitted from the ministry to get care;

**Whereas** we have examples from Scripture of care given to aged leaders that resulted in mutual benefit from a close relationship: Moses & Joshua, David & his young men, Elijah & Elisha, Paul & Timothy, or the prophecy from Jesus (John 21:17b-19). In all these cases there was a close, local, and defined relationship;

**Therefore, be it resolved** that _BCO_ 46-4 is amended as follows (underlining for additions; strikethrough for deletions): **46-4** . Associate members are of two kinds: 1. tThose believers temporarily residing in a location other than their permanent homes. Such believers may become associate members of a particular church without ceasing to be communicant members of their home churches. 2. A Teaching Elder in this Church that is Honorably Retired may continue to hold their membership with and are accountable to their Presbytery, and associate membership may be approved by both the Presbytery and the Session. every hr TE shall be a member of a presbytery and have communicant fellowship in any PCA or NAPARC church. His Presbytery may request a Session to exercise pastoral care over him in its behalf. HR TEs may only vote at presbytery or ga An associate member shall have all the rights and privileges of that church, with the exception of voting in a congregational or corporation meeting, and holding an office in that church. _Adopted by the Northwest Georgia Presbytery at its 57_ _[th]_ _stated meeting,_ _April 1, 2025_ _Attested by TE Robby Baxter, stated clerk_
