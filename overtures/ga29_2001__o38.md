# GA29 O38 — Erect a New Presbytery from Remaining Westminster Presbytery Churches

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** overture 38 From Westminster Presbytery (to MNA)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 183](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p183)*

---

“Erect New Presbytery” westminster presbytery hereby notifies the General Assembly of its intention to withdraw from the Presbyterian Church in America, effective at the January 2002 stated meeting of Presbytery; and the General Assembly be overtured to take the following action: That the General Assembly, under the powers granted to it under the PCA Constitution to erect new presbyteries and to unite and divide presbyteries per their request, hereby established a new presbytery, in accordance with the following criteria: those churches and ministers which have certified to the stated clerk of Westminster Presbytery by the January 2002 stated meeting of said presbytery that they wish to remain in Westminster Presbytery, shall be constituted as Westminster Presbytery; while those churches and ministers which have not certified to the stated clerk of westminster presbytery by the january 2002 stated meeting of said presbytery that they wish to remain in westminster Presbytery, shall be constituted as a new Presbytery, effective that date, with the same boundaries as the present Westminster Presbytery. Adopted by Westminster Presbytery on May 15, 2001. Attested by: Is/ D. Steven Meyerhoff, Stated Clerk 25. that communication 5 from the Westminster Presbytery be received by the 29th General Assembly as supporting information to overture 38, and that, in order to honor the original conditions under which Westminster Presbytery joined the PCA, the communication be spread upon the minutes of the 29th General Assembly. the committees response to Recommendation 24 above answers Communication #5 in Recommendation 25. communication 5 From Westminster Presbytery (to MNA) “Erect New' Presbytery” Dear Dr. Taylor, May 16, 2001 a ta called meeting of westminster presbytery on may 15, 2001, the Presbytery took action to withdraw from the Presbyterian Church in America, effective at its January Stated Meeting, and to overture the PCA General Assembly to recognize this fact and constitute a new Presbytery, with the same geographic boundaries, out of the teaching elders and churches that do not desire to leave the PCA. a copy of the Presbytery’s action is enclosed with this letter. Please consider the enclosed to be both a Communication and an Overture to the 29th General Assembly, informing and requesting action, on this matter. Also enclosed is a copy of the CCB advise to Westminster Presbytery, which we sought to follow in our action, and a copy of action taken by Westminster Presbytery and the General Assembly in 1974, reserving to Westminster Presbytery the right to withdraw from the PCA. We are exercising our right to withdraw in this action, but we are also seeking to be considerate of the current BCO and practices within the PCA. Please convey this material to the proper channels for action by the Assembly. Sincerely, in Christ, /s/ D. Steven Meyerhoff, Stated Clerk
