Hill, PEF’s second executive director, Ben Wilkinson, and its preaching evangelists found a hunger among Presbyterians for a denomination that would uphold Scripture as the inspired Word of God, to the Reformed faith, and to the Great Commission PEF played a vital role in the formation of the PCA. Bill Hill, James Baird, and Ben Wilkinson, representing PEF along with representatives of three additional bodies became a group of twelve within the PCUS to form what was to be known as the Steering Committee for a Continuing Church. With our commitment to the Great Commission, PEF sensed the need for overseas evangelism and formed the Executive Committee for Overseas Evangelism (ECOE). ECOE’s twenty one missionaries, candidates and staff, became the initial nucleus for what is now Mission to the World. With these missionaries came a $100,000 financial gift from PEF to establish this newly formed mission agency. Not only in the foreign field, but also for domestic missions, PEF provided a framework in which the new denomination could begin its program. A structure known as Mission to the United States was established. When it was turned over intact to the new denomination, it had helped establish 35 congregations in the PC A The transfer included 12 organizing pastors and $40,000. After 40 years, PEF is still training, assisting, and practicing Biblical evangelism anywhere, anytime, with anyone. Presently, we are doing Biblical evangelism in the US and 20 countries. We cooperate with agencies of Presbyterian and Reformed Churches that promote Biblical evangelism. We would be glad to come to your churches to help train your congregation in evangelism. PEF can provide resources like pamphlets and tracts on evangelism Please pray with us as we are asking God to raise up 35 new PEF missionaries in 1998. Also, pray for us as we go into the 21st century for the glory of God and the salvation of souls. if we can be of assistance to you and your church in preaching, evangelistic and revival services, mission’s conferences or training in evangelism, please contact PEF Once again, happy 25th anniversary Presbyterian Church in America . ITEM 1. That BCO 13-6 be amended as follows: [see minutes of 25th GA, 25-45, III, 1, page 178] 13-6. Ministers seeking admission to a Presbytery from other Presbyteries in the Presbyterian Church in America shall be examined on Christian experience, and also touching their views in theology, the Sacraments, and church government. If applicants come from other denominations, the Presbytery shall examine them thoroughly in knowledge and views as required by BCO 21-4 and also require them to answer in the affirmative the questions put to candidates at their ordination. Ordained ministers from other denominations being considered by Presbyteries for reception may come under the extraordinary provisions set forth in BCO 21-4. Grounds: As it reads it allows for men to be ordained in the PCA without giving satisfaction to the presbytery of their knowledge in any area. We are permitted to examine a man only with reference to his views whether he comes from another PCA Presbytery, a sister Reformed denomination, or an independent, Charismatic, Baptist, or some other denomination. This seems to be a weakness in our polity. We have sought to maintain the highest standards for a PCA teaching elder. It is our contention that the BCO should require that if a man is coming to us from another denomination he must sustain the same exam for ordination, in knowledge and views, as any candidate.
