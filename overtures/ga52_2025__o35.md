“Request AC to Study and Report on GA Meeting Locations”

**Be it resolved,** the 52 [nd] General Assembly (GA) requests the Administrative Committee (AC) to research and report to the 54 [th] GA on necessary considerations and best practices for selecting and affording appropriate host cities and venues, and the impact of location on broad commissioner attendance across the PCA. **Rationale** — We understand that in the post-COVID environment, the hospitality industry has experienced significant cost pressures, coinciding with historic highs in GA Commissioner attendance over the last four years. With growth comes the need for larger spaces. Therefore, it is important to determine the best locations for future GAs, considering several factors. For example, should we consider larger cities with hub airports or continue to gather ina mix of mid-size markets in the Southeast and elsewhere? Would commissioners prefer to meet, for example, in ATL every year? Additionally, the AC should evaluate how well we're representing the entire PCA at GA as the denomination continues to expand beyond the Southeast. This includes considering site selection across the country and its impact on reaching commissioner attendance that broadly represents the PCA. Two years ago, at the 50 [th] GA in Memphis, 38% of our 87 Presbyteries had between 0 and 4 Ruling Elder (RE) commissioners, totaling just 42 RE commissioners.
