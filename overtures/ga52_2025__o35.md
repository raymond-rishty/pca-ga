“Request AC to Study and Report on GA Meeting Locations”

**Be it resolved,** the 52 [nd] General Assembly (GA) requests the Administrative Committee (AC) to research and report to the 54 [th] GA on necessary considerations and best practices for selecting and affording appropriate host cities and venues, and the impact of location on broad commissioner attendance across the PCA. **Rationale** — We understand that in the post-COVID environment, the hospitality industry has experienced significant cost pressures, coinciding with historic highs in GA Commissioner attendance over the last four years. With growth comes the need for larger spaces. Therefore, it is important to determine the best locations for future GAs, considering several factors. For example, should we consider larger cities with hub airports or continue to gather ina mix of mid-size markets in the Southeast and elsewhere? Would commissioners prefer to meet, for example, in ATL every year? Additionally, the AC should evaluate how well we're representing the entire PCA at GA as the denomination continues to expand beyond the Southeast. This includes considering site selection across the country and its impact on reaching commissioner attendance that broadly represents the PCA. Two years ago, at the 50 [th] GA in Memphis, 38% of our 87 Presbyteries had between 0 and 4 Ruling Elder (RE) commissioners, totaling just 42 RE commissioners. This is the same number of REs who attended from Covenant Presbytery alone. Put another way, 38% of our Presbyteries supplied 6% of the RE commissioners. Last year, at the 51 [st] GA in Richmond, just 8 of our Presbyteries (9%) provided over 33% of all RE commissioners. This disparity may be related, at least in part, to where GA is held. Only one of 52 GA has been held west of the Rockies (1989 La Mirada, CA). Only 7 have been west of the Mississippi River (5 in Texas). Because many commissioners in the western half of the US probably need to fly to GA, having GA in a city with an airline hub could be beneficial. This also means very few western churches or Presbyteries have had the opportunity to plan or lead a GA worship service. Below are the 16 states where our 52 GAs have been held. Sixty percent have been in six states: Tennessee, Alabama, Georgia, Texas, Florida, and Virginia (32 of 53; 60%).
