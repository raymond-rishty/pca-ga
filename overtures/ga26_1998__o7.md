# GA26 O7 — Divide Central Florida Presbytery to Establish North Florida Presbytery

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 7 From the Presbytery of Central Florida  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 175](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p175)*

---

“Change Boundaries to Establish North Florida Presbytery”

Whereas, a presbytery confined to a smaller geographic area can lead to more efficient oversight and cooperation. As our presbytery group has grown larger, it has experienced a declining spirit of unity and has become nessarily less personal

Whereas, the history of Central Florida Presbytery supports the notion that a healthy division can produce growth.

Whereas, greater regionalization will allow for greater focus on the unique needs of a particular area

Whereas, our present presbytery’s geographic expanse is too large for efficient involvement especially when Saturday meetings are involved.

Whereas, smaller presbyteries should equal shorter meetings.

Whereas, newer presbyteries will allow for fresh approach and possibly a better approach to “doing” presbytery.

Whereas, greater geographic ownership in church planting should lead to greater involvement and investment.

Whereas, this division meets all the guidelines established by the PCA for the formation of a new presbytery.

Therefore be it resolved that General Assembly at its July 1998 meeting divide the presbytery of central florida and form the north florida Presbytery to include all churches and missions found within the borders of Hamilton, Suwanee, Lafayette, Dixie, Columbia, Baker, Union, Alachua, Gilchrist, Nassua, Duval, Bradford, Clay, Putnam, St. Johns, Flagler counties and churches and missions affected are, Christ Church (Jacksonville), Northshore (Jacksonville), Ortega (Jacksonville), Pinewood (Middleburg), Ponte Vedra (Ponte Vedra), Redeemer (Jackson
