“Erect a Study Committee on Ministry Amidst Ethnic Diversity”

Whereas Christ calls us to penetrate cultural boundaries with the gospel, and consequently embrace the multi cultural fellowship our Lord creates, and

Whereas our own nation has not escaped the ravages of racism and ethnic oppression that typically afflict our fallen world, and

Whereas Chesapeake Presbytery adopted the following affirmations at its stated meeting on 16 February 2002 : Affirmations of Ethnic Diversity. 1. Evangelism of people from various racial backgrounds and social classes, with the accompanying reconciliation of such believers in the Body, is an essential element of the Church’s calling to preach the Gospel (Acts 15). 2. A faithful presentation of the gospel to the particular people groups in our region demands cultural sensitivity (1 Cor 9:19-23). 3. The gospel that reconciles Jews and Gentiles in the New Testament is the same gospel that reconciles all believers regardless of race and class. This work of reconciliation is not optional (Gal. 3:26-29; Eph. 2:14-18; Col. 3:11). 4. We must guard against superficial unity that is an affront to the dignity of God’s image bearers and damages an authentic presentation of the gospel (Acts 6:1-7). 5. Our leaders and church members need to be equipped in order to reach the diverse people of their changing cultural environment (Eph. 4:1-13). 6. We confess and repent of sins of disunity (racism, prejudice, injustice, ethnic superiority, bitterness, lack of forgiveness, cultural idolatries, etc.,) (Gal. 2:11; 1 Cor 11:17-22; James 2:1-7). 7.
