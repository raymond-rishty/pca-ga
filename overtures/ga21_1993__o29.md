# GA21 O29 — Spread Memorial Calling PCA to Fidelity to Its Founding Declarations

**Assembly:** 21st (1993)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 29 From Westminster Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Adopted (final)

*Source: [ga21_1993 p. 150](../markdown/ga21_1993.md#ga21-p150)*

"Memorial from Concerned Presbyterians" Westminster Presbytery, meeting on April 17, 1993, adopted the following memorial and requests that it be spread upon the Minutes of the Twenty-First General Assembly. Attested by: /s/ Larry E. Ball Stated Clerk "A Memorial Addressed to the 21st General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America: a declaration of concern and a call to the Presbyterian Church in America to be true to her Declarations" TO: The Membership and especially the Ruling and Teaching Elders of the Presbyterian Church in America FROM: The Undersigned Concerned Presbyterian Ruling and Teaching Elders of the Presbyterian Church in America On December 4, 1973, the Presbyterian Church in America held its first General Assembly, and did at that time declare the intention of the newly forming denomination as to the kind of Church that was being brought into existence. The commissioners of the First General Assembly adopted a "Message to All Churches of Jesus Christ Throughout the World," which was signed by the members of that Assembly. In the course of the two decades that have elapsed since that founding date, the Church has not only failed to reach the goals set forth in the "Message to All Churches," but she has in a number of ways actually turned away from these goals.
