“Revise BCO 12-2, add 12-11, revise 25-6 and 15-1 Regarding Procedures to Be Used if a Session Cannot Function Or Needs to Be Augmented”

Whereas, in God’s providence, some of the organized churches within the Presbyterian Church in America may find themselves without any qualified ruling elders (due to death, ill health, relocation, etc.); and

Whereas, if there are no ruling elders there is no way for a Session to meet (BCO 12-1), call a congregational meeting (BCO 25-2); examine prospective officers (BCO 24-1); exercise formal church discipline; or carry out any of the duties of (BCO 12-5); and

Whereas, many would argue that the implication of lack of a Session and the language of BCO 5 and 13-9 indicate that the only choices for a Presbytery in the face of the above situation are either to dissolve the church completely or to dissolve it and reconstitute it as a mission work; and

Whereas, BCO 15-1 seems to provide another alternative when it speaks of a commission serving as an interim Session, but it makes no mention of who determines that there is to be an interim Session, how such a determination is made, or how the rights of the congregation are protected; and

Whereas, the aforementioned options may not be either the most prudent or acceptable to the congregation; and

Whereas, if a church has only one ruling elder and no teaching elder it can still be an organized church but would seem to be faced with the same problems as those facing a church with no ruling elders in that it does not have a Session (see BCO 12-1, para 2); and
