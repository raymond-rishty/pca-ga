# GA30 O25 — Revise BCO 12-2, Add 12-11, and Revise 25-6 and 15-1 for Sessions That Cannot Function

**Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 25 from Ascension Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)  ·  **BCO:** 12-2

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 272](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p272)*

---

“Revise BCO 12-2, add 12-11, revise 25-6 and 15-1 Regarding Procedures to Be Used if a Session Cannot Function Or Needs to Be Augmented”

Whereas, in God’s providence, some of the organized churches within the Presbyterian Church in America may find themselves without any qualified ruling elders (due to death, ill health, relocation, etc.); and

Whereas, if there are no ruling elders there is no way for a Session to meet (BCO 12-1), call a congregational meeting (BCO 25-2); examine prospective officers (BCO 24-1); exercise formal church discipline; or carry out any of the duties of (BCO 12-5); and

Whereas, many would argue that the implication of lack of a Session and the language of BCO 5 and 13-9 indicate that the only choices for a Presbytery in the face of the above situation are either to dissolve the church completely or to dissolve it and reconstitute it as a mission work; and

Whereas, BCO 15-1 seems to provide another alternative when it speaks of a commission serving as an interim Session, but it makes no mention of who determines that there is to be an interim Session, how such a determination is made, or how the rights of the congregation are protected; and

Whereas, the aforementioned options may not be either the most prudent or acceptable to the congregation; and

Whereas, if a church has only one ruling elder and no teaching elder it can still be an organized church but would seem to be faced with the same problems as those facing a church with no ruling elders in that it does not have a Session (see BCO 12-1, para 2); and

Whereas, BCO 12-1 states that where there is only one ruling elder he “...should report to the Presbytery any matter needing the action of a church court”, but there is no suggestion in the Book of Church Order as to how the Presbytery is to handle such matters (unless there is an implication in BCO 15-1); and

Whereas, it may also bethe case that a church has a session which meets the requirements of BCO 12-1, but both the congregation and Session agree that it would be profitable to the spiritual interests of the congregation to have more ruling elders, even if there are not qualified men in the congregation; and

Whereas, the lack of a clear mechanism for handling any or all of the above situations may put pressure on congregations to elect men to the office of ruling elder who are not qualified; and

Whereas, the RPCES had a provision which allowed for “borrowed elders” and the OPC currently has such a provision; and

Whereas, such provisions appear to us to be consistent with our nature as a connectional church wherein we seek to bear on another’s burdens; and

Whereas, we believe that it is important that the governance of a congregation not pass to ones who have not been approved by the congregation (which BCO 15-1 may allow), particularly in light of the strongly held view of the PCA that the members of a congregation must be involved in choosing those who will rule them (as per Acts 6, and BCO 20 and 24);

Therefore be it resolved that the Presbytery of the Ascension overture the 30th General Assembly, asking that it address these issues by beginning the process of amending the Book of Church Order as follows: a. Add the following as a new 12-2 (with the remaining paragraphs renumbered accordingly) “If a Session becomes so small that it cannot meet the quorum requirements above, the congregation may ask the Presbytery either to appoint one or more elders from other churches in the Presbytery to augment the Session or to appoint a commission to act as the interim Session of the church. Presbytery may appoint the commission or additional elders for a period not to exceed one year. The appointment may be renewed at the request of the congregation.” “a. If the congregation asks for the appointment of additional elders it may specify the number of men to be appointed and it may specify the men it would like to serve in this capacity (subject to the concurrence of Presbytery), or it may leave these choices to the Presbytery. In either case such elders shall be installed after the form of BCO 24-5.” “b. If the congregation asks for a commission (see BCO 15-1), the teaching elder or ruling elder remaining from the Session must be a member of the commission unless he chooses to resign or becomes disqualified.” b. Add the following as a new 12-11. “If a Session and congregation concur that the interests of the church would be best served by having additional elders on the Session and that there are not additional qualified men in the congregation, then Presbytery, at the request of the congregation, may appoint one or more elders from other churches in the Presbytery to augment the Session, subject to the provisions of BCO 12-2.” (Note the reference here is to the proposed new 12-2.) - **c.** Add the following as a new 25-6 and renumber the remaining paragraphs accordingly. “If a Session is unable to act to call a congregational meeting because it has too few members to constitute a quorum under the provisions of BCO 12-1, members of the congregation shall direct their petition for a congregational meeting to Presbytery. Presbytery, either itself or through a commission, shall then be empowered to take the actions required of a Session by BCO 25-2 and 25-4.” - **d.** Amend BCO 15-1 by inserting after the words “interim Session” the words “(as per BCO 12-2)” 18. That Overture 27 from Eastern Canada Presbytery (“Add to BCO 26-1 Procedure for Dealing with Alleged Conflicts Between Scripture and the Constitution of the PCA”) be answered in the negative. [See also 30-29, III, p. 102.] Adopted Grounds: The proposed procedure would vitiate all the benefits of settled law by allowing a temporary supermajority to over rule the Constitution.
