# GA47 O25 — Support Christian Education

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 25 from the Suncoast Florida Presbytery (to CDM)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 655](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p655)*

---

“Support Christian Education”

**Whereas** the Presbyterian Church in America has seen fit to establish Covenant College and Covenant Theological Seminary to educate students in accordance with a Biblical worldview, including studies in covenantal and Reformed theology, as well as studies touching upon our Lord’s Great Commission and the extension of His Kingdom; and

**Whereas** the Scriptures command faithful Biblical instruction be given to God’s covenant children as well, those little ones being brought up in the context of God’s covenant people and in accordance with their union with Christ (Deut 6:7-15; Prov. 22:6; Mt. 19:14, 15; Eph. 6:4), making a distinction between their education and that of unbelievers; and

**Whereas** the wisdom of God being expressed through His people reveals itself to be “pure, then peaceable, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy and good fruits, impartial, and sincere…” (James 3:17); and

**Whereas** a considerable number of parents in our churches, increasing in their knowledge of God’s Word and works (Ps. 78:4-7), desire an educational context for their covenant children consistent with God’s Word, a context which encourages them to rejoice in Him as Creator, Redeemer, and Lord over all; a context which encourages them to exercise dominion as His image-bearers for His honor, one which will enable them to be informed by and conformed to His Truth (John 17:17); and

**Whereas** a considerable number of materialistic/secular world view ideas (such as those touching upon gender identity issues, new definitions of marriage and family, sexual boundaries, questions of who has ultimate authority over the children, etc.) become increasingly politicized and passed into law; and

**Whereas** Christian families are experiencing an increase of tension in the public school arena as school policies, approved practices, classroom instruction, discussion, and textbooks oppose and in some instances, vilify, their Biblical convictions; indeed, sometimes resulting in legal action on the part of the state (more recently in reference to gender identity issues); and

**Whereas** parents of baptized children make vows to bring the child up in the nurture and admonition of the Lord ( _BCO_ 56-5); and

**Whereas** the congregation also makes a vow to assist these parents in the aforesaid endeavor ( _BCO_ 56-5); and

**Whereas** the elders and deacons of Christ’s church work together in promoting the spiritual well-being of church members (including the covenant children) encouraging, praying, and supporting if necessary (and possible) what tends to their spiritual growth and good. ( _BCO_ 6-1, 3; _BCO_ 7-2; _BCO_ 8-3; _BCO_ 9-1; _BCO_ 12-5); **Therefore**

**be it resolved** that the Presbyteries, Sessions, and Diaconates of the Presbyterian Church in America, be encouraged to embrace and provide a supportive posture for Christian education in our churches and communities by: a. Teaching on the nature, purpose, and tasks involved in bringing covenant children up in the nurture and admonition of the Lord (Ephesians 6:4). b. Faithfully addressing the challenges Christian families face who enroll their covenant children in public educational settings, seeking to provide resources and support which will equip their students to be salt and light, and to remain faithful to the truth as expressed in Christ and His Word. c. Considering/dialoguing with members in our covenant communities regarding the privileges and benefits of a wholly Christian education in which the primary goal is the development of a mind and heart consistently conformed to the worldview of the Bible as it touches upon all aspects of living in union with Christ- in God’s amazing world under Christ’s reign (Genesis 1:1; Psalm 2:6, 11, 12; Psalm 19:1; Psalm 24; Romans 12:1, 2). d. By offering, whenever possible, financial, spiritual and instructional assistance helpful to those families expressing a desire to pursue a wholly Christian education for their children. e. By encouraging the establishment of sound Christian schools in our churches and communities through prayer and support as our Lord provides. _Adopted by Suncoast Florida Presbytery at its stated meeting, February 12, 2019_ _Attested by /s/ TE David Stewart,_ _stated clerk_
