# GA28 O9 — Amend BCO 24-1 Regarding Election of Officers

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 9 From the Presbytery of the Ascension  ·  **Disposition:** Referred  ·  **BCO:** 24-1

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 280](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p280)*

---

“Amend BCO 24-1”

Whereas, the 26th GA adopted a proposed amendment to BCO 24-1 (printed below); and

Whereas, the grounds cited by Central Carolina Presbytery (which brought the original overture) and by the Bills &amp; Overtures Committee were that the requirements of BCO 24-1 requiring (1) that there be at least one month’s prior notice of a meeting for electing officers and (2) that Session inform the congregation before election day of those eligible for election “can place undue time constraints on the Session.” (M26GA, pp. 208-209); and

Whereas, that amendment is still pending because there were not sufficient presbyteries voting in favor of it (M27GA, pp. 58-60); and

Whereas, at least 13 presbyteries have voted against this proposed amendment (M27GA, pp. 58-60); and

Whereas, the reasons for the negative votes seem to be tied to (1) the absence in the new language of a clear statement that it is the Session which must examine the candidates and (2) confusion regarding the meaning of the second paragraph of the amendment [i.e., does the “30 days” requirement mean that the names of eligible nominees must be reported to the congregation at the time of the call for the meeting (which the order of the events in 24-1 seems to imply) or does it simply speak to the required notice for the meeting while leaving the Session the discretion as to when to report the names of eligible nominees (and thus allowing the Session to not report those names prior to the meeting if it so desires)?]; and

Whereas, it seems to us that it is reasonable to require 30 days notice of a meeting for an election, but not necessarily 30 days notice of who are eligible nominees; and

Whereas, it seems reasonable to us to require that the names of eligible nominees be given to the congregation before the day of election; and

Whereas, it would seem to be good stewardship to seek to address those concerns before the proposed amendment is finally adopted, thus avoiding the need for future amendments; Therefore Be it Resolved, that the Presbytery of the Ascension overture the 28th General Assembly to adopt the following language in lieu of the pending amendment to BCO 24-1: [Additions to the pending proposal are underlined, while deletions are struck through.] A. Substitute the following for the first paragraph: 24-1. Every church shall elect persons to the office of ruling elder and deacon in the following manner: At such times as determined by the Session, communicant members of the congregation may submit names to the Session, keeping in mind that each prospective officer shall be an active male member who meets the qualifications set forth in 1 Timothy 3 and Titus 1. After the close of the nomination period nominees for the office of ruling elder and/or deacon shall receive instruction in the qualifications and work of the office. Each nominee shall then be examined by the Session in: [NOTE: continue with the areas of examination.] B. Substitute for the second paragraph (i.e., “The Session shall examine...”) the following: If there are candidates eligible for election, the Session shall report to the congregation those eligible, giving at least 30 days prior notice of the time and place of a congregational meeting for election. The Session shall give at least 30 days prior notice of the time and place of the congregational meeting for elections. It shall also report to the congregation before election day those eligible for election. And be it Further Resolved that, if the Committee on Constitutional Business and the Bills &amp; Overtures Committee agree that the revised language has merit, that the latter be permitted to offer a partial report at the time of voting on the adoption of the pending amendment so that commissioners may have the opportunity to adopt this revised language as a proposed amendment before voting on whether or not to finally adopt the amendment which is currently before the presbyteries. Adopted at the January Stated Meeting of the Presbytery of the Ascension on January 29, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Frederick R. Neikirk, Stated Clerk
