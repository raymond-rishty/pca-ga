# GA7 O25 — Allow Designated Giving Through Five-in-Five Campaign

**Assembly:** 7th (1979)  ·  **Source:** Central Georgia Presbytery to the Seventh General Assembly  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga07_1979 p. 38](../markdown/ga07_1979.md#ga07-p38)*

---

Whereas, the Capital Funds Program known as “The Five-in-Five Campaign” was approved by the 1977 General Assembly, and Whereas, representatives of the General Assembly in its first year presented the program so as to permit pledges and cash gifts to be designated for specific work, and Whereas, the 1978 General Assembly changed this in such a manner that donations may not be given through the “5-in-5” to specific works, thus breaking faith with former commitments, Be it therefore resolved, that Central Georgia Presbytery hereby overture the General Assembly that the Assembly make it clear that any donating church, presbytery, or individual, who made his commitment prior to the 1978 General Assembly may designate its pledges and gifts for the building or land purchase, and designate the same to a PCA work of their choosing, and That every such pledge and/or gift shall be recorded and the receipt thereof acknowledged by the M.U.S. Committee of the PCA. Adopted by Central Georgia Presbytery April 10, 1979. Walter V. Worsham Stated Clerk Answered by reference to Minutes of the Sixth General Assembly. § 7-29, III, 18; p. 91.
