# GA29 O9 — Amend BCO 33-1 on Procedures for Assuming Original Jurisdiction Over Church Members

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 9 From Evangel Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 33-1

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 204](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p204)*

---

(to CCB &amp; B&amp;O) “Procedures on Assuming Original Jurisdiction Over Church Members” A New BCO 33-1 Process against all church members, other than ministers of the Gospel, shall be entered before the Session of the church to which such members belong, except in cases of appeal. This principle of original jurisdiction is designed to protect the right of the Session to investigate and adjudicate matters of sin within its bounds. Additionally, this principle of original jurisdiction is designed to protect the church member against judicial charges from those outside the Session who do not necessarily have access to the facts or access to the right of investigation. However, if there are other sessions in the same Presbytery which are not satisfied with how the Original Jurisdiction Session (OJS) has dealt with a matter of allegation against one of its members, then the following process shall be followed: 1. Those other sessions in the same Presbytery must formally request the OJS to conduct a BCO 31-2 investigation of allegations against or reports surrounding this member within the OJS’s jurisdiction. 2. If the OJS conducts a BCO 31-2 investigation, then the matter will remain totally within the jurisdictional bounds of the OJS, subject, of course, to BCO provisions of complaint appeal, review and control. 3.
