# GA29 O9 — Amend BCO 33-1 on Procedures for Assuming Original Jurisdiction Over Church Members

**Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 9 From Evangel Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative  ·  **BCO:** 33-1

*Source: [ga29_2001 p. 204](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p204)*

---

(to CCB &amp; B&amp;O) “Procedures on Assuming Original Jurisdiction Over Church Members” A New BCO 33-1 Process against all church members, other than ministers of the Gospel, shall be entered before the Session of the church to which such members belong, except in cases of appeal. This principle of original jurisdiction is designed to protect the right of the Session to investigate and adjudicate matters of sin within its bounds. Additionally, this principle of original jurisdiction is designed to protect the church member against judicial charges from those outside the Session who do not necessarily have access to the facts or access to the right of investigation. However, if there are other sessions in the same Presbytery which are not satisfied with how the Original Jurisdiction Session (OJS) has dealt with a matter of allegation against one of its members, then the following process shall be followed: 1. Those other sessions in the same Presbytery must formally request the OJS to conduct a BCO 31-2 investigation of allegations against or reports surrounding this member within the OJS’s jurisdiction. 2. If the OJS conducts a BCO 31-2 investigation, then the matter will remain totally within the jurisdictional bounds of the OJS, subject, of course, to BCO provisions of complaint appeal, review and control. 3. If these other sessions in the same Presbytery (which followed step one above) do not believe that a BCO 31-2 investigation has taken place within a reasonable time period, these other sessions may request their Presbytery to assume original jurisdiction. 4. If at least two of these other sessions in the same Presbytery (who followed step one above) make such a request for their Presbytery to assume original jurisdiction, the matter shall be dealt with at the next stated meeting of the Presbytery at which time a commission will be appointed to conduct a preliminary hearing as to whether or not the OJS has conducted a BCO 31-2 investigation prior to the time of the commission's preliminary hearing. The commission may consider written briefs and oral presentations from the OJS and the two (or more) accusing sessions. The only question to be considered at this hearing is this: At the time of this commission's preliminary hearing, has the OJS conducted a *BCO 31-2* investigation of the alleged behavior or belief of its member. 5. If the commission determines that the OJS has conducted the *BCO 31-2* investigation, then the commission shall rule that the OJS has acted to exercise its right of original jurisdiction. 6. If the commission determines that the OJS has not conducted the *BCO 31-2* investigation (even though it has been properly asked to do so and has had sufficient time to do so), then the commission shall rule that the OJS has forfeited its right of original jurisdiction, shall rule that Presbytery has assumed the right of original jurisdiction and shall try the case as the court of original jurisdiction.
