# GA52 O45 — Change the Boundaries of Covenant & Hills & Plains Presbyteries

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 45 from the Hills and Plains Presbytery (to MNA)

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1297](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1297)*

---

“Change the Boundaries of Covenant and Hills & Plains Presbyteries” _**OVERTURE 21 WITHDRAWN BY COVENANT PRESBYTERY**_ **Whereas**, the general guidelines for dividing Presbyteries approved by the 41 [st] General Assembly of the PCA state “Presbytery boundaries should be such that her member churches have a common commitment to the region within their boundaries and a deep commitment to their shared responsibility to cover the region with the Gospel through evangelism and church planting.” **Whereas**, the same general guidelines for dividing presbyteries further state “A Presbytery should have regional cohesiveness. The Presbytery should have a geographical spread that is as reasonable as possible to facilitate planting churches, encouraging fellowship, participating together in shared ministry and conducting the necessary business of the Presbytery.” **Whereas**, the 16 [th] General Assembly of the PCA adopted guidelines for Presbytery boundaries stating, “A presbytery should have a radius of 2 1/2 hours maximum driving distance.” **Whereas**, Sebastian County, Arkansas, where Covenant Presbyterian Church of Fort Smith is located on the farthest western border of Covenant Presbytery and is adjacent to the border of Hills & Plains Presbytery, and **Whereas**, the Session of Covenant Presbyterian Church of Fort Smith wants to regularly attend Presbytery meetings but finds it difficult at times due to extended travel, and **Whereas**, the locations of the stated meetings of Hills & Plains Presbytery are much closer to Fort Smith than the stated meetings of Covenant Presbyter
