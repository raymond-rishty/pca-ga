# GA8 O18 — Invite the Orthodox Presbyterian Church to join the PCA

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** Pacific Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 37](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p37)*

---

Whereas, the Orthodox Presbyterian Church is a sister of the Presbyterian Church in America, holding the same doctrinal standards and practicing the same polity, and Whereas, there is a need today fora united presbyterian voice for the historic christian faith, a viable alternative to theologically liberal Presbyterianism on the North American continent, and Whereas, the Pacific Presbytery is one of three PCA presbyteries most affected by our Lord's mandate to build His church within the same geographical boundaries as those within which the OPC is at work, sometimes to the confusion of the world and even other parts of Christ's body. Therefore, the Pacific Presbytery overtures the Eighth General Assembly to humbly extend an invitation to the Orthodox Presbyterian Church to join the Presbyterian Church in America, thereby creating one church, one united witness in the nation and to the world and one fellowship as true brethren in Christ. Furthermore, should the Eighth General Assembly act upon the above overture in the affirmative, that the Pacific Presbytery be authorized to immediately discuss with the Northern California, Southern California and Northwest Presbyteries of the Orthodox Presbyterian Church our mutual concerns and possible redrawing of boundaries should our presbyteries be joined. Adopted by Pacific Presbytery January 26. 1980. Attest: William F. Woodhall Stated Clerk Answered by letter of invitation. See § 8-43, IV, 5; p. 80ff.
