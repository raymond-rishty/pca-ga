# GA52 O9 — Amend BCO 14-1 to Make AC Members Non-voting Advisory Members

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 9 from the James River Presbytery (to CCB, OC, AC, CC, CDM, CTS,  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 122](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p122)*

---

Geneva, MNA, MTW, PCAF, RH, RUF) “Amend _BCO_ 14-1 to Make AC Members Appointed by Committees and Agencies Non-voting Advisory Members” **Whereas** the permanent committees and agencies of the General Assembly exist to serve the church and to promote the Great Commission ( _BCO_ 14-1.5); **Whereas** the PCA has, since its founding, been committed to a grass roots system of church government; **Whereas** the General Assembly’s Administrative Committee exercises tremendous influence over the PCA’s national operations, including the nomination of the Stated Clerk; the preparation of budgets for the permanent committees and agencies; arranging for the General Assembly to meet each year; and the hiring, retention, and dismissal of numerous employees; **Whereas**, as presently constituted in _BCO_ 14-1.12 and _RAO_ Article V, the Administrative Committee consists of eleven (11) members elected at large by the General Assembly ( _BCO_ 14-1.12.a; _RAO_ 5-1.a) and nine (9) members appointed by the permanent committees and agencies ( _BCO_ 14-1.12.b, _RAO_ 51.b); **Whereas** this means that 45% of the votes on the Administrative Committee, as presently constituted, are cast by men appointed to the Committee rather than elected; **Whereas**, per _BCO_ 14-1.12 and _RAO_ 5-3, “The chief administrative officers of the program Committees and Agencies may attend any meeting of the Administrative Committee.
