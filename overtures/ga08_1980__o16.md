# GA8 O16 — Amend BCO 14-1 on teaching/ruling elder balance

**Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Source:** the Presbytery of the Mid-Atlantic  ·  **Disposition:** Approved & ratified (1981)  ·  **Ratified**  ·  **BCO:** 14-1, 15-1

*Source: [ga08_1980 p. 41](../markdown/ga08_1980.md#ga08-p41)*

---

Whereas, in those Presbyteries where there are a large number of ministers, a better balance between ruling and teaching elders is needed; and Whereas, a review of the statistics for the end of 1978 shows that one-half of the Presbyteries of the Presbyterian Church in America have a disproportionate ratio of teaching elders to ruling elders; and Whereas, in those areas where the church has officers, educational institutions, or even an aggressive church planting ministry, the ratio of ministers to organized churches becomes disproportionate; Therefore, be it resolved that the Book of Church Order be amended by the addition to chapter 14-1 as follows — “ Amend paragraph 14-1 by adding "When a Presbytery has 50% more Teaching Elders on its roll than the number of churches on its roll, each church may be represented by two Ruling Elders for the first 350 communing members or fraction thereof." Adopted at the Winter Stated Meeting of the Mid-Atlantic Presbytery, 22 January 1980. Attest: Leon F. Wardell Stated Clerk Answered in the affirmative. § 8-88, III, 19; p. 118.
