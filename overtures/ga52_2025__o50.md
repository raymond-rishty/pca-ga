“Encouragement to Discernment and Compassion regarding Immigrants”

**Whereas**, Scripture commands believers to submit to and pray for governing authorities as instituted by God for the good of society and the restraint of evil (Romans 13:1–7; 1 Peter 2:13–17; 1 Timothy 2:1–2); and

**Whereas**, the moral law of God, revealed in both Old and New Testaments, calls for the protection of human life and the care of the vulnerable— including the fatherless, the widow, the sojourner, and the oppressed (Exodus 22:21; Deuteronomy 10:18–19; Proverbs 31:8–9; Matthew 25:35–40); and

**Whereas**, the _Westminster Larger Catechism_ teaches that the sixth commandment requires “all careful studies, and lawful endeavors, to preserve the life of ourselves and others” ( _WLC_ Q.135), and forbids “the neglecting or withdrawing the lawful and necessary means of preservation of life” ( _WLC_ Q.136); and

**Whereas**, the Lord Jesus Himself was once a political refugee, having fled as an infant with His parents into Egypt to escape the violent policies of Herod (Matthew 2:13–15), and He taught that welcoming the stranger is integral to the life of discipleship (Matthew 25:35–36); and

**Whereas**, many immigrants in the United States have entered due to political instability, persecution, or extreme poverty, and live peacefully and productively in our communities—often without access to legal status—yet remain vulnerable to exploitation, fear, and injustice; and
