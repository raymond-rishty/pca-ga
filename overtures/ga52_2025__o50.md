# GA52 O50 — Encourage Discernment and Compassion Toward Immigrants

**Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 50 from the Chesapeake Presbytery (to OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Other

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 1305](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p1305)*

---

“Encouragement to Discernment and Compassion regarding Immigrants”

**Whereas**, Scripture commands believers to submit to and pray for governing authorities as instituted by God for the good of society and the restraint of evil (Romans 13:1–7; 1 Peter 2:13–17; 1 Timothy 2:1–2); and

**Whereas**, the moral law of God, revealed in both Old and New Testaments, calls for the protection of human life and the care of the vulnerable— including the fatherless, the widow, the sojourner, and the oppressed (Exodus 22:21; Deuteronomy 10:18–19; Proverbs 31:8–9; Matthew 25:35–40); and

**Whereas**, the _Westminster Larger Catechism_ teaches that the sixth commandment requires “all careful studies, and lawful endeavors, to preserve the life of ourselves and others” ( _WLC_ Q.135), and forbids “the neglecting or withdrawing the lawful and necessary means of preservation of life” ( _WLC_ Q.136); and

**Whereas**, the Lord Jesus Himself was once a political refugee, having fled as an infant with His parents into Egypt to escape the violent policies of Herod (Matthew 2:13–15), and He taught that welcoming the stranger is integral to the life of discipleship (Matthew 25:35–36); and

**Whereas**, many immigrants in the United States have entered due to political instability, persecution, or extreme poverty, and live peacefully and productively in our communities—often without access to legal status—yet remain vulnerable to exploitation, fear, and injustice; and

**Whereas**, God's law commands compassion and justice for the sojourner: “You shall not wrong a sojourner or oppress him… You shall treat the stranger who sojourns with you as the native among you, and you shall love him as yourself” (Exodus 22:21; Leviticus 19:34); and

**Whereas**, the people of God are themselves former aliens, now brought near by the blood of Christ, and made one new humanity in Him (Ephesians 2:12–19); and

**Whereas**, Christ commissioned His Church to make disciples of all nations (Matthew 28:18–20), and Scripture affirms that God's saving purposes include all people—immigrants, refugees, and sojourners among them (Isaiah 56:6–8; Revelation 7:9–10); and

**Whereas**, Acts 17:26-27 states that God allows the movement of people “so that they would seek him and perhaps reach out for him and find him,”; and

**Whereas**, God is bringing the nations to us, many of whom are Christians or likely to become Christians, according to missiologists; and

**Whereas**, the 5 [th] and 14 [th] Amendments to the Constitution of our nation were added to ensure due process protections for all those living within its boundaries, and informing persons of how they can avail themselves of those due process protections is good and just;

**Therefore be it resolved** that the Church should view immigrants, regardless of documentation status, primarily as neighbors and image-bearers whom Christ has called us to love, evangelize, disciple, and enfold into His body; and

**Be it further resolved**, that the 52 [nd] General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America encourages all churches, Presbyteries, officers, and members to exercise gospel-informed discernment and compassion in responding to their new neighbors, including refugees, immigrants, asylum seekers, international students, and those currently without legal documentation; and

**Be it further resolved**, that churches and members of the PCA be exhorted to respect and pray for civil authorities (1 Timothy 2:1–2), obey just laws (Romans 13:1–7), and encourage immigrants toward legal pathways when available, while also recognizing the divine mandate to preserve life, show mercy, and seek justice (Micah 6:8; Proverbs 31:8–9); and

**Be it further resolved**, that PCA churches be encouraged to offer tangible care and support to immigrants—including compassionate outreach, ESL tutoring, vocational assistance, legal aid referrals, and inclusion in church fellowship—recognizing them as image-bearers of God and neighbors in need; and

**Be it further resolved**, that the PCA encourages its churches to speak and act toward the sojourner in ways shaped not by political rhetoric but by the gospel, remembering our own identity as once-strangers, now brought near by grace (Ephesians 2:13); and

**Be it further resolved**, that the PCA affirm the call to love, evangelize, disciple, and enfold immigrants into the body of Christ, remembering that the Great Commission includes all nations and peoples— regardless of legal status—as worthy recipients of the gospel of grace (Matthew 28:19–20; Isaiah 56:8; Revelation 7:9); and

**Be it further resolved**, that when our churches and agencies interact with individuals who have no valid legal pathway to attain legal immigrant status, we should exercise pastoral wisdom by advising them to obey US immigrant laws, which might include voluntarily departing the US for their home country, or if this would be life-threatening, to a nation (if any) that might allow them legal residence; and **Be it finally resolved**, that the PCA commends to all believers a life of faithful citizenship—honoring authority, paying taxes, and living peaceably (Romans 13:6–7; 1 Peter 2:12)—while also walking courageously in obedience to God (Acts 5:29), bearing witness to the love, justice, and mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ. _Adopted by the Chesapeake Presbytery at its stated meeting, May 13, 2025_ _Attested by TE Donald M.
