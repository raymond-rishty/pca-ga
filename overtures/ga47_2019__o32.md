# GA47 O32 — Amend BCO 14-1.10, 14-1.11, & 14-1.12 to Allow Non-ordained Committee Members

**Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 32 from Tennessee Valley Presbytery (CCB, OC)  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference  ·  **BCO:** 14-1, 14-1.10, 14-1.11, 14-1.12

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 666](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p666)*

---

“Amend _BCO_ 14-1.10, _14-_ 1.11, and 14-1.12 to Allow Non-ordained Persons to Serve on Committees and Boards (Version 4)

**Whereas**, vigorous participation of women in the life of the early church is attested by the New Testament: they helped select (Acts 1:14) and train (Acts 18:26) leaders of the church, were prophets (Acts 21:8-9), served the church through deeds of mercy in vital ways (Acts 9:36–41), were instrumental in the planting of churches (Acts 16:12–15; 17:4,12) and other missionary endeavors (Acts 18:1f), and were acknowledged as important leaders for the church (Rom. 16:3-4); and

**Whereas**, the Presbyterian Church in America believes Christ has called his church to be a “spiritual commonwealth” ( _BCO_ 3-1), in which members elect godly men to be ordained as elders who are given the power of jurisdiction as they act jointly in the courts of the church ( _BCO_ 3-2); and

**Whereas**, a robustly complementarian perspective affirms the oversight of these elders and the proper, vital and full expression of the gifts given by Christ to men and women for his service; and

**Whereas**, as part of the calling of church leaders to “equip the saints for the work of ministry for building up the body of Christ” (Eph. 4:12), many sessions throughout the PCA identify gifted men and women to serve on committees of the church, such as missions, building, and pastoral search committees; and

**Whereas**, in contrast with a commission in the PCA, which is "authorized to deliberate and conclude the business referred to it," committees are appointed by courts “to examine, consider and report” to their constituting court concerning the matters that court delegates to them ( _BCO_ 15-1) and thus do not exercise governing authority; and

**Whereas**, in accordance with this principle, Permanent Committees established by the General Assembly are “to serve and not to direct any Church judicatories. They are not to establish policy, but rather execute policy established by the General Assembly” ( _BCO_ 14-1.7); and

**Whereas** neither the NT evidence nor the constitutional evidence cited above seem to have been given adequate consideration by the 17th GA in their denial of O.24 asking for a study committee to determine whether "women could be included on some or all of the permanent committees and agencies of the GA," which was the precedent cited for rejecting the overtures presented last year to open participation on Boards and Permanent Committees to non-ordained members; and

**Whereas**, these considerations suggest there is no impediment to an unordained member in good standing in the PCA serving on a Permanent Committee or Agency of the General Assembly established in _BCO_ 141.12; and

**Whereas**, the 45th General Assembly adopted the recommendation of The Ad Interim Committee on Women Serving in the Ministry of the Church that stated, “sessions, presbyteries and the General Assembly strive to develop, recognize, and utilize the gifts, skills, knowledge, and wisdom of godly women in the local, regional, and national church, and particularly consider overtures that would allow qualified women to serve on appropriate committees and agencies within the church”; and

**Whereas**, the 46th General Assembly answered in the negative an overture to allow non-ordained members on General Assembly Agency Boards on the grounds that _BCO_ 14-1.11 had to be amended prior to amendment of the PCA Bylaws, which governs the Agencies,
