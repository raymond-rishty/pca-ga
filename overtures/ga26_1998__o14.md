# GA26 O14 — Amend BCO 41 to Provide Oversight of Teaching Elders Residing Out of Bounds

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 14 From SE Louisiana Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference  ·  **BCO:** 41

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 201](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p201)*

---

“Amend BCO 41 to Clarify Ministerial Service Out of Bounds” Whereas, The Book of Church Order of the Presbyterian Church in America, chapter 46-1, requires the teaching and ruling elders of the congregation to maintain oversight of a member who has moved beyond the bounds of the local church, as far as possible, and Whereas, the same chapter requires the teaching and ruling elders of the congregation to notify the teaching and ruling elders of a congregation within whose bounds that member has moved, in order that said member be encouraged to transfer his membership, and Whereas, this is done for the spiritual benefit of such a member who has moved beyond the bounds of his local congregation, and Whereas, there are many teaching elders, whose membership is help by the Presbytery rather than the local congregation, who are without call and who have been forced by the requirements of supporting their families to move beyond the geographical bounds of the Presbytery holding their membership, and Whereas, there is no provision in the Book of Church Order for any Presbytery to receive or take oversight of a teaching elder who is without a call, but who resides within the bounds of said Presbytery, and it is impossible for a Presbytery to provide adequate oversight or counsel from a great distance, and Whereas, it would seem obvious that a teaching elder in such a situation would be in, at least, as great a need of spiritual oversight and support as any other member of the church.
