# GA26 O14 — Amend BCO 41 to Provide Oversight of Teaching Elders Residing Out of Bounds

**Assembly:** 26th (1998)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 14 From SE Louisiana Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference  ·  **BCO:** 41

*Source: [ga26_1998 p. 201](../markdown/ga26_1998.md#ga26-p201)*

---

“Amend BCO 41 to Clarify Ministerial Service Out of Bounds”

Whereas, The Book of Church Order of the Presbyterian Church in America, chapter 46-1, requires the teaching and ruling elders of the congregation to maintain oversight of a member who has moved beyond the bounds of the local church, as far as possible, and

Whereas, the same chapter requires the teaching and ruling elders of the congregation to notify the teaching and ruling elders of a congregation within whose bounds that member has moved, in order that said member be encouraged to transfer his membership, and

Whereas, this is done for the spiritual benefit of such a member who has moved beyond the bounds of his local congregation, and

Whereas, there are many teaching elders, whose membership is help by the Presbytery rather than the local congregation, who are without call and who have been forced by the requirements of supporting their families to move beyond the geographical bounds of the Presbytery holding their membership, and

Whereas, there is no provision in the Book of Church Order for any Presbytery to receive or take oversight of a teaching elder who is without a call, but who resides within the bounds of said Presbytery, and it is impossible for a Presbytery to provide adequate oversight or counsel from a great distance, and

Whereas, it would seem obvious that a teaching elder in such a situation would be in, at least, as great a need of spiritual oversight and support as any other member of the church. Therefore, we request that a provision be added to the Book of Church Order which would require a teaching elder, without a call, who has cause to move outside the geographical bounds of the Presbytery which holds his membership, to request the Presbytery into whose bounds he has moved, to receive him as an associate member of Presbytery, under similar conditions to the Book of Church Order provisions for associate members of a congregation in chapter 46-4. Such associate members of Presbytery shall be examined by the receiving Presbytery as to their views, shall be permitted to fill the pulpits within the Presbytery upon request, and can serve on committees at the discretion of Presbytery, but may not hold office or chair a committee, nor shall he have the right to vote in the Presbytery until such time as he shall receive a call and become a full member of Presbytery. Presbytery shall provide such oversight, care, and counsel as needful until such time as the associate member shall accept another call and be examined and received by the Presbytery within whose bounds such a call originated, or until divestiture from the ministry takes place. Such divestiture should be accomplished by the Presbytery of which said teaching elder is a full member, and can be done either at the instigation of the Presbytery of last full membership, or by request to that Presbytery from the one holding associate membership, or at the request of the teaching elder. This provision will eliminate the sad experience of those teaching elders who are without call and far removed from the bounds of the Presbytery holding their membership, requiring them to be out of fellowship through no fault of their own.
