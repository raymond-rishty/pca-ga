# GA32 O15 — Affirm Support for a Federal Marriage Amendment Defining Marriage

**Assembly:** 32nd (2004)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 15 from Missouri Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered by reference

*Source: [ga32_2004 p. 193](../markdown/ga32_2004.md#ga32-p193)*

---

“Support Marriage Amendment to U. S. Constitution – Version 4”

**Whereas** the Bible presents marriage as a creation ordinance and defines it to be the union of one man and one woman (Genesis 1-3; Matthew 19:5-6); and

**Whereas** the Westminster Confession of Faith teaches that “Marriage is to be between one man and one woman” ( _WCF_ 24.1); and

**Whereas** over the last few years there has been a growing movement in many countries to undermine the biblical definition of marriage and promote the idea of homosexual “marriage” and civil unions; and

**Whereas** the differing views on the definition of marriage have triggered much political debate, particularly in the United States; and

**Whereas** the United States Congress has considered an amendment to the United States Constitution (H.J. Res. 56, S.J. Res. 26) stating that marriage in the United States shall consist only of the union of a man and woman. Neither this Constitution or the constitution of any State, nor state or federal law, shall be construed to require that marital status or the legal incidents thereof be conferred upon unmarried couples or groups; and
