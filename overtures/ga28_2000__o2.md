# GA28 O2 — Grant Full Constitutional Status to the Directory for Worship

**Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Source:** OVERTURE 2 From Grace Presbytery  ·  **Disposition:** Answered in the negative

*Source: [ga28_2000 p. 282](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p282)*

---

“Grant Full Constitutional Status to the Entire Directory of Worship”

Whereas, it has always been the practice of Presbyterian denominations around the world to include some form of a directory for the public worship of God within their constitutional standards; and

Whereas, the public worship of God is a principal, and the most visible, act performed by the Church of the Lord Jesus Christ; and

Whereas, the Bible enjoins all Christians to worship in a fitting and orderly way (1 Corinthians 14:40); and

Whereas, the matter of public worship is one of considerable debate and increasing tension within the Presbyterian Church in America; and

Whereas, the current Directory for Worship of the Presbyterian Church in America is only “an approved guide” and “does not have the force of law and is not to be considered obligatory” in most of its parts;

Therefore, be it resolved that Grace Presbytery overture the General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America to rescind the current prefatory statement to the Directory For Worship, and in so doing grant the Directory for Worship full constitutional status and weight. Adopted this 11th day of May, in the year of our Lord, nineteen hundred, ninety- nine, by the presbytery of grace in its ninety-fourth stated meeting, at Hazlehurst, Mississippi. Attested by: /s/ E. C. Case, Stated Clerk 5. That OVERTURE 12 from Potomac Presbytery (“Prohibit Women from Preaching in Public Worship”) be answered in the affirmative as amended Add a new clause at BCO 12.5d., after the words “Bible study groups;”, as follows: to encourage the women in the church to hold meetings and give instruction appropriate to their calling (e.g. Titus 2:3-5), while prohibiting women from expounding ©f and preaching the word of god a san ordinance of public worship (cf. BCO 4-4; 53-2), as this is clearly forbidden in Scripture (1 Tim. 2:11-12). Adopted as amended Grounds: The overture is very narrowly drafted, applicable by virtue of the parenthetical only to the expounding and preaching of the word of god a san ordinance of public worship (i.e., the sermon) in the local church. Making this restriction explicit will protect and proclaim the well-grounded biblical consensus that exists in the PCA on this matter.
