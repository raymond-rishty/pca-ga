# Overture Extraction — Specification

The overture catalogue, alongside **Judicial Cases** (`SPEC-JUDICIAL-CASES.md`) and **Constitutional
Inquiries** (`SPEC-INQUIRIES.md`). An overture is a formal **proposal or request** from a presbytery
(or a committee) to the General Assembly — to amend the Constitution, answer a question, or take an
action. This catalogue answers *"Has the PCA considered this before, and how was it answered?"*
*(Status: BUILT — `18_structure.py`, `21_overture_titles.py`, `22_dispositions.py`, rendered to
`index/OVERTURES.md` and the `overtures` DB table; 2,028 overtures across 51 of 52 volumes.)*

## 1. The model (what an overture record is)

> An overture record is **one proposal to one Assembly** — its number, source presbytery, subject,
> the page(s) it appears on, and its **final disposition** (including, for constitutional
> amendments, the cross-Assembly ratification outcome).

Distinct from the other two layers:
- vs. **Judicial Case** — an overture *proposes*; a case *adjudicates* parties. Overtures are
  legislative, not judicial.
- vs. **Constitutional Inquiry** — an overture asks the GA to *do* something; an inquiry asks the CCB
  what the Constitution *means*. They touch: an overture may be *referred to the CCB as a
  constitutional inquiry* — that referral belongs to the inquiry layer (`SPEC-INQUIRIES.md`).

## 2. Where overtures live

An overture surfaces in **2–3 places per Assembly**, which is the central modeling fact:
1. **Submitted / referred** — in the journal under a lettered section ("B. OVERTURES TO THE Nth
   GENERAL ASSEMBLY") and a committee referral ("TO THE COMMITTEE OF COMMISSIONERS ON …"), as
   "Overture N: From <Presbytery>" (scanned) or "**OVERTURE N** from <Presbytery> (to CCB, OC)"
   (born-digital).
2. **Answered** — in the relevant Committee of Commissioners report, with the recommendation and the
   GA's action (adopted / answered in the negative / answered by reference / referred / etc.).
3. **Index** — a back-of-book index row.

These are de-duplicated to **one catalogue entry per (Assembly, number, distinct subject)**, keeping
the committee-referral context so the entry traces proposal → answer.

## 3. Detection (`18_structure.py`, `_OVERTURE`)

A line is an overture header if it begins (allowing `**`/`#`) with `Overture N` / `OVERTURE N`
**and** carries a source marker (`:` or `from`), so prose mentions ("as noted in Overture 5") are
skipped. Guards reject non-overtures: back-of-book index rows and lines whose "source" reveals they
aren't real overtures. A quoted overture title is kept so two genuinely-different overtures sharing a
number (early GAs reuse numbers) are not collapsed. Each becomes an in-structure node
`{number, source, page}` nested under its enclosing committee-grouping → journal section → PART, and
is added to a per-volume `overtures` catalogue in `index/structure/<vol>.json`.

## 4. Two representations (the cardinal rule)

There are **two separate artifacts**, and adding to one does NOT add to the other:
- the **structural index** (`index/structure/*.json`, queryable: `18_structure.py overtures
  <substring>` → every matching overture with page + committee context), and
- the **rendered markdown** the reader sees (`markdown/*.md` via `format_md`), where an overture is a
  `#### Overture N: <source>` heading **nested under** its committee referral (`####`) under its
  lettered journal section (`###`).
Both must be updated to keep the markdown and the index showing the same hierarchy.

## 5. Subject titles (`21_overture_titles.py`)

Each overture gets a short **subject title** ("Amend BCO 5-1 re: …") generated by an LLM workflow
(Sonnet) from the overture text — so the catalogue is scannable by topic, not just by number.
~1,971 of 2,028 titled.

## 6. Dispositions & ratification (`22_dispositions.py`)

Each overture's **final disposition** is captured. Simple outcomes come from the answering committee
report: *Adopted (final)*, *Answered in the negative*, *Answered by reference*, *Referred*, *Carried
over*, *Out of order*, *Other*.

**Constitutional BCO amendments need the cross-Assembly ratification chain** — "approved & ratified"
is a three-round process: (1) a GA approves and sends the amendment to the presbyteries, (2) ≥2/3 of
presbyteries ratify, (3) the *next* GA affirms. So the disposition links a year-N affirmative
amendment to year-N+1/N+2 ratification records **by BCO section**. Ground truth for what actually
changed is the pcahistory.org BCO-changes ledger, cross-checked against the primary minutes (the
ledger guides where to look; the minutes are authoritative). Ratification states include *Approved &
ratified*, *Approved but not ratified*, and *Approved → sent to presbyteries; ratification not
located*. Implemented as `prep` (extract candidate disposition/ratification lines per GA) → a
workflow → `link` (join by BCO section), folded into the DB by `19_export`.

## 7. Index (`OVERTURES.md`) & storage

`index/OVERTURES.md`, grouped by Assembly: **Overture | Subject | Outcome | Source | Pages**, with
every page **deep-linked** to its anchor in the volume markdown (`#ga<ord>-pN`, shared with the case
index). Backed by the `overtures` DB table `{vol, ga_ordinal, year, number, source, pdf_page,
printed_page, context, title, pages, disposition, final_disposition, ratification_note}` (the
queryable layer; the markdown is generated from it).

## 8. Invariants (acceptance)

1. One catalogue entry per (Assembly, number, distinct subject); the same overture appearing in
   submission + committee report + index is **one** entry, not three.
2. The structural index and the rendered markdown show the **same** overture hierarchy.
3. Page references are deep-linked and resolve; cite-only prose mentions are not catalogued.
4. Dispositions/ratifications are grounded in the minutes (the BCO ledger only points where to look);
   "ratification not located" is the honest label when the chain can't be confirmed.

## 9. Honest limitations

- **Number reuse**: early GAs reuse overture numbers within an Assembly for different subjects; dedup
  is deliberately conservative (keyed on subject too), and the catalogue was cleaned 3,474 → 2,028.
- **Coverage**: 51/52 volumes (GA4 = 0 — earliest format); some scanned-era sources are imperfect.
- **Titles** are LLM-generated (~97% coverage); a few may be imprecise.
- **Ratification**: 46 amendments are "sent to presbyteries; ratification not located" — the
  cross-year chain couldn't be confirmed from the minutes, so it's labeled rather than guessed.
