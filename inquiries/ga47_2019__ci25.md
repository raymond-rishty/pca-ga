# V — Overtures Committee's exclusive authority over constitutional amendments

*The CCB advised that the Overtures Committee has exclusive property over overtures requesting amendment to the Constitution under RAO 11-5.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 11-5  ·  **Disposition:** Yes (exclusive property); Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 lines 21197–21211](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p404)*

---

## Verbatim record

**V. Advice to the Overtures Committee**

The CCB met on June 13, 2018, at the 46 [th] General Assembly to respond
to a request for advice from the Overtures Committee on the question of
the referral of overtures.

The Question:
Does the Overtures Committee have exclusive property over overtures
requesting amendment to the Constitution? Yes. _Adopted by the CCB_

Rationale: The phrase “other than” in _RAO_ 11-5 excludes “an overture
proposing amendment to the Constitution” from the set of Overtures to
be referred to permanent Committees and Agencies. The phrase “other
than” is used throughout the _BCO_ and _RAO_ as a term of exclusion.
