# II.AA — Recording all parts of a church trial under BCO 35-9

*The CCB found an overture requiring that all parts of a trial be recorded under BCO 35-9 to be not in conflict with the BCO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 35-9  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted 7-0-1

*Source: [ga52_2025 lines 14956–14960](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p258)*

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 40 from the Southeast Alabama Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 35-9 to Require Recording All Parts of a Trial”

**Be it resolved** that the 52 [nd] General Assembly revise _BCO_ 35-9 as shown

below (underlining for additions; strikethrough for deletions):

**35-9.** All testimony parts of a trial other than deliberations
shall be recorded (by transcription, audiotape, videotape, or
some other electronic means) and witnesses informed of such
prior to testifying. Such recording becomes part of the Record
of the Case. However, in order to be referenced in written or
oral briefs, such recording must be transcribed and the
transcription authenticated by the trial court. The court of final
appeal may assess the cost of transcription equitably among
the parties

So that _BCO_ 35-9 as amended would read:

**35-9.** All parts of a trial other than deliberations shall be
recorded (by transcription, audiotape, videotape, or some other
electronic means) and witnesses informed of such prior to
testifying. Such recording becomes part of the Record of the
Case. However, in order to be referenced in written or oral
briefs, such recording must be transcribed and the transcription
authenticated by the trial court. The court of final appeal may
assess the cost of transcription equitably among the parties

**Rationale**

1. This clarifies, for example, that objections raised by the parties during a

trial and the court's ruling on those objections, are recorded and available
for transcription in the event of an appeal
