# 29-28 III.3 — Special Rules Pertaining to Process Against a Minister (BCO 34), Divesting a Teaching Elder Without Call

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga29_2001 lines 5873–5878](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p134)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a non-judicial reference from Southern Florida Presbytery by stating that a presbytery is not required to divest a Teaching Elder without call after three years, but it is required to inquire into the matter and "use its discretion" after its inquiry has been concluded.

**Inquiry from:** Southern Florida Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga29_2001 p.134](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p134)

---

## Verbatim record

Reference 3 -- from Southern Florida Presbytery re Divestiture:

Question: Is the Presbytery required to divest Teaching Elders without call, “for a prolonged period, not to exceed three years,” (BCO 13-2) and is the process of divesting a minister of the gospel in BCO 34-10 in conflict with BCO 13-2.

Response: BCO 34-10 and BCO 13-2 are not in conflict, i.e., the Presbytery is not obligated to divest every minister who has been without call for three years, but the Presbytery is required to inquire into the matter and then use its discretion.
Adopted by CCB
