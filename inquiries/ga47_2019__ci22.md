# III.A — Is a member of the General Assembly's Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) eligible for concurrent service on the Board of a General Assembly Agency?

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 8-2.a.1, BCO 15-4, BCO 14-1.12, BCO 15-1, RAO 4, RAO 4-3, RAO 4-5, RAO 4-6, RAO 4-10, RAO 4-11, RAO 4-12, RAO 4-13, RAO 4-14, RAO 4-15, RAO 4-16, RAO 4-19, RAO 4-20, RAO 4-21  ·  **Disposition:** Yes (eligible); Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 lines 21123–21142](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p402)*

## Verbatim record

**A** . Is a member of the General Assembly’s Standing Judicial

Commission (SJC) eligible for concurrent service on the Board of a
General Assembly Agency? Yes. _Adopted by the CCB_

<a id="ga47-p403"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=405 printed_page=403 -->

Rationale: _RAO_ 4 identifies three types of bodies through which the
General Assembly conducts its “affairs and programs”: permanent
Committees, Agencies, and Special Committees. _BCO_ 15-4
specifically prohibits SJC members from serving on “ _permanent_
Committees,” a term that is distinct from Agencies ( _BCO_ 14-1.12,
_RAO_ 4-5, 4-6, 4-10, 4-11, 4-12, 4-13, 4-14, 4-16, 4-19, 4-20, 4-21)
or their Board ( _RAO_ 4-15). Although _RAO_ 4-3 states “The relationship
of the Agencies to the Assembly remains as a Committee . . .” (cf.
_BCO_ 15-1), it does not specify their relationship as being that of a
_permanent_ Committee; therefore, the prohibition does not apply to
serving on an Agency Board.
