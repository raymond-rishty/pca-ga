# IV.B — Congregational meeting minutes as part of session records reviewed by presbytery

*The CCB ruled that congregational meeting minutes are part of session records subject to presbytery review, and sessions must submit them if the presbytery requires it.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 41-3, BCO 40-1, BCO 40, BCO 25-5  ·  **Disposition:** CCB acceded to the reference; Congregational meeting minutes are part of Session records subject to Presbytery review; if Presbytery requires them, Sessions must submit them; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 260](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p260)*

---

## Verbatim record

**B.** On February 12, 2025, the Platte Valley Presbytery submitted the following
question to the General Assembly for advice only ( _BCO_ 41-3) regarding the
Presbytery’s review of Session records:

Must the Minutes of Congregational Meetings be submitted as part of
the Records of the Session that are to be reviewed by Presbytery?

The CCB acceded to the reference and adopted the following response:

Not only a court’s minutes but the entirety of its “records” are subject
to review by a higher court on an annual basis ( _BCO_ 40-1). A higher
court has discretion regarding what records it requires from the lower
court in order for the higher court to fulfill its responsibilities under
_BCO_ 40. At a minimum, the higher court has the authority to require
a lower court to submit for review any document that the Constitution
requires the lower court to keep. While the _BCO_ does not expressly
require Sessions to keep the minutes of congregational meetings, it is
clear by implication that such records must be maintained in order to
demonstrate whether such substantial matters as the election of

officers have been properly conducted. Sessions are required to
receive congregational meeting minutes ( _BCO_ 25-5). Therefore, if a
Presbytery requires congregational meeting minutes as part of its
review of Session records, then Sessions in that Presbytery are
required to submit congregational meeting minutes to the Presbytery.
_See_ _Michelson v. Northwest Georgia Presbytery_, SJC Case No. 202112, _M49GA_ (2022), 822–825, 823.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
