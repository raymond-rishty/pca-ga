# 29-28 V — Including the pastor on a search committee for an associate pastor

*The CCB found the overture in conflict with the constitution, as teaching elders are not congregational members and may not serve on the congregation's pulpit committee.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 20-2, BCO 20  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga29_2001 lines 6035–6038](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p139)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 3 from Susquehanna Valley Presbytery ("Amend BCO 20-2 to Include Pastor in Search Committee for Assistant or Associate Pastor") was in conflict with the other parts of the Constitution. TEs are not members of the local congregation and therefore cannot be a member of the congregation's pulpit committee. The proposed mandate of the overture that they be so would have interfered with the right of the congregation to determine those who will rule over them, a privilege which undergirds BCO 20.

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 3 from Susquehanna Valley Presbytery (to CCB &amp; B&amp;O)

“Amend BCO 20-2 to Include Pastor in Search Committee for Assistant or Associate Pastor”

Whereas, the *Book of Church Order* does not address the matter of searching for an assistant or associate pastor, and

Whereas, for the peace and unity of the church it is vital that there be harmony among the pastoral staff of a church, and

Whereas, any new assistant or associate pastor must be agreeable to and compatible with the senior pastor,

Therefore be it resolved that the following be added to the BCO 20-2:

“When a search committee for an associate pastor is formed by a congregation, the senior pastor shall automatically be a member of the committee. Before proposing a candidate to the congregation, the committee shall assure itself that the candidate is acceptable to and has the full support of the senior pastor.

“In the case of a call to an assistant pastor, Session may act as the search committee or it may appoint church members as a search committee to bring a candidate for Session approval. In either case, the senior pastor shall be a member of the search committee.”

Adopted by the Susquehanna Valley Presbytery on September 16, 2000.

Attested by: D. F. Rundle, Stated Clerk

### CCB advice

##### Overture 3 from Susquehanna Valley Presbytery: Amend BCO 20-2 to Include Pastor in Search Committee for Assistant or Associate Pastor

It is the opinion of the CCB that Overture #3 is in conflict with the other parts of the Constitution. Teaching Elders are not members of the local congregation and therefore cannot be a member of the congregation’s pulpit committee. The proposed mandate of the overture that they be so would interfere with the right of the congregation to determine those who will rule over them, a privilege which undergirds BCO 20. Having said that, the Committee does recognize the wisdom of consulting with the senior pastor in the selection of an Assistant or Associate Pastor.
*Adopted by CCB*
