# 29-28 III.2 — Session authority to raise supermajority voting threshold for officers

*A Session cannot raise the voting requirement beyond a majority for calling a pastor, dissolving a pastoral relation, or electing a church officer.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Inquiry / reference  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 20-4, BCO 24-4  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a non-judicial reference from Eastern Carolina Presbytery, by stating that a Session cannot change the voting requirements of BCO 20-4 and BCO 24-4 by increasing the number of voters beyond a majority needed to call a pastor, dissolve a pastoral relationship, or elect a church officer.

**Key words:** BCO 20-4, BCO 24-4

**Inquiry from:** Eastern Carolina Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga29_2001 p.134](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p134)

## Verbatim record

Reference 2 -- from Eastern Carolina Presbytery re Voting Requirements:

Question: Does a Session or the Moderator of a Congregational meeting have the authority to require a greater vote than that required by the Book of Church Order for the call of a pastor or for the dissolution or a pastoral relationship of for the election of a church officer? That is, may the provision of BCO 20-4 and 24-4, “a majority of all the voters present shall be required to elect,” be made more restrictive?

Response: It is not within the purview of the Session or Moderator to require a greater than majority vote for the election of a pastor or church officer. The BCO nowhere gives such powers to Sessions or Moderators. Allowing them to claim such power would open the door to the possible thwarting of the right of the members of the Congregation to determine those who will rule over them, a right which undergirds BCO 20 and 24.
Adopted by CCB
