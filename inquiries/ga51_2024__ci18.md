# II.R — Mandatory background checks for church office under BCO 13-6

*The CCB found a proposed overture requiring background checks for church office to be in conflict with the BCO and WCF Preliminary Principles.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 51st (2024)  ·  **Provisions:** WCF 23.3, BCO Preliminary Principle II, BCO 13-6  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0

### CCB advice

**R.** Overture 23 from Missouri Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 13-6, 21-4, and 241 to Require Background Checks for Church Office”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 23 is **in conflict** with (1) WCF 23.3
because, insofar as it adds a requirement for Presbytery to “cause . . . to be
performed” something that the civil magistrate might withhold or abolish, it
cedes to the civil magistrate an effective authority he may not assume, namely,
the right to deny admittance to the body of Christ and (2) _BCO_ Preliminary
Principle II in that it deprives members of the church of the right to determine
who shall be admitted to its communion.

CCB also notes that should this Overture and Overture 2 be adopted, there
should be clarity regarding how the proposed amendments interact to amend
_BCO_ 13-6.

_Adopted 8-0-0_
