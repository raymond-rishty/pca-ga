# CI #12 — Effect of appeal on suspension of disciplinary censure during review

*The CCB advised that a judgment of divestiture is suspended during appeal to the General Assembly unless the lower court separately places the censure into immediate effect.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 14th (1986)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 30-1, BCO 30-5, BCO 34-10, BCO 42-6  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga14_1986 p. 335](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p335)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

Constitutional Inquiry (appended to Case 4). From the Great Lakes Presbytery. 'The Appellant ... Is he divested from office or is that action of Presbytery suspended until the General Assembly's Commission has acted?' ANSWER: Yes the judgment is suspended, unless the court for sufficient reasons, which are duly recorded, by separate action puts the censure into effect. GROUNDS: See BCO 30-1, 30-5, 34-10, and 42-6.
