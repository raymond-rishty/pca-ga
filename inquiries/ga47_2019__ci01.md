# II.A — Permitting additional ruling elder representation at General Assembly

*The CCB found an overture from Northwest Georgia Presbytery to revise BCO 14-2 permitting additional ruling elder representation at the General Assembly was not in conflict with the Constitution.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 14-2  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 lines 20888–20895](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p398)*

---

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 1 from Northwest Georgia Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Revise _BCO_ 14-2 to Permit Additional Ruling Elder Representation at
the General Assembly”

**Whereas**, the PCA General Assembly ratio of Ruling Elders (RE) to

Teaching Elders (TE) is in severe crisis, perhaps signaling a lack of
support for GA among our Sessions [the numerical decline is apparent
from the attached chart]; and

**Whereas**, _BCO_ 14-1 (9) calls for equal representation of TEs and REs on

GA committees, exhibiting the value, principle, and prudence of parity,
which assuredly applies to every level of the court; and

**Whereas**, all other courts of the PCA have greater RE participation ratios

than the steeply declining ratio at our General Assemblies (see also _BCO_
8-9); and

**Whereas**, the broader church may not support actions well ( _BCO_ 14-7) if

adequate representation of the congregations and their Sessions are not
present; and

**Whereas**, the formula for GA representation ( _BCO_ 14-2) has not been

updated since 1991 and may be altered by due process without
disenfranchising others or affecting any other part of the constitution;
and

**Whereas**, the proposed formula herein could augment revenue for the GA,

especially in conjunction with another overture to lower the registration
fee for ruling elders (although we never wish to limit or depress ruling
elders’ attendance due to revenue concerns); and

**Whereas**, the proposed, if sent down to the presbyteries, would allow more

ruling elders to voice their opinion on this potential increase in GA
participation;

<a id="ga47-p582"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=584 printed_page=582 -->

**Therefore**, Northwest Georgia Presbytery overtures to amend _BCO_ 14-2 to

read (strike-through for deletions; underlining for new wording):

The General Assembly, which is a permanent court, shall
meet at least annually upon its own adjournment. It shall
consist of all teaching elders in good standing with their
Presbyteries, and ruling elders as elected by their Session.
Each congregation is entitled to four ~~two r~~ uling elder
representatives for the first 350 communing members or
fraction thereof, and two ~~one~~ additional ruling elders for each
additional 500 communing members or fraction thereof.

So that it reads:

The General Assembly, which is a permanent court, shall
meet at least annually upon its own adjournment. It shall
consist of all teaching elders in good standing with their
Presbyteries, and ruling elders as elected by their Session.
Each congregation is entitled to four ruling elder
representatives for the first 350 communing members or
fraction thereof, and two additional ruling elders for each
additional 500 communing members or fraction thereof.

_Adopted by Northwest Georgia Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 3, 2018_
_Attested by TE P. Legree Finch, stated clerk_
