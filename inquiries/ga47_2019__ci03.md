# II.C — Amend RAO 10-3 to Promote the Exercise of Godly Prudence in Planning and Leading Times of Corporate Prayer and Worship at General Assembly

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 10-3  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 lines 20910–20918](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p398)*

---

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 3 from Northern New England Presbytery (to AC, CCB)

“Amend _RAO_ 10-3 to Promote the Exercise of Godly Prudence in
Planning and Leading Times of Corporate Prayer and Worship at
General Assembly”

**Whereas** _BCO_ 47-5 states: “Public worship must be performed in spirit and

in truth. Externalism and hypocrisy stand condemned. The forms of
public worship have value only when they serve to express the inner
reverence of the worshipper and his sincere devotion to the true and
living God.”;

**Whereas** times of corporate worship and prayer at General Assembly should

give expression to the unity of our church by providing an opportunity
for all in attendance to call upon the Lord in sincerity and good
conscience;

**Whereas** the following sentences were used in a corporate confession of sin

at the official pre-Assembly prayer gathering at the 46 [th] General
Assembly (2018):

<a id="ga47-p588"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=590 printed_page=588 -->

We grieve the disunity of your church. We grieve that
many women in our congregations feel unvalued and
unwanted, unheard and uncared for. We grieve that we have
acted as though being male was more valuable than being
female, that we have communicated that only men were wanted
in service and care, that only male voices could be and should
be heard, that only men were valued. We grieve not loving our
mothers and sisters in Christ well.

We grieve that the poor, the blue collar, the working class,
and the middle class find our churches uninviting and
unwelcoming. We grieve that we have played favorites,
seeking to reach the wealthy and powerful with the Gospel
while avoiding those who could not advance our influence. We
grieve that our status symbols — cars, clothes, vacations,
schools — have kept people away from the very place where
we declare that he who was rich became poor for our sakes so
that we might be rich in him.
We grieve that black and brown people have found our
churches to be overwhelming and unsympathetic places, more
loyal to “whiteness” than to the red blood of Jesus shed on the
Cross. We grieve that our loyalties to politicians who play on
racial fears have been stronger than our loyalties to our black
and brown brothers and sisters in Christ. We grieve that we
have wittingly and unwittingly erected once again the very wall
of hostility that Christ’s cross had torn down. We grieve that
those who have spoken the Gospel truth on our racial unity-indiversity have been shouted down as “cultural Marxists” or
“social justice warriors” rather than celebrated as servants of
the Gospel.

**Whereas** many PCA officers and members do not think that they are guilty

of the specific sins mentioned in the above sentences, nor do they agree
with some of the assumptions that seem to be implied in these sentences,
and therefore they would not be able to pray these things in sincerity and
good conscience;

**Whereas** other controversial practices and ideas have been included in times

of corporate worship and prayer at previous General Assemblies;

**Therefore be it resolved**, that the General Assembly of the Presbyterian

Church in America amend _RAO_ 10-3 by adding the following sentence
(additions are underlined):

<a id="ga47-p589"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=591 printed_page=589 -->

10-3. Worship services shall be included as approved by the
Assembly. The Administrative Committee must approve any
non-PCA speaker. Those involved in the organization and
leadership of times of corporate worship and prayer at General
Assembly are to exercise godly prudence as they strive to
ensure that all that is said and done at these gatherings gives
expression to the unity of our church by providing an
opportunity for all in attendance to call upon the Lord in
sincerity and good conscience.

The newly amended section would then read as follows:

10-3. Worship services shall be included as approved by the
Assembly. The Administrative Committee must approve any
non-PCA speaker. Those involved in the organization and
leadership of times of corporate worship and prayer at General
Assembly are to exercise godly prudence as they strive to
ensure that all that is said and done at these gatherings gives
expression to the unity of our church by providing an
opportunity for all in attendance to call upon the Lord in
sincerity and good conscience.

_Adopted by the Northern New England Presbytery at its stated meeting,_
_September 29, 2018_
_Attested by TE Per Almquist, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**C** . Overture 3 from Northern New England Presbytery: “Amend _RAO_

10-3 to Promote the Exercise of Godly Prudence in Planning and
Leading Times of Corporate Prayer and Worship at General
