# II.I — Limiting congregational voting to members in good standing under BCO 25-1

*The CCB found the proposed amendment consistent with current BCO language but noted it did not resolve a possible conflict with BCO 25-7 and 25-11.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 25-1, BCO 20-3, BCO 24-3, BCO 25-7, BCO 25-11  ·  **Disposition:** consistent with current BCO language but does not address possible conflict with BCO 25-7 and 25-11; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 pp. 253–254](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p253)*

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 13 from the Great Lakes Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 25-1 to Specify Only Members in Good Standing May
Vote”

**Whereas** _BCO_ 24-3 states that “All communing members in good and regular

standing, but no others, are entitled to vote.”

**Whereas** _BCO_ 25-1 states that all communing members are entitled to vote

and does not qualify that only members in good and regular standing
may vote, it is inconsistent with the _BCO_ .

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 25-1 be amended to specify which communing

members are entitled to vote (underlining for additions; strikethrough
for deletions):

**25-1.** The congregation consists of all the communing members
of a particular church, and they only members in good and
regular standing are entitled to vote.

So that the amended paragraph would read:

**25-1.** The congregation consists of all the communing members
of a particular church, and only members in good and regular
standing are entitled to vote.

_Adopted by the Great Lakes Presbytery at its stated meeting, January 11, 2025._
_Attested by Ryan Potter, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**I.** Overture 13 from the Great Lakes Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 25-1 to Specify
Only Members in Good Standing May Vote”
