## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 16 from Georgia Foothills Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend the _RAO_ to Provide for a Moderator-Elect and Vice-Moderator
by Revision of _RAO_ 1-4, Addition of _RAO_ 1-6, 1-7,
and 1-8, and Revision of _RAO_ 2-1, 2-3”

**Whereas**, the role of Moderator of the General Assembly is a complicated

responsibility. it will be helpful fora man to have a year’s notice in order
to prepare for his duties, and

**Whereas**, some PCA presbyteries have a Moderator-In-Nomination rule in

their Standing Rules, and

**Whereas**, other Presbyterian denominations, such as the Associate

Reformed Presbyterian Church and the Evangelical Presbyterian Church
elect a Moderator-Elect or Vice-Moderator, and

**Whereas**, the Assembly subsequent to the election of a Moderator-Elect or

Vice-Moderator still would have the option of electing another man as
Moderator, and

**Whereas**, since we use electronic voting rather than voting cards or paper

ballots, it is no longer necessary to specify that paper ballots be used, and

**Whereas**, it is customary that the Moderator yield the chair at times. The

revision to _RAO_ 2-1 specifies who the Moderator _pro tempore_ may be,
and

**Whereas**, under _Robert’s Rules of Order_ the term “privileges of the floor”

only means that one is allowed to be present during a meeting and does
not mean that one has the right to speak ( _RONR_ [11 [th] ed.] p. 29, footnote).
The original purpose of _RAO_ 2-3 was to give a former Moderator of the
General Assembly the right to participate in debate. One must be a
commissioner in order to vote.

**Therefore, be it resolved** that Georgia Foothills Presbytery overtures the

Forty-seventh General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America
to amend the _Rules of Assembly Operations_ by revision of _RAO_ 1-4; by
addition of _RAO_ 1-6, 1-7, and 1-8; and by revision of _RAO_ 2-1, 2-3 to
provide for a Moderator-Elect and a Vice-Moderator as follows:

**ARTICLE I. ORGANIZATION OF A GENERAL**

**ASSEMBLY’S MEETING**

**1-4.** If more than one commissioner is nominated, election
shall be by secret ballot, ~~on ballots provided by the Stated~~
~~Clerk.~~ ~~Tellers appointed by the Stated Clerk shall gather and~~
~~count the ballots, and report the tabulation to the Stated Clerk.~~
If no nominee receives a majority of the votes cast, a second
ballot shall be called on the two nominees who received the
highest number of votes on the first ballot. The Moderator
shall declare an election when a nominee receives a majority
of the votes cast by the commissioners present and voting.

**1-5.** No change.

**1-6.** After the Moderator has been elected, he shall preside over
the election of a Moderator-elect. There shall be only one
nominating speech not to exceed five minutes for each
nominee. No seconding speeches shall be permitted.
**1-7.** If more than one commissioner is nominated, election
shall be by secret ballot. If no nominee receives a majority of
the votes cast, a second ballot shall be called on the two
nominees who received the highest number of votes on the first
ballot. The Moderator shall declare an election when a
nominee receives a majority of the votes cast by the
commissioners present and voting.

**1-8.** The Moderator-Elect shall be placed in nomination for
Moderator at the subsequent General Assembly. Additional
nominations may be made from the floor.

**ARTICLE II. THE MODERATOR**

**2-1.** The Moderator shall preside at all sessions of the
Assembly except when he may invite the Moderator-Elect
(Vice-Moderator), a former Moderator, or another commissioner
to act temporarily as the presiding officer.

**2-2.** No change.

**2-3.** Any former Moderator attending a General Assembly
shall have the privileges of the floor, may participate in debate,
and, if he is a commissioner, may vote.

_Approved by Georgia Foothills Presbytery at its stated meeting, January 26,_
_2019_
