# CI #7 — Voting rights of an associate pastor when not moderating the session

*The CCB advised that an associate pastor, as a session member, retains full voting rights even when not serving as moderator.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 13th (1985)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 4-3, BCO 12-1, BCO 22-2  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga13_1985 lines 12377–12384](../markdown/ga13_1985.md#ga13-p243)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

That the advice regarding Constitutional Inquiry 7 be ratified [voting by associate pastor]. Constitutional Inquiry 7: From the Session of Evangelical Presbyterian Church of Levittown, PA. 'Does the associate pastor, when he is not moderating Session, have a vote in that court?' ANSWER: The BCO 4-3, 12-1, 22-2 lists the members of Session which includes Pastors, Associate Pastors, and Ruling Elders. Every member of the Session, including the Moderator, is entitled to vote by virtue of his membership in the Session.
