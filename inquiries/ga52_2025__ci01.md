# II.A — Court's authority to investigate before filing charges under BCO 32-2

*The CCB found the proposed amendment to BCO 32-2 clarifying a court's investigative authority both in conflict with and ambiguous in relation to the BCO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 32-2, BCO 29-1, BCO 32-2.e, BCO 32-2.f, BCO 31-9, BCO 31-6, BCO 31-7  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict (and ambiguous); Adopted 8-0-0

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 1 from Northern New England Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 32-2 to Clarify that a Court May Investigate Whether
Charges Should Be Filed”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 32-2 be amended by striking the current language and

replacing it with the following (deletions indicated by strikethrough;
additions by underlining).

Existing language:
**32-2.** Process against an offender shall not be commenced unless
some person or persons undertake to make out the charge; or unless
the court finds it necessary, for the honor of religion, itself to take the
step provided for in _BCO_ 31-2.

Proposed language:
**32-2.** Prior to commencing process against an alleged offender,
regardless of how allegations arose, the court shall investigate to the
extent necessary to determine:

1) i fit is a personal or general offense
2) if personal, whether Matthew 18 has been followed
3) if the nature of the alleged offense rises to a level

warranting judicial process
4) if the accuser is like any of those described in _BCO_ 31-8
5) if there is a strong presumption of guilt
6) if it is reasonable to expect the offense can be proven

through judicial process.

**Rationale**

The current language of _BCO_ 32-2 has sometimes been read to allow an
individual to force a court of the Church to institute process by the filing of
charges, taking the court’s discretion to investigate and determine if discipline
in the restricted and technical sense of judicial process ( _BCO_ 27-1.b) is the
necessary avenue for achieving the ends of discipline as laid out in _BCO_ 27-3.

Thus, in Case 2011-15, the SJC declares that “in general _BCO_ 32-2 requires
that a court commence process upon the filing of charges.” [1] The concurring
opinion to case 2010-04 lays out this two-fold path to judicial process in more
detail.

There are two (2) basic ways for such concerns to be resolved
by a trial.

First, a trial may result from a _BCO_ 31-2 investigation, which
leads to a finding o fa strong presumption of guilt. Upon such a
finding, a prosecutor is appointed, an indictment drawn, Parties
cited, and a trial is conducted.

Second, a trial may result when someone, who believes an
offense has occurred and after practicing Matthew 18:15, files
"charges" under _BCO_ 32-2.

It is at this point that some err in understanding how
our Constitution instructs us to proceed. Some,
incorrectly, believe that when _BCO_ 32-2 “charges” are
made, the Constitution requires (1) a _BCO_ 31-2
investigation and (2) a finding o fa strong presumption of
guilt, before proceeding to trial. The correct interpretation
of the Constitution, as set forth in the Decision, is that
when _BCO_ 32-2 "charges" are made out (and Matthew
18:15 has been practiced and _BCO_ 31-8 does not apply),
there is NO investigation under _BCO_ 31-2, but rather the
Court goes directly to appoint a prosecutor, an indictment
is drawn, the Parties are cited, and a trial is conducted. [2]

### CCB advice

**A.** Overture 1 from Northern New England Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 32-2 to
Clarify that a Court May Investigate Whether Charges Should Be Filed”

<a id="ga52-p252"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=254 printed_page=252 -->

In the opinion of the CCB Overture 1 is **in conflict** with the Constitution in the
following ways:

- The proposed amendment creates a conflicting constitutional
threshold regarding the nature of an offense that is subject to judicial
process. Specifically, Overture 1 requires that “the alleged offense
rises to a level warranting judicial process,” but, according to _BCO_ 291, the “proper object of judicial process” is “anything in the doctrines
or practice of a Church member professing faith in Christ which is
contrary to the Word of God”;

- The proposed amendment creates a conflicting constitutional
threshold regarding the standard of proof required to commence
judicial process when charges are presented by a voluntary prosecutor.
Specifically, Overture 1 requires that there must be “a strong
presumption of guilt” (proposed _BCO_ 32-2.e.) and (assuming the list
is conjunctive) that “it is reasonable to expect the offense can be
proven through judicial process” ( _BCO_ 32-2.f.), but _BCO_ 31-9 only
requires “probable cause” for charges from a voluntary prosecutor;
and

- The proposed amendment removes agency from someone seeking to
become a voluntary prosecutor. Specifically, the logic of the Rules of
Discipline presumes that individuals have the right to bring charges
and prosecute them voluntarily (see, e.g., _BCO_ 31-6, -7, -9), but
Overture 1 places an absolute investigatory and high moral burden on
the court in every case where charges are laid before the court by an
individual.

Additionally, in the opinion of CCB, Overture 1 is **ambiguous** in that it is
unclear whether the items listed are conjunctive or disjunctive.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
