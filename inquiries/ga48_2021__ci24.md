# II.X — Confession timing requirements in cases without process

*The CCB found that amending BCO 38-1 regarding timing of confession in cases without process was not in conflict with the BCO, and the overture was adopted.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 48th (2021)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 38-1, BCO 42-2  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted

**Rationale:**

The revision makes it clearer that the court can accept a confession, and
handle it as a case without process, at different stages – pre-investigation,
during investigation, or even post-indictment. But the court is never required
to handle a confession as a case without process. It always remains entirely
the court’s discretion.
