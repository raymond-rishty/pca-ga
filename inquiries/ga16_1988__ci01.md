# 16-67 V,6 — Dividing presbytery into parts to hear ordination candidate sermons

*BCO 21-4 does not permit presbyteries to divide simply into committees to hear ordination sermons; division into commissions is not prohibited but should be exceptional, not regular practice.*

, 6. That the Constitutional Inquiry: "Does BCO 21-4 forbid or permit presbyteries to divide into committees or parts in order to hear the sermons of candidates for ordination?", referred to the Committee on Judicial Business, be answered, as follows: BCO 21-4 does not permit presbyteries simply to divide into committees or parts in order to hear the sermons of candidates for ordination. It would not be contrary to the express provisions of the BCO to divide a presbytery into commissions. The General Assembly recommends that this practice not be resorted to regularly but that it be viewed as exceptional. The procedure might be subject to abuse and could be detrimental to the health and strength of the church. Also, there are more suitable alternatives.

## Verbatim record

6. That the Constitutional Inquiry: "Does BCO 21-4 forbid or permit presbyteries to divide into committees or parts in order to hear the semons of candidates for ordination?", referred to the Committee on Judicial Business, be answered, as

<a id="ga16-p167"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=16 pdf_page=167 printed_page=null -->

follows:

*BCO* 21-4 does not permit presbyteries simply to divide into committees or parts in order to hear the sermons of candidates for ordination. It would not be contrary to the express provisions of the *BCO* to divide a presbytery into commissions.

The General Assembly recommends that this practice not be resorted to regularly but that it be viewed as exceptional. The procedure might be subject to abuse and could be detrimental to the health and strength of the church. Also, there are more suitable alternatives.
*Adopted*
