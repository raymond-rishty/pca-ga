# 29-28 III.1 — Presbytery-wide withdrawal from the General Assembly

*The Constitution is silent on whether a whole presbytery may withdraw from the GA, though individual congregations may withdraw as a group witheach congregation's consent.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 25-11  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga29_2001 lines 5844–5864](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p133)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a non-judicial reference from Westminster Presbytery by advising the Assembly that the constitution is silent on the question of whether a presbytery as a whole may withdraw from the General Assembly (though the 2nd General Assembly received Westminster Presbytery with a provision recognizing its right to withdraw, cf. 2001, p. 143, 29-28, Item 1). The CCB referenced BCO 25-11 regarding procedures to be followed if individual churches withdraw from a presbytery in a group, and stated the requirement that, if a group of churches should choose to leave the PCA in order to form a new affiliation, or to continue an affiliation that they perceive as antecedent to the PCA, then they can peaceably withdraw as a group subject to the consent of each congregation in the group.

**Key words:** BCO 25-11

**Inquiry from:** Westminster Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga29_2001 p.133](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p133)

## Verbatim record

#### Reference 1 – Westminster Presbytery:

Question 1 – There is a desire on the part of some within Westminster Presbytery to leave the PCA, carrying with them the cherished name of Westminster Presbytery. They do not want to simply withdraw as individual churches, but rather as a group, taking with them the name Westminster Presbytery. Can this be done in the manner proposed? If not, what remedy for these brethren do you suggest that would satisfy their desires and be in accord with the BCO?”

Response: It is the advice of the CCB in reference to the inquiry of Westminster Presbytery (4/21/2001) related to withdrawal from the PCA as a presbytery that:

a. There is no provision within our Constitution for a presbytery as a whole to withdraw.

b. The right of an individual church to affiliate with whomever it will, however, cannot be impinged upon by a higher court (BCO 25-11; cf.11-3, 4). The principle of the BCO that is most closely related is that of

<a id="ga29-p134"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=29 pdf_page=136 printed_page=134 -->

Chapter 25-11, which allows, “A particular church may withdraw from any court of this body at any time for reasons, which seem to it sufficient”.

c. Should a group of churches choose to leave the PCA in order to form a new affiliation, or to continue an affiliation that they perceive as antecedent to the PCA, then they can peaceably withdraw as a group subject to the consent of each congregation in the group.

d. In the event of a group of churches withdrawing from the PCA with the consent of each church involved, those congregations remaining within the bounds of that presbytery that decline to withdraw with the departing group would stay within their present denomination. Those congregations not departing would constitute the continuing presbytery of the PCA.

e. There are no standards regarding continuing the name of a departing group and such matters should be left to the discretion and charity of the brothers involved. Additionally, any division of assets or attachment of corporate identity, where applicable, will need to be determined by the brothers involved.
Adopted by CCB
