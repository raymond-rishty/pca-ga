# III.A — Whether all RAO amendments require CCB review before GA vote

*The CCB ruled that all RAO amendments require CCB review per RAO 8-2.b.3, but no provision requires Overtures Committee approval for RAO amendments originating from CRPR or the SJC.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 8-2.b.3, RAO 15-1  ·  **Disposition:** All RAO amendments require CCB review per RAO 8-2.b.3; no provision requires Overtures Committee approval for RAO amendments from CRPR or SJC; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 pp. 258–259](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p258)*

---

## Verbatim record

**A.** the CCB received a request for advice from the stated clerk ina
communication dated May 31, 2024:

[W]hether future _RAO_ changes originating from any source should be
required to have CCB review and Overtures Committee approval
before being voted upon by the General Assembly.

It was moved and carried to adopt the following advice to the Stated Clerk:

_Rules of Assembly Operations_ 8-2.b.3 requires that the Committee on
Constitutional Business “[p]rovide advice concerning the effect of any

<a id="ga52-p259"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=261 printed_page=259 -->

proposed amendment to the Constitution or to the _Rules of Assembly_
_Operations_ .” Therefore, all amendments to the _RAO_, no matter the
source, must be reviewed by CCB. However, no provision in our
Constitution or _RAO_ requires the Overtures Committee to approve
_RAO_ amendments that arise from CRPR or the SJC (cf. _RAO_ 15-1).
_Adopted 8-0-0_
