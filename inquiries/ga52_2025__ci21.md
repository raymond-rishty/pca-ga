# II.U — Elevating a censure without a new trial under BCO 36

*Proposed amendment to BCO 36 providing for censure elevation without a wholly new trial was found to be ambiguous under the BCO.*

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 32 from the Houston Metro Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 36 to Provide for Elevation of a Censure without an

Entirely New Trial”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 36 be amended to add a new paragraph, _BCO_ 36-8,

as follows (underlining for additions):

**36-8** . When members of a court wish to consider the elevation of an

indefinite suspension, whether from office or from the Sacraments
( _BCO_ 36-5) to the censure of deposition or excommunication, it shall
not be required to initiate process _de novo_ according to _BCO_ 31-2 or
_BCO_ 32-2. The indefinitely suspended person is not in good standing
and has already been found impenitent, resulting in indefinite
suspension ( _BCO_ 30-3). However, the indefinitely suspended person
has aright to a hearing in the matter. The court supervising the

<a id="ga52-p1267"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1269 printed_page=1267 -->

indefinite suspension may consider a motion to find him “incorrigible
and contumacious” ( _cf_ . _BCO_ 30-4) and present evidence to that effect.
The court shall give at least ten (10) days’ notice of such a motion to
the indefinitely suspended person and permit him the privilege of the
floor to plead and give a defense before the original trial court. The
court, upon completing its hearing, shall vote to determine whether or
not to elevate the indefinite suspension to deposition or
excommunication.

So that _BCO_ 36-8 as amended would read:

**36-8** . When members of a court wish to consider the elevation of an

indefinite suspension, whether from office or from the Sacraments
( _BCO_ 36-5) to the censure of deposition or excommunication, it shall
not be required to initiate process _de novo_ according to _BCO_ 31-2 or
_BCO_ 32-2. The indefinitely suspended person is not in good standing
and has already been found impenitent, resulting in indefinite
suspension ( _BCO_ 30-3). However, the indefinitely suspended person
has aright to a hearing in the matter. The court supervising the
indefinite suspension may consider a motion to find him “incorrigible
and contumacious” ( _cf_ . _BCO_ 30-4) and present evidence to that effect.
The court shall give at least ten (10) days’ notice of such a motion to
the indefinitely suspended person and permit him the privilege of the
floor to plead and give a defense before the original trial court. The
court, upon completing its hearing, shall vote to determine whether or
not to elevate the indefinite suspension to deposition or
excommunication.

**RATIONALE:**

The _Book of Church Order_ currently does not include any express provision
for the elevating of censure of indefinite suspension from either office or the
Sacraments to a greater censure of deposition or excommunication. This has
led to confusion as to what is the proper process for elevating the censure for
a person whom the court has deemed unrepentant. Should such a person be
entitled to completely _de novo_ process, that is, the court must charge the person
anew, marshal evidence and witnesses, and conduct a trial as if the person were
presumed innocent? While a censured person is entitled to some rights, clearly
he is not entitled to all rights under the Rules of Discipline. One who has been

<a id="ga52-p1268"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1270 printed_page=1268 -->

judged guilty by a court does not have the right to a presumption of innocence.
There is no person to make out a _BCO_ 32-2 charge, nor are there common
fame reports to provoke a _BCO_ 31-2 investigation. The court has already been
dealing with the indefinitely suspended person for some time, and by its
previous action, the court has already found indefinitely suspended person
impenitent.

The SJC revisited this issue, first decided in Case 2001-25 _TE Anthony_
_Dallison v. North Florida Presbytery_ ( _M30GA,_ 2002, pages 156-162). In that
case, the SJC decided that _no_ process beyond a vote of the court was required
to elevate a censure. In Case 2023-09 _TE Aaron Myers v. Illiana Presbytery_
( _M51GA_, 2024, pages 824-834), the SJC rejected two possible options: either
a _de novo_ process with a new trial or elevation by pure legislative act. Instead,
it reasoned that a process which permitted the censured person to present
evidence of his penitence without giving him the full rights of an uncensured
person under the Rules of Discipline was the best path forward. This
amendment would codify this path in our constitution.

_Adopted by Houston Metro Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 14, 2025_
_Attested by RE /s/ Eric Manthei, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**U.** Overture 32 from the Houston Metro Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 36 to
Provide for Elevation of a Censure without an Entirely New Trial”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 32 is **ambiguous** in that it is not clear
whether the “hearing” in the added paragraph is governed by other provisions
in the Rules of Discipline, such as _BCO_ 32-10, -13, -15, -16, -17, -18; and _BCO_
35, and whether the accused has the right to Appeal an elevated suspension.

CCB notes that the phrase “original trial court” is potentially ambiguous in
that it is not clear if the phrase refers to the exact commission or group of
presbyters who heard the evidence at the original trial; whether the phrase

<a id="ga52-p257"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=259 printed_page=257 -->

refers simply to the Session or Presbytery in which the trial was held; or
whether the phrase could include another court that has assumed jurisdiction
under _BCO_ 37-7.

CCB also notes that this overture is in conflict with Overture 38.

_Adopted 8-0-0_
