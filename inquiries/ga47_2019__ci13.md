# II.M — Permitting video testimony by witnesses in judicial process

*A Houston Metro Presbytery overture to amend BCO 32-8 to allow video testimony by witnesses in church judicial proceedings was found in conflict and adopted by the CCB.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 32-8, BCO 35-10, BCO 32-13  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict; Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 400](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p400)*

---

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 17 from Houston Metro Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 32-8 to Allow for Video Testimony by Witnesses”

**Whereas,** an important aspect of any case of process is the gathering and

presenting of evidence, including testimony produced by witnesses;

**Whereas**, testimony by witnesses before the court can be inconvenient due

to geographic distance or other circumstances;

**Whereas**, it is important that witnesses be examined with an opportunity to

be cross-examined by both parties ( _cf._ _BCO_ 32-13); and

**Whereas**, technology has made available the possibility of having a virtual

presence with video and audio interaction even when a person is
geographically distant from a meeting or court room, and the courts of
the civil magistrate allow for video testimony by witnesses;

**Therefore, be it resolved**, that the General Assembly of the Presbyterian

Church in America amend _BCO_ 32-8 with the following (additions are
underlined, deletions are struck through):

**32-8** . When the offense with which an accused person stands
charged took place at a distance, ~~and~~ or it is inconvenient for
the witnesses to appear before the court having jurisdiction,
that court may either (a) appoint a commission of its body, or
(b) request the coordinate court contiguous to the place where
the facts occurred, to take the testimony for it, or (c) have the
testimony taken by videoconference, which shall employ
technical means that ensure that all persons participating in the
meeting can see and hear each other at the same time, and
which allows for live cross-examination by both parties. The
accused shall always have reasonable notice of the time and
place of the meeting of this commission or coordinate court.

_Adopted by Houston Metro Presbytery at its stated meeting, January 18, 2019_
_Attested by /s/ TE Lou Veiga, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**M** . Overture 17 from Houston Metro Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 32-8 to

Allow for Video Testimony by Witnesses”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 17 **is in conflict** with other parts
of the Constitution. _BCO_ 35-10 deals with the same subject matter
but is not amended under this proposed Overture. _BCO_ 32-13
requires that witnesses “be examined in the presence of the accused.”
_Adopted by the CCB_
