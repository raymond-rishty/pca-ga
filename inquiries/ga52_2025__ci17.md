# II.Q — Associate membership class for honorably retired teaching elders

*Proposed amendment to BCO 46-4 creating associate membership for honorably retired TEs was found in conflict with the BCO.*

“Amend _BCO_ 46-4 to Add a Class of Associate Membership for
Honorably Retired TEs”

**Whereas** our current _BCO_ 46-4 defines an associate member as those

believers temporarily residing in a location other than their permanent
homes;

**Whereas** the aim of this overture is pastoral and not for the purpose of

changing our polity; or the needs of TEs called to works which are not
to a local church. (Those differing calls as far as a defined relationship
to a local church require a more complex study);

**Whereas** ministers of the gospel who have served the church well and received

Honorably Retired (HR) status may need ongoing pastoral care and
vital relationships that local churches can give but is not practical for
Presbytery Shepherding committees or Pastoral Care Committee to
implement. The pastoral care of an HR minister would be a good
application of I Timothy 5:10 coupled with the charge of John 13:14
and more suitable for deacons and elders of a local church to tend to

their needs, Paul’s Partnership with the Philippian church was vital to
his wellbeing and sanctification. (Phil.1:1-4);

**Whereas** we already have a provision to assign ministers under discipline to a

local church ( _BCO_ 46-8). We hold to two types of discipline, one
being the regular preaching of the Word, and the other which is
discipline for the unrepentant/repenting brother or sister. The
assigning of an HR TE as an Associate member of a local church for
what we describe as good discipline and retaining membership in a
Presbytery would give to an HR what the _WCF_ speaks of “so as to
conduce to their mutual good, both in the inward and outward man…
and saints are bound to maintain a holy fellowship and communion in
the worship of God.” ( _WCF_ 26.1-2);

**Whereas** for an HR minister they may need to locate close to children and

grandchildren for ongoing support and not be able to travel to
Presbytery several times during the year. The gifted men on a church
Session and diaconate may treat him and his wife pastorally (I

### CCB advice

**Q.** Overture 25 from the Northwest Georgia Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 46-4
to Add a Class of Associate Membership for Honorably Retired TEs”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 25 is **in conflict** with _BCO_ 13-1 and 132 in that it applies permissive language to a Teaching Elder holding
membership in his Presbytery; further, it creates a constitutional conflict by
using mandatory language requiring all Honorably Retired Teaching Elders to
hold associate membership in a local congregation. To the extent the language
of the proposed amendment is deemed to apply to existing Honorably Retired
Teaching Elders, it appears to be in conflict with Preliminary Principle 2.
Finally, the Overture uses the phrase “communicant fellowship” which is a
category undefined by our Constitution.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
