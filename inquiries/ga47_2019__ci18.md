# II.R — Non-ordained persons serving on GA committees and boards

*This overture to allow non-ordained persons to serve on GA committees and boards was found by the CCB to be in conflict with the BCO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 14-1.10, BCO 14-1.11, BCO 14-1.12, RAO 5-2, RAO 4-21.c.6, RAO 4-5  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict; Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 lines 21074–21087](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p401)*

---

##### OVERTURE 32 from Tennessee Valley Presbytery (CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 14-1.10, _14-_ 1.11, and 14-1.12 to Allow Non-ordained
Persons to Serve on Committees and Boards (Version 4)

**Whereas**, vigorous participation of women in the life of the early church is

attested by the New Testament: they helped select (Acts 1:14) and train
(Acts 18:26) leaders of the church, were prophets (Acts 21:8-9), served
the church through deeds of mercy in vital ways (Acts 9:36–41), were
instrumental in the planting of churches (Acts 16:12–15; 17:4,12) and
other missionary endeavors (Acts 18:1f), and were acknowledged as
important leaders for the church (Rom. 16:3-4); and

**Whereas**, the Presbyterian Church in America believes Christ has called his

church t ob ea “spiritual commonwealth” ( _BCO_ 3-1), in which members
elect godly men to be ordained as elders who are given the power of
jurisdiction as they act jointly in the courts of the church ( _BCO_ 3-2); and

**Whereas**, a robustly complementarian perspective affirms the oversight of

these elders and the proper, vital and full expression of the gifts given by
Christ to men and women for his service; and

**Whereas**, as part of the calling of church leaders to “equip the saints for the

work of ministry for building up the body of Christ” (Eph. 4:12), many
sessions throughout the PCA identify gifted men and women to serve on

<a id="ga47-p665"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=667 printed_page=665 -->

committees of the church, such as missions, building, and pastoral search
committees; and

**Whereas**, in contrast with a commission in the PCA, which is "authorized to

deliberate and conclude the business referred to it," committees are
appointed by courts “to examine, consider and report” to their
constituting court concerning the matters that court delegates to them
( _BCO_ 15-1) and thus do not exercise governing authority; and

**Whereas**, in accordance with this principle, Permanent Committees

established by the General Assembly are “to serve and not to direct any
Church judicatories. They are not to establish policy, but rather execute
policy established by the General Assembly” ( _BCO_ 14-1.7); and

**Whereas** neither the NT evidence nor the constitutional evidence cited above

seem to have been given adequate consideration by the 17th GA in their
denial of O.24 asking for a study committee to determine whether
"women could be included on some or all of the permanent committees
and agencies of the GA," which was the precedent cited for rejecting the
overtures presented last year to open participation on Boards and
Permanent Committees to non-ordained members; and

**Whereas**, these considerations suggest there is no impediment to an

unordained member in good standing in the PCA serving on a Permanent
Committee or Agency of the General Assembly established in _BCO_ 141.12; and

**Whereas**, the 45th General Assembly adopted the recommendation of The

Ad Interim Committee on Women Serving in the Ministry of the Church
that stated, “sessions, presbyteries and the General Assembly strive to
develop, recognize, and utilize the gifts, skills, knowledge, and wisdom
of godly women in the local, regional, and national church, and
particularly consider overtures that would allow qualified women to
serve on appropriate committees and agencies within the church”; and

**Whereas**, the 46th General Assembly answered in the negative an overture

to allow non-ordained members on General Assembly Agency Boards
on the grounds that _BCO_ 14-1.11 had to be amended prior to amendment
of the PCA Bylaws, which governs the Agencies,

**Therefore be it resolved** to amend _The Book of Church Orde_ r 14-1.10, 14
1.11, and 14-1.12 as follows: [Proposed additions underlined and
deletions noted by strike out.]

**R** . Overture 32 from Tennessee Valley Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 14

on Committees and Boards” (Version 4)

In the opinion of the CCB, Overtures 8, 14, 19, 21, and 32 **are in**
**conflict** with _RAO_ 5-2 as the Overtures establish additional
requirements for the Chairman. It should also be noted that _RAO_ 421.c.6 does not explicitly mention non-ordained members.
Additionally, _RAO_ 4-5 would need to be changed editorially to
properly reference the newly numbered sections in _BCO_ 14-1 upon
passage and adoption of the Overtures. _Adopted by the CCB_
