# 31-56 VI — Oversight of temperate language in SJC dissenting opinions

*The CCB found the overture in conflict with the constitution, as no mechanism exists for commissioners to object to language in a concluded judicial case.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 31st (2003)  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga31_2003 lines 8517–8525](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p161)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 9 from Central Carolina Presbytery ("Oversight of Temperate Language in SJC Concurring or Dissenting Opinions") was in conflict with other parts of the constitution, as there was no constitutional mechanism by which a commissioner can object to language in a judicial case since the case itself has been decided and is not on the floor of the GA for any action whatsoever.

**Inquiry from:** Central Carolina Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga31_2003 p.161](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p161)

### CCB advice

**Overture 9** _from Central Carolina Presbytery: “Oversight of Temperate_
_Language in SJC Concurring or Dissenting Opinions”_

[See text and action at 31-57, III, 10, p. 198.]
It is the opinion of the CCB that this Overture _is_ in conflict with other
parts of the constitution. There is presently no constitutional mechanism by
which a commissioner can object to language ina judicial case since the case
itself has been decided and is not on the floor of the GA for any action
whatsoever. _Adopted by CCB_
