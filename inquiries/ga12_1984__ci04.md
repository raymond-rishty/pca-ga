# 12-53 II, 54 — Teaching elder without a call and BCO 13-5 exceptions

*The CCB advised that BCO 13-5 allows a presbytery to retain a teaching elder without a call in cases it deems necessary, subject to General Assembly review.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 12th (1984)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 13-5  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga12_1984 lines 7797–7810](../markdown/ga12_1984.md#ga12-p138)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

That the Assembly ratify the answer to the following Constitutional inquiry: Constitutional Inquiry 4: from the Presbytery of Eastern Canada: 'The Presbytery of Eastern Canada requests of the Committee on Judicial Business clarification of BCO 13-5 with reference to a situation such as that of (a teaching elder without call).' ANSWER: BCO 13-5 reads that 'ordinarily' there must be a call to a definite work. The two exceptions listed are in the cases of honorably retired ministers and 'those cases deemed necessary by the Presbytery, subject to the review of the General Assembly.' In the case of (teaching elder in question), the Presbytery must use its judgment regarding the necessity.
