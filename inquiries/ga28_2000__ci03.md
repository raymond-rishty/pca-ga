# 28-19 III.3 — Which GA entities are subject to the permanent committee ineligibility rule

*The CCB clarified that the "permanent committee" ineligibility provision in RAO 13-2 applies only to the four committees enumerated in RAO 4-2, not to other GA entities.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 4-2, RAO 4-3, RAO 4-4, RAO 13-2, RAO 14-2, BCO 14-1.12  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

The CCB answered a non-judicial reference from Ascension Presbytery inquiring as to "which of the entities in RAO 4-2, 4-3 and 4-4 are covered by the 'ineligibility provision' of RAO 13-2 [now RAO 14-2], and why." The CCB answered that the term "permanent committee" in RAO 13-2 [now RAO 14-2] refers only to the four [now five] committees mentioned in RAO 4-2 (see BCO 14-1.12).

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

## COMMUNICATION 4 From the Presbytery of the Ascension (to CCB)
“Regarding the Interpretation of RAO 4-2; 4-3; 4-4; and 13-2”

Whereas, RAO 13-2 states “Commissioners serving on permanent committee or sub-committees of the Assembly or the staffs thereof are not eligible to serve on any Committees of Commissioners;” [emphasis added] and

Whereas, this has traditionally been the basis for barring ones serving the entities discussed in RAO 4-2 and 4-3 from service on Committees of Commissioners; and

Whereas, at one recent Assembly commissioners were told at the Committees of Commissioners’ briefing that this provision also barred ones serving on the Committee on Constitutional Business, Nominating Committee, Review Committee, Standing Judicial Commission, Theological Examining Committee, etc., from serving on Committees of Commissioners, thus disenfranchising some who fell into this category and had been elected by their presbyteries to serve at that Assembly; and

Whereas, the July 1999 edition of the *Handbook for Presbytery Clerks* states that RAO 13-2 “excludes SJC members from serving” while “[m]embers of the Nominating Committee and Committee on Review of Presbytery Records are eligible to serve;” and

Whereas, the Constitutional basis for this distinction is not clear, nor does it provide guidance as to the eligibility of members of some of the other committees noted above; and

Whereas, BCO 14-1.12 states “The Assembly permanent committees are the Administrative Committee of General Assembly, Committee on Christian Education and Publications, Committee on Mission to North America, and Committee on Mission to the World;” and

Whereas, RAO 4-2 also designates these four committees as the “permanent committees;” and

Whereas, this limited definition of “permanent committees” seems to mean that RAO 13-2 can only be seen as limiting persons serving on four committees (and their sub-committees and staffs) from service on Committees of Commissioners, which conclusion is much narrower than past practices; and

Whereas, we are therefore confused as to the proper understanding of RAO 13-2; and

Whereas, we do not wish to run the risk of being disenfranchised from Committees of Commissioners either because we elected someone to serve who would be excluded under one of the broader understandings of RAO 13-2, or because we did not elect someone for fear he would be excluded when he is in fact eligible; and

Whereas, we therefore need clarification on these matters before we can choose our members for the Committees of Commissioners of the 28th General Assembly; and

Whereas, the Stated Clerk of the General Assembly advised our Stated Clerk that this question should be addressed to the Committee on Constitutional Business; and

Whereas, BCO 41-2 gives us among the proper subjects for reference “…questions involving the Constitution and legal procedures respecting which the lower court feels the need of guidance;”

Therefore Be in Resolved that the Presbytery of the Ascension address the following non-judicial reference to the General Assembly (and its Committee on Constitutional Business): “Which of the entities listed in RAO 4-2, 4-3, and 4-4 are covered by the ‘ineligibility provision’ of RAO 13-2 and why?”

Adopted at the January Stated Meeting of the Presbytery of the Ascension on January 29, 2000. Attested by: /s/ Frederick R. Neikirk, Stated Clerk

### CCB advice

## Reference 3 – (Communication 4) Presbytery of the Ascension re: RAO 4-2; 4-3; 4-4 and 13-2:

Question – Which of the entities listed in RAO 4-2, 4-3 and 4-4 are covered by the “ineligibility provision” of RAO 13-2, and why?

Response – It is the advice of the Committee that the term “permanent committee” is a technical term referring only to the four committees mentioned in RAO 4-2 (cf. *BCO* 14-1.12). Therefore, only those serving on those four committees (or their sub-committees and staffs) are barred from serving on committees of commissioners (cf. RAO 4-10.a). Service on any other committee, commission or agency of the General Assembly would not, under our current rules, bar one from serving on a committee of commissioners unless he would be serving on a committee of commissioners that oversees the committee or agency on which he serves (see Roberts Rules sec. 44).

The response was adopted by proper motion, 8-0-0.
