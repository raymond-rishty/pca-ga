# 13-31 — Trustee with expired term filling another's unexpired vacancy

*The CCB reaffirmed that BCO 14-1-12 permits an expired-term trustee to fill an unexpired vacancy only if agency bylaws approved by the Assembly specifically allow it.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Inquiry / reference  ·  **Assembly:** 13th (1985)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 14-1-12  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

following constitutional inquiry from the floor was referred to the Committee on Judicial Business: "Does the Book of Church Order 14-1-12 permit a trustee of an agency whose term has expired to be nominated to fill the unexpired term of another trustee who has resigned from his position?" 1985, 13-31, p. 91 ... The following response was adopted: "BCO 14-1-12 (4) permits such election only if the bylaws of the agency (as approved by the Assembly) so specify."

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

### 13-27 Constitutional Inquiry.

The following constitutional inquiry from the floor was referred to the Committee on Judicial Business:

“Does the Book of Church Order 14-1-12 permit a trustee of an agency whose term has expired to be nominated to fill the unexpired term of another trustee who has resigned from his position?”

### CCB advice

# 13-31 Response to Constitutional Inquiry.

TE Rodney T. King, Judicial Business Committee Chairman, led in prayer and presented a partial report of the Committee relating to the constitutional inquiry in 13-27, p. 86. The following response was proposed:

“BCO 14-1-12 (4) permits such election only if the bylaws of the agency (as approved by the Assembly) so specify.” Adopted
