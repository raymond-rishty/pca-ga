# 30-29 Item B — Presbytery's right to reclaim a matter from a commission

*A court may reclaim a matter from a commission before completion, but may not reverse a lower court's decision without actually hearing the appeal per BCO 42.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 42  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga30_2002 lines 4783–4811](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p105)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a two-part constitutional inquiry from Philadelphia Presbytery regarding 1) the right of Presbytery to take back from a commission (before the commission completed its task and rendered judgment) a matter committed to it, and 2) to reverse, without hearing the appeal, the decision of a Session. In the opinion of the CCB, 1) a court does have the right to take back from a commission a matter committed to it. However, 2) a higher court may not reverse the decision of a lower court without actually hearing the appeal. It must follow the procedures detailed in BCO 42.

**Key words:** BCO 42

**Inquiry from:** Philadelphia Presbytery

## Verbatim record

B. Considered a constitutional inquiry from Philadelphia Presbytery:
“At its May 11, 2002, stated meeting, Philadelphia Presbytery voted to
make a constitutional inquiry to the Constitutional Business Committee
of the General Assembly, to wit:
having accepted an appeal from a church member against a judgment
rendered against him by the session of his church, and having referred
that appeal to a duly appointed commission properly erected by
Presbytery, does the Presbytery have the right:
1. to take back from said commission the matter committed to it
before the commission can complete its task and render a
judgment; and
2. to reverse, without hearing the appeal, the decision of the
Session, which found the appellant guilty and suspended him
from the office of ruling elder?
The actual case in dispute has been in the commission's hands for sixteen
months and the record of the case has still not be enable to be finalized to
the satisfaction of the appellant, the session, and the commission.
Philadelphia Presbytery’ needs and requests the advice of our fathers and
brothers. ”
It is the opinion of the CCB that in reference to question #1 of the
inquiry, our answer is yes, the Presbytery may take back from said
commission the matter committed to it before the commission has
completed its task and rendered a judgment.
In reference to question #2, our answer is no, the Presbytery may not
reverse, without hearing the appeal, the decision of the Session. The
Presbytery must follow the procedures detailed in BCO 42.
Respectfully Submitted,
/s/ TE Craig D. Childs, Sr., Chairman
/si TE Mark A. Rowden, Acting Secretary
