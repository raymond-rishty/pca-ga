# 29-28 Item 2 — Ordination vows require compliance with Assembly rules of order

*The CCB advised that delegates are bound by ordination vows to follow PCA constitutional rules, and that changing unscriptural standards requires proper amendment procedures, not suspension.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 21-5.2, BCO 21-5.3, BCO 21-5.4, BCO 21-5.5, BCO 24-6.2, BCO 24-6.3, BCO 24-6.4, BCO 24-6.5, BCO Preface III, BCO 45, BCO 21-5.2-5, BCO 24-6.2-5  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB received a constitutional inquiry requesting advice on how the Assembly is to suspend its Rules of Operation (BCO, RAO, SJC Manual, and Robert's Rules of Order) in order to obey the King of the Church as expressed in the Word of God. The CCB advised that GA delegates are bound by ordination vows to obey our Constitution and other adopted rules "as fully and fairly as possible" (see BCO 21-5.2-5 and BCO 24-6.2-5; Preface III), and noted that "if our standards are shown to be out of accord with Scripture, then there are proper procedures to follow, at each level, in order to change those standards" (see BCO 45). The CCB was unwilling to affirm "the presumption that we need to suspend our rules in order to obey Christ."

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

The following Constitutional Inquiries were referred to CCB: (1) What is the status of an SJC Concurring Opinion that has been reported to the General Assembly and that has not been found by the SJC to be in “temperate language” (14-7)? and (2) “Request advice on how the Assembly is to suspend our rules of operation (BCO, RAO, SJC Manual and Robert’s Rules or Order) in order to obey the king of the Church-Jesus Christ-as He has spoken to us in His Word. What is the motion, or procedure to live by and obey God’s Word, when we are fenced in by our standing rules?” [See the CCB action on p. 147.] RE Nusbaum concluded the report with prayer).

### CCB advice

2) CONSTITUTIONAL INQUIRY #4

Request advice on how the Assembly is to suspend our Rules of Operation: *BCO, RAO, SJC Manual and Robert’s Rules of Order to obey the King of the Church – Jesus Christ as He has spoken to us in His Word.* What is the motion, or procedure to live by and obey God’s Word when we are fenced in by our Standing Rules?

Response:

It is the advice of the CCB that the documents referenced in the inquiry are either part of our Constitution¹ or are rules we have adopted as prudential applications of Biblical patterns of polity². As such, the delegates to the General Assembly are bound by their ordination vows (*BCO* 21-5, 3&amp;4 and 24-5, 3&amp;4) to operate under these as fully and fairly as possible. Thus, for example, if a provision of Robert’s Rules contradicts the *BCO*, the latter takes precedence and the former is set aside. Similarly, if one of the documents contains provisions for its suspension, then the Assembly may avail itself of that procedure. The absence of such a procedure in certain documents means that the General Assembly is bound to operate under the provisions of those documents unless or until they are amended.

We note this always allows those who feel that Scriptural principles have been ignored to access the means of redress available in our Constitution, including the mechanisms of *BCO* 45. Additionally, they (and all of us) should pray that the Holy Spirit will ultimately affirm what is correct for the Church as the Church properly follows its standards for operations and revisions in the future. Private concerns between individuals should be handled according to Matthew 18.

The presumption that we need to suspend our rules in order to obey Christ is an opinion that the CCB is not willing to affirm. If our standards are shown to be out of accord with Scripture, then there are proper procedures to follow, at each level, in order to change those standards.

Approved by CCB 7-0-0

¹ Which consists of the Westminster Confession of Faith, together with the Larger and Shorter Catechisms, and the Book of Church Order, all of which are subordinate to the Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments (Preliminary Principle III)

² e.g., RAO, SJC Manual, Robert’s Rules of Order
