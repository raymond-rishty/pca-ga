# 30-29 III — Overture to abolish the Standing Judicial Commission

*The CCB found the overture to abolish the SJC in conflict with BCO 14-1 and 15, and also unconstitutionally vague about the basis for alternative decisions.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 14-1, BCO 15, BCO 15-4, BCO 15-5  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga30_2002 pp. 100–101](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p100)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 6 from Westminster Presbytery ("Revise BCO 15-4 and BCO 15-5 to Abolish the Standing Judicial Commission") was in conflict with other parts of the constitution, including but not limited to BCO 14-1, 15. The overture was also unconstitutionally vague as to what will be the basis of making the alternative decisions indicated in the proposed BCO 15-5.

**Key words:** BCO 14-1, BCO 15, BCO 15-4, BCO 15-5

**Inquiry from:** Westminster Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga30_2002 p.100](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p100)

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 6 from Westminster Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)

“Revise BCO 15-4 and 15-5 to Abolish the Standing Judicial Commission”

Whereas, the Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) was
established for the noble goal of seeking to guarantee
consistency of Constitutional interpretation; and

Whereas, throughout its relatively brief history the SJC has
been the object of serious criticism of its procedures,
policies, and judicial conclusions; and

Whereas, the seriousness of earlier criticism led to the
appointment of an Ad Interim Committee on Judicial
Procedures (AICJP), which gave its final report at the
1996 General Assembly, and whose recommendations
were adopted by the denomination; and

Whereas, we believe that it is time to re-visit the question of
the wisdom of having a Standing Judicial Commission; and

Whereas, among the abuses of the system which we have
recently witnessed include the admittance to record of
statements by members of the SJC which clearly
crossed the bounds of propriety; and

Whereas, a significant portion of the 29th General Assembly
clearly was frustrated by the inability to rectify the
situation; and

Whereas, under our present rules, there are no Assembly-
wide checks and balances on the SJC; and

Whereas, placing that much power in the hands of a relatively
few individuals is not wise; and

Whereas, there is increasing frustration among many in the
PCA arising from a perception that the decisions of
the SJC do not reflect predictable justice; and

<a id="ga30-p256"></a>

Whereas, we believe there is a better, more economical, and
more efficient way of adjudicating cases;

Now therefore, be it resolved, that Westminster Presbytery
hereby overtures the 30th General Assembly to begin
the process of amending the BCO by replacing 15-4
and 15-5 with the following:
15.4 Every General Assembly shall commit all
judicial cases within its jurisdiction to one or more
commissions which shall be appointed in the
following manner:
1. The General Assembly shall divide the presbyteries
into no less than six geographical regions.
2. when a case arises from a Presbytery, the
commissioners from the previous General Assembly
who are from the other presbyteries in the same region
as that Presbytery shall be put into a pool, from which
shall be drawn, at random, alternation between ruling
elders and teaching elders, the names of fourteen
commissioners.
3. if a persons o selected declines to serve or is
ineligible to serve, then another name will be selected
in his place.
4. Those who serve on such a commission shall be
ineligible to serve on another commission until the
next General Assembly, unless the pool of eligible
commissioners within that region has been exhausted.
A quorum shall consist of at least ten
members, at least five of whom must be ruling elders.
15.5 Eveiy General Assembly shall declare each of the
commissions appointed under BCO 15-4 to be its own.
Each of these commissions, when it has completed its
work, shall report to the next General Assembly, which
shall, without debate, vote on said report. If the
General Assembly approves the commission report, it
shall be admitted to record as the action of the General Assembly. If the General Assembly does not approve
the commission report, then the court shall appoint a
commission to readjudicate the matter or shall itself
adjudicate it.

BCO 34-1 or BCO 41 for adjudication of a matter

### CCB advice

##### Overture 6 from Westminster Presbytery: “Revise BCO 15-4 and 15-5 to

Abolish the Standing Judicial Commission”
It is the opinion of the CCB that this overture is in conflict with other parts of
the constitution, including but not limited to BCO 14-1(15). The overture is
also unconstitutionally vague as to what will be the basis of making the
alternative decisions indicated in the proposed BCO 15-5, thus creating

<a id="ga30-p101"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=30 pdf_page=103 printed_page=101 -->

constitutional tensions without process for resolution. In addition, it will
create numerous conflicts with our General Assembly RAO.
Not as constitutional advice, but as pastoral concern and brotherly love, the
CCB also asks the members of the B&O committee of commissioners to be
very cautious regarding passing on intemperate language and personal
accusations to the GA through the reasoning, premises and recitals of overtures
(WLC 144 & 145). [For text and action, see 30-53, III, 8, p. 254.]
Adopted by CCB
