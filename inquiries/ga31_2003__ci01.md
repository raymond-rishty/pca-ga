# 31-56 III.1 — Status of a presbytery case after SJC rules it out of order

*When the SJC rules a case judicially out of order, the case reverts substantively to the status it held when the presbytery completed its action.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Inquiry / reference  ·  **Assembly:** 31st (2003)  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga31_2003 lines 8424–8431](../markdown/ga31_2003.md#ga31-p159)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a non-judicial reference from Evangel Presbytery asking what the status was of a case at Presbytery level that the SJC had ruled "judicially out of order?" The CCB answered that the status of a case that the SJC has ruled "judicially out of order" was substantively that of the case at the time the Presbytery completed its action.

**Inquiry from:** Evangel Presbytery
