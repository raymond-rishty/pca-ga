# II.T — Whole-court vote required to suspend officer's functions during process

*Proposed amendment requiring the entire court to vote before suspending an officer's functions during BCO 31-10 process was found not in conflict with the BCO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 31-10  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 31 from the Houston Metro Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 31-10 to Require Vote of the Entire Court to Suspend
an Officer’s Official Functions during Process”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 31-10 is amended as follows (underlining for

additions):

**31-10** . when a member o fa church court i sunder process, all his
official functions may be administratively suspended at the court’s
discretion; but this shall never be done in the way of censure, and this
requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the entire court, even if the
process is being conducted by a commission.

So that _BCO_ 31-10 as amended would read:

**31-10** . when a member o fa church court i sunder process, all his
official functions may be administratively suspended at the court’s
discretion; but this shall never be done in the way of censure, and this
requires a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the court itself, even if the
process is being conducted by a commission.

**RATIONALE:**

There are occasions when a church officer (that is, “a member of a church
court”) comes under process. Such process could potentially take a period of
time to conclude. At the same time, it may be necessary for a number of
reasons, including the honor of religion, the health of the court, and even the
well-being of the accused, to suspend the accused from his official functions.
Current _BCO_ 31-10 rightly points out that this is not a censure but is only an
administrative suspension.

<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1268 printed_page=1266 -->

Often process is conducted by a _BCO_ 15-3 commission rather than the entire
court. Such a commission may be relatively small when compared to the entire
court: a Presbytery commission must be at least four ( _BCO_ 15-2), but a Session
commission could be as few as two members. In the case of a Teaching Elder,
suspending him could result in a significant effect on both the Teaching Elder
and his congregation. For example, the church could be required to find pulpit
supply during the Teaching Elder’s suspension, or a church could be unable to
celebrate the sacraments if another Teaching Elder is unavailable to them.

Because a commission is acting _as the court_ ina case of process, the provision
of _BCO_ 31-10 can currently be enacted by the members of the commission
itself, rather than the larger court. in the case o fa six-member commission, for
example, that would mean that four members of the larger court could suspend
a man from “all his official functions.” This could occur even in a Presbytery
which had membership of more than 100 (Teaching Elders and Ruling Elder
commissioners). If a court is going to take the step of suspending an officer
from “all his official functions,” this should be done by a greater number of
officers of the court than a 2/3 majority of a commission.

_Adopted by Houston Metro Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 14, 2025_
_Attested by RE /s/ Eric Manthei, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**T.** Overture 31 from the Houston Metro Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 31-10 to
Require Vote of the Entire Court to Suspend an Officer’s Official Functions
during Process”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 31 is **not in conflict** with the Constitution.

CCB notes that the word “administratively” is not otherwise constitutionally
defined and may therefore create an ambiguity.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
