# Supplemental II.2 — Floor nominations for Stated Clerk when AC brings no nominee

*The CCB ruled that floor nominations are permitted under RAO 3-4 even if the Administrative Committee does not bring a Stated Clerk nominee.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 3-4, RAO 4-9, RAO 8-4.i, RAO 13, BCO 14-1.14  ·  **Disposition:** Yes — floor nominations are allowed; RAO 3-4 second sentence provides GA right to select Stated Clerk regardless of AC nominee; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 pp. 263–264](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p263)*

---

## Verbatim record

2. In the opinion of the CCB, yes. The _RAO_ does not expressly
contemplate a set of facts where the Administrative Committee does
not bring a nominee for the office of Stated Clerk. _RAO_ 3-4 states:
“The Administrative Committee of General Assembly has the
_responsibility_ of nominating to the General Assembly the person to
fill the office of the Stated Clerk” (emphasis added). Further, _RAO_ 49 states: “The Administrative Committee _shall_ nominate annually a
Stated Clerk for election by the General Assembly” (emphasis added).
_RAO_ 3-4 and _RAO_ 4-9 require the Administrative Committee to bring
a nominee. The second sentence of _RAO_ 3-4 simply assumes the
Committee is bringing a nominee and provides an additional method
for presentation of nominees.

In the event that the Administrative Committee declines to fulfill its

duty under _RAO_ 4-9, the second sentence of _RAO_ 3-4 should be
interpreted as acknowledging the General Assembly’s right to select a
Stated Clerk of its own choosing, regardless of the nominee (or lack
of nominee) put forward by the Administrative Committee. A contrary
reading would result in the Assembly having no mechanism under the
_Rules_ (without suspension thereof) to elect a Stated Clerk when the
Administrative Committee does not proffer a nominee. The phrase
“additional nominations” has been read in an analogous way regarding
floor nominations when the Nominating Committee does not proffer
a nominee for any position ( _RAO_ 8-4.i.).

Further, absent a recommendation from the Administrative
Committee, the election of a Stated Clerk will not come before the
Assembly in the ordinary course, and the election may only be taken
up as new business under _RAO_ 13.

Finally, no nominee can be placed before the General Assembly unless
and until he has been approved by the Theological Examining
Committee ( _BCO_ 14-1.14). We observe that this constitutional
requirement is mandatory and cannot be suspended.

_Adopted 8-0-0_
