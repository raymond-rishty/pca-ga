# CI #15 — Whether congregation may dissolve the session by vote

*A congregation cannot dissolve the session; it may by majority vote request individual officer removals under BCO 24-6, but the final decision rests with the session, and presbytery may assume jurisdiction under BCO 13-9.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 14th (1986)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 24-6, BCO 13-9  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga14_1986 lines 17131–17137](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p338)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

Constitutional Inquiry #15. From Covenant Presbyterian Church. Question: May a congregation, by vote at a congregational meeting, dissolve the session? ANSWER: The congregation may not dissolve the Session. The congregation may in accordance with BCO 24-6 by a majority vote in a regularly called congregational meeting request the Session to dissolve the relationship of officers individually, but the ultimate decision rests with the Session. Presbytery may assume original jurisdiction under provisions of BCO 13-9.

**Key words:** BCO 24-6, BCO 13-9
