# 7-41 III, 21 — Ordination questions and re-ordination for ministers from other denominations

*The CCB advised that BCO 13-6 requires ordination questions be put to any minister from another denomination, and presbytery may require re-ordination if deemed necessary.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Inquiry / reference  ·  **Assembly:** 7th (1979)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 13-6  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga07_1979 lines 6081–6084](../markdown/ga07_1979.md#ga07-p101)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

a) That the BCO 13-6 states that anyone coming from another denomination must be asked the questions for ordination, and b) That Presbytery also has the authority to require an ordination that is in accord with Scripture, if deemed necessary.

**Key words:** BCO 13-6

**In the minutes:** [ga07_1979 p.104](../markdown/ga07_1979.md#ga07-p101)

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

1. Concerning correspondence from Rev. Laurie Vidal, Faith Presbyterian Church, dated December 10, 1978, regarding whether a woman could serve as a trustee of a church, the Committee answered that a trustee is not ordained and is not an officer of the church, and therefore election of a female communing member on the roll of the church as a trustee does not contravene the BCO 26-6 and 26-7.

### CCB advice

3. That answer 1 to the Rev. Laurie Vidal concerning election of a female communing member as a trustee of a church be approved, and that he refer this request to Presbytery for further advice.

The Committee answered that a trustee is not ordained and is not an officer of the church, and therefore election of a female communing member on the roll of the church as a trustee does not contravene the BCO 26-6 and 26-7.
