# II.E — Proposal to disallow memorials via RAO amendment

*An overture from Pacific Northwest Presbytery to amend RAO 11 and 13 to disallow memorials was found not in conflict and adopted by the CCB.*

##### OVERTURE 6 from Pacific Northwest Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _RAO_ 11 and 13 to Disallow Memorials”

(All parts should be considered as a single proposal, considered together.)

1. Add new _**RAO**_ **11-12** .

**11-12.** No communication or overture shall be received that

**13-2.** New business must be presented to the General

**Grounds** :

1. There are problems with the current _RAO_ 13-6, which became most

apparent last year at the 2018 GA regarding Overtures 31, 36 and 37.
a) There is currently no provision for the Assembly to consider

approval/disapproval of a memorial, should it wish tod o so.
b) _RAO_ currently has no provision for amending any resolution in a

memorial overture.

c) _RAO_ currently does not restrict content. So, for example, it could

allow memorial resolutions with differing views of the person and
his contribution to the General Assembly.
d) Currently, the text of any memorial overture would appear in an

appendix of the Assembly Minutes, because its entirety would be
included along with other Overtures. But a reader might be confused
as to whether and how the Assembly acted on the memorial.
Furthermore, if a memorial was in order per _RAO_ 13-6 and thus
“included by the Stated Clerk in the minutes of the General
Assembly” (which would be a second inclusion), it would add to the
confusion.

2. John Calvin was buried on Sunday, May 28, 1564, in an unmarked grave

(per his request), a ta secret location in Geneva. Earlier, Calvin had
written the following about the death of Moses:

“It is good that famous men should be buried in unmarked
graves.”
_Commentaries on the Four Last Books of Moses_, Vol. 4, 406
(Grand Rapids: Baker, 1979), on Deut. 34:6: “So Moses the
servant of the Lord died there in the land of Moab, according
to the word of the Lord, and he buried him in the valley in the
land of Moab opposite Beth-peor; but no one knows the place
of his burial to this day.” (ESV)
