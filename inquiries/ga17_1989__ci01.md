# 17-82 III — Certification of presbytery dismissal by committee chairman's letter

*A letter from a presbytery committee chairman is insufficient evidence of dismissal; BCO 46-6 requires a certificate bearing the stated clerk's signature per BCO 10-4.*

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

6. The Committee was instructed to respond to a communication received by the Review and Control Committee asking what was acceptable documentation of the dismissal of a Teaching Elder from one Presbytery to another. The question asked was:

"Would it be proper to accept as evidence the letter of a chairman of a presbytery committee stating that a dismissal had been granted."

ANSWER:

No. BCO 46-6 requires that the presbytery issue a certificate when dismissing a minister, licentiate, or a candidate. According to BCO 10-4, the only official certification of an action of a court is that which is issued over the signature of the stated clerk.

Recommendation 6. That the answer of the Judicial Business Committee regarding Assignment #6 be ratified.
