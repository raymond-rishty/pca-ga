## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a non-judicial reference from Westminster Presbytery on who may approve severance packages for ministers, by stating that sessions can approve severance packages without congregational approval because these are budgetary matters (BCO 12-5b).

**Key words:** BCO 12-5b

## Verbatim record

D. Non-judicial Reference 1 from Westminster Presbytery: “…whether
severance packages for ministers must be approved by the
congregation or may they be approved by the session alone?”

It is the opinion of the CCB that sessions can approve severance
packages because these are budgetary matters. Approval of
budgetary matters are the responsibility of the Session ( _BCO_ 12-5b).
There is no constitutional mandate that severance packages be
approved by the congregation. _Adopted by the CCB_
