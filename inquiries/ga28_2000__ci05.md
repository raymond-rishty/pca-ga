# 28-19 V — Re-ordination of former PCA ministers who were divested without censure

*The CCB found no technical constitutional conflict but raised concerns about the nature of ordination and inconsistency between restoration after deposition versus divestiture.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 34-10, BCO 21-5, BCO 36-7, BCO 37-5  ·  **Disposition:** not in technical conflict

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 13 from Susquehanna Valley Presbytery ("Re-ordination of Former PCA Ministers") was not in technical conflict with any other provisions of the Constitution. However, the Committee pointed out that BCO 34-10 does not mandate divestiture in every case and was also concerned that the language proposed for the new BCO 21-5 raised questions about the nature and importance of the call to the ministry ("...simply a lack of call...."). Finally, the Committee noted the apparent inconsistency in the handling of one who had been removed from office by discipline (deposition, BCO 36-7) who would be restored as per BCO 37-5, and one who is divested without censure (BCO 34-10) who could be restored only after re-examination and re-ordination.

Whereas, this often puts pressure on presbyteries to approve “calls” which are questionable, and

Whereas, one reason for this failure of presbyteries to act is that both the man and his presbytery are hoping a call will come and if it does after he is divested he would have to go through the entire ordination process again,

Therefore, the Presbytery of Susquehanna Valley hereby overtures the General Assembly to add a new section 5 as follows to chapter 21 of the BCO and renumber the following sections:

NEW 21-5: Modified procedures may be used for the re-ordination of a former PCA minister who receives a call to a particular church after he was divested without censure at his request (38-2) or was divested of his office without censure against his will (34-10). The presbytery in which he is being called shall carefully investigate the circumstances under which he was divested. If the presbytery decides that the reason he was divested was simply a lack of call, it may proceed to give him the trials for ordination outlined in 21-4 without going through the normal candidate, licensure, and internship procedures. At its discretion, the presbytery may accept his previous ordination in lieu of examining him in the Greek, and Hebrew languages, church history, and the history of the PCA.

Adopted by the Presbytery of Susquehanna Valley on February 18, 2000.

### CCB advice

## OVERTURE 13 – From Susquehanna Valley Presbytery, “Re-ordination of Former PCA Ministers.”

**Response** – It is the opinion of the Committee that Overture 13 as presented is not in technical conflict with any other provisions of the Constitution. However, the Committee would point out that *BCO* 34-10 does not mandate divestiture in every case (“...it shall be the duty of the Presbytery...to inquire...and, if necessary, to institute judicial proceedings...If it shall appear that his neglect proceeds only from his lack of acceptance to the church, Presbytery may...divest him of his office...”). The Committee is also concerned that the language proposed for the new *BCO* 21-5 raises questions about the nature and importance of the call to the ministry (“...simply a lack of call...”). Finally, the Committee notes the apparent inconsistency in the handling of one who has been removed from office by discipline (deposition – *BCO* 36-7) who would be restored as per *BCO* 37-5 and one who is divested without censure (*BCO* 34-10) who could be restored only after reexamination and re-ordination. Perhaps a proposed amendment clarifying the relationship between divestiture and deposition would be helpful.

The response was adopted by proper motion, 8-0-0. [Text and action at 28-72, III, 11, p. 287.]
