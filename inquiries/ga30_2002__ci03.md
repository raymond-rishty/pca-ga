# 30-29 III — Amending the Constitution of the Church (BCO 26), Procedure for Dealing with Alleged Conflicts Between Scripture and the Constitution of the PCA

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 26-1, BCO 26-2, BCO 15-1, BCO 15-5  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga30_2002 lines 4658–4685](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p102)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 27 from Eastern Canada Presbytery ("Add to BCO 26-1 Procedure for Dealing with Alleged Conflicts Between Scripture and the Constitution of the PCA") was in conflict with other parts of the Constitution. Specific areas of conflict included, but were not limited to the following: 1) the Overture would have allowed a General Assembly to avoid the provisions of the BCO without appropriate constitutional process and would have resulted in serious constitutional uncertainty (BCO 26-2), and 2) the Overture was in conflict with BCO 15.1 and BCO 15.5, in that it allowed for modification of a commission report.

**Key words:** BCO 26-1, BCO 26-2, BCO 15-1, BCO 15-5

**Inquiry from:** Eastern Canada Presbytery

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 27 from Eastern Canada Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)

“Add to BCO 26-1 Procedure for Dealing with Alleged
Conflicts Between Scripture and the Constitution of the PCA”

Whereas, the 29th General Assembly found itself in the position
that its rules compelled it to distribute in its permanent
records material harmful to the reputation of certain
people and groups; and

<a id="ga30-p274"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=30 pdf_page=276 printed_page=274 -->

Whereas, the Constitutional Business Committee reported to
the 29th General Assembly that we had no option but to
follow BCO, even if it appeared to conflict with
Scripture, with the only remedy being to amend BCO
after the fact to avoid such action in the future; and

Whereas, this was not a matter of judicial process against these
people; and

Whereas, this is disobedience to God’s command, as our
church understands it (“The ninth commandment
requires ... the preserving and promoting of ... the
good name of our neighbor, ... a charitable esteem of
our neighbors; ... sorrowing for, and covering of their
infirmities” [Larger Catechism 144] and forbids “... all
prejudicing ... the good name of our neighbors, ...
unnecessary discovering of infirmities ...” [Larger
Catechism 145], based on Scriptures such as: Prov 17:9
“He who covers a transgression seeks love, but he who
repeats a matter separates friends”; 1 Pet 4:8 “And
above all things have fervent love for one another, for
‘love will cover a multitude of sins’”; Prov 10:12
“Hatred stirs up strife, but love covers all sins”; Prov
12:16 “A fool’s wrath is known at once, but a prudent
man covers shame”); and

Whereas, according to our confessional understanding, this
would be an offence even if the statements should be
true; and

Whereas, Christ warns us against even the least sin, saying, “if
your eye causes you to sin, pluck it out and cast if from
you. It is better for you to enter into life with one eye,
rather than having two eyes, to be cast into hell fire”
(Matthew 18:9); and

Whereas, it is critically important that the PCA keep the
Scripture as our supreme authority under God in
practice a swell a sin theory;

Therefore, the Presbytery of Eastern Canada overtures the 30th
General Assembly to add to BCO 26-1:
“The Scripture is above the Constitution, and the
Westminster Confession and Catechisms as our uniting,

<a id="ga30-p275"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=30 pdf_page=277 printed_page=275 -->

agreed interpretation of the Scriptures are above the
Book of Church Order; and overrule the Book of Church Order; in case of conflict.
a) If a majority of the General Assembly agrees that
following the BCO appears to bring the General Assembly, its agencies or any of its members into
conflict with the Scriptures, the matter shall be referred
to the Constitutional Business Committee. Action shall
be stayed on all matters relating to the presumed
conflict until the Committee reports. The Committee’s
report shall include a proposal for dealing with the
matter in a way that eliminates the alleged conflict and
a recommendation as to whether or not there is conflict.
A three-quarters majority of the Assembly is required
at this point to determine there is conflict. If a
constitutional amendment is required, the action or part
of an action which is deemed to be in conflict with
Scripture shall be stayed until the constitutional process
is complete.
The actual judgment of a judicial commission may not
be changed or delayed by this process, but any other
proposed action of the Assembly or which appears in
the minutes or appendices of the Assembly, including
reasons for judicial commission judgments, may be
modified under this procedure.
b) In the lower courts, similar procedures shall be
followed. The court may appoint a constitutional
committee, or choose to act as such committee itself.
However, if it appears that amendment of the Book of Church Order is necessary, the issue shall be referred
to the General Assembly and its Constitutional
Business Committee for its action.”

### CCB advice

##### Overture 27 from Eastern Canada Presbytery: Add to BCO 26-1 Procedure

for dealing with alleged conflicts between scripture and the constitution of
the PCA
It is the opinion of the CCB that the Overture is in conflict with other parts of
the Constitution. Specific areas of conflict include, but are not limited to:
1. The Overture would allow a General Assembly to avoid the provisions
of the BCO without appropriate constitutional process and would
result in serious constitutional uncertainty. (BCO 26-2).
2. The Overture allows modification of virtually any action of the General Assembly or commission thereof, with the sole limitation that an actual
judgment of a judicial commission may not be changed or deleted,
thereby undermining the very principle of orderly Church Government
which the BCO is designed to protect. [See BCO Preface II.2],
3. The Overture is in conflict with BCO 15.1 and 15.5, in that it allows
for modification of a commission report.
4. The Overture requires the CCB to propose business, inconsistent with
RAO 7-2 and the Operations Manual for CCB.
5. Finally, reference is made to the recommendation of the CCB with
respect to Constitutional Inquiry #4 of the 29th General Assembly:
“The presumption that we need to suspend our rules in order to obey
Christ is an opinion that the CCB is not willing to affirm. i four standards

<a id="ga30-p103"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=30 pdf_page=105 printed_page=103 -->

are shown to be out of accord with Scripture, then there are proper
procedures to follow, at each level, in order to change those standards. ”
[For text and action, see 30-53, III, 18, p. 273.] Adopted by CCB
