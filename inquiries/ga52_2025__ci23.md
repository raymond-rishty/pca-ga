# II.W — Requiring session and diaconate membership reporting in RAO 16

*The CCB found an overture amending RAO 16 to mandate statistical reporting on session and diaconate membership and duties to be in conflict with existing BCO provisions.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 16, BCO 9-2  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 lines 14912–14921](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p257)*

---

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 34 from the Savannah River Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _RAO_ 16 to Require Reporting on Session and Diaconate
Membership and Duties”

**Whereas** the 45 [th] General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America

adopted the 4th recommendation from the Study Committee on the
Role of Women in the Ministry of the Church which, in its final form
stated: “That sessions, if possible, establish a diaconate of qualified
ordained men.”; and

**Whereas** the rationale in the body of the report explaining this

recommendation stated that “There is at least anecdotal evidence that

some PCA churches are choosing not to establish an ordained
diaconate, even with qualified candidates, because the church wishes
to be free to establish a body of unordained servants, both male and
female ( _Book of Church Order_ 9-7)”, and similar reports of this
practice persist within our denomination; and

**Whereas** _BCO_ 9-7 recognizes an unofficial and unordained role within the

church called “assistants to the deacons” comprised of “godly men
andS women of the congregation to assist the deacons in the caring for
the sick, the widow, the orphans, the prisoners, and other who may be
in distress or need”; and

**Whereas** at its 50 [th] General Assembly, the PCA approved an amendment to

the _Book of Church Order_ ( _BCO_ ) 7-3 and affirmed it at its 51 [st] General
Assembly which prohibits assigning the titles connected to the
ecclesial offices of pastor, elder, or deacon to non-ordained members;

**Therefore be it resolved** the Savannah River Presbytery respectfully

overtures the 52 [nd] General Assembly of the PCA to protect the
constitutional practice of ordaining a Diaconate by adding the
following to _RAO_ 16-4.c.1 (underlining for additions; strikethrough
for deletions):

A current Directory of Presbytery, including (1) a list of all teaching
elders, with their addresses and their status; and of all churches and
missions with the address of the church, and the name and address of
the Clerk of Session, (2) the list of persons who comprise the Session

<a id="ga52-p1271"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1273 printed_page=1271 -->

and the Diaconate of each church arranged by their ordained or given
titles; and if no Diaconate exists, a report why it has not been
established and whether or not the duties have devolved upon the
ruling elders, unordained diaconal assistants, or any other committee
or member of the church, (23) a listing of all officers and committee
members of the regular committees of presbytery, and (34) a list
identifying all candidates under care, interns, and licentiates of the
presbytery with their addresses and their status.

**Be it further resolved** to add the following to _RAO_ 16-6.c.1:

Exceptions of substance: Apparent violations of the Scripture or
serious irregularities from the Constitution of the Presbyterian Church
in America, actions out of accord with the deliverances of the General
Assembly, and matters of impropriety and important delinquencies,
and any non-compliance with _RAO_ 16-3.e.5-6 and _RAO_ 16-4.c.1

_Attested by RE W. Glynn Dyer, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**W.** Overture 34 from the Savannah River Presbytery: “Amend _RAO_ 16 to
Require Reporting on Session and Diaconate Membership and Duties”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 34 is **in conflict** with the Constitution in
that if a church does not have Deacons, _BCO_ 9-2 requires that the duties of the
office “shall devolve upon the ruling elders” of that church but the amendment
as presently written suggests that the duties of the diaconate may devolve upon
other persons or entities other than the ruling elders.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
