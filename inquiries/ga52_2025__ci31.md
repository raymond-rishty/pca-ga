# IV.A — CCB authority to review SJC compliance and prior decisions

*The CCB declined a reference from Highlands Presbytery, ruling it lacks authority to render opinions on prior SJC decisions, which are final decisions of the General Assembly.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Inquiry / reference  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 41-3, BCO 39-3.1, BCO 43-10, BCO 40-2, BCO 15-5  ·  **Disposition:** CCB declined to accede to the reference; SJC decisions are final decisions of the GA (BCO 15-5); CCB does not render opinions on previous SJC decisions; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 lines 15010–15035](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p259)*

---

## Verbatim record

**A.** On December 6, 2024, the Highlands Presbytery submitted the following
questions to the General Assembly for advice only ( _BCO_ 41-3) regarding the
Presbytery’s compliance with SJC Case No. 2023-16:

1. Does the SJC have the remit from the General Assembly to address
a question not raised by a complainant? ( _BCO_ 39-3.1)
2. Does the SJC have the remit from the General Assembly to rule in
any other way than to either: (a) uphold the complaint, (b) deny the
complaint, or (c) refer the case back to the Presbytery? ( _BCO_ 39-3.1,
43-10; _OMSJC_ 14.4.c)
3. Does the SJC’s ruling constitute an injunction contrary to the 50th
General Assembly’s approval of Highlands Presbytery’s minutes?
( _M50GA_, pp.564–565)
4. If the SJC gives a lower court an unlawful injunction (cf., _BCO_ 402), what options are available to the lower court in its response?

<a id="ga52-p260"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=262 printed_page=260 -->

The CCB declined to accede to the reference and provided the following

comment:

The decisions of the SJC are final decisions of the GA ( _BCO_ 15-5).
The CCB does not render opinions on previous SJC decisions.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
