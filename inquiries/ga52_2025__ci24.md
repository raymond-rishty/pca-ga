# II.X — Session member serving as moderator in judicial cases

*The CCB found an overture allowing a session to elect one of its own members as moderator in judicial cases to be not in conflict with the BCO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 12-3  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 p. 257](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p257)*

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 37 from the Southeast Alabama Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 12-3 so that a Session May Elect One of Its Members
as Moderator in Judicial Cases”

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 12-3 is amended as follows (underlining for additions;

strikethrough for deletions):

**12-3.** when a church is without a pastor, the moderator of
the Session may be either a minister appointed for that purpose
by the Presbytery, with consent of the Session, or one invited
by the Session to preside on a particular occasion, or one of its
own members elected to preside. In judicial cases, the Session
may elect one of its members to preside as moderator shall be
a or invite a minister of the presbytery to which the church
belongs to preside as moderator.

So that _BCO_ 12-3 as amended would read:

**12-3.** when a church is without a pastor, the moderator of
the Session may be either a minister appointed for that purpose
by the Presbytery, with consent of the Session, or one invited
by the Session to preside on a particular occasion, or one of its
own members elected to preside. In judicial cases, the Session
may elect one of its members to preside as moderator or invite

<a id="ga52-p1275"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1277 printed_page=1275 -->

a minister of the Presbytery to which the church belongs to
preside as moderator.

**RATIONALE:**

Sessions should have discretion to elect a moderator in judicial cases. Some
Sessions may have ruling elders who are highly experienced in church
discipline and cases of process, and they should have the liberty to elect such
a ruling elder to preside. In instances where this is not the case, the revised
provision retains the right of the Session to invite a minister of the Presbytery
to preside.

_Adopted by the Southeast Alabama Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 22,_
_2025_

_Attested by TE Kevin Corley, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**X.** Overture 37 from the Southeast Alabama Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 12-3
so that a Session May Elect One of Its Members as Moderator in Judicial
Cases”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 37 is **not in conflict** with the Constitution.
_Adopted 8-0-0_
