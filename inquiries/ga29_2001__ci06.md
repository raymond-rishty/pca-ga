## Verbatim record

Reference 6 From Philadelphia Presbytery re Complaints

Question 1: May a person charged with an offense or offenses, who is undergoing process in a trial before a court, complain to the next higher judicatory about the manner in which the trial is being conducted?
Response: A person may not make a complaint to a higher court until after the lower court has acted on the complaint in accordance with BCO 43-2 and 43-3. “A complaint shall first be made to the court whose act or decision is alleged to be in error.” (BCO 43-2) The CCB notes the right of individuals under BCO 43-1 to complain against actions taken during a trial so long as the procedures of BCO 43-2 and 43-3 are followed.
Adopted by CCB
(6-2-0 and TE Childs requested that his negative vote be recorded)

Question 2: Do the grounds for appeal in BCO 42-3 negate the right of an accused person to complain about the manner in which a trial is being conducted?
Response: No. BCO 42-3 refers to appeals and does not negate the right to complain. However, if an appeal is taken in a judicial case, no complaint is then allowable, (BCO 43-1).
Adopted by CCB
(6-2-0 and TE Childs requested that his negative vote be recorded)
