# II.J — Court's permissible interactions with an offended party under BCO 38-1

*The CCB found the proposed amendment to BCO 38-1 specifying ways a court may interact with an offended party not inc onflict with the Constitution.*

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 14 from the Great Lakes Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 38-1 to Indicate Ways a Court May Interact with an
Offended Party”

**Whereas** _BCO_ 38-1 requires that “in any instances… pertinent to the offense

against him or her;” and

**Whereas** informing offended person(s), inviting offended person(s) to provide

the court comment, and encouraging offended person(s) to enlist the

<a id="ga52-p1196"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1198 printed_page=1196 -->

help of an advisor in preparing such comments are all the
responsibility of the court; and

**Whereas** compliance with these responsibilities ordinarily requires multiple

meetings of the court; and

**Whereas** in some circumstances it may be preferable for a designated

individual to interact with an offended person rather than the court
itself;

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 38-1 be amended by inserting the following

language (underlining for additions; strikethrough for deletions):

In any instances involving a personal offense ( _BCO_ 29-3), the
court Moderator of the court or his designee shall attempt to
inform the offended person(s) of that part of the Confession the
court deems pertinent to the offense against him or her. The court
Moderator of the court or his designee shall invite the offended
person to provide the court comment on the Confession prior to
final approval of the Confession by the confessor and the court.
The court Moderator of the court or his designee shall encourage
the offended person to enlist the help of an advisor in preparing
any such comments. In all such instances, the court shall report
the way such offended persons were informed of the parts of the
Confession pertinent to them.

So that the amended paragraph would read:

In any instances involving a personal offense ( _BCO_ 29-3), the
Moderator of the court or his designee shall attempt to inform the
offended person(s) of that part of the Confession the court deems
pertinent to the offense against him or her. The Moderator of the
court or his designee shall invite the offended person to provide
the court comment on the Confession prior to final approval of
the Confession by the confessor and the court. The Moderator of
the court or his designee shall encourage the offended person to
enlist the help of an advisor in preparing any such comments. In
all such instances, the court shall report the way such offended

persons were informed of the parts of the Confession pertinent to
them.
