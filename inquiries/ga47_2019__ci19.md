# II.S — Adding a Trinitarian vow to ordination vows

*This overture proposing to add a vow regarding the Trinity to BCO 57-5 was found not in conflict with the Constitution.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture advice  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 57-5  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 lines 21088–21094](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p402)*

---

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 33 from Tennessee Valley Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 57-5 to Add Vow Regarding Trinity"

**Whereas**, there is but one only, living, and true God, who exists as three

persons—God the Father, God the Son, and God the Holy Spirit—of one
substance, power, and eternity (WCF 2; WLC 9; WSC 6); and

<a id="ga47-p671"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=673 printed_page=671 -->

**Whereas**, the doctrine of the Trinity is foundational, essential, and vital to

the Christian faith; indeed, presbyteries have been instructed to give due
and serious consideration to “Appendix W,” which includes the
affirmation: “the confession of the Trinity is the heartbeat of the
Christian religion” (as adopted by the 45 [th] General Assembly, _Minutes,_
29 and 624); and

**Whereas**, the visible church is made up of those who profess faith in the

Lord Jesus Christ ( _BCO_ 2-1), and such profession of faith is a profession
of the whole Christ, who is both truly God and truly man; and

**Whereas**, we live ina pluralistic age, where there is confusion over the

essential being of God, illustrated by the fact that the vast majority of
self-professing evangelicals in America affirm that “Jesus is the first and
greatest being created by God” (Ligonier Ministries and LifeWay
Research, “The State of Theology” 2018); and

**Whereas**, the doctrine of the Trinity has been and is currently under assault

by the unbelieving world, by cults, by unitarians, and by various
religions; and

**Whereas**, Mormons, Jehovah’s Witnesses, and other non-Christian faiths

could affirm our current membership vows as stated; and

**Whereas**, the organization of a particular church in the Presbyterian Church

in America includes the pronouncement and declaration of its
organization in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy
Spirit ( _BCO_ 5-8); and

**Whereas**, when unbaptized persons apply for admission into the Church,

they shall make a public profession of their faith, in the presence of the
congregation, and thereupon be baptized in the name of the Father, and
of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit ( _BCO_ 56-6; 57-3), as shall the children
of believing parents; and

**Whereas**, “every Christian Church, or union or association of particular

churches, is entitled to declare the terms of admission into its communion
and the qualifications of its ministers and members” (Preface to the _BCO_
II.2); and

**Whereas,** at least three fellow NAPARC churches include a membership

vow requiring affirmation of the Holy Trinity; and

**Whereas**, the current membership vows of the Presbyterian Church in

America reference “God” ( _BCO_ 57-5), but do not require faith in God as
He exists in three persons;

**Therefore, be it resolved** to amend the _Book of Church Order_ 57-5 by

adding the following question to the membership vows [as question 2,
thereby renumbering questions 2–5 as questions 3–6]:

<a id="ga47-p672"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=47 pdf_page=674 printed_page=672 -->

**2. Do you believe in the one, true, and living God who**

**—**
**exists in three persons** **God the Father, God the Son,**
**and God the Holy Spirit—who are of the same**
**substance, equal in power and glory?**

So that the membership vows in _Book of Church Order_ 57-5 would read:

**1.** **Do you acknowledge yourselves to be sinners in the**
**sight of God, justly deserving His displeasure, and**
**without hope save in His sovereign mercy?**
**2.** **Do you believe in the one, true, and living God who**

**—**
**exists in three persons** **God the Father, God the**
**Son, and God the Holy Spirit—who are of the same**
**substance, equal in power and glory?**
~~**2.**~~ **3.** **Do you believe in the Lord Jesus Christ as the Son**
**of God, and Savior of sinners, and do you receive**
**and rest upon Him alone for salvation as He is**
**offered in the Gospel?**
~~**3.**~~ **4.** **Do you now resolve and promise, in humble**
**reliance upon the grace of the Holy Spirit, that you**
**will endeavor to live as becomes the followers of**

**Christ?**
~~**4.**~~ **5.** **Do you promise to support the Church in its**
**worship and work to the best of your ability?**
~~**5.**~~ **6.** **Do you submit yourselves to the government and**
**discipline of the Church, and promise to study its**
**purity and peace?**

_Adopted by the Tennessee Valley Presbytery at its stated meeting, April 13, 2019_
_Attested by TE Jacob A. Bennett, stated clerk_

### CCB advice

**S** . Overture 33 from Tennessee Valley Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 57-5

to Add Vow Regarding Trinity”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 33 is **not in conflict** with other
parts of the Constitution. _Adopted by the CCB_
