# 30-29 III — Proposed overture language for handling exceptions to Westminster Standards

*The CCB found the proposed overture in conflict with BCO Preliminary Principles, eroding presbyteries' exclusive authority to evaluate candidates' subscription.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO Preliminary Principle II.1, BCO Preliminary Principle II.7, BCO Preliminary Principles  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 100](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p100)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, the proposed constitutional language in Overture 4 from Ohio Valley Presbytery ("Handling Exceptions to the Westminster Standards") was in conflict with the BCO Preliminary Principles. Furthermore, the proposed language created constitutional ambiguity in four ways, including 1) that the proposed overture language "either in his private life" was in conflict with BCO Preliminary Principle II.1 and II.7, and 2) that the proposed overture language requiring Presbyteries to record every man's views disagreeing with the Constitution would have eroded the Presbyteries' exclusive authority to determine whether a candidate receives and adopts the Confession of Faith and the Catechisms of the PCA as containing the system of doctrine taught in the Holy Scriptures.

**Key words:** BCO Preliminary Principle II.1, BCO Preliminary Principle II.7, BCO Preliminary Principles

**Inquiry from:** Ohio Valley Presbytery

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 4 from Ohio Valley Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)

“Handling Exceptions to the Westminster Standards”
Add two new paragraphs in BCO 21-4 where deemed
appropriate by the Committee on Constitutional Business:
“When Presbytery allows a man to hold an
exception to the standards, it shall also determine
the man may hold the exception in belief only,
whether he may teach it, and whether he may
practice it either in his private life or in his public
ministry all so long as he does not disturb the peace
and purity of the church.”
“The Stated Clerk of Presbytery shall cause the
man’s allowed exception(s) stated in his own
words, along with the Presbytery’s action on the
exception(s), to be recorded along with the
ministerial obligation he is required to sign. This
document will be attached to the minutes of
Presbytery as an appendix.”

### CCB advice

##### Overture 4 from Ohio Valley Presbytery: “Handling Exceptions to the

Westminster Standards ”
It is the opinion of the CCB that the proposed constitutional language creates
constitutional conflicts with the BCO Preliminary Principles. Furthermore, the
proposed language creates constitutional ambiguity.
1. The proposed overture language “either in his private life” is in
conflict with BCO Preliminary Principle II. 1 and II.7.
2. The proposed overture language requiring Presbyteries to record every
man’s views disagreeing with the Constitution will erode the
Presbyteries’ exclusive authority to determine if a candidate receives
and adopts the Confession of Faith and the Catechism of the PCA as
containing the system of doctrine taught in the Holy Scriptures. See
BCO 21-5. This is in conflict with BCO Preliminary Principle II.2
that recognizes the right of each court to determine its membership.
(BCO Preface II.2, BCO 21-1 through 7; BCO 13-1; BCO 13-9 a,c,d;
BCO 11-3 & 4).
3. An ambiguity created by the proposed language is the introduction of the
phrase: “exception to the standards.” This phrase is nowhere else found
in our constitution. The phrase elicits a plethora of interpretations, many
of which go beyond our present Constitutional parameters.
4. It has not normally been the mission of the CCB to decide on the
placement of Constitutional language. [For text and action, see 30-50,
III, 3, p. 238.] Adopted by CCB
