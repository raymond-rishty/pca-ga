# II.BB — Scope of Administrative Committee statistical reporting under RAO 4-11

*The CCB found an overture specifying the scope of statistical reporting by the Administrative Committee to be not in conflict with the BCO or RAO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** RAO 4-11  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0

*Source: [ga52_2025 lines 14961–14966](../markdown/ga52_2025.md#ga52-p258)*

---

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 43 from the Calvary Presbytery (to CCB, OC, AC)

“Amend _RAO_ 4-11 regarding Data on Age and Ethnicity”

**Whereas** _RAO_ 4-11 authorizes the Administrative Committee to collect

statistical information for the purpose of providing and publishing
“guidelines for the Partnership Share Giving Program of the PCA,
including the Ministry Ask” and for no other stated reason; and,

**Whereas** _RAO_ 4-11 specifies that said “guidelines shall include statistical

information expressed both in per capita giving terms and in
proportional (percentage) giving terms to demonstrate the giving
necessary to attain full funding for budgets of the General Assembly
Committees and Agencies” and nothing more; and,

**Whereas** the Office of the Stated Clerk, through a note contained ina box on

the bottom of page 2 of the 2024 Annual Statistical Report Form,
informed the congregations of the PCA that “two new line items”
regarding “communicant member ethnicity and age” would appear
“on the 2025 Annual Statistical Report Form” to be sent out for
completion in 2026; and,

<a id="ga52-p1290"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1292 printed_page=1290 -->

**Whereas** the practical difficulties of collecting information on “communicant

member ethnicity and age” are greater than perhaps the Stated Clerk’s
Office has yet considered due to members’ reticence to share their
ages or uncertain of how best to categorize their ethnicity, with many
of our members being the children of interracial/multiethnic unions;
and,

**Whereas** the theological commitments of the PCA derived from Scripture and

expressed in the PCA’s subordinate Standards and Constitute militate
against the collection of data pertaining to ethnicity, for the PCA
confesses with Scripture that though our members come from many
different backgrounds (ethnic and otherwise), our Triune God has
united us together as “one body” (1 Cor. 12:12), with Christ Himself
being “our peace, who made both groups into one and broke down the
barrier of the dividing wall” (Eph. 2:14), baptizing all “by one
Spirit…into one body, whether Jews or Greeks, whether slaves of
free,” and making all “to drink of one Spirit” (1 Cor. 12:13), even
while acknowledging that Old Testament historical narratives,
ceremonial laws, and prophecies frequently refer to people groups by
familial origin, tribal associations, ethnic affinities, nations, and what
some commentators might refer to as racial distinctions, yet never in
such a way so as to accept as appropriate the continuing use of such
distinctions in the formal categorization of members of the church of
Jesus Christ; and,

**Whereas** the kingdoms of this world may collect (and even act upon) certain

demographic information, yet God has neither directed nor authorized
the church to collect such information pertaining to ethnicity; and,

**Whereas** there are two classes of people with which the church should be

concerned, in its formal statistical record-keeping: covenant-keepers
and covenant-breakers ( _BCO_ 56-4.j.), recorded in the PCA as those
who are in covenant with God through their own “profession of faith
in Christ” ( _BCO_ 6-2; see also 57-3, 4) or “through the covenant and
by right of birth” as “non-communing members of the church” ( _BCO_
6-1), thus furnishing the church with two classes of those who are
deemed in covenant with God—communing members and noncommuning members (WCF 25.2; WLC 62)—and one additional

<a id="ga52-p1291"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1293 printed_page=1291 -->

record of those who break covenant with God and come under the

censure of excommunication ( _BCO_ 30-1, 4); and,

**Whereas** there is no clear usefulness or theological justification for further

subdividing either of these two categories (i.e., covenant-keepers and
covenant-breakers) in terms of either age or ethnicity; and,

**Whereas** the collection of data pertaining to age or ethnicity gives the

appearance of the sins of partiality (James 2:9) and pride, which must
not be indulged even at the first stirring of the same (James 1:14-15),
as illustrated in King David’s sinful (and deadly) census recorded in 2
Samuel 24 and cited in 1 Chronicles 21; and,

**Whereas** the resolution of the conflict between Hellenistic Jews and native

Hebrews in Acts 6 does not constitute an approved example that would
lead us by necessary inference to collect denomination-wide
demographic data pertaining to ethnicity or age; and,

**Whereas** the collection of demographic data pertaining to ethnicity or age is

not necessary for the undershepherds called and ordained by Christ to
know the flock in their care; and,

**Whereas** the church is called to “examine everything carefully; hold fast to

that which is good; abstain from every form of evil” (1 Thess. 5:2122).

**Therefore, be it resolved** that Calvary Presbytery hereby overtures the 52 [nd]

General Assembly to amend _RAO_ 4-11 by adding the following to the
third paragraph (underlining for additions):

Such statistical information shall not include data pertaining to age or
ethnicity.

So that the amended section will read as follows:

**4-11.** The budget for each permanent Committee and
Agency shall be submitted to the Administrative Committee of
General Assembly, which shall independently evaluate the
proposed budget of each permanent Committee and Agency

<a id="ga52-p1292"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1294 printed_page=1292 -->

and report to the Assembly its considered opinion on the
adoption. To protect the ministry and fiduciary responsibilities
of the Committees and Agency Boards, the financial
coordination and independent evaluation of the budgets by the
Administrative Committee shall be for review, discussion, and
recommendation to the Assembly.

Should modifications in budgets be deemed necessary by the
Assembly, special care shall be taken that changes not be made
in such a way as to threaten the continuity or effectiveness of
the Committee’s or Agency’s ministry. Budgets of permanent
Committees and Agencies that are agreed upon by the
Administrative Committee may be changed only by a twothirds vote of the Assembly commissioners present and voting
at the time the budget is submitted for adoption. In the event
the Administrative Committee disagrees with the budget
submitted by one of the Committees or Boards, in whole or any
item thereof, the General Assembly may adopt either the
Committee’s or Board’s budget or the Administrative
Committee’s recommended budget by simple majority. The
requirement of a two-thirds vote of the General Assembly
applies only to changes not recommended by the
Administrative Committee, initiated on the floor of the
Assembly. With respect to the Committees and Agencies,
financial coordination is through the Administrative
Committee to the Assembly and from the General Assembly
to the Committees and Agencies. The Administrative
Committee is a “filter upward” and has no downward
authority.

The Administrative Committee shall annually provide and
publish guidelines for the Partnership Share Giving Program
of the PCA, including the Ministry Ask. These guidelines shall
include statistical information expressed both in per capita
giving terms and in proportional (percentage) giving terms to
demonstrate the giving necessary to attain full funding for
budgets of the General Assembly Committees and Agencies.
Such statistical information shall not include data pertaining to
age or ethnicity.

<a id="ga52-p1293"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1295 printed_page=1293 -->

The Committees and Agencies receiving support through the
PCA Partnership Share Giving Program shall present their
financial needs and their Ministry Ask to the Administrative
Committee for review and recommendation in the course of

the regular budgeting process. Each Committee and Agency
shall then be free to request from the PCA churches and
constituency the contributions necessary to fund its approved
budget.

While all churches remain free to give their contributions as
they by God’s guidance determine, all churches are
encouraged to give, as they are able, at least ten percent (10%)
of their tithes and offerings to the PCA causes of presbyteries
and General Assembly.

The Administrative Committee shall maintain a manual

containing the Affirmation of Core Financial Standards. This
information, by which each PCA General Assembly ministry
(Committee or Agency) shall function and by which the budget
process is governed for each Committee and Agency, shall be
available to all constituencies

_Adopted by Calvary Presbytery at its stated meeting of April 24, 2025._
_Attested by /s/ RE Melton L. Duncan, Stated Clerk_
