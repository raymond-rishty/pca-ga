# 30-29 Item 3 — Session's right to access congregation's financial and attendance records

*CCB advised that the session and diaconate have a right to access financial and attendance records as needed, limited only by prudence and protection of reputation.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 8-3, BCO 12-5.a, BCO 12-5.b, WLC 145  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga30_2002 lines 4841–4877](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p106)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a constitutional inquiry from Southeast Alabama Presbytery about whether a Session has unrestricted access to financial and attendance records of the congregation. In the opinion of the CCB, BCO 8-3 and BCO 12-5.a, b give a Session and Diaconate the right to access financial and attendance records as they deem necessary to fulfill their responsibilities to the church. The only restrictions on this right are those imposed by prudence and the Biblical calling to protect our neighbor's good name (cf. WLC 145).

**Key words:** BCO 8-3, BCO 12-5.a, BCO 12-5.b, WLC 145

## Verbatim record

3. Received a constitutional inquiry from Southeast Alabama Presbytery,
to wit:
The duties of Elders, as set forth in the Book of Church Order
includes:
To watch diligently over the flock committed to their
charge, that no corruption of doctrine or of morals
enter therein. (BCO 8-3)
Which includes overseeing communicant members who have
vowed:
“...to support the church in its worship and work to
the best of (your) ability.”

<a id="ga30-p107"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=30 pdf_page=109 printed_page=107 -->

In the fulfillment of their responsibilities, do elders have the right to
access to such attendance and financial giving records of the local
congregation as they deem necessary?
Does the BCO restrict, in any way, the access of elders and/or deacons
of a local church to such records?
The CCB answered:
The inquiry asks about the rights of elders in the context of their duty to
take spiritual oversight over their flock. It is the opinion of the CCB
that BCO 8-3 and 12-5.a,b give a session the right to access attendance
and financial records as they deem necessary in the fulfillment of their
responsibilities. In carrying out these responsibilities there are no
restrictions on the session, as a court, beyond those that would be
imposed by prudence and the Biblical responsibility to protect our
neighbors’ good name (cf. WLCQ 145).
In the furtherance of the duties specified in BCO 9-2, the Deacons may
access financial giving and attendance records. They are restricted by their
responsibility to act under the “supervision and authority of the Session”
(BCO 9-2, para. 2), the parameters of their BCO 9-2 responsibilities, and the
limitations noted in the last sentence of the preceding paragraph.
Respectfully Submitted,
/s/ TE Craig D. Childs, Sr., Chairman
/s/ TE Mark A. Rowden, Acting Secretary
