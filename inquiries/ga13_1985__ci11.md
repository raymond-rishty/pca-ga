# CI #15 — Scope of presbytery commissions under BCO 15-2

*The CCB advised that BCO 15-2's list of matters assignable to commissions is typical but not exhaustive, with other matters assignable subject to review and control.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 13th (1985)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 15-2  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga13_1985 lines 12625–12634](../markdown/ga13_1985.md#ga13-p247)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

, 55. That the advice regarding Constitutional Inquiry 15 from the Presbytery of Western Carolinas regarding BCO 15-2 be ratified.
1985, p. 246, Appendix I, 5. Constitutional Inquiry 15: From the Presbytery of the Western Carolinas. (That) we request the Judicial Business Committeeof the General Assembly to interpret BCO 15-2 as to whether or not commissions are limited to the five matters included in the first paragraph and to give an interpretation of the whole paragraph, including the meaning of "Among the matters ...". ANSWER: The language "Among the matters that may be properly executed..." provides a typical but not exhaustive list. Those items specifically listed may be assigned to commissions. Other matters may be assigned, with the understanding that the assignment to a commission will be subject to review and control.

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

5. Constitutional Inquiry 15: From the Presbytery of the Western Carolinas. See Recommendation 45.

(That) we request the Judicial Business Committee of the General Assembly to interpret *BCO 15-2* as to whether or not commissions are limited to the five matters included in the first paragraph and to give an interpretation of the whole paragraph, including the meaning of “Among the matters . . .”.

ANSWER:

The language “Among the matters that may be properly executed . . .” provides a typical but not exhaustive list.

Those items specifically listed may be assigned to commissions. Other matters may be assigned, with the understanding that the assignment to a commission will be subject to review and control.
