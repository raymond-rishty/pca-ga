# 30-29 III — Mandatory reporting of ordination exceptions to General Assembly

*An overture requiring presbyteries to record and report all ordination exceptions to the GA was found in conflict with the Constitution because it would allow a presbytery to bind a man's conscience beyond what the BCO permits.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 21-4, BCO Preface II.1, BCO Preface II.7  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga30_2002 lines 4687–4703](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p103)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 29 from Westminster Presbytery ("Amend BCO 21-4 to Record All Exceptions with Presbytery and Have Stated Clerk Report All Exceptions to the General Assembly") was in conflict with the Constitution for three reasons, including that it allowed a presbytery to determine whether a man may or may not teach what he believes, even if it is not out of accord with any fundamental of the system of doctrine, thus going beyond our constitution to bind the man's conscience (BCO Preface II.1 and II.7).

**Key words:** BCO 21-4, BCO Preface II.1, BCO Preface II.7

**Inquiry from:** Westminster Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga30_2002 p.103](../markdown/ga30_2002.md#ga30-p103)

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 29 from Westminster Presbytery (to CCB & B&O)

“Amend BCO 24-1 to Record All Exceptions with Presbytery
and Have Stated Clerk Report All Exceptions to the General Assembly”

Whereas, several of our sister presbyteries have overtured to
amend the Book of Church Order with respect to the
matter of doctrinal subscription; and

Whereas, we, too, are desirous of promoting the purity, peace,
and unity of the church; and

Whereas, we believe that in order to do so, it is important to
implement wording that the entire church can accept;

Now therefore, be it resolved, that Westminster Presbytery
hereby overtures the General Assembly of the
Presbyterian Church in America to begin the process
of Constitutional amendment by adopting the
following and sending it down to the presbyteries for
advice and consent:
To insert, in BCO 21-4, the following language:
It is the right and responsibility of the Presbytery, as
the court of original jurisdiction, to determine if the
candidate is out of accord with any of the statements
of these doctrinal standards and, as a consequence,
may not be able sincerely to receive and adopt the
Confession of Faith and Catechisms of this Church as

<a id="ga30-p231"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=30 pdf_page=233 printed_page=231 -->

containing the system of doctrine taught in the Holy
Scriptures (cf. BCO 21-5, Q. 2; 24-5, Q.2).
In examining a candidate for ordination the Presbytery
shall inquire not only into the candidate’s knowledge
and views in the areas specified above, but also shall
require the candidate to state the specific instances in
which he may differ with the Confession of Faith and
Catechisms in any of their statements and/or
propositions. The court may grant an exception to any
difference of doctrine if in the court’s judgment the
candidate’s declared difference does not strike at the
vitals of religion, and is not a serious departure from
the system of doctrine.
when a presbytery allows a man to hold an exception
to the standards, it shall also determine whether the
man may hold the exception in belief only, whether he
may teach it, or whether he may practice it so long as
he does not disturb the peace and purity of the church.
The Stated Clerk of Presbytery shall cause the man’s
allowed exception(s) stated in his own words, along
with the Presbytery’s action on the exception(s), to be
recorded in the minutes of Presbytery along with the
ministerial obligation he is required to sign. All
exceptions permitted by presbyteries will be reported
to the General Assembly Stated Clerk, to be included
in his report to the General Assembly.

### CCB advice

##### Overture 29 from Westminster Presbytery: Amend BCO 24-1 to Record All

Exceptions with Presbytery and Have Stated Clerk Report All Exceptions to
the General Assembly ”
It is the opinion of the CCB that Overture 29 from Westminster Presbytery is
in conflict with the Constitution. Specifically:
1. it allows a presbytery to determine whether a man mayor may not
teach what he believes, even if it is not out of accord with any
fundamental of the system of doctrine,thus going beyond our
constitution to bind the man’s conscience. (BCO Preface II.1 and II.7)
2. It is also unconstitutionally vague in not identifying what the
prohibition to teach would restrict or allow.
3. The last paragraph unconstitutionally provides a mechanism that puts
the final approval for presbytery membership at the GA instead of the
presbytery level. (BCO Preface II.2, BCO 21-1 through 7, BCO 13-1;
BCO 13-9a,c,d; BCO 11-3&4). [For text and action, see 30-50, III, 2,
p. 218.] Adopted by CCB
