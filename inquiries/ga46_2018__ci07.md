# App. O II.P — Definite Suspension as Conclusion of Judicial Process Under BCO 30

*The CCB found Philadelphia Presbytery's overture on definite suspension in conflict with BCO 30-1, which states that definite suspension concludes the judicial process.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 46th (2018)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 30-3, BCO 37-1, BCO 30-1  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga46_2018 lines 15747–15754](../markdown/ga46_2018.md#ga46-p319)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 20 from Philadelphia Presbytery ("Amend BCO 30-3 and BCO 37-1 Regarding Definite Suspension") was in conflict with other parts of the Constitution. The addition to BCO 37-1 is in conflict with BCO 30-1, which states that the censure of definite suspension "concludes the judicial process."

**Key words:** BCO 30-3, BCO 37-1, BCO 30-1

**Inquiry from:** Philadelphia Presbytery

**In the minutes:** [ga46_2018 p.319](../markdown/ga46_2018.md#ga46-p319)

**30-3.** Suspension from Sacraments is the

### CCB advice

**P** . Overture 20 from Philadelphia Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 30-3 and

37-1 Regarding Definite Suspension”
In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 20 **is in conflict** with other
parts of the Constitution. The addition of _BCO_ 37-1 is in conflict
with _BCO_ 30-1 which states that the censure of definite suspension
“concludes the judicial process.” _Adopted by the CCB_
