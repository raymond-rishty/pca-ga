# II.S — Calling and dissolution of TE relationships for needful work

*Proposed amendments to BCO 8-4, 20-1, 21-1, 21-12, and 23-1 regarding TE relationships for needful work were found in conflict with and ambiguous under the BCO.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 8-4, BCO 20-1, BCO 21-1, BCO 21-12, BCO 23-1, BCO 13-2, BCO 13-5  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict (and ambiguous); Adopted 8-0-0

**23-1** . When any minister shall tender the resignation of his pastoral
charge to his Presbytery, the Presbytery shall cite the church to appear
by its commissioners, to show cause why the Presbytery should or
should not accept the resignation. If the church fails to appear, or if

If any church desires to be relieved of its pastor, a similar
procedure shall be observed. But whether the minister or the church
initiates proceedings for a dissolution of the relation, there shall
always be a meeting of the congregation called and conducted in the
same manner as the call of the pastor. In any case, the minister must
not physically leave the field until the Presbytery or its commission
empowered to handle uncontested requests for dissolution has
dissolved the relation.

The associate or assistant pastors may continue to serve a
congregation when the pastoral relation of the senior pastor is
dissolved, but they may not normally succeed the senior pastor
without an intervening term of service in a different field of
labor. however a congregation by a secret ballot with four-fifths (4/5)
majority vote may petition Presbytery for an exception which by a
three-fourths (3/4) majority vote Presbytery may grant. Presbytery
needs to determine if the dissolution of the pastoral relationship with
the senior pastor was brought about in Christian love and good order
on the part of the parties concerned.

**RATIONALE:**

The current provisions of the BCO allow for calls to come from entities other
than congregations but do not specify how those calls should be presented and
prosecuted or dissolved. Additionally, the ordination and installation questions
presume a mani s being ordained and installed into a pastoral role ina
congregation. That means that the questions are inapplicable in the context of
a campus minister ora chaplain, for example.

Current _BCO_ 8-4 is the result of an editing process from material in the _BCO_
of the PCUS, which included additional material describing “works as may be
needful to the Church” as follows:

“When a minister is appointed t ob ea teacher ina school of divinity, or to give
instruction in the doctrines and duties of religion to youth assembled in a
college or university, it appertains to his office to take a pastoral oversight of
those committed to his charge, and to be diligent' in sowing the seed of the
Word and gathering the fruit thereof, as one who watches for souls.”

There are several common categories of calls to a Teaching Elder that are not
to a congregation. Among them would be campus minister (RUF), military and
other chaplains (MNA), missionary (MTW), and teachers (CTS and CC).
Other permanent committees and agencies (e.g., Ridge Haven, Geneva
Benefits) may or could issue calls to a teaching elder. The Stated Clerk ( _RAO_
3-1) is called by the General Assembly. All of these calls are legitimate to the
ministry (teaching elder) and require a minister to hold his membership ina
Presbytery ( _BCO_ 13-2). The Overture seeks to regularize how those calls are
presented and dissolved and to avoid the confusion that has sometimes resulted
from a lack of clarity.

The Overture sacrifices brevity for the sake of clarity in the ordination and
installation of men in the case of a needful work. It adds a new subsection to

_BCO_ 21 to set forth questions applicable in a non-congregational context to
avoid numerous footnotes or an entire new chapter to the _BCO_ that would
necessitate renumbering of all subsequent chapters.

Finally, the Overture seeks to give the permanent committees and agencies the
same flexibility with respect to calls that congregations currently enjoy while

<a id="ga52-p1265"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1267 printed_page=1265 -->

underscoring the responsibility of Presbyteries to approve or dissolve calls,
just as they do with congregational pastoral calls.

### CCB advice

**S.** Overture 30 from the Houston Metro Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 8-4, 20-1,
21-1, 21-12, and 23-1 re the Calling and Dissolution of TE Relationships for
Needful Work”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 30 is **in conflict** with our Constitution in
that it purports to give Presbyteries authority over the installation of Teaching
Elders to calls outside of churches of the Presbytery and the PCA as well as
the dissolution of such calls.

Additionally, in the opinion of the CCB, Overture 30 is **ambiguous** regarding
the relation between a “needful work” ( _BCO_ 8-4) and “a work not under the
jurisdiction of his Presbytery” ( _BCO_ 13-2; cf. _BCO_ 13-5), which is often called
an “out of bounds call.” it is unclear whether a “needful work” encompasses
all “out of bounds calls,” or whether there is still a category of “out of bounds
calls” that are different from “needful works.”

_Adopted 8-0-0_
