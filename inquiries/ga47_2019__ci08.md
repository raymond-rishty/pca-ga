# II.H — Amending filing methods and deadlines for judicial cases

*An overture amending BCO 42-4, 43-2, and 43-3 to update methods and deadlines for filing judicial cases was found not in conflict and adopted by the CCB.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 42-4, BCO 43-2, BCO 43-3  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted by the CCB

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 9 from Platte Valley Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 42-4; 43-2; 43-3 regarding Method and Deadlines
of Filing Cases”

**Whereas**, the Standing Judicial Commission ruled in Case 2016-02

( _M45GA_, 2017, pp. 510–14), that notice of complaints must be made by
hard copy, and that electronic filing of complaints are administratively
out of order; and

**Whereas**, the language of both _BCO_ 42-4 (filing appeals) and _BCO_ 43-3

(filing complaints) confusingly refer to two kinds of notification: the
notification of the lower court’s original decision, and the notification of
appeal/complaint by the appellant/ complainant; and

**Whereas** _,_ _BCO_ 42-4 and _BCO_ 43-3 explicitly provide that the “notification”

(that is, the notification of the lower court’s original decision) may be
made electronically, but do not explicitly forbid notification of appeals
and complaints from being made electronically; and

**Whereas**, _BCO_ 43-2 does not describe multiple types of “notification,” but

still does not explicitly define that complainants may not file a complaint
electronically; and

**Whereas** _,_ the _Operating Manual_ (18.10.b) of the Standing Judicial

Commission much more clearly defines proper, timely filing:

Documents required or permitted to be filed by a party shall be
filed with the Stated Clerk. Such filing shall not be timely
unless the documents are received in the office of the Stated

Clerk within the time fixed for such filing, except that papers
shall be deemed filed on the day of mailing if certified,
registered or express mail of the United States Postal Service
or any private service where verifying receipt is utilized.
Neither facsimiles nor Email will be allowed for purposes of
filing. Interested parties should be aware that responsibility for
such filings rests with them and that delays in delivery or nondelivery are the sole responsibility of the filing party. and

**Whereas**, this language would also help Sessions and Presbyteries clarify

proper, timely filing of appeals and complaints if included in the _BCO_ ;
and

**Whereas** _,_ the authors of the SJC Concurring Opinion on Case 2016-02

acknowledged this confusion of language, writing, “Accordingly we
believe that _BCO_ 43-3 should be clarified by amendment offered through
an Overture” ( _M45GA_, 2017, p. 514);

### CCB advice

**H** . Overture 9 from Platte Valley Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 42-4, 43-2,

and 43-3 regarding Method and Deadlines of Filing Cases”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 9 is **not in conflict** with other
parts of the Constitution. While both Overtures 9 and 34 are not in
conflict with the Constitution, they are in conflict with each other
and only one can be adopted. _Adopted by the CCB_
