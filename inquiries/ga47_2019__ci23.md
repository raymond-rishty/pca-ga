# III.B — Consecutive service on different GA committees without sabbatical

*The CCB advised that a GA permanent committee member who completed a full term is not eligible for consecutive service on a different committee or agency board without a sabbatical year.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 47th (2019)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 8-2.a.1, BCO 14-1.12  ·  **Disposition:** No (not eligible); Adopted by the CCB

*Source: [ga47_2019 p. 403](../markdown/ga47_2019.md#ga47-p403)*

---

## Verbatim record

**B** . is a member of a general assembly permanent committee who has

served a full term, eligible for consecutive service on a _different_
permanent Committee or Agency Board without taking a sabbatical
year? No. _Adopted by the CCB_

Rationale: _BCO_ 14-1.12 states, “Persons who have served for a full
term… on _one of_ the Assembly’s permanent committees or agencies
shall not be eligible for re-election to _an_ Assembly committee until
one year has elapse. . . .” The inclusiveness of the first italicized
word, combined with the fact that the second is an indefinite article
suggests that membership on any Assembly Committee/Board
makes one ineligible for election to _any other_ Committee until a
sabbatical year has passed. This has been the long-standing
interpretation of the Assembly’s Nominating Committee. However,
exceptions may be permitted in Agency bylaws approved by the
Assembly.
