# 29-28 Item 4 — SJC authority to opine on legitimacy of General Assembly action

*The CCB advised that the SJC may only address matters assigned to it, but once assigned a matter may render an opinion on the legitimacy of the GA action that prompted the referral.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Inquiry / reference  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga29_2001 lines 6237–6259](../markdown/ga29_2001.md#ga29-p145)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

The CCB answered a constitutional inquiry arising from SJC Case 99-1 asking whether the SJC has the authority to render an opinion on the legitimacy of an action of the General Assembly if not specifically asked to do so. The CCB advised the Assembly that the SJC may only render an opinion on the matters assigned to it. Once it is assigned a matter, the SJC may render an opinion even as to the legitimacy of an action in which the GA refers business to the SJC.

### CCB advice

## 4) CONSTITUTIONAL INQUIRY #2 FROM TE JOHN VAN DEVENDER

The Assembly received the following Constitutional Inquiry from TE John Van Devender and referred it to CCB:

WHEREAS, SJC Case 99-1 regarding the John Wood matter is scheduled to be presented to this General Assembly; and

WHEREAS, a supplemental report of a concurring opinion on this case, namely that one signed by RE Jack Williamson and others, is included in this report; and

WHEREAS, this particular supplemental report purports to comment on the action of the 28th General Assembly and further to conclude that the 28th General Assembly erred in assuming original jurisdiction of the matter involving John Wood; and

WHEREAS, BCO 15-4 and 5 authorizes the SJC to try and decide only the judicial cases committed to it; and

WHEREAS, the SJC does not seem to have any explicit authority to comment upon or rule on an action of the General Assembly; and

THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the 29th General Assembly directs the Committee on Constitutional Business to consider the following question and report back an answer prior to the consideration of the report from the SJC: “Does the Standing Judicial Commission have the authority to render an opinion on the legitimacy of an action of the General Assembly if not specifically asked to do so, particularly the legitimacy of an action in which the General Assembly refers business to the SJC; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that, if the Committee on Constitutional Business returns with a negative answer, that the 29th General Assembly move immediately to remove this specific concurring opinion from the SJC report and further that the Assembly direct members of the SJC to refrain from making such actions in the future.

<a id="ga29-p146"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=29 pdf_page=148 printed_page=146 -->

Answer:
It is the opinion of the CCB that SJC may only address what is assigned to it. It may not render any opinion unless it is first assigned a matter. Once it is assigned a matter, the SJC may render an opinion even as to the legitimacy of an action in which the GA refers business to the SJC. MSC
