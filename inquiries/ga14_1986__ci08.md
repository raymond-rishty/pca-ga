# CI #3 — Grounds and procedures for removing members from PCA church rolls

*Declining to address case-specific questions, the CCB outlined the three BCO provisions by which members may be removed from church rolls: transfer, discipline, or relocation.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 14th (1986)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 38-3, BCO 27-5, BCO 46-2, BCO 46-3, BCO 46-4  ·  **Disposition:** declined to answer specific questions; general advice given

*Source: [ga14_1986 lines 16825–16837](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p332)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

Constitutional Inquiry #3 from the Presbytery of North Texas. The questions were in regard to judicial procedure in a particular case. ANSWER: The Committee on Judicial Business is not able to answer the specific questions as stated. The questions ask more than an interpretation of the constitutional issues and go beyond the scope of our authority as stated in the Manual of Operations for the Committee on Judicial Business (5-2, c, 4, a). We offer the following information. Members (and officers) may be removed from the rolls of the Presbyterian Church in America through the following provisions: 1. BCO 38-3 upon their joining another church. 2. BCO 27-5 (see also BCO 46-2) after full disciplinary process. 3. BCO 46-2, 3, 4 when they have moved beyond the bounds of the church.
