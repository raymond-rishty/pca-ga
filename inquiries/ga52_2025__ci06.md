## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 9 from the James River Presbytery (to CCB, OC, AC, CC,

CDM, CTS, Geneva, MNA, MTW, PCAF, RH, RUF)
“Amend BCO 14-1 to Make AC Members Appointed by Committees
and Agencies Non-voting Advisory Members”

**Whereas** the permanent committees and agencies of the General Assembly

exist to serve the church and to promote the Great Commission ( _BCO_
14-1.5);

**Whereas** the PCA has, since its founding, been committed to a grass roots

system of church government;

**Whereas** the General Assembly’s Administrative Committee exercises

tremendous influence over the PCA’s national operations, including
the nomination of the Stated Clerk; the preparation of budgets for the
permanent committees and agencies; arranging for the General
Assembly to meet each year; and the hiring, retention, and dismissal
of numerous employees;

**Whereas**, as presently constituted in _BCO_ 14-1.12 and _RAO_ Article V, the

Administrative Committee consists of eleven (11) members elected at
large by the General Assembly ( _BCO_ 14-1.12.a; _RAO_ 5-1.a) and nine

<a id="ga52-p1182"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1184 printed_page=1182 -->

(9) members appointed by the permanent committees and agencies
( _BCO_ 14-1.12.b, _RAO_ 5-1.b);

**Whereas** this means that 45% of the votes on the Administrative Committee,

as presently constituted, are cast by men appointed to the Committee
rather than elected;

**Whereas**, per _BCO_ 14-1.12 and _RAO_ 5-3, “The chief administrative officers

of the program Committees and Agencies may attend any meeting of
the Administrative Committee. They shall be entitled to the privilege
of the floor, but shall not have a vote and must be excluded when an
executive session is called;” and

**Whereas** the PCA will be better served if the voting members of the

Administrative Committee are directly elected by the General
Assembly, making the Committee more directly accountable to the
will of the Assembly; now, therefore,

**Be it resolved** that _BCO_ 14-1.12 is amended as follows (underlining for

additions):

By inserting the words “non-voting, advisory” into the stem of _BCO_ 14-1.12.b
between the word “One” and the word “member” and by inserting the
following sentence at the end of the second paragraph: “The non-voting,
advisory members appointed to the Administrative Committee pursuant to
subparagraph (b) above shall have the privilege of the floor but shall not be
entitled to vote and must be excluded when an executive session is called.”

These changes shall result in an amended _BCO_ 14-1.12 that reads:

12. The Assembly permanent committees are the Administrative

Committee of General Assembly, Committee on Discipleship
Ministries, Committee on Mission to North America, Committee
on Mission to the World, and Committee on Reformed
University Fellowship.

The Administrative Committee of General Assembly
(AC) shall consist of twenty (20) members:

a. Eleven members in classes elected through the

standard nomination and election procedure,

<a id="ga52-p1183"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1185 printed_page=1183 -->

b. One non-voting, advisory member each from the

following program committees or agencies:
1. Committee on Discipleship Ministries (CDM);
2. Covenant College (CC);
3. Covenant Theological Seminary (CTS);
4. Mission to North America (MNA);
5. Mission to the World (MTW);
6. PCA Foundation (PCAF);
7. Geneva Benefits Group, Inc. (Geneva);
8. Reformed University Fellowship (RUF);
9. Ridge Haven Conference Center (RH).

The eleven members at large shall serve a term of four
years. The chairman of the Administrative Committee shall be
one of its members at large. The non-voting, advisory members
appointed to the Administrative Committee, pursuant to
subparagraph (b) above, shall have the privilege of the floor but
shall not be entitled to vote and must be excluded when an

executive session is called.

Each program committee and agency shall designate its
member each year at the last meeting of the committee or board
before the meeting of General Assembly. The chief
administrative officers of the program committees and agencies
may attend any meeting of the Administrative Committee. They
shall be entitled to the privilege of the floor but shall not have a
vote and must be excluded when an executive session is called.

Committee on Discipleship Ministries, Committee on
Mission to North America, Committee on Mission to the World,
and Committee on Reformed University Fellowship shall consist
of fifteen (15) men divided into five classes of three men each,
with two men being TEs and one RE or two men being REs and
one TE on alternate years, elected to serve five-year terms.
Committees on Discipleship Ministries, Mission to North
America, Mission to the World, and Reformed University
Fellowship shall have one ruling and one teaching elder as
alternates to fill any vacancy that may occur during the year.

Persons who have served for a full term, or for at least two
years of a partial term, on one of the Assembly’s permanent
committees or agencies shall not be eligible for re-election to an

<a id="ga52-p1184"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1186 printed_page=1184 -->

Assembly committee until one year has elapsed. (Exceptions
may be permitted in agency bylaws approved by the Assembly.)

**Be it further resolved** that in order to harmonize the language in the _Rules of_

_Assembly Operation_ with this amendment to the _Book of Church_
_Order_, at the time that this amendment is finally and fully adopted by
the General Assembly, having met with the approval of 2/3 of the
presbyteries, _RAO_ 5-1.b is amended as follows (underlining for
additions):

By inserting the words “non-voting, advisory” in the first line between the
words “One” and “member” such that an amended _RAO_ 5-1 reads:

**5-1.** The Administrative Committee of General Assembly (AC) shall

consist of twenty (20) members:

a. Eleven members in classes elected through the

standard nomination and election procedure,
b. One non-voting, advisory member each from the

following program committees or agencies:
1. Committee on Discipleship Ministries (CDM);
2. Covenant College (CC);
3. Covenant Theological Seminary (CTS);
4. Mission to North America (MNA);
5. Mission to the World (MTW);
6. PCA Foundation (PCAF);
7. Geneva Benefits Group, Inc. (Geneva);
8. Reformed University Fellowship (RUF);
9. Ridge Haven Conference Center (RH).

**Be it further resolved** that in order to harmonize the language in the _Rules of_

_Assembly Operation_ with this amendment to the _Book of Church_
_Order_, at the time that this amendment is finally and fully adopted by
the General Assembly, having met with the approval of 2/3 of the
presbyteries, _RAO_ 5-2 is amended by adding the following sentence at
the end the end of the paragraph, “The non-voting advisory members
appointed to the Administrative Committee pursuant to _RAO_ 5-1.b
shall have the privilege of the floor but shall not be entitled to vote and
must be excluded when an executive session is called.” This

<a id="ga52-p1185"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1187 printed_page=1185 -->

amendment will result in an amended _RAO_ 5-2 that reads (underlining
for additions):

**5-2.** The eleven members at large shall serve a term of four years. The

chairman of the Administrative Committee shall be one of its

members at large. The non-voting, advisory members appointed
to the Administrative Committee, pursuant to _RAO_ 5-1.b, shall
have the privilege of the floor but shall not be entitled to vote and
must be excluded when an executive session is called.

_Adopted by the James River Presbytery at its stated meeting, January 18,_
_2025._
