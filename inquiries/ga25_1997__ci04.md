# 25-22 V — Whether a church court may accept a member's resignation

*The CCB advised there is no constitutional bar preventing a court from accepting a member's resignation and removing that person's name from the roll.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 25th (1997)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 46-5, BCO 38-4  ·  **Disposition:** advice given — no constitutional bar to accepting resignation

*Source: [ga25_1997 lines 7887–7900](../markdown/ga25_1997.md#ga25-p131)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

When a person 'resigns' from membership in a church or presbytery, is this not tantamount to renouncing the jurisdiction of the body and is there a constitutional bar to a court accepting this 'resignation?' Response: It is the advice of the CCB that there is no constitutional bar that would prevent a court from accepting the 'resignation' and removing the person's name from the roll.
