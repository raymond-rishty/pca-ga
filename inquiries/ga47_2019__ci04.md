##### OVERTURE 5 from Central Indiana Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 23-1 on Procedures for Dissolving Various Pastoral
Relations”

Central Indiana Presbytery hereby overtures General Assembly to amend
_BCO_ 23-1 as follows in order to clarify the procedures for the dissolution of
the various pastoral relations:

23-1. When any minister shall tender the resignation of his
pastoral charge to his Presbytery, the Presbytery shall cite the
church to appear by its commissioners, to show cause why the
Presbytery should or should not accept the resignation. If the
church fails to appear, or if its reasons for retaining its pastor
be deemed insufficient, his resignation shall be accepted and
the pastoral relation dissolved.

without an intervening term of service in a different field of

**Rationale:**
The current language in the second paragraph of _BCO_ 23-1 is unclear
regarding the proper procedure for a congregation to pursue in the interest of
dissolving the different pastoral relations. As it currently stands, it could be
understood to imply that even in the case of an assistant pastor, who is called
only by the Session and not the congregation ( _BCO_ 22-3), the congregation
must first vote to approve the dissolution before the request to dissolve the
relationship is presented to the Presbytery. The proposed amendment simply
seeks to clarify the proper procedure in the case of a pastor, associate pastor,
or assistant pastor.

### CCB advice

**D** . Overture 5 from Central Indiana Presbytery: “Amend _BCO_ 23-1 on

Procedures for Dissolving Various Pastoral Relations”

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 5 is **not in conflict** with other
parts of the Constitution. CCB notes the following constitutional

consequences:

- It removes the explicit requirement for a congregational meeting
when the minister resigns.

- It removes the reference that the meeting is “called and
conducted in the same manner as the call of the pastor” ( _BCO_
20-3, 4) with requirements regarding who presides, how votes
are counted, the method of voting, etc. _Adopted by the CCB_
