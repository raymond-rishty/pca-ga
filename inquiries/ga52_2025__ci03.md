# II.C — Flexibility for candidates applying to come under presbytery care

*The CCB found the proposed amendment to BCO 18-2 allowing flexible application procedures for candidates not in conflict with the Constitution.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 52nd (2025)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 18-2  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict; Adopted 8-0-0

## Verbatim record

### As referred / posed

##### OVERTURE 6 from Rocky Mountain Presbytery (to CCB, OC)

“Amend _BCO_ 18-2 to Allow Flexibility on Applications to Come
Under Care”

**Be it resolved:** That _BCO_ 18-2 be amended by the current language as follows
(underlining for additions, strikethrough for deletions):

**18-2** . Every applicant for the ministry must put himself under the care
of Presbytery, which should ordinarily be the Presbytery that has
jurisdiction of the church of which he is a member. The endorsement
of his Session must be given to the Presbytery, consisting of
testimonials regarding his Christian character and promise of
usefulness in the ministry. The endorsement should also describe the
activities of ministry the applicant has participated in with brief
evaluation.

Every applicant for care shall be a member of the
congregation whose session provides an endorsement for at least six
months before filing his application, except in those cases deemed
extraordinary by the Presbytery.

Every applicant must should file his application with the clerk
of the Presbytery at least one month before the meeting of the
Presbytery (unless a pres byte ry has a different stated application
deadline). An applicant for care may not be received under care and
examined for ordination at the same meeting of the Presbytery, since
he must serve a period of at least one year of internship prior to
ordination (see _BCO_ 19-7 and 21-4). An applicant for internship is
obliged to be under care and may be licensed to preach the Gospel;
further, one who is not already under care may be taken under care, be

licensed to preach the Gospel, and become an intern at the same
meeting of Presbytery.

So that the amended section will read as follows:

**18-2** . Every applicant for the ministry must put himself under the care
of Presbytery, which should ordinarily be the Presbytery that has
jurisdiction of the church of which he is a member. The endorsement
of his Session must be given to the Presbytery, consisting of
testimonials regarding his Christian character and promise of
usefulness in the ministry. The endorsement should also describe the
activities of ministry the applicant has participated in with brief
evaluation.

Every applicant for care shall be a member of the
congregation whose session provides an endorsement for at least six
months before filing his application, except in those cases deemed
extraordinary by the Presbytery.

Every applicant should file his application with the clerk of
the Presbytery at least one month before the meeting of the Presbytery
(unless a pres byte ry has a different stated application deadline). An
applicant for care may not be received under care and examined for
ordination at the same meeting of the Presbytery, since he must serve
a period of at least one year of internship prior to ordination (see _BCO_
19-7 and 21-4). An applicant for internship is obliged to be under care
and may be licensed to preach the Gospel; further, one who is not
already under care may be taken under care, be licensed to preach the
Gospel, and become an intern at the same meeting of Presbytery.

**Rationale**

As previously written _BCO_ 18-2 restricts the ability of Presbyteries to receive
the applications of applicants for the ministry any time after one month before
a Presbytery meeting.

In the case of some Presbyteries, this deadline has delayed the licensure and
ordination process for ministry applicants by setting an inflexible application
deadline. In the case of some Presbyteries (that meet three times a year), the
process for ministry applicants can be delayed more than five (5) months.

<a id="ga52-p1173"></a>
<!-- PAGE ga=52 pdf_page=1175 printed_page=1173 -->

The adopted text of the 1 [st] General Assembly required a candidate to file his
application “at least two months before the meeting of the Presbytery.” The
17 [th] General Assembly adopted a change in the requirement, replacing “two
months” with “one month”, thus requiring an applicant to submit an
application “at least one month before the meeting of Presbytery.”

The change adopted by the 17 [th] Assembly was a reasonable change that
reflected the amount of time needed for Stated Clerks to collect and

disseminate applications in preparation for Presbytery meetings. This change
reflected cultural changes in communication with the advent of Fax and email.

Given the ability to disseminate applications more quickly via internet, email,
Dropbox, etc. Another change to 18-2 is reasonable.

This amendment seeks to eliminate an inflexible deadline for ministry
applicants (with a change to _should_ from _must_ ) and give discretion to
individual Presbyteries in setting their own application deadline. This could
alleviate the delay of ordination for some ministry candidates and will better
reflect the current time needed to collect and disseminate applications in
preparation for Presbytery meetings.
