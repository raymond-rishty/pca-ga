# CI #10 — Effect of GA refusal to accept a judicial reference on lower-court proceedings

*The committee's recommendation and the Assembly's determination refusing a judicial reference apply to the entire judicial process in PCA courts, clarifying that no parties were left without recourse.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Constitutional inquiry  ·  **Assembly:** 14th (1986)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 41-5  ·  **Disposition:** advice given

*Source: [ga14_1986 pp. 334–335](../markdown/ga14_1986.md#ga14-p334)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

That the answer of the Permanent Committee concerning Constitutional Inquiry #10 from the Presbytery of Eastern Canada, be ratified. 1986, p. 332, Appendix I, 10. Constitutional Inquiry #10. From the Presbytery of Eastern Canada regarding the refusal of the 13th General Assembly to accept a judicial reference. '...In our understanding of the ruling of the Assembly none of the parties could pursue judicial process, and thus, any complaint or charges could not be evaluated. We may, however, have misunderstood or overlooked something and would appreciate any clarification....' ANSWER: The recommendation of the committee and the determination of the Assembly have application to the judicial process in the courts of the PCA.
