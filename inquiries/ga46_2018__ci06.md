# App. O II.O — Thirty-Day Notice for Church Withdrawal and Congregational Competency

*The CCB found Western Canada Presbytery's overture requiring thirty days' notice to leave the PCA was not in conflict, but a minority argued it violated congregational competency and civil sufficiency clauses.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Type:** Overture/amendment advice  ·  **Assembly:** 46th (2018)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 25-11  ·  **Disposition:** not in conflict

*Source: [ga46_2018 lines 15736–15746](../markdown/ga46_2018.md#ga46-p319)*

---

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 17 from Western Canada Presbytery ("Amend BCO 25-11 to Require a Thirty-Days Notice to Leave PCA") was not in conflict with the Constitution. However, the dissenting opinion of the minority stated that the creation of a more stringent requirement that applies only to churches wishing to withdraw is in conflict with the congregational competency and civil sufficiency clauses of BCO 25-11.

**Key words:** BCO 25-11

**Inquiry from:** Western Canada Presbytery
