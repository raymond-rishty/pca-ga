# 28-19 V — Disciplinary measures against a presbytery tried on GA floor

*An overture targeting Tennessee Valley Presbytery conflicted with the Constitution by predetermining guilt and prescribing censures without following BCO 40-4 through 40-6 or referral to the SJC.*

**Body:** Committee on Constitutional Business (CCB)  ·  **Assembly:** 28th (2000)  ·  **Provisions:** BCO 40-4, BCO 40-5, BCO 40-6, BCO 15-1, BCO 15-4  ·  **Disposition:** in conflict

*Source: [ga28_2000 lines 3897–3903](../markdown/ga28_2000.md#ga28-p78)*

## Digest headnote
*Editorial summary from the PCA Digest, Part II (Interpretations of the Constitution) — this is the Digest's wording, not the verbatim minutes. The authoritative text is the verbatim record below / linked above.*

In the opinion of the CCB, Overture 21 from Louisiana Presbytery ("Disciplinary Measures Against Tennessee Valley Presbytery") as worded was in conflict with our Constitution since it pre-determined the guilt of a presbytery and prescribed censures. In order for such a determination to be made, and such a censure to be administered, the procedures of BCO 40-4 through BCO 40-6 would need to be followed. Additionally, our Constitution does not allow for the "the conduct of a trial on the floor of the Assembly" since all judicial matters are referred to the SJC (BCO 15-1), and this matter could be referred to the SJC by the GA (BCO 15-4).

**Key words:** BCO 40-4, BCO 40-5, BCO 40-6, BCO 15-1, BCO 15-4

**Inquiry from:** Louisiana Presbytery

##### OVERTURE 21 From Louisiana Presbytery

“Disciplinary Measures Against Tennessee Valley Presbytery”

**Whereas**, several presbyteries of the Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) have formally brought to the attention of Tennessee Valley Presbytery (TVP) the fact that one of its churches has allowed women to preach; and

**Whereas**, TVP, to date, has refused to deal adequately with the situation; and

Whereas, our Constitution tells us that it is “incumbent” upon a higher court
that has been “well advised” of serious neglect or irregularity, to “take
cognizance of the same, and to examine, deliberate and judge in the
whole matter” (BCO 40-4), and

Whereas, the 14th General Assembly, in approving the report of the blue-ribbon

(a) ignore the failure to act, or (b) counsel, advise, exhort, and urge the

Whereas, given the great turmoil which TVP’s failure to act has engendered

### CCB advice

## OVERTURE 21 – From Louisiana Presbytery, “Disciplinary Measures Against Tennessee Valley Presbytery.”

**Response** – It is the opinion of the CCB that Overture 21 as worded is not consistent with our Constitution since it pre-determines the guilt of a presbytery and prescribes censures. In order for such a determination to be made, and such a censure to be administered, then the procedures of *BCO* 40-4 through 40-6 would need to be followed. These procedures always provide for a hearing of specific charges in an appropriate court and with opportunity for defense before any individual or entity is reprimanded or rebuked. The Review and Control process is one way that such a matter might properly come before the Assembly so that a presbytery’s actions could be reviewed.

Additionally our Constitution does not allow for the “the conduct of a trial on the floor of the Assembly” since all judicial matters are referred to the SJC (*BCO* 15-1); and this matter could be referred to the SJC by the GA (*BCO* 15-4).

The response was adopted by proper motion, 8-0-0.
