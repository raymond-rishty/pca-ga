#### I. SUMMARY OF THE FACTS

At its summer stated meeting in July 1999, ECP ruled that a transferring licentiate’s view regarding the length of the creation days was not an exception to the Westminster Standards. TE Black’s complaint against this ruling was considered at ECP’s fall stated meeting in October 1999. A motion to deny the complaint failed. A motion to sustain the complaint also failed. In November 1999, TE Black complained to the General Assembly. An SJC Panel heard his complaint on March 2, 2000. The Panel affirmed the ruling of the Presbytery. The dictionary defines the “impenitent” as (1) not repenting of sin or (2) incapable of repenting.

This along with the statement in *BCO* 36-5 that the offender “give satisfactory evidence of repentance” clearly places the burden upon Dr. Shive.

Under *BCO* 37-3 it is stated that:

“. . . When the court shall be satisfied as to the reality of the repentance of an indefinitely suspended offender, he shall be admitted to profess his repentance, either in the presence of the court alone or publicly. . .”

When the penitent is restored the language of restoration in *BCO* 37-3 states that:

“Whereas, you, ______________, have now manifested such repentance as satisfies the Church. . .”

The reality of Dr. Shive’s repentance is a matter of discretion and judgment which must be determined to the satisfaction of the Session. The Session in the resolution which it adopted (ROC 75) stated that:

“. . . the Session failed to bring Dr. Shive to a point of admission of guilt and repentance to the particulars of the judgments against him (with exception of the judgment of scandalous conduct); and whereas the Session now at what it considers an impasse, does consider Dr. Shive’s condition to be incorrigible and contumacious.”

Obviously Dr. Shive did not manifest “such repentance that satisfied the Church”.

The Session has been patient. Dr. Shive did not give satisfactory evidence of repentance to the Session. The Session is not satisfied as to the reality of his repentance. After the SJC remanded Case 97-9 to the Session to reduce the censure to suspension of Sacraments, the Session immediately complied and formed a Commission to shepherd Dr. Shive and to attempt to lead him to repentance. The Shive Commission met many times and had much correspondence with him. The Session has concluded that Dr. Shive did not meet his burden of proof of repentance and therefore the Session had the right to proceed to excommunication in accordance with *BCO* 30-3.
