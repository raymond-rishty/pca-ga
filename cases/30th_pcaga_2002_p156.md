Case 2001-19
APPEAL OF TE SUNG KEON KIM
VS.
KOREAN CAPITAL PRESBYTERY

#### I. Statement of the Facts

1. The Appellant TE Sung Keon Kim was called to be assistant pastor by
the Session of Korean Presbyterian Church Washington (KPCW) and began
his ministry on July 15, 2000.
2. Prior to his arrival, division developed within the leadership of Korean
Presbyterian Church Washington.
3. The situation escalated to the point that there were two groups both
claiming to be the true Session of KPCW. There was reported physical
violence at the church and police were called to resolve disputes. The
congregation was advised by the civil authority that if the turmoil continued,
the church might be prohibited from meeting. A total of 16 references,
petitions, accusations and complaints were filed with KCP regarding the
situation at KPCW.
4. To deal with these 16 petitions, references, complaints and
accusations, on October 9, 2000 at the reconvened portion of its 17th Stated
Meeting KCP appointed a commission to “suspend the functions of the KPCW
church Session and have the Presbytery replace the functions and report to the
Presbytery meeting in three months.”
5. The Presbytery Commission, known as the Administrative
Commission and the Judicial Commission (it had been assigned both
functions), began its work on October 16, 2000 and commenced to act for the
session and as a judicial commission.
6. Turmoil continued at KPCW and the commission, acting as that
session, sought and obtained as civil court order to enforce its administration
of the church.
7. Under these circumstances, TE Kim participated in efforts to call a
special meeting of KCP. When no meeting was scheduled, TE Kim
participated in the call of a meeting in accordance with BCO 13-12.
8. Charges against TE Kim for participating in the call of an
“unconstitutional” meeting of Presbytery were filed on December 22, 2000.
9. KCP did have a properly called special meeting on January 8, 2001, at
which it appointed a commission to deal with the charges against TE Kim.
10. The Commission summoned TE Kim to appear and answer charges on
February 3, 17 and March 3, 2001 but he did not respond.
11. On March 12, 2001 the commission heard testimony and received
evidence and found TE Kim guilty of the charges and contumacy and deposed
him from the ministry.

12. At the 18th Stated Meetingof KCP on April 2, 2001 the report of the
commission along with the deposition of TE Kim was approved
13. On April 25, 2001 TE Kimappealed hisdeposition to theStanding
Judicial Commission.
14. A five-man panel of the SJC heard the appeal on December 4, 2001 at the
McLean Presbyterian Church in McLean, VA.

#### II. Statement of the Issue

Did the Judicial Commission follow BCO procedures in instituting and
conducting judicial process against the appellant?

#### III. Judgment

No, the commission did not follow the procedures of BCO 32, 34; and,
therefore, the case is remanded to Presbytery for a new trial in compliance
with BCO 32, 34.

#### IV. Reasoning and Opinion

It is clear that no indictment was ever served upon the accused. It is
not clear whether or not an indictment was ever prepared. The Record of the
Case does not contain any minutes of the proceeding whether or not it was a
trial.
BCO 32-3 provides in part that:
“When a charge is laid before the Session or Presbytery, it
shall be reduced to writing, and nothing shall be done at the first
meeting of the court, unless by consent of parties, except:
(1) to appoint a prosecutor,
(2) to order the indictment drawn and a copy, along with
names of witnesses then known to support it, served on the
accused, and
(3) to cite all parties and their witnesses to appear and be
heard at another meeting which shall not be sooner than ten
days after such citation.”
It is not clear from the Record of the Case whether or not a prosecutor
was appointed formally. The proper service of an indictment together with a
list of witnesses is critical and because that did not happen here the case must
be remanded.
We are not passing upon the adequacy of the citations, copies of which
are part of the record.
Because our decision is on the procedural aspects of the case we do
not comment on the merits.
