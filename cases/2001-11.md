# Case 2001-11 — Complaint RE Byung Han Yoo vs. Korean Northwest Presbytery

**Court:** SJC  ·  **Assembly:** 30th (2002)  ·  **Disposition:** dismissed  ·  **BCO cited:**  43-2 

*Source: [ga30_2002 p. 142](../markdown/ga30_2002.md)*

---

#### III. Judgment

No, such original jurisdiction should be with the Session; therefore,
the Presbytery’s action and/or the Special Judicial Commission of Presbytery’s
actions in this matter are reversed.

#### IV. Reasoning and Opinion

The court of original jurisdiction for communicant members in any
local church is the Session of that church. The Appellant in this case was a
member of the Korean Presbyterian Church of Washington and thus, any
judicial action or censure against a member must originate within the court of
original jurisdiction, the Session. Sinceneither theKCP nor itsCommission
was the proper court of original jurisdiction,the KCPCommission’s action to
adjudicate charges against the Appellant was contrary to the constitutional
standards, and the action of the lower court is reversed.
