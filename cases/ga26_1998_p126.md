#### II. Statement of the Issues

Did Rocky Mountain Presbytery err in receiving and immediately acting upon a
motion to cite Rev. Bruce Nickoley for contumacy and rendering the judgment to
depose him from the ministry without giving him reasonable opportunity to defend
himself under the due process provisions for a trial as outlined in the Book of Church
Order?

#### III. Judgment

Yes. This case is remanded to Rocky Mountain Presbytery with the
recommendation that it either (a) institute a prosecution against Rev. Bruce Nickoley as
a case under process on the charge of contumacy and/or other charges under the due
process provisions of the Book of Church Order for a trial, beginning with BCO §32-3.
Or (b) revoke the judgment of deposition from the ministry against Rev. Bruce
nickoley and reinstate hi mas a member of rocky mountain presbytery in good
standing.

#### IV. Reasoning and Opinion

Appellee relies on several Book of Church Order (BCO) provisions in its
justification for its action in “deposing from ministry” Rev. Bruce Nickoley. In the
ROC and in Appelle’s brief, app el lee relies o nits interpretations of b co provisions
§38-1, 34-4, 32-6, and 35-12 as constitutional justification for this action. We are of the
opinion that r mp has misinterpreted the applicability of these b co provisions to this
case. we shall give our reasoning and opinion as to the proper interpretation of these
BCO provisions to this case.

BCO §38-1
BCO §38-1 deals w ithaca se without process where a person “shall come
forward and make his offense known to the court.” RMP cites this provision as its
constitutional basis for judging that “Rev. Bruce Nickoley by refusal to submit to the
order of Rocky Mountain Presbytery admits his contumacy ” (ROC II-A-6). Likewise,
Appellee’s brief contains these arguments, to-wit:
1. “Because TE Nickoley continued to refuse to submit to RM P’s Order,
RMP found said refusal to be an admission of guilt for his contumacious
behavior.”
2. “TE Nickoley implicitly confessed his contumacy by continually
refusing to obey the Court’s order in the presence of all the presbyters.”
3. “Contumacious behavior is not an ordinary statement in the presence of
the Court It is sinful behavior which shows a lack of respect for the
Court Such rebellious and obstinate behavior is in actuality making his
offense known to the Court.”
4. “Failing to repent, TE Nickoley ‘made his offense known to the Court’
and left the presbytery no choice except to find him guilty of contempt .”
Thus by relying on BCO §38-1 as a justification for its judgment, RMP admits
that it did not follow the regular due process for a trial as prescribed by the BCO We
agree with Dr. Morton Smith’s interpretation of BCO §38-1 as he quotes 19th century
theologian Rev F. P. Ramsey, viz :
“In handling a confession of guilt it is essential that the person intends to confess
and permit the Court to render judgment without process. Statements made by him
in the presence of the court must not betaken as the basis of judgment without
process except by consent, for that would be to deprive him of his right ”
The facts in this case do not support a voluntary “coming forward” of rev
Bruce Nickoley. But, the facts in this case clearly contradict any voluntary confession
by him that would justify trying the case without due process The undisputed facts, in
the ROC, are:
1 “When TE Nickoley saw how BCO 38-1 was being used. . . he clearly
stated in unequivocal terms that he was admitting to no guilt whatsoever
regard the charge of contumacy ” (ROC 1-3).
2. “It was clear to RMP that TE Nickoley was not intending to confess to
the charge of contumacy and a full statement of the facts were never
given to the accused for his approval before the court proceeded to a
judgment ” (ROC 1-4).
3. “In the light of TE Nickoley’s clear denial of his guilt in the charge of
contumacy, the BCO is clear as to the process by which we must proceed
- if the accused . plead and take issue the trial shall proceed.” (ROC I-
6).
We are, therefore, of the opinion that since Rev. Bruce Nickoley did not invoke
BCO §38-1, rm phase r re din denying him aright to a trial, the right to be heard and
defend his actions, and the right to due process under the provisions of the BCO.
