**II. STATEMENT OF THE ISSUES**

1. Did Appellant demonstrate any mistake or injustice in the judgment

and censure?

2. Did Appellant demonstrate any irregularity in the proceedings of the

lower court?

3. Did Appellant demonstrate any receiving of improper or declining

to receive proper evidence?

**III. JUDGMENT**

1. Yes. The Record fails to provide adequate evidence to render a guilty

verdict. Therefore, the Appeal is sustained, and the verdicts and
censure are reversed in whole.

2. See Judgment 1.

3. See Judgment 1.

**IV. REASONING AND OPINION**

As in most appeals, the Appellant sought higher court review of several things,
including the nature of the charges and the weight and propriety of the alleged
evidence against him. Here is an opening statement in the Appeal: "Appellant
contends that MAP erred in its judgments and censures against me, and in
support of this Appeal offering the following." Below is a concluding
paragraph in his four-page Appeal, reformatted for emphasis.

Appellant maintains that MAP

[1] erred in charging him for failing to maintain qualifications of

an elder, failing to love his wife, and failing to uphold
ordination vows six and seven and

[2] then inflicting the censure of deposition from office, and

[3] that these alleged offenses took place between May 8, 2021 and

September 20, 2022. Further,

[4] MAP erred by taking testimony that fell outside of the sixteen
month period specified in the Indictment, and

[5] drawing conclusions of alleged events that had not occurred

within the time period MAP itself had determined. (emphasis,
and brackets with numbers, added)

In an appeal, if a single specification of error warrants sustaining that appeal,
there is little reason to address any others. In this Case, given the evidence and
testimony presented by the prosecution, and by the defense, it was
unreasonable to reach guilty verdicts and thus, an error to impose any censure.
Thus, the Appellant demonstrated a mistake and injustice in the judgment and
censure. Therefore, this Appeal is sustained on specifications of error 1 and 2
above, and the verdicts and censure are reversed in whole.
