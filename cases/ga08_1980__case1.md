**Case 3: Complaint of Teaching Elders Byron Snapp and James M. Hope against Calvary Presbytery.**

The text of a complaint against the actions of Calvary Presbytery in the matter of accepting a candidate for ordination under the extraordinary clause when there was no reason for using the extraordinary clause. The said candidate had chosen not to take more than one quarter of Hebrew as he did not at that time plan to come into the PCA.

<!-- PAGE ga=8 pdf_page=51 printed_page=null -->

The undersigned hereby file a complaint regarding the following motion that was adopted by Calvary
Presbytery at its stated winter meeting Thursday, Jan. 24, 1980: "That (the candidate) be examined by
Presbytery under the extraordinary clause (Book of Church Order 22 A ) "
Respectfully submitted,
Byron Snapp
James M. Hope
Date, January 28, 1980
REASONS FOR THE COMPLAINT
1) It appears that more than one quarter of Hebrew is required to meet the standards set forth in BCO 22-4
in light of the following: a) All the approved Seminaries of the PCA recognize the need of more than one quarter
of Hebrew to meet the Hebrew requirement set forth in BCO 22-4; b) On page 190 of the Minutes of the 7th
General Assembly of the PCA the 2t2 program has five quarters of Hebrew and Hebrew exegesis recommended
as a necessary part of the curriculum; c) By bringing the candidate in under the extraordinary clause, the
Presbytery, in effect, went on record as stating that his Hebrew preparation was insufficient to meet the
requirements of BCO Tl-A.
2) The candidate took but one quarter of Hebrew. He passed it. He did not take further courses although
further courses were available. In light of this fact, the use of the extraordinary clause appears to us to be
improper.
3) We believe that the extraordinary clause is to be used only in extraordinary cases. We do not believe
this to be an extraordinary case as a) the candidate is a young man;b) he passed his first quarter of Hebrew; c) he
could be a student supply at the church which called him and continue his study of Hebrew at Columbia Bible
College Graduate School, Columbia, S.C. of which he is an alumnus, as he is to be pastor of northeast chapel
in Columbia, S.C. ;d) The reasons which the candidate gave for not taking more Hebrew are as follows: “ To be
honest, i took only one quarter of hebrews imply because i thought i would fail the next quarter. Grades were
give nona curve basis and i was always at the bottom. I might add that the PCA was no part of my vocational
plans at the time, so my main motive in securing a hebrews kill was simply so that i could lookup words and
stumble through commentaries, not taking any certain number of hours.' ’
4) If the extraordinary clause is used in this manner, the precedent is set for the relaxing of any
requirement set forth in BCO 22-4.
5) We desire to have the following question answered: "What is the proper use of the extraordinary
clause?''.

The meeting was opened with prayer. On motion duly made, seconded, and
carried, TE Rodney King was elected chairman. On motion duly made,
seconded, and carried, TE Edwin Cunningham was elected secretary.
The chairman exhorted the commission in accordance with BCO 33-12, and
portions of chapters 33 and 43 of BCO were read for the instruction of the court.
The complete record of the case was read by the chairman. The complainants
made opening arguments and introduced, with permission of the respondent, a
written explanation and expansion of the argument. Respondent made opening
argument. Neither party desired to question the other. Members of the court
posed questions to both parties.
The court, by unanimous vote, went into executive session to discuss the merit
of the case and to express opinion.
The vote was then taken on the single specification of the complaint in the form
prescribed by BCO 43-7. The commission voted to sustain the specification of
error. The secretary and the chairman were directed to draw up a minute
explanatory of the action for perfection and adoption by the commission.
A motion was made, seconded, and carried, that the ordination be found
contrary to the Book of Church Order due to this error.
A motion was made, seconded, and carried, that this matter be remanded to
Calvary Presbytery for correction.
The following statement was adopted by the commission as the minute ex
planatory: ‘ ‘The Presbytery has not demonstrated that the use of the 'extraordi
nary clause' was justified by either extraordinary circumstances or proven
extraordinary gifts of the m an.”
A motion was made, seconded, and carried that the Sub-committee on Judicial
Business be directed to prepare an answer to the question, “ What is the proper
use of the extraordinary clause?", and report to the Ninth General Assembly
A motion was made, seconded, and carried to report these actions to the
complainants and to the respondent.
The minutes were perfected and approved. A motion was made, seconded, and
carried to approve the report as a whole.
A motion was made, seconded, and carried that the commission adjourn.
The meeting was adjourned with prayer.
Respectfully submitted,
