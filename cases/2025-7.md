**CASE 2023-05**

**MS. EMILY HYLAND**

**v.**

**CHICAGO METRO PRESBYTERY**

#### DECISION ON COMPLAINT

**October 17, 2024**

**A minute explanatory** : The vote on the following decision was 12-12-0;
therefore, the decision of the Presbytery stands by virtue of an equally divided
Standing Judicial Commission.

**CASE SUMMARY**

Complainant was a church employee who was terminated by the Pastor of the
church. She subsequently began, but did not complete, the _BCO_ 43 complaint
process with her Session and filed employment discrimination and wage
claims with two agencies of the State and subsequently filed charges against
the Pastor and the Associate Pastor of the church. Presbytery received those
charges as accusations and investigated in accordance with _BCO_ 31-2,
declining to indict the accused after failing to raise a strong presumption of
guilt. Complainant then lodged a complaint against Presbytery for its decision
not to indict, which complaint Presbytery then denied for being out of order
due to lack of standing. Complainant then carried her complaint to the General
Assembly.

The case was received by the SJC and assigned to a Panel. That Panel
determined that the Complaint was Judicially Out of Order due to _OMSJC_ 10.5
defects, but at its March (2024) Stated Meeting the SJC appointed a new Panel
to further consider the matter ( _OMSJC_ 10.6). The second Panel conducted a
full hearing on April 5, 2024 and reached the same conclusion as the original
Panel. This Complaint is Judicially Out of Order.
