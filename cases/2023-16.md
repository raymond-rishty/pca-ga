#### DECISION ON COMPLAINT

**JULY 22, 2024**

**I. SUMMARY OF THE FACTS**

10/25/22 SJC ruled in case 2022-04 that HP erred in both matters brought
before it by Arden Presbyterian Church's ("APC") previous
pastor, stating, “Presbytery is debarred from prosecuting any of
the allegations embraced by the subject matter of this case.”

11/13/22 RE Brett Callaway wrote the APC Session expressing concern
over an APC Session proposal to send a letter to the Highlands
Presbytery to “affirm the Christian character of Kevin, Jill, and
Delaney Martin.”

11/17/22 Despite RE Callaway's concerns, APC Session approved the
sending of the letter to HP.

12/5/22 APC Session sent a letter to HP stating:

As part of our shepherding responsibility, we desire to affirm
the Christian character of Kevin, Jill, and Delaney Martin, and
further, affirm the position we took in the summer of 2020
when we unanimously referred their concerns to Highlands
Presbytery for Biblical resolution. As shepherds, our hearts
are distressed because of the pain and anguish the Martin
family is enduring due to the fact that their concerns were not
resolved, either by a court finding orb ya reconciliation. We
pray that ... God will ... be a source of healing for the
resolution they were denied.

12/8/22 RE Brett Callaway of the APC Session filed a complaint to the
APC Session regarding the APC Session decision to send a letter
to HP to “affirm” the Martins’ “Christian character.”

1/19/23 APC Session denied RE Callaway’s complaint. The Session failed
to specify a reason for the denial.

1/25/23 RE Callaway filed a “Notice of Dissent Against an Action of
Arden Presbyterian Church Taken on January 19, 2023” with the
APC.

2/16/23 APC Session refused to record RE Callaway's Dissent and spread
it upon it minutes on the grounds that it “was found to include
intemperate language.” The Session failed to specify what
language in the Dissent it found intemperate.

2/25/23 At its stated meeting, HP received the letter from the APC Session
commending the Martins and received it into its record.

3/27/23 TEs Bulkeley and Gillikin filed a “Dissent Against An Action of
Highlands Presbytery (PCA) on February 25, 2023” (the
"Dissent") for receiving the APC Session letter into its record.

5/2/23 HP denied the Dissent on the grounds that it was found “to not be
couched in temperate language, per _BCO_ 45–5, and therefore not

[to] be recorded in the minutes of Highlands Presbytery.” Nothing
in the minutes indicates what language in the Dissent was
determined to be phrased in intemperate language. HP also
removed TE Gillikin’s name from the Dissent because he was not
present at the February 25 meeting of HP where the letter was
received, and therefore he was not able to join in the Dissent.

5/18/23 TE Bulkeley, TE Gillikin, RE Callaway, and RE Conley Brown
filed a complaint with HP for not recording the Dissent per _BCO_
45-5.

8/5/23 HP denied the Complaint stating:

(1) No, Presbytery did not err in not recording the dissent
because it found the Dissent to “not be couched in temperate
language” ( _BCO_ 45–5). (2) No, Presbytery did not err in not
recording the reasons for finding the Dissent intemperate,
because (a) the _BCO_ nowhere requires such recording of
reasons, and (b) to record such reasons would amount to
recording the intemperate dissent in the minutes in violation
of _BCO_ 45–5.

HP also removed RE Brown from the Complaint since he was not
a commissioner at the meeting in which the action complained of
took place.

8/23/23 TEs Bulkeley and Gillikin and RE Callaway carried their
Complaint to the SJC.

3/29/24 The SJC Panel conducted a hearing via Zoom technology. RE
John Pickering chaired the hearing. TE Art Sartorius, RE Steve
Dowling, and TE Mike Ross served on the panel. RE Mel Duncan
was unable to participate. For the Complainants, TE Dominic
Acquila served as spokesman and presented their arguments. TE
Craig Bulkeley and TE Skip Gillikin were present. The
Respondents for HP were TE David Hina and TE Patrick Lafferty.
RE Brett Callaway and RE Conley Brown were also in attendance.
