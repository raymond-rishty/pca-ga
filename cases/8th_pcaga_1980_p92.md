# Case p92 — et al. v. Mid-Atlantic Presbytery

**Court:** CJB  ·  **Assembly:** 8th (1980)  ·  **Disposition:** —  ·  **Vote:** 8-43  ·  **BCO cited:**  44-4 22-4 

*Source: [ga08_1980 pp. 92–93](../markdown/ga08_1980.md)*

---

8-53 Change in Debate Times
A motion was made and seconded to increase the time for extensions of debate
during the remainder of the Assembly to ten-minute increments, the individual’s time
remaining three minutes. On motion this was amended to apply only to the report of the
Committee of Commissioners on Interchurch Relations. The amended motion was then
adopted.
8-54 Change in Docket
A motion was made and seconded to proceed to the morning’s worship service at
this time, and to proceed with the Committee of Commissioners on Interchurch Rela
tions at 1:30 p.m. This motion was amended to allow TE Mitchell Hall, Jr. the privilege
of addressing the Assembly briefly at 1:30 p.m ., and then to proceed with the report. The
motion as amended was then adopted.
8-55 Worship Service and Recess
The Assembly was led in the morning worship by the Permanent Committee on
Mission to the World. RE L. B. Austin III, chairman of the Committee, opened the
service, and TE R. Thomas Cheely led in prayer.
TE David M. Bryan preached the sermon, and closed the service with prayer.
MINUTES — WEDNESDAY AFTERNOON
June 18, 1980
Seventh Session
8-56 Assembly Reconvened
The Assembly reconvened at 1:30 p.m. with prayer by RE Kenneth L. Ryskamp.
8-57 Mission Work in Atlanta
TE William T. Iverson introduced TE Mitchell Hall, Jr., who spoke to the Assembly concerning the black mission work in Atlanta.
8-58 Committee of Commissioners on Interchurch Relations (continued)
Chairman Don K. Clements continued the report of the Committee of Commissioners on Interchurch Relations.
Recommendations 5-9 were adopted at this time. See § 8-43; p. 79, for the text.
8-59 Korean American Presbyterian Church Visitors
TE Dwight Linton introduced the moderator and clerk of the Korean American
Presbyterian Church, Teaching Elders Hyun Tal Yi and Limuel Chang. Moderator Yi
addressed the Assembly briefly.
8-60 Judicial Commission to Adjudicate Case No. 4
TE Robert C. Cannada, Jr. led the Assembly in prayer and presented the report of
the Judicial Commission to adjudicate Case No 4: Complaint of RE James H. Campbell,
et al. Against Mid-Atlantic Presbytery. The Assembly voted to adopt the recommendation of the Commission that the Minutes of the Commission be approved and accepted as
the action and judgment of the Eighth General Assembly in this case.
MINUTES OF THE JUDICIAL COMMISSION APPOINTED BY
THE EIGHTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY TO ADJUDICATE THE COMPLAINT
OF RULING ELDER JAMES H. CAMPBELL, ET AL AGAINST
THE PRESBYTERY OF MID-ATLANTIC
The Commission convened pursuant to the call of the Convener, W. J. Stanway,
June 16, 1980 at the Savannah Civic Center, Savannah, Georgia at 8:45 p.m.
A Quorum was present with the following in attendance:
Ruling Elders Teaching Elders
EarlJaggers Red Whited R. C. Cannada Andrew Silman Allan Story
John Spencer Mel Channell Lynn Downing W. J. Stanway Leonard Van Horn

Present too were the respondents of the Presbytery of Mid-Atlantic, Ruling Elder
Dan Ice and Teaching Elder Harry Miller, and Teaching Elder Robert C. Schoof. The
complainant, Ruling Elder James H. Campbell, was not present.
The meeting was opened with prayer by the Convener. On motion duly made and
seconded, Teaching Elder R. C. Cannada was elected Chairman. On motion duly made
and seconded, Teaching Elder Andrew Silman was elected Secretary.
A brief overview of the case was presented by Respondent Harry Miller. Copies of a
written record of the substance of the complaint were presented to each Commissioner.
The Chairman reminded the Commission of Book of Church Order, Chapter 33-12,
and portions of Chapters 43-45.
Upon motion duly made and seconded, the Commission recessed until 12:45 p .m .,
June 17, 1980 to allow the complaintant time to appear.
The Commission reconvened at 12:45 p.m ., June 17, 1980 in the Savannah Civic
Center, Savannah, Georgia.
A quorum was declared present with the following in attendance:
Ruling Elders Teaching Elders
William F. Joseph R. C. Cannada
Earl Jaggers Lynn Downing (alt.)
John Spencer Jack Eubank
Red Whited Andrew Silman
Mel Channell W. J. Stanway
Allan Story
Leonard Van Hom
Present too were Ruling Elder Daniel B. Ice and Teaching Elder Harry Miller,
respondents for the Presbytery of Mid-Atlantic, and Teaching Elder Robert C. Schoof.
The complainant, Ruling Elder James H. Campbell, was not present.
The meeting was opened with prayer by the Chairman. W. J. Stanway was elected
temporary Clerk.
The Chairman called the attention of the Commission to BCO 44-4 and 43-10.
The following motion was duly made and seconded: “ In the light of Paragraph 44-4
and Paragraph 43-10, and in the light of the absence of the complaintant or his counsel to
pursue his complaint, the Commission recommends that the Assembly take cognizance
of these facts and allow the judgment of the Presbytery of Mid-Atlantic to stand, this
action to be effective as of the end of the second day of General Assembly. ’ ’
A motion was duly made, seconded and approved to table the previous motion.
A motion to recess and reconvene immediately after the program presentation on
Wednesday morning, June 18, 1980 was seconded and approved.
The meeting was recessed with prayer by Ruling Elder Earl Jaggers.
The Commission reconvened on Wednesday, June 18 at 9:25 a.m. in the Savannah
Civic Center, Savannah, Georgia with prayer by Andrew Silman.
The following were present:
