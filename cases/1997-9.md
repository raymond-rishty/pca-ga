# Case 1997-09 — Judicial Case 97-9

**Court:** SJC  ·  **Assembly:** 29th (2001)  ·  **Disposition:** —  ·  **BCO cited:**  39-3 30-4 42-9 36-5 

*Source: [ga29_2001 pp. 122–124](../markdown/ga29_2001.md)*

---

Case 97-9 was in error.

Issue 5. “Did central carolina pres byte rye rr by affirming the decision of christ
Covenant Church Session’s infliction upon Dr. Robert M. Shive the highest censure
based on the record of the Case?”

Judgment 5. “Yes. central carolina presbytery erred in affirming and the session of
christ covenant church erred in going straight to the highest censure of
excommunication in light of the lack of compelling evidence in the record of the case
to reflect that Dr. Shive is contumacious or impenitent. See BCO 30-4 and 33-3.
Pursuant to BCO 42-9, we reverse that portion of the judgment by the session of christ
covenant church inflicting the censure of excommunication on Dr. Shive, and we
remand the matter back to the session of christ covenant church for it to impose the
censure of indefinite suspension from the sacraments upon Dr. Robert M. Shive in
accordance with BCO 36-5, and with the instruction that, until satisfactory evidence of
repentance is given to the session of christ covenant Church, to impose such
conditions concerning Dr. Shive’s involvement in the life of christ covenant church as
the Session may find.” (Emphasis added).

In this judgment in Case 97-9 there is absolutely no language that gives any
indication that the four guilty charges against Dr. Shive were reversed or remanded.
Only “that portion of the judgment by the session of christ covenant church inflicting
the censure of excommunication on Dr. Shive” was remanded to impose the censure of
“indefinite suspension from the Sacraments upon Dr. Robert M. Shive”. We think the
language is clear and unambiguous that the verdicts of guilty were not changed nor
reversed and the findings of guilt of Dr. Shive remained in force. Only “that portion of
the judgment” dealing with censure was remanded. The penalty was reduced from “the
censure of excommunication on Dr. Shive” to the censure of “indefinite suspension
from the Sacraments upon Dr. Shive” . There is no language in the judgment that affects
any other part of the decision by christ covenant Church. We conclude that the sin and
guilt were not overturned by the decision in SJC Case 97-9.
That the sin and guilt were not overturned is further evidenced by the
recommendation of indefinite suspension from the l ord’s Supper as well as by further
instructions “that, until satisfactory evidence of repentance is given to the session of
Christ Covenant Church” that the Session has the right to impose any further
“conditions concerning Dr. Shive’s involvement in the life of christ covenant church
as the Session may find.”

1. SJC Manual 11.11 (2) II defines “a statement of the Issues” as a concise
framing by the court of the specific issues being judged in the case.
2. SJC Manual 11.11 (2) III defines “the Judgm ent” as a definite conclusion
rendered on specific issues being judged.
3. SJC Manual 11.11 (2) IV defines “the reasoning and opinion of the Court” as
the explanation by the court of the resolution of disputed facts and reasons for
the judgment rendered.
4. it thus seems t oust hat the heart and decisive portion of any judicial decision is:
its “Statement of the Issues” and “the Judgm ent.”

the summary of the facts is defined as “a presentation in chronological order of
the relevant history and facts that bear on the case,” and the reasoning and opinion of
the Court simply deals with certain facts and factors as they are applied to the principles
set forth in the Book of Church Order and sjc Manual. We therefore suggest that after
looking at the whole case, a person should center on the heart of the case which is “a
statement of the Issues” and “the Judgm ent.” we think the failure of the Appellant, Dr.
Robert M. Shive, to properly interpret the decision in SJC Case 97-9 is a failure to
center on the clear and unambiguous language stating these specific issues being judged
in the case and the definite conclusions rendered on the specific issues. Dr. Shive
interpreted the SJC decision in Case 97-9 that the “decision clearly challenged and
struck down the allegations” of the session and reversed the verdicts of guilt on all four
charges. (ROC 56-57). On the other hand, the discipline commission of the session of
christ covenant church stated its interpretation of the decision, as follows:

“. . . In so doing we are working with the understanding that General Assembly,
based on the record of the Case, has altered the censure of excommunication to
indefinite suspension. Neither Presbytery nor General Assembly have
overturned any of the Session’s judgments with respect to your guilt. . ."
(Emphasis added).

We think Dr. Shive misinterpreted the SJC decision in Case 97-9 and the
discipline commission of the session properly interpreted it. This is supported by the
interpretation given by Dr. paul gilchrist in his letter of april 16, 1998 (ROC 61) in
which he stated the following, to-wit:

“. . . first the heart of the decision is in II. statement of the issues
and III. judgment in the case . . . the SJC did not reverse the charges
of sin and the finding of guilt against Dr. shive .. . ”

t woof the panel members on sjc case 97-9 confirmed that interpretation. We concur.

in the light of these facts and factors, we suggest we look at Issues 4 and 5 and
Judgments 4 and 5 in SJC 97-9.
