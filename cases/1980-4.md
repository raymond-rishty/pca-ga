Case 5: Complaint of Teaching Elder Carl W. Bogue et al Against the Presbytery of the Ascension
March 3, 1980
The Rev. Morton H. Smith, Stated Clerk
General Assembly, Presbyterian Church in America
P.O. Box 312
Brevard, N.C. 28712

Whereas, in the action of the Presbytery of the Ascension of the Presbyterian Church in America, at the
stated meeting February 26, 1980, in Greensburg, Pennsylvania, respecting the sustaining of the trials for
ordination and the approving of the call to a campus ministry of Mr. Sam Serio, which action is contrary to The
Westminster Confession of Faith and the Book of Church Order, the undersigned hereby complain to The
General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America of this action of the Presbytery of the Ascension.
There are two complaints which here follow, along with the reasons supporting the complaints.
Complaint #1
The trials for ordination of Mr. Sam Serio were sustained by the Presbytery of the Ascension which
action was contrary to a fundamental teaching of the Westminster Confession of Faith.
History: Under examination before the Candidates for Church Vocations Committee and before the
whole Presbytery Mr. Serio affirmed his belief that none of the miraculous gifts had ceased since the Apostolic
age. With specific regard to the miraculous gift of speaking in an “ unknown tongue” Mr. Serio affirmed its
contemporary practice, which when miraculously interpreted by another person, communicated knowledge
from God. Specifically, the origin of such knowledge from God was said to be God the Holy Spirit speaking
through the individual in an unknown tongue. Neither Mr. Serio nor any member of Presbytery indicated that
the candidate’s answers were to be construed as other than specified in this summary paraphrase. Since such
knowledge is here affirmed as being directly given by the Holy Spirit through the miraculous gift of speaking a
language unknown and never studied by the speaker and through the corresponding miraculous gift of
interpretation, the complainants understand such a phenomenon as direct revelation from God. This conclusion
was not disputed, even though the candidate was sustained.
Reasons for the Complaint:
(1) Such a view is in direct conflict with the Scriptural position of our Confession of Faith in 1:1
which declares such revelation to be commited “ wholly unto writing" for the reason that
"those former ways of God's revealing His will unto His people being now ceased."
(2) Such a view is in direct conflict with the Scriptural position of our Confession of Faith
summarized in 1:6 concerning the sufficiency of Scripture, "unto which nothing at any time is
to be added, whether by new revelations of the Spirit or traditions of men. ’’
(3) such a view is indirect conflict with the Book of Church Order, Section 7-1, which says in
part: “ Such officers and gifts related to new revelation have no successors since God
completed His revelation at the conclusion of the Apostolic Age."
(4) Such a view is in direct conflict with the non-constitutional but approved "Pastoral Letter” of
the Second General Assembly (Minutes, pp. 171-175):
“ The General Assembly suggests however, that: 1. Any view of the tongues as
experienced in our time which conceives of it as an experience by which revelation is
received from God is contrary to the finalized character of revelation in Scriptures” (p.
173).
“ Such miracles were signs by which God communicated divine truth or confirmed that
the speaker indeed spoke from God. These miracles related to revelation have ceased,
since revelation was completed with the closing of the Canon in the New Testament
era” (pp. 173-174).
“ ... The General Assembly would recommend:..................................................................
3. Careful training, examination and selection of officers and others in the place of
teaching and leadership in the church to assure a consistency of commitment to
Scripture and the Reformed faith and to the health and unity of the church;
4 . Preaching and teaching of the Doctrine of the Holy Spirit and His work as taught in
Scripture and the Standaids of the church . . . "(pp. 174- 175).
( 5 ) Such an exception to the Confession is open to grave abuse, not the least of which is a
defection from the doctrines of the canon and sola Scriptura.
Complaint # 2
The approval of a call to Mr. sam se riot oa campus ministry was contrary to the Book of Church Order
provisions for such calls.
Reasons for the complaint:
(1) such approval is contrary to the Book of Church Order, Section 21-1, that: “ A proper call
must be written and in the hands of the Presbytery prior to being acted upon by a Presbytery. It
must include . . . assurance that the definite work will afford the liberty to proclaim and
practice fully and freely the whole counsel of God, as contained in the Scriptures and
understood in the Westminster Confession of Faith such written assurance wa snot in the
call; thus it was not in proper order.
( 2 ) Section 21-1 of the Book of Church Order also states: “ It shall be in accord with the Book of Church Order, Chapter 8. ” In Section 8 of Chapter 8 a teaching elder ins uc ba ministry is

“ to make full proof of his ministry by disseminating the Gospel for the edification of the
Church. ’' Such proof was not and could not be made since our Church is not present in that
area to be edified, nor is the planting of such a Church the goal of this ministry. Rather Mr.
Serio will be under a board made up of leaders of The American Baptist Church, the
Assemblies of God Church, and the Church of God. There is no consistent Reformed witness
of any kind with which Mr. Serio would have any relationship.
(3) Such a call does not conform to the non-constitutional but approved "Manual for Campus
Ministries, Presbyterian Church in America” of the Seventh General Assembly (Minutes, pp.
205-218). Such ministers, even if working with para-church organizations, are to be "under
the guidance and supervision” of PCA Churches or Presbyteries, or the GA MUS Subcommittee on Campus Ministries (pp. 209, 214). “ The ministry is a work of the Presbyterian Church in America” (p. 211), and "the calling and sending body is the commissioning,
ordaining, or installing body, as the case may require” (p. 214). Since the calling body is
utterly non-Presbyterian, it could hardly ordain and install a PCA Teaching Elder.
The undersigned complainants do hereby complain against the action of the Presbytery of the Ascension
and ask that the General Assembly review this matter and declare the action of the February 26, 1980, meeting
of the Presbytery of the Ascension in sustaining the trials for ordination and approving the call of Mr. Sam Serio
to be null and void.
For the cause of Christ and His Kingdom,
Carl W. Bogue, Teaching Elder
Gary Spencer, Ruling Elder
David G. McKay, Teaching Elder

Resolution 1: From RE G. O. Runnels and TE John S. Ragland

Whereas, on several preceding General Assemblies the Presbyterian Church in America affirmed in
faithful obedience to the Lord and Sovereign our Saviour Jesus Christ the 6th commandment's prohibition
against abortion on demand as presently practiced in this country resulted in the loss of more than 6,000,000
human lives in the past decade; and

Whereas, our Confessional subordinate standards make it very clear that God’s commandments com
mand specific things for God's people to doa swell a sn otto do; and

Whereas, our Larger Catechism in question 135, "What are the duties required in the sixth command
ment?" says, among other things, "The duties required in the sixth commandment are, all careful studies and
lawful endeavours, to preserve the life of ourselves and others by . . . comforting and succouring the distressed,
and protecting and defending the innocent."

Whereas, the Committee on Abortion report to the Sixth General Assembly recommended that we remind
all Christians of their duty to show compassionate love and understanding to families in distress as a result of
pregnancies, and to offer to these families sympathetic counsel and help for physical needs where required.
This duty is especially incumbent upon us as we minister to the person contemplating abortion.

Be It Therefore Resolved, that the Eighth General Assembly of the Presbyterian Church in America
recommends to its churches that they make a concerted effort to develop positive ministries to those
considering abortions such as evangelism, Christian nurturing, Christian counseling, provision of financial
and material needs to the mother and her baby, aid with obtaining medical service, aid with adoption when
indicated, and Christian homes (homes for unwed mothers and Christian families who will share their home
with the pregnant woman in need of a place t olive ora home away from their immediate family).

And That It Be Further Resolved, that this resolution be placed in the Messenger, and in a letter to all
the churches of the Presbyterian Church in America

each ballot. If, after this procedure is carried out to the end, no one receives

final vote.

commissioners present and on the floor of the Assembly shall be eligible to

The fact that 41 percent of our missionaries were showing year-end deficits was

factors appear to be inflation and devaluation of the dollar.

Three new cooperative agreements are reported to the Eighth General Assembly. One of these, with Overseas Crusades, Incorporated, was reported to have
theological language which was not in keeping with our standards. Accord
ingly, the Committee of Commissioners reviewed these agreements, with
particular emphasis upon the statement of belief of Overseas Crusades, Inc.
3 . Change of Policy Regarding Short-Term Missionaries.
Judging from the response of young people at Urbana and that which is being
found across the Denomination, a large number of young people would be
interested in a short-term assignment with Mission to the World. The interest
comes from both within and without the Denomination. To utilize this potential
would require changes in the Mission to the World “ Missions Manual". The
committee spent considerable time considering this matter.

Question 1. Does an invitation from the General Assembly to other ecclesiastical
bodies require a three-fourths vote of the presbyteries in order to be issued, on the
assumption that an invitation presupposes receipt of the body upon acceptance of the
invitation?
Response 1. The BCO does not speak to the issue of extending an invitation to other
ecclesiastical bodies but it does speak very clearly to receiving other ecclesiastical
bodies. It is the opinion of this sub-committee that the invitation could be extended
by the General Assembly without presbytery consent, but that in doing so the
General Assembly should remind those ecclesiastical bodies that their reception

would be contingent upon the consent of three-fourths of the presbyteries of the PCA
(BCO 15-6).
Question 2. What is the constitutional meaning of the phrase "the doctrine of the
purity of the church?"
Response 2. In responding to the question, the sub-committee has in mind the
statement in the fifth line of the “ Letter’’, which reads, " the doctrine of the purity of
the visible Church” . Such language is not found in the standards, but concern for the
purity of the visible Church is clearly expressed in Westminster Confession of Faith
25:4, 5, the “ Preface to the Book of Church Order” , 11(4), and Book of Church Order 2-1, 2-2, 22-5(6), and 25-5.

Report of the Judicial Commission appointed by the Eighth General Assembly
to Adjudicate the Complaint of Carl W. Bogue et al,
as members of the Presbytery of the Ascension, Complainants
vs.
The Presbytery of the Ascension, Respondents
Statement of Facts
The pertinent facts which are not disputed are as follows:
1. On February 26, 1980, the Presbytery of the Ascension at a stated meeting
sustained the trials for ordination and approved the call to a campus ministry of
Mr. Sam Serio.
2. On March 3, 1980, teaching elder Carl W. Bogue et al, complained against
these actions of the Presbytery in writing, fulfilling the conditions of the BCO
44-4 and 43-4 that a complaint “ may be initiated if filed within fifteen (15) days
following the meeting of the court, with reasons supporting.”
3. The call of Mr. Serio by the Koinonia Christian Fellowship did not include in
writing an assurance that his work would afford “ the liberty to proclaim and
practice fully and freely the whole counsel of God, as contained in the Scrip-

tures and understood in the WCF," as required by the BCO 21-1.
4. The complainants and respondents indicated substantial agreement that in his
trial for ordination before the Presbytery, while indicating that he himself was
not practicing the matter under discussion, Mr. Serio:

- **a)** affirmed his belief that none of the gifts of the Spirit have ceased since the apostolic age.
- **b)** affirmed his belief that currently the gift of speaking in an unknown tongue, when interpreted by the gift of interpretation, communicates revelation from God, although the nature of the revelation involved was not clearly defined by Mr. Serio.

Statement of the Major Issue in Dispute
The major issue in dispute was whether or not Mr. Serio's statement concerning the
continuation of all the gifts of the Spirit, and the continuation of “ revelation" from God
were of such a nature that they substantially contradicted the fundamental teaching of the
WCF and BCO concerning the cessation of the extraordinary gifts of the Spirit, of gifts
related to revelation, and of the finalizing of revelation in Scripture.
Explanatory Opinion
1. The respondents concede as an error of oversight rather than an error of intent
that the Presbytery failed to conform to the requirements of the BCO 21-1
referred to above. This acknowledgment has the effect of providing specific
grounds for sustaining count 2 of the complaint.
2. The Commission judges that the Presbytery failed in allowing expressions
about continuing revelation by Mr. Serio to remain unclarified in the examination, so that the fundamental teaching of the WCF and the BCO concerning the
sufficiency and finality of revelation in Scripture was not adequately protected.
The Commission judges that simply affirming that the canon is closed, and that
supposed new revelations from God add nothing to the deposit of truth already
found in Scripture does not cover all the negations concerning continuing
revelation from God found in the WCF I, 1 ,6 , and the BCO 7-1. These
statements of the standards also negate the idea that any extraordinary ways still
continue in addition to Scripture as ways by which God verbally uncovers His
will to His people. According to the testimony of complainants and respon
dents, Mr. Serio affirmed belief in extraordinary revelation by tongues in
terpreted, while at the same time affirming that such revelation from God was
not to be added to the canon of Scripture.

Judgement of the Commission.
1. In balloting according to BCO 45, the Commission voted as follows:
To sustain the complaint: 10
Not to sustain the complaint: 0
To sustain the complaint in part: 5
of those voting to sustain the complaint in part,
0 indicated they were sustaining count 1, and
5 indicated they were sustaining count 2.
The combined result of the balloting was as follows:
To sustain count # 1 of the complaint: 10 yes, 5 no
To sustain count # 2 of the complaint: 15 yes, 0 no
2. The Commission remands this matter to the Presbytery of the Ascension to
secure-a proper call that conforms with the BCO 21-4.
3. The Commission remands this matter to the Presbytery of the Ascension to
re-examine Mr. Serio.
