## CASE 99-2

## COMPLAINT OF TE AURELIANO TAN VS SOUTH TEXAS PRESBYTERY

The Complaint of TE Tan was declared administratively out of order, per *SJC Manual* 10.1(a) *SJC Manual* 19.10(b) and *BCO* 43.3, as not timely filed. The action complained against occurred at the July 30-31 meeting of STP. The Complaint, though dated and sent by Fax on August 28, was not mailed until September 4, 1999; exceeding the 30-day period specified in BCO 43-3.

Further, the Complaint was not received by the Stated Clerk of General Assembly and Presbytery until September 7, 1999 by regular U S Mail. *SJC Manual* requires that “filings shall not be timely unless the documents are received in the office of the Stated Clerk within the time fixed for such filing, except that papers shall be deemed filed on the date of mailing if certified, registered or express mail of the United States Postal Service or any private service where verifying receipt is utilized. Neither facsimiles nor E-mail will be allowed for purposes of filing.” (*SJC Manual* 19.10).
