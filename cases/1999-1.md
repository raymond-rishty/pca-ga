I. Background Information
1. At its March 1999 stated meeting, Western Carolina Presbytery asked Tennessee
Valley Presbytery (TVP) to investigate allegations that “Cedar Springs
Presbyterian Church, Knoxville, Tennessee, has had women preachers to fill its
pulpits.” The alleged actions took place at successive Sunday evening worship
services on August 16 and 23, 1998.
2. At its spring 1999 stated meeting, TVP, having been advised about Western
Carolina Presbytery’s request and concern, discussed the matter. There was
further discussion on this matter at TVP’s summer 1999 stated meeting (see text
of overture #1 adopted by TVP on 10/12/99). the stated clerk oft vp
formally acknowledged receipt of western Carolina Presbytery’s
communication by a letter dated August 3, 1999.
3. It was reported to TVP at its fall meeting (October 12, 1999) that three teaching
elders from the presbytery “met with TE John Wood, moderator of the cedar
Springs Session, to thoroughly discuss this decision” [i.e., “the decision of the
session of cedar springs pct o allow a woman on staff, under their authority
and guidance, to speak from the pulpit on two Sunday evenings, to explain her
biblical approach to Christian Education”].
4. Western Carolina Presbytery (on July 20, 1999), Calvary Presbytery (on July 24,
1999) and Ascension Presbytery (on July 31, 1999) adopted resolutions
requesting the General Assembly to assume original jurisdiction over a teaching
elder whom they allege allowed women to fill the pulpit in a PCA church. They
were invoking the provisions of b co 34-1, which deals with process against a
minister.
5. At its fall stated meeting (October 12,1999), TVP discussed the matter again and
adopted the following resolutions:

#### II. Statement of the Issue

Is this matter properly before the General Assembly?

#### III. Judgment of the Case

No. This matter is not properly before the General Assembly.

## IV. Reasoning and Opinion

The matter is not administratively in order and not properly before the General Assembly:

1. The Session has jurisdiction over worship in the local church.

The wording of the resolutions adopted by the three Presbyteries requested the General Assembly to assume original jurisdiction over a teaching elder against whom allegations of public scandal were raised. The Presbyteries allege that the teaching elder “violated the Biblical teaching on worship in having a woman filling the pulpit, preach, give instruction and exhort the congregation from the Word of God.” However, the conduct of worship services in the local church is under the jurisdiction of the Session and under its responsibility (BCO 12-5 [e]). The teaching elder cannot be held liable for actions that are properly the responsibility of the Session. If the alleged incidents of women preaching from the pulpit did occur, the matter must be addressed to the Session.

2. The Presbytery did not refuse to act.

BCO 34-1 deals with process against a minister. When a minister is accused of doctrinal error or having engaged in matters perceived to lead to public scandal, then his presbytery should investigate the allegations and take appropriate action resulting from the investigation (BCO 31-2). However, if his presbytery refuses to act on the allegations, then at least two presbyteries may request the General Assembly to assume original jurisdiction over the man.

However, in TVP’s judgment, the issue before them was not the actions of a teaching elder, but the actions of a Session, hence BCO 34-1 was not applicable. As stated above, the conduct of worship is under the jurisdiction of the Session and TVP dealt with the matter in light of the BCO 12-5 [e].

But in any case, it is clear that TVP did act on the allegations when, at its fall stated meeting on October 12, 1999, TVP adopted two resolutions on this matter. Since there was no refusal to act the resolutions of the three Presbyteries are administratively out of order and not properly before the General Assembly.

## ATTACHMENT 1

Overtures adopted by the TVP at its Fall Stated Meeting on October 12, 1999, at Lookout Mountain PC:

Overture #1

Whereas, the TVP has discussed at its Spring Stated Meeting, 1999, and its Summer Stated Meeting, 1999, the decision of the Session of Cedar Springs PC to allow a woman on staff, under their authority and guidance, to speak from their pulpit on two Sunday evenings, to explain her biblical approach to Christian Education; and

Whereas, the Session of Cedar Springs PC did not do this for the purpose of promoting women preachers in the PCA; and

Whereas, the TVP has met in executive session during its Summer Stated Meeting of 1999 to discuss this issue more fully; and

Whereas, other elders and presbyteries of the PCA have communicated to the TVP and its members their concerns about this decision of the Cedar Spring PC Session; and

Whereas, TEs Cal Boroughs, Randy Nabors, and Render Caines have met with TE John Wood, moderator of the Cedar Springs Session, to thoroughly discuss this decision;

Therefore Be It Resolved that the Sessions of St Elmo PC, New City Fellowship and Covenant (Chattanooga) PC request that the TVP meeting at its Fall Stated Meeting of 1999 find that the Session of Cedar Springs PC

- is in conformity with the standards of the PCA
- has not acted in any way injurious to the testimony of Christ or His church
- is in submission to their fellow elders, and are eager to maintain the peace and purity of the church; and

Be It Further Resolved, that this action of the TVP be communicated to the Stated Clerk of the General Assembly.

## Overture #2

Whereas, the Scriptures enjoin us to “keep living by the same standards to which we have attained” (Philip 3:16); and

Whereas, we affirm that the Bible “is the one rule to direct us how we may glorify and enjoy Him” (WSC, 2); and

Whereas, the Scriptures emphatically teach: “Let a woman quietly receive instruction with entire submissiveness. But I do not allow a woman to teach or exercise authority over a man, but to remain quiet” (I Tim. 2:11-12); and

Whereas, the Bible also definitely says, “Let the women keep silent in the churches; for they are not permitted to speak, but let them subject themselves, just as the Law also says. And if they desire to learn anything, let them ask their own husbands at home; for it is improper for a woman to speak in church” (I Cor 14:34-35J); and

Whereas, our constitutional documents take note of the care that should be taken in the public reading and preaching of the Word (WLC, 156, 158; BCO 50:1-2; 53), and

Whereas, these are not minor issues of conscience but effect our presbytery’s public witness among the PCA and even in the whole world;

Therefore Be It Resolved, that the session of Mountain View PC overtures the TVP to openly and plainly declare that it is not proper for women to preach and teach in the church’s corporate worship;

and

Be It Further Resolved, that a copy of this overture be forwarded to the Stated Clerk of the PCA.

## CASE 99-2

## COMPLAINT OF TE AURELIANO TAN VS SOUTH TEXAS PRESBYTERY

The Complaint of TE Tan was declared administratively out of order, per *SJC Manual* 10.1(a) *SJC Manual* 19.10(b) and *BCO* 43.3, as not timely filed. The action complained against occurred at the July 30-31 meeting of STP. The Complaint, though dated and sent by Fax on August 28, was not mailed until September 4, 1999; exceeding the 30-day period specified in BCO 43-3.

Further, the Complaint was not received by the Stated Clerk of General Assembly and Presbytery until September 7, 1999 by regular U S Mail. *SJC Manual* requires that “filings shall not be timely unless the documents are received in the office of the Stated Clerk within the time fixed for such filing, except that papers shall be deemed filed on the date of mailing if certified, registered or express mail of the United States Postal Service or any private service where verifying receipt is utilized. Neither facsimiles nor E-mail will be allowed for purposes of filing.” (*SJC Manual* 19.10).
