IV. RECOMMENDATIONS RE: CASE 97-5.
In Case 97-5 - Mt. Carmel Session vs. New Jersey Presbytery, the required 1/3
of commission members dissenting (9) have filed a minority report and as a result the
Case has been referred to the 26th General Assembly for action as provided for in BCO
15.5, as discussed above.
the chairman reviewed the provisions oft hes jc manual in cases where
the required 1/3 of commission members dissenting file a minority report and a
case is referred to the General Assembly. Case 97-5 is such a case.
RE John White, TE Paul Fowler, and TE Dominic Aquila presented the
#### Majority Opinion on Case 97-5. TE David Hall and RE Dale Peacock presented
the Minority Opinion. RE Jack Williamson presented the Majority Response.
on a point of order, the Moderator ruled that commissioners who were
members of NJ Presbytery at the time this case arose would not be allowed to
vote based on BCO 39-2.
The Minority Report (pp. 117-123) was defeated. The Majority Report
was adopted.
Several commissioners requested that their affirmative votes on the
Minority Report and negative votes on the Majority Report be recorded (see
below at 26-30, p. 159).
RE John White led the Assembly in prayer. The Assembly joined in
singing “I Sing the Mighty Power of God.”
