99-5
APPEAL OF LAWRENCE ROUNTREE
VS.
COVENANT PRESBYTERY

#### I. SUMMARY OF THE FACTS

In May, 1992, Covenant Presbytery licensed Lawrence Rountree and approved him as student supply at the First Presbyterian Church, Indianola, MS. He was approved for ordination and installation as pastor of First Church, Indianola, at presbytery’s October 1992 stated meeting.

In May, 1993, Presbytery dissolved the pastoral relationship between TE Rountree and First Church.

At its October 1994 stated meeting Presbytery approved TE Rountree remaining on its rolls without call and outside its geographical bounds; TE Rountree having moved to Austin, TX.

In October, 1995, and February, 1997, TE Rountree reported to Presbytery on his general situation as minister without call. In April, 1997, TE Rountree communicated with Presbytery’s Church Care Committee explaining his need for “a letter … recognizing the fact that I am a presbyter in good standing and free to minister the gospel” and requesting permission to labor out of bounds in conducting a Bible study in Austin.

The Committee met on April 22, 1997, and voted to deny TE Rountree’s request. On April 28, 1997, the chairman of South Texas Presbytery Candidates and Credential Committee wrote the chairman of Covenant Presbytery Church Care Committee to request official notification that TE Rountree “is approved to labor outside of your bounds until such time as it is appropriate for us to issue a call to him.”

Covenant Presbytery at its May, 1997, stated meeting voted to delay action of the Church Care Committee’s recommendation until the Committee communicates with South Texas Presbytery and brings a recommendation based on that communication.

On September 24, 1997, Covenant Presbytery’s Church Care Committee sent a pastoral letter to TE Rountree urging him to allow Presbytery to divest him without censure. TE Rountree responded on October 1, 1997, advising that he would respond by the next meeting of Presbytery.

TE Rountree reports to Covenant Presbytery and the chairman of the Church Care Committee responds advising TE Rountree to request that he be dismissed to South Texas Presbytery.

At its stated meeting on February 3, 1998, Covenant Presbytery approved TE Rountree’s request to be dismissed to South Texas Presbytery pending his reception by that Presbytery.

Covenant Presbytery at its May stated meeting rescinded the transfer of TE Rountree and instructed its Church Care Committee to investigate the report of TE Rountree’s divorce. At the October 1998 stated meeting of Presbytery, the committee reported that it had not found the strong presumption of guilt necessary to bring charges against TE Rountree. A motion to transfer TE Rountree to South Texas Presbytery was made. Presbytery instead passed a substitute motion to begin the two meeting process of involuntary divestiture.

On January 11, 1999, TE Rountree submits a request to demit the ministry voluntarily. At its February 1999 stated meeting Covenant Presbytery begins the process of voluntary divestiture of TE Rountree.

On April 1, 1999, TE Rountree advises that his request to demit voluntarily was made under protest; and at its May 1999 stated meeting Presbytery again begins process of involuntary divestiture.

At its October 1999, stated meeting Covenant Presbytery rejects TE Rountree’s request to be granted emeritus and votes to divest him without censure. On October 26, 1999, TE Rountree appealed his divestiture.

On November 27, 2000, a Standing Judicial Commission Panel heard the appeal.

## II. STATEMENT OF THE ISSUE

Did Covenant Presbytery err in its divestiture without censure of TE Lawrence Rountree (BCO 34-10)?

## III. JUDGMENT

No. Presbytery acted within its constitutional prerogatives in divesting without censure TE Rountree.

## IV. REASONING AND OPINION

Ministers are required by the Book of Church Order to hold “his membership in the Presbytery within whose geographical bounds he resides unless there are reasons which are satisfactory to his Presbytery why he should not do so.” (BCO 13-2) That same section also provides that when a minister continues on the rolls “without a call to a particular work for a prolonged period, not exceeding three years, the procedure as set forth in BCO 34-10 shall be followed.”

TE Rountree had remained on the rolls of Covenant Presbytery without call and residing out of the geographical bounds of that presbytery since May of 1993 when his pastoral relationship with First Church of Indianola was dissolved. Despite various attempts to start works in Austin, TX, where he now resided, he was unable to do so.

In April of 1997 efforts were initiated to transfer TE Rountree to South Texas Presbytery. Those efforts did not produce results, principally because there was no particular work to which TE Rountree could be called.

In an effort to assist TE Rountree, Covenant Presbytery suggested that he voluntarily demit the ministry and seek readmission to South Texas or another presbytery at such time as he had a valid call to a particular ministry. After several false starts, Covenant Presbytery began the process of involuntary divestiture. TE Rountree then agreed to demit the ministry voluntarily, but subsequently decided that he was doing so under protest. At that point Covenant Presbytery began the two-meeting process of divestiture without censure.

After the first vote, TE Rountree requested that he be allowed to retire. Presbytery denied that request and completed the process of divestiture without censure.

In total, TE Rountree has been on the rolls of Covenant Presbytery without call for over seven years, four year longer than provided for in the Book of Church Order. To date, he still has no prospects for a call to a particular ministry. Residing out of the bounds of Covenant Presbytery, it is difficult for that presbytery to provide oversight and maintain pastoral relations. Covenant Presbytery therefore acted in accordance with the Book of Church Order to fulfill its responsibilities in BCO 13-2 and 34-10.

This opinion was written by John B. White, Jr., with the full concurrence of TEs Ben W. Konopa, Jr. and Dominic A. Aquila.

## 99-6
## APPEAL OF ROBERT M. SHIVE
## VS.
## CENTRAL CAROLINA PRESBYTERY

### I. SUMMARY OF THE FACTS

There was a prior Judicial Case (97-9) involving these same parties. Both at the presbytery level (ROC 1) and at the Judicial Panel hearing of this case (99-6) the parties, both Appellant and Appellee, agreed that the Record of this Case (ROC) rightly begins with the General Assembly’s adoption of the decision in SJC Case 97-9 at its 26th General Assembly June 30-July 3, 1998 (26th GA Min. p.134-144).

Any person reading this decision or the decision in SJC Case 97-9 should remember the important fact that Dr. Robert M. Shive is blind. All written material in these cases has to be read to him. It is an unfortunate fact which makes it more difficult for him to grasp the meaning of all written information in these cases.

The Statement of the Issues and Judgment in that Case (97-9) were as follows:

#### Statement of the Issues

1. Was it reversible error, under the particular circumstances, for the Session of Christ Covenant Church to have instituted judicial process against Dr. Shive prior to allowing personal contact between Dr. Shive and the ladies against whom he sinned in an effort to seek the reconciliation of these parties?

2. Did the Session of Christ Covenant Church commit reversible error in not recusing several members of the court who had stated an opinion concerning the case and was there a reversible prejudice present in this case?

3. Was it reversible error, under these particular circumstances, for the prosecutor of the charges filed against Dr. Shive, who was also a member of the court, to be involved in the court’s deliberations?

4. Were there procedural and substantive errors committed by Central Carolina Presbytery in this case which require a remand in this case?
5. Did Central Carolina Presbytery err by affirming the decision of Christ Covenant Church Session’s infliction upon Dr. Robert M. Shive the highest censure based on the Record of the Case?

## Judgment in the Case

1. No. The Session acted under its authority of BCO 31-7 when it instituted the judicial process without allowing a personal meeting between Dr. Shive and the ladies against whom he committed sin.
2. No. The record does not reflect that the actions mandating recusal under BCO 32-17 occurred. Furthermore, two of the three members of the court complained of by Dr. Shive voluntarily recused themselves from the court prior to the court’s final deliberations as to the judgment and censure to be imposed.
3. No. While we believe it was an error of judgment and an error of application of BCO 31-7, especially in light of BCO 32-19, for the prosecutor to have been a part of the court during its deliberations, we do not believe that this error requires a reversal of the judgment and a remand for a new trial in this particular case.
4. No. We do not find that errors were committed by Central Carolina Presbytery requiring reversal and remand.
5. Yes. Central Carolina Presbytery erred in affirming and the Session of Christ Covenant Church erred in going straight to the highest censure of excommunication in light of the lack of compelling evidence in the Record of the Case to reflect that Dr. Shive is contumacious or impenitent. See BCO 30-4 and 33-3. Pursuant to BCO 42-9, we reverse that portion of the judgment by the Session of Christ Covenant Church inflicting the censure of excommunication on Dr. Shive, and we remand the matter back to the Session of Christ Covenant Church for it to impose the censure of indefinite suspension from the Sacraments upon Dr. Robert M. Shive in accordance with BCO 36-5, and with the instruction that, until satisfactory evidence of repentance is given to the Session of Christ Covenant Church, to impose such conditions concerning Dr. Shive’s involvement in the life of Christ Covenant Church as the Session may find.”

Under BCO 14-7 this decision in SJC 97-9 was “binding and conclusive on the parties” who are the same parties as in this case. In SJC 97-9, the Appellant, Dr. Shive, alleged that the Session of Christ Covenant Church (Session) erred in 5 areas. These 5 areas are listed as “Issues” in the decision (supra). This SJC 97-9 decision sustained the verdicts of the Session and the Presbytery in the first four allegations and issues. In the 5th area, the decision in SJC 97-9 sustained in part Appellant’s specification of error. It reversed “that portion of the judgment by Session of Christ Covenant inflicting the censure of excommunication.” The decision did sustain all other portions of the 5th area, which included the verdict of guilty against Dr. Shive in 4 areas. (ROC 33 and 42-43)

On January 9, 1998, the full Standing Judicial Commission (SJC) reviewed and affirmed the decision of the Panel in SJC 97-9, which was later in June-July 1998 adopted by the General Assembly (GA).

Less than one week after the Full SJC reviewed and affirmed the Panel’s decision, the Session began to implement the decision on January 16, 1998 by assigning a 4 man (later 2 added) Discipline Commission (DC) to work with Dr. Shive (ROC 26). The Session immediately followed the recommendation of the SJC by reducing the penalty on Dr. Shive from excommunication to “indefinite suspension from the Sacraments.” On March 6, 1998 the DC informed Dr. Shive (who is blind), in writing of this action by the Session, to-wit:

“Whereas you, Dr. Robert M. Shive, member of Christ Covenant Presbyterian Church, are convicted by sufficient proof of the sins of sexual immorality, abuse, licentiousness, premeditated sexual exploitation, lying and bearing false witness and scandalous living, we the Christ Covenant Session, in the name and by the authority of the Lord Jesus Christ, do now declare you suspended from the Sacraments of the Church, until you give satisfactory evidence of repentance. (ROC 53) (Emphasis added).

In the 16 months from January, 1998 to April, 1999 the DC met 11 times, had numerous communications with Dr. Shive, and met formally with him 4 times (ROC pages 42-77, summarized ROC pages 16-18, and ROC 104).

In the course of these meetings and communications with Dr. Shive, it became obvious early on (from such documents as the April 1998 letter of Dr. Shive to Mr. Bernie Lawrence, Chairman of the DC (ROC 56-57) in which Dr. Shive refers to his meeting with the DC on March 21, 1998) that there was a “fundamental disagreement between you and me and that obvious sticking point is in the interpretation and intent of the SJC final document.” (ROC 56).

In this letter to the DC, Dr. Shive refers to a telephone conversation with the Chairman of the DC in which the Chairman indicated he had telephone conversations with RE Tom Leopard and TE Dominic Aquila (SJC Panel Member in SJC 97-9) and with Dr. Paul Gilchrist, Stated Clerk of the GA. Because of this “fundamental difference” between the Session’s and DC’s interpretation of the SJC decision in Case 97-9 and Dr. Shive’s interpretation, the Chairman of the DC sought assistance from these individuals as to the proper interpretation of the decision. Dr. Shive interpreted the SJC decision in Case 97-9 as reversing the verdicts of guilt on all 4 charges (ROC 56-57).

Writing to Mr. Bernie Lawrence, Dr. Shrive stated:

“You clearly indicated to me that you believed the SJC’s document of January 1988 did in no way countermand Christ Covenant’s position on my guilt.” (ROC 56).
