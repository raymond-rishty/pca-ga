Case 2001-9
APPEAL OF RE CHARLES KIM
VS.
KOREAN CAPITAL PRESBYTERY

#### I. Statement of the Facts

1. Beginning in 1999, division developed within the leadership of Korean
Presbyterian Church Washington (KPCW). In response to the turmoil and the
filing of 16 petitions, references and charges, Korean Capital Presbytery
(KCP) suspended the session and appointed a commission to act for the
session.
2. Complaints against KCP for its action were filed and subsequently RE
Kim participated in efforts to call a special meeting of KCP for among other
things the purpose of dissolving the commission.
3. Charges against RE Kim for allegedly participating in an
“unconstitutional” meeting of Presbytery were filed on December 22, 2000.
4. KCP did have a properly called special meeting on January 8, 2001, at
which it appointed a commission to deal with the charges against RE Kim.
5. The Commission summoned RE Kim to appear and answer charges on
February 3 and 17, 2001 but he did not respond.
6. On March 12, 2001 the commission heard testimony and received
evidence, found RE Kim guilty of the charges of schism and contumacy and
excommunicated him. The sentence was finalized on March 26, 2001.
7. At the 18th Stated Meeting of KCP on April 2, 2001 the report of the
commission along with the excommunication of RE Kim was approved.
8. On April 16, 2001 RE Kim appealed his excommunication to the
Standing Judicial Commission.

9. A five-man panel of the SJC heard the appealon December4, 2001 at
the McLean Presbyterian Church in McLean, VA.

#### II. Statement of the Issue

Was Presbytery and/or its Special Judicial Commission appointed on
January 8, 2001 the proper court to assume original jurisdiction to receive and
adjudicate the charges against the Appellant Charles C. Kim?

#### III. Judgment

No, such original jurisdiction should be with the Session; therefore,
the Presbytery’s action and/or the Special Judicial Commission of Presbytery’s
actions in this matter are reversed.

#### IV. Reasoning and Opinion

The court of original jurisdiction for communicant members inany
local church is the Session of that church. The Appellant in this case was a
member of the Korean Presbyterian Church of Washington and thus, any
judicial action or censure against a member must originate within the court of
original jurisdiction, the Session. Since neither the KCP nor its Commission
was the proper court of original jurisdiction, the KCP Commission’s action to
adjudicate charges against the Appellant was contrary to the constitutional
standards, and the action of the lower court is reversed.
The Statement of the Facts of this case was prepared by RE John
White. The Issue and Judgment were prepared by the entire Panel of five
members. The Reasoning and Opinion was written by TE James Smith with
concurrence by TE Charles McGowan, RE Jack Williamson, RE Grant
McCabe and RE John White.

Case 2001-10
APPEAL OF RE CHARLES KIM
VS.
KOREAN CAPITAL PRESBYTERY

#### I. Statement of the Facts

1. Beginning in 1999, division developed within the leadership of Korean
Presbyterian Church Washington (KPCW). In response to the turmoil and the
filing of 16 petitions, references and charges, Korean Capital Presbytery
(KCP) suspended the session and appointed a commission to act for the
session.
2. On September 7, 2000 REs Charles Kim and Moon K. Ham held a
session meeting (without a quorum) and voted to replace KPCW trustees.
3. On September 15, 2000 certain officers of KPCW including RE Kim
filed in civil court a petition for appointing church trustees. Said petition
indicated that the trustees were determined in the September 7, 2000 session
meeting.
On November 14, 2000 five KPCW members filed a written
accusation against seven KPCW officers and members including RE Kim for
replacing and registering the church trustees improperly.
4. KCP, at a called special meeting on January 8, 2001, appointed a
commission to deal with the charges against RE Kim.
5. The Commission summoned RE Kim to appear and answer charges on
February 3 and 17, 2001 but he did not respond.
6. On March 12, 2001 the commission heard testimony and received
evidence, found RE Kim guilty of
a) contumacy

- **b)** the charges of church division and church destruction by holding an unconstitutional meeting and
- **c)** making a false statement on a official document filed with the civil court.

The commission inflicted the censure of excommunication.
7. At the 18th Stated Meeting of KCP on April 2, 2001 the report of
the commission along with the excommunication of RE Kim was
approved.
8. On April 16, 2001 RE Kim appealed his excommunication to the
Standing Judicial Commission.
9. Appellant and Appellee waived hearing of the case and a five-man
panel of the SJC decided the appeal on December 5, 2001 at the McLean
Presbyterian Church in McLean, VA.

#### II. Statement of the Issue

Was Presbytery and/or its Special Judicial Commission appointed on
January 8, 2001 the proper court to assume original jurisdiction to receive and
adjudicate the charges against the Appellant Charles C. Kim?
