Case 2001-3
APPEAL OF TE SUNG KEON KIM
VS.
KOREAN CAPITAL PRESBYTERY

#### I. Statement of the Facts

1. The Appellant TE Sung Keon Kim was called to be a assistant pastor
by the Session of Korean Presbyterian Church Washington (KPCW) and began
his ministry on July 15, 2001.
2. Prior to his arrival, division developed within the leadership of KPCW.
3. The situation escalated to the point that there were two groups both
claiming to be the true Session of KPCW. There was reported physical violence
at the church and police were called to resolve disputes. The congregation was
advised by civil authority that if the turmoil continued, the church might be
prohibited from meeting. A total of 16 references, petitions, accusations and
complaints were filed with KCP regarding the situation at KPCW.

4. To deal with these 16 petitions, references, complaints and
accusations, on October 9, 2000 at the reconvened portion of its 17th Stated
Meeting KCP appointed a commission to “suspend the functions of the KPCW
church Session and have the Presbytery replace the functions and report to the
Presbytery meeting in three months.”
5. The Presbytery Commission, known as the Administrative
Commission and the Judicial Commission (it had been assigned both
functions), began its work on October 16, 2000 and commenced to act for the
session and as a judicial commission.
6. Beginning with a meeting on September 10, 2000 TE Sung Keon Kim
served as the moderator of one of the KPCW sessions.
7. On December 4, 2000 charges were fded with the judicial
commission charging TE Kim with numerous actions including

- **a)** “cooperating with the illegal session as moderator”,
- **b)** participation in a meeting where one of the sessions decided to withdraw KPCW from presbytery without congregational approval,
- **c)** disruption of worship services and
- **d)** obstruction of the commission in its official duties.

8. The commission on November 4, 2000 summoned and admonished
TE Kim for divisive activities.
9. TE Kim was summoned to appear for trial on December 30, 2000. TE
Kim did not respond to that summons. He was judged to be guilty of heresy
and schism (BCO 34-5) and was suspended from ministry for five years.
10. At a called meeting of KCP on January 8, 2001 Presbytery approved
the actions of its commission and upheld the suspension of TE Kim.
11. On January 11, 2001 TE Kim appealed his suspension to the Standing Judicial Commission.
12. A five-man panel of the SJC heard the appeal on December 4, 2001 at
the McLean Presbyterian Church in McLean, VA.

#### II. Statement of the Issue

Did the Judicial Commission follow BCO procedures in instituting and
conducting judicial process against the appellant?

#### III. Judgment

No, the commission did not follow the procedures of BCO 32 and 34;
and, therefore, the case is remanded to Presbytery for a new trial in compliance
with BCO 32 and 34.

#### IV. Reasoning and Opinion

It is clear that no indictment was ever served upon the accused. It is
not clear whether or not an indictment was ever prepared. Further, there is
nothing in the Record of the Case to show what occurred when the judgment
was entered by the lower court. It appears to have been entered by default
