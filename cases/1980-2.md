A. BOC 36-11 disqualifies a member of a court who has given testimony in a case from sitting as a judge if either party makes objection. Mr. Dunkerley in effect, by his accusatory statements about Appellant was giving testimony against him. Appellant objected to his voting.

B. BOC 33-19 excludes from sitting in judgment on a case one who has acted as counsel for the accused. Mr. Dunkerley so acted when he repeatedly defended and spoke on behalf of the session on the floor of Presbytery.

C. BOC 40-2 states that “When the proceedings of a lower court are brought before a higher court, the members of the lower court shall not lose the right to sit, deliberate and vote in the higher court, except in cases of appeal or complaint.” (Emphasis added) Mr. Dunkerley voted against my appeal (twice) and also against reconsidering it as a complaint.

2. Mr. Dunkerley prosecuted the case before hand by virtue of his many prejudicial statements against the Appellant as a person from the floor before the Presbytery voted. He charged the Appellant with the following offenses;

A. Accusing the innocent
B. Refusing to stand trial
C. Holding heretical views.
D. Renouncing the authority of the session
E. Having a past record of stirring up trouble in churches.

These unfounded charges may well have persuaded the Presbytery adversely in regard to the Appellant’s cause.

3. Mr. Dunkerley intimidated and applied pressure to the Presbytery before they voted by suggesting that a vote against McIlwain might well result in McIlwain withdrawing from the P.C.A.

4. McIlwain failed to abide by BOC 43-5 by NOT sending the record of the case up to Presbytery.

### III. THE BIBLE TEACHES THAT ACCESS TO THE HIGHEST COURT SHOULD ALWAYS BE AVAILABLE

1. Members of God’s Kingdom always have access to God’s Throne, the highest court in the universe, through prayer, and with a Divine advocate.

2. In ancient Israel there was always access to the highest court in the land, first, with Moses in that seat of judgment, and later, when kings reigned and ruled over the highest court in the land.

Yea, the Bible everywhere teaches that God loves Justice and Judgment and that it is wrong to pervert a just cause. Christ particularly denounced the legalistic ways of his opponents who sought by the traditions of men to escape their responsibilities and to nullify the law of God.

IV. Wherefore, Appellant respectfully prays this court to take whatsoever measures it deems appropriate, and, in the interest of truth and justice, to rectify the grievances stated above.

### E. JUDICIAL CASES — COMPLAINTS

**Case 2: Complaint of Teaching Elder Rod Mays Against New River Presbytery**

Nov. 22, 1979

Mr. Bob Kresge
Stated Clerk, New River Presbytery
Presbyterian Church in America

I, Rod Mays, a teaching elder of the New River Presbytery of the Presbyterian Church in America do hereby issue a complaint to the General Assembly concerning the failure of the New River Presbytery to deal with one of its ministers (Mr. Linwood Wilkes) who was reported to be guilty of writing slanderous letters concerning another minister in New River Presbytery, (Mr. Bill Fitzhenry).

Whereas, these accusations of slander were reported to the Presbytery’s committee on Ministers and Candidates,

Whereas, that committee moved to take action against Mr. Wilkes,

Whereas, before the accusations were brought before Presbytery, a motion was moved, seconded, and approved to drop the discussion of matters relating to the accusations of Mr. Wilkes,

Therefore, I do protest and complain (BCO-44) about this failure of presbytery to deal with reported scandalous activity involving one of its ministers.

See § 8-13, 2; p. 62, and § 8-84; p. 111.

Rod S. Mays
