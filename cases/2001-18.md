Case 2001-18
APPEAL OF TE SANG SOO RYOO
VS.
KOREAN CAPITAL PRESBYTERY

#### I. Statement of the Facts

1. The Appellant TE Sang Soo Ryoo was called to be an assistant pastor
by the Session of Korean Presbyterian Church Washington in 1998.
2. Beginning in 1999, division developed within the leadership of Korean
Presbyterian Church Washington (KPCW). Initially the dispute centered
principally on questions about the finances of Washington Theological
Seminary which was connected with KPCW.
3. On December 30, 1999 the Session of KPCW submitted to Korean
Capital Presbytery (KCP) a reference asking KCP to help the session address
the financial question referred in item #1.
4. The situation escalated to the point that there were two groups both
claiming to be the true Session of KPCW. There was reported physical
violence at the church and police were called to resolve disputes. The
congregation was advised by the civil authority that if the turmoil continued,
the church might be prohibited from meeting. A total of 16 references,
petitions, accusations and complaints were filed with KCP regarding the
situation at KPCW.
5. To deal with these 16 petitions, references, complaints and
accusations, on October 9, 2000 at the reconvened portion of its 17th Stated
Meeting KCP appointed a commission to “suspend the functions of the KPCW
church Session and have the Presbytery replace the functions and report to the
Presbytery meeting in three months.”

6. The Presbytery Commission, known as the Administrative Commission
and the Judicial Commission (it had been assigned both functions), began its
work on October 16, 2000 and commenced to act for the session and as a
judicial commission.
7. Turmoil continued at KPCW and the commission acting as that
session sought and obtained a civil court order to enforce its administration of
the church.
8. The commission also called three congregational meetings including
one that dissolved the official relationships with two ruling elders.
9. Under these circumstances, TE Ryoo participated in efforts to call a
special meeting of KCP. When no meeting was scheduled, TE Ryoo
participated in the call of a meeting in accordance with BCO 13-12.
10. Charges against TE Ryoo for participating in the call of an
“unconstitutional” meeting of Presbytery were filed on December 22, 2000.
11. KCP did have a special meeting on January 8, 2001, at which it
appointed a commission to deal with the charges against TE Ryoo.
12. The Commission summoned TE Ryoo to appear and answer charges
on Februaiy 3, 17 and March 3, 2001 but he did not respond.
13. On March 12, 2001 the commission heard testimony and received
evidence and found TE Ryoo guilty of the charges and imposed on him a
three-year suspension from the sacraments.
14. At the 18th Stated Meeting of KCP on April 2, 2001 the report of the
commission along with the suspension of TE Ryoo from the sacraments was
approved
15. On April 25, 2001 TE Ryoo appealed his suspension from the
sacraments to the Standing Judicial Commission.
16. A five-man panel of the SJC heard the appeal on December 4, 2001 at
the McLean Presbyterian Church in McLean, VA.

#### II. Statement of the Issue

Did the Judicial Commission follow BCO procedures in instituting and
conducting judicial process against the appellant?

#### III. Judgment

No, the commission did not follow the procedures of BCO 32, 34; and,
therefore, the case is remanded to Presbytery for a new trial in compliance
with BCO 32, 34.
