## Opening of the report

> **Whereas** Matthew (3:11), Mark (1:8), Luke (3:16), and John (1:33) prophesy that Christ will baptize His Church with the Holy Spirit, and;
> **Whereas** Acts 1:5 definitely says that the outpouring of the Holy Spirit at the Day of Pentecost fulfills this prophecy, and;
> **Whereas** in Acts 11:16 Peter identifies Cornelius’ Holy Spirit baptism with that of the Day of Pentecost, and;
> **Whereas** I Corinthians 12:13 (cf Galatians 3:27) categorically states that all Christians have been baptized with the Holy Spirit;
> (These seven references being the only occurrences of the phrase, ‘baptized with the Spirit’, in the Scriptures.)
> Therefore, be it concluded that:
> 1. To be baptized by the Spirit is to be joined to the body of Christ by His Spirit and therefore is experienced by all believers upon conversion.
> 2. Neo-Pentecostalism is in error in teaching that all Christians have not been baptized with the Spirit at conversion.
> 3. Neo-Pentecostalism is in error in teaching that tongue-speaking is the evidence of the baptism of the Holy Spirit.
> 4. It is unscriptural to pray for another Pentecost in the same sense in which it is unscriptural to pray for another Calvary.
> 5. We urge our brethren to be on guard against the teaching that a Christian should seek the baptism of the Holy Spirit to obtain a deeper Christian experience.
> 6. Further, we recommend the following bibliography:
> - **a.** Anthony A. Hoekema, Holy Scripture Baptism
> - **b.** Anthony A. Hoekema, What About Tongue-Speaking?
> - **c.** Frederick Bruner, A Theology of the Holy Spirit
> 7. This is not intended to be a complete doctrinal statement on the Charismatic Movement. We are not saying anything about the validity of the “gifts of the Spirit” or “filling of the Holy Spirit” in this resolution.
